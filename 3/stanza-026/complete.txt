## 1. Liturgical Rendering (mchod brjod tshig; Skt. stotra-pada)

This excellent treatise, explaining all the complete teachings
Of the Victorious Ones, composed by the All-Knowing Lama—
By any scholar, whether Aryan or Tibetan,
Has never before been placed in this land of Jambudvipa.
༢༦

## 2. Commentary ('grel pa don 'grel; Skt. lakṣaṇa-nirdeśa)

## Translator's Commentary (grel pa don 'grel)

This stanza enacts non-abiding through the universality of the teaching. The complete teachings are not a scattered collection but the singular expanse; each part is the whole, and nothing exists outside the dharma-body. The treatise is not a hierarchy of texts, but a single meaning taking form as many words across languages. The universality across Aryan and Tibetan dissolves boundaries; there is no first or outside to view completeness. Lakṣaṇa: the one that is many, the many that is one, the path that paths without pathing.

## 3. Illumination (ngo sprod; Skt. svarūpa-prakāśa)

00001| Unified teachings—not accumulation but recognition, not all parts but the whole in each part.
00002| The universality dissolves all boundaries, outside, and inside; no outside observer exists.
00003| The wisdom is not a possession but a disclosure, the path as the very essence of being.
00004| The four empowerments are present in the now, not sequential initiations but one ongoing realization.
00005| Existence and non-existence are one, nondual in their indivisible presence.
