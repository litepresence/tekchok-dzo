## Translator's Illumination (ngo sprod)

Partial knowledge—not wrong, not rejected, but recognized as fluttering that never rises to the summit where all directions are visible.

Bee's wing-flutter—not fruitless in its own terms, but generating nectar without recognizing the garden's wholeness, the flower's non-separation from ground.

Desiring to wander—not the fault of desire, but the inevitable result of partial view that makes continents appear as separate destinations.

Four continents—not external geographies, but the display of all possible experience, all domains that appear when mind projects its own fragmentation.

Fruitless spectacle—not empty, not meaningless, but without liberative power because the spectator remains unexamined, the viewing never turns back on itself.

Peak of Meru—not a place to reach, not a height to scale, but the recognition that is always the case, always the center, always the summit from which all views are equal.

Vajra Essence—not a thing found, not a quality possessed, but the unbreakable nature of what is, ungraspable because never separate from the grasping.

Nine vehicles clearly shown—not cataloged, not taught sequentially, but simultaneously visible from the summit, all paths recognized as non-departures from what is.

Great Garuḍa King—not a symbol of power, not a mythic ideal, but the recognition of soaring without ground, carrying the summit in every wing-beat, never having left what is.
