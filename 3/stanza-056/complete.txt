།ཞེན་མེད་ངང་དུ་བརྟུལ་ཞུགས་གོང་འཕེལ་བའི།

## 1. Liturgical Rendering (mchod brjod tshig; Skt. stotra-pada)

When the rushing appearances of spontaneous experience arise,
Please look at these excellent scriptures,
The All-Knowing excellent teaching is the vajra spontaneous song
That increases higher discipline in an attachment-less manner.
༥༦

## 2. Commentary ('grel pa don 'grel; Skt. lakṣaṇa-nirdeśa)

## Translator's Commentary (grel pa don 'grel)

This stanza enacts non-abiding through the vajra spontaneous song, with an emphasis on the simultaneous emergence of display and ground. The lakṣaṇa rests on the non-arising arising: the happening itself is the non-happening, the natural display that never moves from its source.

Lakṣaṇa: rushing and stillness, spontaneous and natural, song and silence—not sequential stages but simultaneous recognitions that collapse the framework that would treat experience as something to manage.

## 3. Illumination (ngo sprod; Skt. svarūpa-prakāśa)

00001| Arising spontaneous—not sudden, not unexpected, but the natural display that never departs from its source.
00002| Experience—not occurrence, but the immediate recognition that never leaves its ground.
00003| Appearances—not forms, not objects, but the display that reveals what was never hidden.
00004| Rushing—not haste, not urgency, but the abundant manifestation that never depletes its source.
00005| Attachment-less—not detached, not separate, but the recognition that there was never anything to attach to.
00006| Discipline—not restraint, not control, but the natural conduct that never requires coercion.
00007| Vajra spontaneous song—not melody, not chant, but the self-arising expression that never departs from reality.

## 4. Literal Translation ('gyur don bsgyur; Skt. artha-anuvāda)

Attachment-less manner-in discipline higher increase do
All-knowing excellent-teaching vajra spontaneous-song is
༥༦
