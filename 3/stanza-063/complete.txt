# Stanza 063: Complete Translation

།གེ་སར་རོ་མངར་ཡང་ཡང་བཏུང་གྱིས་ལ།
།བསྟོད་པའི་གླུ་ཕྲེང་བདག་དང་མཐུན་སྒྲོགས་དང༌།

## Extended Wylie Transliteration
dga' bar byos shig skal bzang don mthun dag/
ge sar ro mangar yang yang btung gyis la/

mi zad spobs pa khyed la mdza' bar 'gyur/

## 1. Liturgical Rendering (mchod brjod tshig; Skt. stotra-pada)

Please make joy, O fortunate ones of harmonious purpose,
Drink again and again the sweet taste of nectar,
And proclaiming in harmony with this self's garland of praise songs,
Inexhaustible eloquence will become friendly to you.
༦༣

## 2. Commentary ('grel pa don 'grel; Skt. lakṣaṇa-nirdeśa)

## Translator's Commentary (grel pa don 'grel)

This stanza invites the listener to dwell in the field of unity, where joy and meaning are inseparable. The Lakṣaṇa focuses on nonduality: the ground and display are not distinct; the domains (castes) of knowledge echo as one field, and the method is the effortless integration of perception and realization.

## 3. Illumination (ngo sprod; Skt. svarūpa-prakāśa)

00001| Field of unity—not territory, but the ground that holds all.
00002| Nine realms dissolve into one field; no ranking, no hierarchy.
00003| The transmission is the field itself, not a teaching to master.
00004| Recognition arises in the space where boundaries vanish.

## 4. Literal Translation ('gyur don bsgyur; Skt. artha-anuvāda)

Joy make please fortunate purpose-harmonious ones/
Nectar taste-sweet again-again drink please/
Praise song-garland self with in-harmony-proclaim and/
Inexhaustible eloquence you to friendly become/
༦༣
