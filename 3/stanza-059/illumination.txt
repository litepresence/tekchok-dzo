## Translator's Illumination (ngo sprod)

Reality-grasping—not belief, not conviction, but the fundamental assumption that something is truly existent.

Delusion—not confusion, not ignorance, but the misrecognition of what is always already clear.

Very firm—not stubborn, not rigid, but the deeply entrenched habit that seems impossible to uproot.

True grasping—not understanding, not comprehension, but the conviction that something is ultimately real.

Heap of bubbles—not many, not multitude, but the aggregation of what was never substantial.

Illusion—not deception, not trickery, but the display that reveals there was never anything displayed.

Pith instructions—not secrets, not hidden teachings, but the direct pointing to what was never concealed.
