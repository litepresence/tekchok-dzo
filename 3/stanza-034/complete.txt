## 1. Liturgical Rendering (mchod brjod tshig; Skt. stotra-pada)

If one knows this meaning, the Vajra Essence path,
The profound key of the secret Heart Essence like a heart—
Other scholarly treatises that do not show this meaning,
Though their bodies may be beautiful, are like corpses without hearts.
༣༤

## 2. Commentary ('grel pa don 'grel; Skt. lakṣaṇa-nirdeśa)

## Translator's Commentary (grel pa don 'grel)

This stanza uses the heart/corpse metaphor to articulate non-dual awareness: the naked heart is neither absence nor mere presence; the set of terms points to a unity that cannot be captured by ordinary polarities. The Vajra Essence is not an external tradition but the living form of realization appearing as words.

The line about the “other scholarly treatises” shows that superiority and competition drop away when the truth of non-dual awareness is seen. The heart and corpse metaphor reveals the futility of aesthetics as a source of truth; beauty and form do not make the truth, they express it when seen through the veil of conceptuality.

Lakṣaṇa: the knowing that knows without knower, the essence not essential, the path that paths without pathing; the heart that hearts without heart.

## 3. Illumination (ngo sprod; Skt. svarūpa-prakāśa)

00001| Naked straight awareness—not uncovered, not direct, but the naked that is the awareness.
00002| Omniscient Lama's mind—not all-knowing, not intellect, but the mind that perceives omniscience within.
00003| Heart essence—not text, not teaching, but the heart that contains essence.
00004| Form—not shape, not appearance, but the form as awareness.
00005| The path—not method, not sequence, but the essence that is path.
00006| The great door—non-dual, no boundary, all doors together open.

## 4. Literal Translation ('gyur don bsgyur; Skt. artha-anuvāda)

Meaning this know-if Vajra Essence path
Heart Essence secret's profound key heart like
Meaning this not show scholar's treatise other
Body itself beautiful-yet heartless corpse with like
༣༤
