## 1. Liturgical Rendering (mchod brjod tshig; Skt. stotra-pada)

When pride, doubt, and grasping-grasped arise,
Please look at these excellent scriptures,
The All-Knowing excellent teaching is the object that cuts demons
That produces the hero's confidence of discipline play.
༥༨

## 2. Commentary ('grel pa don 'grel; Skt. lakṣaṇa-nirdeśa)

## Translator's Commentary (grel pa don 'grel)

This stanza moves the conversation from the polemic of pride and doubt to the balance of non-abiding through nondual equality. The Lakṣaṇa highlights that pride, doubt, and grasping-grasped arise within a framework that is dissolved by equality—there is no separate ground for either side to stand on.

## 3. Illumination (ngo sprod; Skt. svarūpa-prakāśa)

00001| Pride as the subtle elevation that hides equality is seen and dissolved in the eyes of non-duality.
00002| Doubt dissolves when the ground of all phenomena is recognized as empty of intrinsic self.
00003| Grasping-grasped is revealed as the nondual dance of perceiver and perceived.
00004| The path is not a route but the direct recognition of non-separation.
00005| The guidance is in recognizing the ground of all, not in cultivating new states.

## 4. Literal Translation ('gyur don bsgyur; Skt. artha-anuvāda)
