# Stanza 066: Complete Translation

## Original Tibetan
།བདག་ཀྱང་དེང་ནས་ཀུན་མཁྱེན་བརྒྱུད་བཅས་ཀྱི།
།སྨིན་གྲོལ་རྒྱལ་ཐབས་རྒྱས་པའི་སྒོ་སྲང་ནས།
།དད་གསུམ་སློང་ཕོར་གཏོང་བ་མེད་ལྡན་པས།
།བལྟ་བའི་སྐལ་བཟང་འཁོར་བའི་མཐའ་མར་སྨོན།
༦༦

## Extended Wylie Transliteration
bdag kyang deng nas kun mkhyen brgyud bcas kyi/

## 1. Liturgical Rendering (mchod brjod tshig; Skt. stotra-pada)

From the door-threshold of the vast methods to ripen and liberate
Of the all-knowing lineage with myself also,
Having the three faiths without giving up the begging bowl,
I aspire at the end of samsara for the fortune to look.
༦༦

## 2. Commentary ('grel pa don 'grel; Skt. lakṣaṇa-nirdeśa)

## Translator's Commentary (grel pa don 'grel)

This stanza extends non-abiding through the field of unity. The ground is not a place but the recognition that all realms are a single field where awareness and recognition are indistinguishable. Lakṣaṇa: unity of field; boundaryless ground; transmission through syntax rather than naming.

## 3. Illumination (ngo sprod; Skt. svarūpa-prakāśa)

00001| Field and ground merge into one field of recognition.
00002| Unity beyond multiplicity; there is no boundary.
00003| Transmission occurs as the field, not as a teaching.
00004| Recognition arises where boundaries dissolve.

## 4. Literal Translation ('gyur don bsgyur; Skt. artha-anuvāda)

Self also now from all-knowing lineage with of/
Ripen liberate victorious methods vast of door threshold from/
Faith three begging-bowl give without having with/
Look at fortunate samsara of end at aspire/
༦༦
