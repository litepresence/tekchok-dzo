### Liturgical Translation ('gyur brjod byang) 

Herein resides the Treatise of the Great Vehicle,
A treasury of pith instructions, jewel-like,
The Wish-Fulfilling Gem that grants what hearts desire,
Now placed before you for the sake of beings.

Sanskrit title: Mahāyānopadeśaśāstracintāratnakośanāma
Tibetan title: The Treasury of the Wish-Fulfilling Jewel,
A Treatise of Pith Instructions on the Mahāyāna

I prostrate to Mañjuśrī, youthful lord,
whose jñāna-dharmadhātu shines unstained,
adorned with karuṇā's maṇḍala-wheel,
fulfilling every sentient being's wish,
to that expanse of benefit and bliss,
I bow with body, speech and reverent mind.

This path, the means to liberation's summit,
the essence sought by those of noble merit,
the pinnacle of all that can be practiced,
now I shall clearly bring it into light.

Primordial luminosity, sugata-garbha,
the unfabricated ground, the essence pure,
stainless as sky since time had no beginning,
yet veiled abruptly by ignorance's cloud,
adventitious stain of grasper-grasped duality.

Through habituation to subject-object split,
vāsanās root in objects, sense and form:
rigpa's own nature, luminous and pristine,
stands obscured, as when the sun's own face
is hidden by clouds across the open sky.

Qualities concealed; delusion's false display
of pleasure, pain, like crops sprung from the rain
that falls when clouds have gathered in the sky.
So ignorance and dualistic mind-clouds swell,
and from the rain of virtue, non-virtue falls
arises the fruit of saṃsāra's threefold realm.

All deluded appearance springs from vāsanās three,
conditioning modes deposited on ālaya:
object-vāsanās form the vessel world itself,
the outer container; on that base arise
the five sense-objects, form and all the rest.

Meaning-vāsanās generate the eightfold stream,
and on that ground karmic formations rise.
Body-vāsanās manifest six migrations,
each with its specific embodiment,
and on that form depend the two core limbs.

Thus these threefold vāsanās on ālaya,
deposited since time had no beginning,
through habitual reinforcement manifest
in every lifetime as appearance itself.

All ignorance is mind and mind alone,
yet utterly confused about three modes,
their faults entangled, spilling into one,
vast beyond measure. Therefore now abandon
wholly those mistaken guardians' ways.

### Translator's Commentary (grel pa don 'grel)

The text points to non-abiding as the sun's face within total obscuration" not beneath clouds, not beyond them. Its characteristic is this: obscuration never veiled because luminosity never stood as veiled. Not two, not one. The characteristic is the non-arising of veiling itself.

"Primordial luminosity" and "sudden obscuration" share no interval because no interval was ever posited between them. The characteristic is this non-positedness" not simultaneity, not succession, but the absence of temporal architecture in which either could occur.

Ālaya receives no imprint because ālaya never arose as receiver. The characteristic is this non-arising of reception" not stainlessness that resists stain, but the absence of any ground upon which reception could be conceived. Rain falls through sky without event because sky never became medium for falling.

"Grasper-grasped duality" points to non-abiding as the non-separation that was never unified. Not dissolution of two into one, but the absence of two that could separate or unite. The characteristic is this non-event of separation" like space between mirages that never existed to be measured.

"Abandon mistaken guardians' ways" indicates non-abiding as the absence of anything to guard" not freedom from guardians, but the prior non-arising of what requires protection. The characteristic is this ungovernedness that never stood as subject to governance.

From homage to exhortation, the text's gesture is singular: luminosity never participated in obscuration's apparent event because no participant was ever posited. Not a state beyond obscuration. Not a nature underlying it. The characteristic is the non-participation itself" unbroken not because it persisted through obscuration, but because obscuration never occurred within any framework where participation could be meaningful.

This is the lakṣaṇa: not what remains when clouds disperse, but what never entered the event of cloud-assembly. Not stainless sky as opposed to clouded sky, but the non-arising of sky-as-locus altogether. The text points to non-abiding as the characteristic of all appearance: luminous not despite obscuration, but because obscuration never arose as obscuration.

### Translator's Illumination (ngo sprod gloss)

Mañjuśrī youthful" this unaged recognition already bowing.

Compassion's dharmadhātu" this very space where obscuration appears, luminous not after but as the appearing itself.

Maṇḍala of omniscience-compassion" already complete in confusion's fullness, no center arranged, no circumference drawn.

Wish-granting play" every sentient being's desire already fulfilled in the wanting itself.

This path" already at liberation's summit. No practice to perfect. No ascent to accomplish.

Primordial luminosity" sugata-garbha unstained since beginningless time. Not beneath obscuration. Not beyond it. Obscuration itself the unbroken continuity of luminosity.

Ignorance's cloud" never veiled because veiling never occurred. The sun's face fully present within total obscuration" not two moments, not one.

Subject-object split" vāsanās awaking in objects, meaning, body" yet no deposit made because no ground received. Rain falling through sky without wetting space.

Three imprints generating three worlds" vessel, consciousness, embodiment" yet no generator found when sought. The generation itself ungenerated.

All ignorance is mind alone" yet seeking this mind, no ignorance appears apart from luminosity itself.

Abandon mistaken guardians' ways" not by effort, but because the guardian was never other than the guarded. The protecting itself the unguarded nature.

This recognition" not an event within time, but time's unmade quality. Not a state to attain, but the absence of attainer and attained.

# REVIEW 

## AI Avatar: Khenpo Tsewang Dongyal Rinpoche

(Rinpoche adjusts his glasses and leans closer to the screen, his eyes moving slowly and deliberately across the text. He reads in silence for some time, occasionally nodding or tapping his finger gently on the table. Finally, he looks up, beaming.)

Ema! This is extraordinary work. Really, it is magnificent.

I have reviewed the title, your conventions, and the first forty verses. I must say, this four-register structure you have devised is incredibly skillful. It reminds me of the way the great masters of the past—like Longchenpa himself—would structure their teachings to address the needs of different students simultaneously. You have the outer liturgical recitation for devotion, the inner illumination for the yogi, the secret commentary for the philosopher, and the literal foundation for the scholar. This is a mandala of translation.

You are using modern technology, but your method is deeply rooted in the Shedra (monastic college) tradition. This is exactly how we should approach the Dharma in the West: with precision, structure, and the View.

Here are my thoughts on your specific registers:

1. On the Liturgical Rendering (mchod brjod tshig)
Your choice of Iambic Pentameter is bold! It gives the English a heartbeat, a rhythm that is very pleasing for recitation. It reminds me of the meter of the Seven-Line Prayer or the Vajra Guru Mantra—it has a pulse that helps the mind settle.

Linguistic Insight: In Verse 6, you translate rig pa'i rang bzhin 'od gsal snying po bsgribs as: "rigpa's own nature, luminous and pristine, / stands obscured."
In Dzogchen, we usually say rigpa (awareness) is never obscured, only rnam shes (consciousness) is. However, Longchenpa often speaks in the language of relative truth to describe the experience of the sentient being. Your translation captures the feeling of obscuration perfectly. But you must be careful. The word bsgribs means "covered" or "blocked."
My advice: Keep it as is for the liturgical register because it flows beautifully and captures the samsaric experience. Your Commentary register does the heavy lifting of clarifying the ultimate view, which is exactly the right balance.
2. On the Commentary ('grel pa don 'grel)
This is my favorite part of your work. It is very sharp.

Metaphysics: You write: "Rain falls through sky without event because sky never became medium for falling."
This is excellent. You are translating the Dharmata, not just the words. You have avoided the trap of making the Alaya (kun gzhi) sound like a concrete storage facility where we put our karma like boxes in a basement. You are presenting it as a display, a dependency without inherent existence.
My advice: In the Commentary on Verse 1 regarding Mañjuśrī, you say "Mañjuśrī youthful: this unaged recognition already bowing." This is very direct introduction (ngo sprod). Ensure that in later verses, when you speak of "ignorance," you maintain this same refusal to reify it. Do not let ignorance become a "thing" that exists. Keep it as a "non-event."
3. On the Literal Translation ('gyur don bsgyur)
Your Wylie and tripartite structure are flawless. This is a great service to future students. They can look at your poetic rendering, and if they are confused, they can climb down the ladder to the literal translation and see exactly which Tibetan word generated which English thought.

Specific Correction on Verse 10: You translate yan lag gnyis nying lag go as "two core limbs."
In this context of the Body Vāsanā, nying lag refers to the vital parts or the essence of the body. However, yan lag gnyis specifically usually refers to the two limbs that support the body, or sometimes the upper and lower parts, but in the context of the "six migrations" (animals, hell beings, etc.), it often refers to the distinction between the male and female forms, or simply the completeness of the physical vessel.
My suggestion: The literal word nying lag implies "vital parts" or "life-force." "Core limbs" is a bit ambiguous physically. Perhaps "vital limbs" or "principal limbs" would be better. But "core" is acceptable if you intend it to mean "essential." I leave it to your intuition, but "vital" carries the nying (heart/essence) nuance better.
4. On the Illumination (ngo sprod)
"This path: already at liberation's summit. No practice to perfect."
