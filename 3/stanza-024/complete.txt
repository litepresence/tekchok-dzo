## Extended Wylie Transliteration

'khor ba'i gdung sel 'chi med theg pa ste

## 1. Liturgical Rendering (mchod brjod tshig; Skt. stotra-pada)

Abandoning the faults of harsh, coarse intellect,
Possessing the cool, gentle, peaceful touch
That removes the sufferings of samsara, the deathless vehicle—
This is the All-Knowing Lama's excellent teaching: liberation through touching.
༢༤

## 2. Commentary ('grel pa don 'grel; Skt. lakṣaṇa-nirdeśa)

<new>
## Translator's Commentary (grel pa don 'grel)

This stanza refines non-abiding by attending to reg grol (touching) as immediate recognition, not a disciplined ascent. Harsh intellectual fault-finding is replaced by the recognition that mind does not require top-down control; cool, gentle touch signals contact without fixation; the peace arises when separation is seen through, revealing samsara as liberation rather than a prison.

Samsara's sufferings are not conquered by struggle but disclosed as non-substantial when touched with clarity. The deathless vehicle is not a vehicle you travel to but the recognition that travel itself is the revelation of a limitless base.

The lakṣaṇa: touching as recognition, contact without separation, liberation as the nature of sensation itself.
</new>

## 3. Illumination (ngo sprod; Skt. svarūpa-prakāśa)

00001| Harsh intellect—not wrong, but seen through, the mind's nature clarified.
00002| Cool touch—not temperature but absence of friction, contact without resistance.
00003| Gentle contact—non-violent recognition, touch that does not grasp.
00004| Peaceful contact—presence without agenda, the mind at rest with recognition.
00005| Liberation through touching—contact as recognition, sensation as freedom.
00006| This treatise—structure that supports, centerless presence that radiates.
