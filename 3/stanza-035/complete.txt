།མངོན་སུམ་ཆོས་ཉིད་རྗེན་པར་སྟོན་པའི་ངག
།ཀློང་རྡོལ་གཏམ་འདི་སྐད་ཅིག་ཐོས་པས་ཀྱང༌།

## 1. Liturgical Rendering (mchod brjod tshig; Skt. stotra-pada)

A song of Vajra Essence, a portrait of realization,
Speech that shows reality manifestly and bare—
Even hearing this expanse-flowing discourse for an instant,
Luminous wisdom arises forcefully in the continuum.
༣༥

## 2. Commentary ('grel pa don 'grel; Skt. lakṣaṇa-nirdeśa)

## Translator's Commentary (grel pa don 'grel)

This stanza expresses non-dual realization without reliance on metaphor. The Vajra Essence song, the portrait of realization, and the realization itself are seen as different expressions of the same nondual field. Lakṣaṇa: the song that sings without a singer; the portrait that portrays without a portrayer; the essence that is not essential.

## 3. Illumination (ngo sprod; Skt. svarūpa-prakāśa)

00001| Song of Vajra Essence—not music but the essence singing itself.
00002| Portrait of realization—not image but realization taking form in sound and light.
00003| Speech showing reality—not words but the showing that discloses the nondual truth.
00004| Reality manifest and bare—not truth as proposition but the truth seen in the manifest.
00005| Expanse-flowing discourse—not streaming but the flow that reveals the expanse.
00006| Hearing for an instant—not a moment of listening but the instant of recognition.
00007| Luminous wisdom arises—wisdom that illuminates without relying on conventional knowledge.
00008| Forcefully arising—not violence but the arising of recognition in the mind.
00009| Continuum—not duration but the continuum that is wisdom itself.

## 4. Literal Translation ('gyur don bsgyur; Skt. artha-anuvāda)

Realization's portrait Vajra Essence song
Manifest reality bare-by show-er speech
Expanse-flowing discourse this instant hear-even
Luminous wisdom continuum-in force-by arise
༣༥
