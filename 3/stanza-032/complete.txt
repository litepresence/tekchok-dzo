## 1. Liturgical Rendering (mchod brjod tshig; Skt. stotra-pada)

Taming beings according to their realms and aspirations,
Cutting through all knowable dharmas, diverse yet unmixed,
Without transgressing from the expanse of ultimate equality—
These excellent teachings are like the complete Buddha.
༣༢

## 2. Commentary ('grel pa don 'grel; Skt. lakṣaṇa-nirdeśa)

## Translator's Commentary (grel pa don 'grel)

This stanza shows the teaching’s adaptive capacity and ultimate non-duality through a minimal, crisp framing. The taming (boddhis) is not about subjugation; it’s about recognizing that beings, realms, and aspirations are not discrete, but facets of a single experiential field. The nine vehicles and mind-investigated path are not a hierarchy; they are a non-dual choreography in which cause and effect are not sequential but mutually illuminating. Lakṣaṇa: perception without dualism, the realm without location, the path that is the essence.

## 3. Illumination (ngo sprod; Skt. svarūpa-prakāśa)

00001| Boundaries dissolve; taming is non-dual awareness in action.
00002| The vehicle is not a ladder but a living expression of realization.
00003| Nine vehicles are not a taxonomy but a single vehicle seen through many perspectives.
00004| Mind-investigated path is not road but the mind seeing itself as path.
00005| Cause and effect are not sequential relations but the same ground seen from two angles.
00006| The expanse of equality arises when duality dissolves; there is no space between the two.
00007| The teaching is not a text to study but a living demonstration of non-dual unity.

## 4. Literal Translation ('gyur don bsgyur; Skt. artha-anuvāda)

Who tamed sentient-realms aspiration thus tamed
Various knowables dharma all unmixed cut
Ultimate equality expanse-from not-transgressing by
Scripture excellent these complete buddha like
༣༢
