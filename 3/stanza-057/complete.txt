## 1. Liturgical Rendering (mchod brjod tshig; Skt. stotra-pada)

When enjoyments of desire objects gather by force,
Please look at these excellent scriptures,
The All-Knowing excellent teaching is the peacock playing with poison
That holds whatever is desired as an ornament in an attachment-less manner.
༥༧

## 2. Commentary ('grel pa don 'grel; Skt. lakṣaṇa-nirdeśa)

## Translator's Commentary (grel pa don 'grel)

This stanza reiterates non-abiding through the peacock playing with poison. The emphasis is on transforming what seems dangerous into a field of revelation. Lakṣaṇa: the poison-play reveals nondual ground; the poison reveals the nectar; the play reveals the knowledge that there was never a separate other.

## 3. Illumination (ngo sprod; Skt. svarūpa-prakāśa)

00001| Peacock poison-play illumining the mind; transformation not fright but fearless recognition.
00002| Desire and attachment transform into wisdom as the poison is recognized as nectar.
00003| The ground of non-duality reveals itself in the play of safety and risk, not a choice between them.
00004| The nine vehicles become a single field; no need to rank them.
00005| The attendant wisdom is present in the recognition that no object has independent solidity.

## 4. Literal Translation ('gyur don bsgyur; Skt. artha-anuvāda)
