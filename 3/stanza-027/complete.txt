## 1. Liturgical Rendering (mchod brjod tshig; Skt. stotra-pada)

When opening the door of the cause-and-effect vehicle's dharma,
Proclaiming the dimension without cause and effect, equal and same—
Such instructions of the All-Knowing Lama,
There is no second scholar adept who explains them.
༢༧

## 2. Commentary ('grel pa don 'grel; Skt. lakṣaṇa-nirdeśa)

## Translator's Commentary (grel pa don 'grel)

This stanza opens the door of causality’s dissolution and reframes it as non-abiding through non-dual awareness. The opening line indicates a move beyond linear cause-effect thinking; the door opens into a space that transcends framework. The lakṣaṇa presents non-duality: causality as appearance, equality as nature—neither negating the other; they were never two.

## 3. Illumination (ngo sprod; Skt. svarūpa-prakāśa)

00001| Door of cause-and-effect—not rejected but opened, opened to what exceeds.
00002| Cause-and-effect vehicle—not wrong but partial; its fullness is immediate presence.
00003| Dimension without cause-and-effect—not other place but same place seen through.
00004| Equal and same—cause not producing other, effect not resulting from separation.
00005| All-Knowing Lama's instruction—not doctrine but direct indication, pointing to what is already.
00006| No second scholar—not claim of uniqueness; no comparison where teaching does not stand.
00007| This instruction—causality as appearance, equality as nature, seeing through the two to the never-having-been-two.
