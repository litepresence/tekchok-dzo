## Where Metaphysics Could Expand (If Not Verse)

If we were to convert certain dense verse sections to prose for expansion, these would be candidates:

### Candidate 1: Chapter 25, Lines 17253-17256
**Current (Verse):**
```
Those sky-space like vast, wisdom samādhi vast,
Immeasurable intention-conduct ocean vast, doing others' benefit vast,
Luminous sun-moon appearance vast, and deeds vast,
Unmoving Dharma-nature appearance vast, greatly entering Dharma-space—may it be!
```

**Potential Prose Expansion:**
> "Those who are vast like sky-space, whose wisdom and samādhi are vast, whose intention and conduct are an immeasurable ocean vast with doing others' benefit, whose luminous appearance is like the sun and moon, and whose deeds are vast, whose unmoving Dharma-nature appearance is vast—may they greatly enter the Dharma-space!"

**Assessment:** The verse format works well here; prose would add words but not necessarily clarity.

### Candidate 2: Chapter 1 Opening, Lines 16-19
**Current (Verse):**
```
From primordial purity elaborations cease into space, beautifully arrayed,
Nature ever-abiding, clear light pervading all three bodies,
Not dwelling in existence or peace, spontaneously accomplished—
To that Vajra-point, supreme possessor, Sugata-essence, I prostrate.
```

**Assessment:** This is actually perfect as verse. The compressed syntax creates poetic power.

## Recommendation

**DO NOT change verse meters.** The current translation successfully navigates the balance between:
- Poetic form (Tibetan-style verses)
- Metaphysical precision (technical terms preserved)
- Readability (breath-sized lines)

The verses that appear "compressed" are intentionally so—that compression creates the vajra impact of the original. Expanding them into prose would dilute their power.

**Exception:** If specific verses feel awkward in proofreading, address case-by-case. But no systematic meter changes are needed.
