[16413] Dharma-kāya's essence: self-arisen wisdom, empty-clarity, free from elaboration;
[16414] Abiding manner,

[16419] Essence's abiding manner: unstained by any flaw, nature pure from beginning.
[16420] That essence's defining characteristic: emptiness possessing awareness-essence.
[16421] That essence's nature: non-mixed with anything.
[16422] That essence's compassion: without opening, closing, or division-making.
[16423] That essence's appearance manner: non-dual.
[16424] Definitive terminology:
[16425] Maturation from labeled enumeration dharmas into kāya—thus without entity,
[16426] Defining characteristic nowhere determined,
[16427] Emptiness nowhere established—therefore called "Dharma-kāya."
[16428] Dharma means: || 20 ||
[16429] Buddha-ground's stainless dharmas connected with awareness;
[16430] Kāya means:
[16431] Marks and signs without face or hands, merely referring to unchanging awareness-essence.
[16432] Divisions:
[16433] Dharma-kāya connected with entity-defining characteristics;
[16434] Dharma-kāya connected with abiding-all-pervading manner;
[16435] Dharma-kāya connected with divisions-appearance manner—three.
