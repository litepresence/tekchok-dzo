[8770] First, the outer:
[8771] Dividing the border of Saṃsāra and Nirvāṇa.
[8772] The inner: engaging the twenty-five Gnosises, offering the Maṇḍala and supplicating.
[8773] The secret: entering through the doors of the four directions of space into the door of mind itself.
[8774] The perfect: indicating awareness primordially pure, transcending conceptual mind, called "vast space."
[8775] These also from the Thalgyur:
[8776] From the very unelaborate,
[8777] Divide below the border of Saṃsāra and Nirvāṇa.
[8778] At that time, from the characteristic of space,
[8779] The obscurations of body, speech, and mind also—
[8780] Are definitely gauged by characteristic.
[8781] If conduct is not pure,
[8782] Purify with virtue suitable to each self.
[8783] Rely upon the symbolic Empowerment,
[8784] Introduce the face of self-knowing clarity,
[8785] Skillfully join instruction of untying symbols.
[8786] Thus it is.
[8787] Fourth: The utterly unelaborate Empowerment:
[8788] Bestowing the Empowerment of introducing the self-nature of spontaneous accomplishment of the nature of reason, following the body's posture, gaze, and stance.
[8789] From four—outer, inner, secret, and perfect:
[8790] The outer shows posture;
[8791] The inner shows gaze;
[8792] The secret: through posture to the four directions,
[8793] And training in the manner of gaze, practicing in the Buddha-field.
[8794] The perfect: applying the meaning of symbolic words such as "song" and so forth.
[8795] Thus also from the Thalgyur:
[8796] To the utterly unelaborate,
[8797] Open the door of the mind's Maṇḍala,
[8798] Definitely begin the body's posture,
[8799] Accustom to that and apply the manner of gaze,
[8800] Direct cognition is the symbol of scripture.
[8801] Thus it is.
