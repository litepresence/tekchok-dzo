## CHAPTER 19: Deity Visualization

**Tibetan:** རིམ་ཁང་བཅུ་དགུ་པ། (Nineteenth Lecture Hall)  
**Location:** Volume 1, Sections 01-19-00-01 through 01-19-01-01  
**Tibetan Lines:** 5963-6703  
**Total Liturgical Lines:** ~740

**Opening Phrase:** ཆོས་ཟད་བློ་འདས་ཀྱི་དགོངས་པ་ཟང་ཐལ་རྗེན་པའི་ཡེ་ཤེས་འཕོ་འགྱུར་མེད་པར་ངོ་སྤྲད་པ།  
"The intention of mind-transcending Dharma exhaustion: direct introduction to changeless wisdom supported by naked Zangthal..."

This pivotal chapter presents the **deity visualization practices** (lha'i rnam pa) of the Great Perfection—sophisticated methods that bridge the generation stage (bskyed rim) of lower tantras with the completion stage (rdzogs rim) of Atiyoga. Longchenpa addresses this teaching specifically to practitioners "free from elaborations"—those who have stabilized recognition through the Vajra Essence Path (Chapter 18) and are ready to apply that recognition in formal deity yoga practice.

The chapter operates on a crucial Dzogchen principle: **deities are not external beings to be worshipped but the spontaneous self-display of primordial wisdom** (ye shes rang snang). The visualization practices transform the mechanical generation-stage practices of lower vehicles into **instantaneous arising from the expanse of rigpa**—not creating something new but recognizing what has always been present. For practitioners, this chapter is essential as it provides the **skillful means** (thabs) for maintaining recognition in formal practice while accumulating the two accumulations necessary for complete realization.

### **Section 0: 01-19-00-01.txt (~451 lines)**  
**Topic:** The Meaning of Mind-Transcending Dharma Exhaustion—Philosophical Foundation

This opening section establishes the **philosophical foundation** for deity visualization in the Great Perfection context, explaining why Dzogchen deity yoga transcends yet includes lower tantric approaches.

**The Four Key Concepts:**

**1. Dharma Exhaustion** (chos zad):
<list> **Not**: Running out of teachings or reaching the end of Dharma
<list> **But**: Exhaustion of conceptual grasping at Dharma itself
<list> **Meaning**: Beyond all philosophical positions, even Buddhist ones—beyond the four extremes, beyond elaboration
<list> **Result**: Naked awareness (rgod pa) without conceptual overlay

<tantra> "When Dharma is exhausted, awareness stands naked."

**2. Mind-Transcending** (blo 'das):
<list> Beyond ordinary conceptual mind (blo) with its dualistic grasping
<list> Transcending the intellect's limitations and categories
<list> Direct recognition without mediation of concepts
<list> The wisdom that knows without a knower

**3. Zangthal** (zang thal):
<list> **Direct**, **straight**, **unimpeded**—like an arrow shot straight to the target
<list> The direct path of Great Perfection without gradual stages
<list> No obstructions, no detours, no winding roads
<list> Immediate recognition of the nature as it is

**4. Changeless Wisdom** ('pho 'gyur med pa'i ye shes):
<list> Wisdom that never changes, transforms, or fluctuates
<list> Not produced by causes, not destroyed by conditions
<list> The Dharmakāya itself—primordially pure, spontaneously present
<list> Unstained by delusion, unchanged by realization

**Deity Yoga in Dzogchen Context:**

**Not Lower Tantra's Approach:**
<list> Not visualizing an external deity separate from oneself
<list> Not petitioning a superior being for blessings
<list> Not fabricating something that is not already present
<list> Not gradually building the form piece by piece

**But Dzogchen's Approach:**
<list> Recognizing the deity as the **self-display of awareness** (rig pa rang snang)
<list> Spontaneous arising from the expanse of emptiness
<list> The deity IS the nature of mind—not separate, not other
<list> Visualization as **recognition**, not creation; **revelation**, not fabrication

**The Two Stages Unified:**

<tantra> "In the Great Perfection, the generation stage is instantaneous self-arising; the completion stage is continuous self-liberation. The two are inseparable like fire and heat."

**Generation Stage** (bskyed rim) in Dzogchen:
<list> Instantaneous, not gradual—complete in a single moment
<list> Self-arisen, not fabricated—emerging naturally from emptiness
<list> The deity appears like a reflection in a mirror, like a rainbow in the sky

**Completion Stage** (rdzogs rim) simultaneous:
<list> Never separate from emptiness—the form IS empty
<list> The deity is the display of awareness, not other than it
<list> Dissolution and generation simultaneous—arising and liberating at once

**The Four Purposes of Deity Visualization:**

**1. Purification** (sbyang ba):
<list> Purifying ordinary impure perception (snang tshul ma dag pa)
<list> Transforming impure vision into pure vision (dag snang)
<list> Seeing the world as the deity's maṇḍala, beings as deities

**2. Accumulation** (tshogs):
<list> Accumulating merit (bsod nams) through pure perception
<list> Gathering the two accumulations: merit and wisdom
<list> Preparing the ground for complete realization

**3. Blessing** (byin rlabs):
<list> Receiving the blessing of the lineage (brgyud pa'i byin rlabs)
<list> Connecting with the wisdom beings (ye shes pa)
<list> Awakening the innate wisdom through resonance with the deity

**4. Recognition Support** (ngos 'dzin rten):
<list> Providing a support (rten) for recognition
<list> Giving the mind a "holy" object to focus on without distraction
<list> Leading to recognition that the deity is one's own nature

**The Five Buddha Families as Self-Display of the Five Wisdoms:**

**1. Vairocana** (Buddha Family, rgyal ba rigs):
<list> **Wisdom**: Dharmadhātu wisdom (chos dbyings ye shes)—universal sphere
<list> **Purity**: Ignorance purified into all-pervading awareness
<list> **Color**: White, like a conch or snow mountain
<list> **Location**: Center, the heart of the maṇḍala
<list> **Self-display**: The universal sphere of reality, the all-pervading space of awareness

**2. Akṣobhya** (Vajra Family, rdo rje rigs):
<list> **Wisdom**: Mirror-like wisdom (me long lta bu'i ye shes)
<list> **Purity**: Anger purified into clear reflection
<list> **Color**: Blue, like a sapphire or deep ocean
<list> **Location**: East, the direction of dawn and awakening
<list> **Self-display**: Clear reflection of all phenomena without distortion

**3. Ratnasambhava** (Jewel Family, rin chen rigs):
<list> **Wisdom**: Equality wisdom (mnyam nyid ye shes)
<list> **Purity**: Pride purified into richness of equality
<list> **Color**: Yellow, like gold or autumn fields
<list> **Location**: South, the direction of increase and prosperity
<list> **Self-display**: Richness of equality, all equally precious

**4. Amitābha** (Lotus Family, pad ma rigs):

**5. Amoghasiddhi** (Karma Family, las rigs):

<list> **Color**: Green, like an emerald or fresh leaves

**The Three Roots:**

**Topic:** The Necessity of Causes—Detailed Practice Instructions

This section provides **practical instructions** for deity visualization practice, emphasizing the causes and conditions necessary for authentic practice and explaining the step-by-step methodology of instantaneous generation.

**1.1 The Need for Causes—Why Preparation Is Essential:**

<tantra> "Spontaneous presence does not mean no effort; effortless arising requires recognition first."

**Why Visualization Requires Preparation:**
<list> Spontaneous presence (lhun grub) doesn't mean "no practice" or "no effort"
<list> Recognition of the ground must be stabilized before authentic deity yoga
<list> The ground must be recognized for the display to appear as deity rather than delusion

**The Three Causes for Authentic Practice:**

<list> **1. Recognition of the Ground** (gzhi rtogs): Must recognize primordial purity first—otherwise deity yoga becomes mere fabrication
<list> **2. Stability of Recognition** (rtogs pa brtan pa): Recognition must be continuous enough to maintain during visualization
<list> **3. Purification of Obscurations** (sgrib sbyang): Merit and wisdom accumulated through preliminary practices

**1.2 Detailed Visualization Instructions—The Stages of Instantaneous Generation:**

**Step 1: Emptiness** (stong pa nyid)—The Dharmakāya Foundation:
<list> Begin with emptiness meditation—not blankness but luminous emptiness
<list> The clear light nature of mind, the Dharmakāya as the basis
<list> All phenomena dissolved into the expanse of reality
<list> **Not**: Nothingness; **But**: The pregnant void from which all arises

**Step 2: Seed Syllable** (sa bon)—The Resonance of Emptiness:
<list> From emptiness, the seed syllable appears spontaneously
<list> Not created by effort but naturally arisen like an echo
<list> The sound and form inseparable—form is sound, sound is form
<list> Examples: HUM for Heruka, HRĪḤ for Vajrasattva, OṂ for peaceful deities

**Step 3: Complete Form** (yongs rdzogs)—The Full Manifestation:
<list> From the seed syllable, the complete deity arises instantaneously
<list> Complete in all aspects—body, ornaments, garments, implements
<list> Not built gradually like a construction but spontaneously present
<list> Like the moon reflected in water—complete without effort

**The Three Seats** (gdan gsum)—The Foundations of Enlightened Form:

**1. Lotus and Moon Seat** (padma zla gdan):
<list> **Lotus**: Purity from saṃsāra, unstained like the lotus rising from mud
<list> **Moon**: Bodhicitta, the mind of awakening, cool and luminous
<list> **Foundation**: For peaceful deities (zhi ba), white and cooling

**2. Sun Seat** (nyi gdan):
<list> **Solar disc**: Wisdom, illumination, the blazing clarity of awareness
<list> **Radiant**: Heat and light, active and transforming
<list> **Foundation**: For wrathful deities (drag po), red and blazing

**3. Lotus-Sun-Moon Combined**:
<list> **Lotus** (method) and **moon/sun** (wisdom) united
<list> Method and wisdom inseparable—the essence of tantra
<list> Complete foundation for all deities, peaceful and wrathful

**The Three Syllables at the Three Doors:**

**OM** (white, forehead)—The Body Vajra:

<list> Family: Vairocana, Buddha family
<list> Purification: Ordinary body purified into vajra body
<list> Essence: The indestructible nature of form

**AḤ** (red, throat)—The Speech Vajra:
<list> Location: Center of throat, the gateway of speech
<list> Family: Amitābha, Lotus family  
<list> Purification: Ordinary speech purified into vajra speech
<list> Essence: The indestructible nature of sound

**HŪṂ** (blue, heart)—The Mind Vajra:
<list> Location: Center of heart, the gateway of mind
<list> Family: Akṣobhya, Vajra family
<list> Purification: Ordinary mind purified into vajra mind
<list> Essence: The indestructible nature of awareness

**The Complete Visualization Components:**

**1. The Palace** (gzhal yas khang)—The Enlightened Environment:
<list> The pure realm (zhing khams) of the deity
<list> Not separate from the world—the world transformed

## KEY TECHNICAL TERMS INTRODUCED

### Deity Yoga Fundamentals
- **Deity** (lha): Enlightened form, the self-display of awareness (rig pa rang snang)
- **Yidam** (yi dam): Personal meditation deity, one's nature as enlightened form
- **Maṇḍala** (dkyil 'khor): The complete enlightened environment, the field of display
- **Generation Stage** (bskyed rim): The arising of the deity visualization
- **Completion Stage** (rdzogs rim): Dissolving into emptiness/nature
- **Instantaneous Generation** (skad cig bskyed): Complete form arising in one moment

### Visualization Components
- **Seed Syllable** (sa bon): The essence from which the deity spontaneously arises
- **Three Syllables**: OṂ (body, forehead, white), AḤ (speech, throat, red), HŪṂ (mind, heart, blue)
- **Three Seats** (gdan gsum): Lotus (method), moon (wisdom, peaceful), sun (wisdom, wrathful)
- **Divine Pride** (lha'i nga rgyal): Confidence of being the deity, recognition of true nature
- **Clear Appearance** (gsal ba): Vivid, clear visualization like physical perception
- **Vivid Clarity** (gsal stong): Clear and empty—appearing yet without substance

### The Three Roots
- **Guru** (bla ma): Source of blessing, embodiment of lineage
- **Yidam** (yi dam): Source of accomplishment, meditation deity
- **Ḍākinī** (mkha' 'gro ma): Source of activity, wisdom messengers

### The Five Buddha Families (rigs lnga)
- **Buddha Family** (sangs rgyas rigs): Vairocana, center, white, Dharmadhātu wisdom, ignorance purified
- **Vajra Family** (rdo rje rigs): Akṣobhya, east, blue, mirror-like wisdom, anger purified
- **Jewel Family** (rin chen rigs): Ratnasambhava, south, yellow, equality wisdom, pride purified
- **Lotus Family** (pad ma rigs): Amitābha, west, red, discriminating wisdom, attachment purified
- **Karma Family** (las kyi rigs): Amoghasiddhi, north, green, all-accomplishing wisdom, jealousy purified

### The Four Activities (phrin las bzhi)
- **Pacifying** (zhi ba): White, overcoming obstacles, illness, conflicts
- **Enriching** (rgyas pa): Yellow, increasing merit, longevity, prosperity
- **Magnetizing** (dbang): Red, attracting what is needed, bringing under influence
- **Destroying** (drag po): Black/dark blue, wrathful subjugation, forceful methods

### Mantra Classifications
- **Essence Mantra** (snying po'i sngags): Heart mantra, most condensed form
- **Close Essence** (nye snying): Near-heart mantra, expanded essence
- **Activity Mantra** (phrin las kyi sngags): Specific purpose mantras for the four activities

### Key Dzogchen Concepts

- **Mind-Transcending** (blo 'das): Beyond ordinary conceptual mind
- **Zangthal** (zang thal): Direct, unimpeded, straight path
- **Changeless Wisdom** ('pho 'gyur med pa'i ye shes): Wisdom that never transforms
- **Self-Display** (rang snang): Spontaneous manifestation of awareness

**The Chapter's Strategic Position:**
Chapter 19 serves as the **skillful means** (thabs) chapter in the view-meditation-conduct structure:
- **Chapter 18**: Recognition—the **view** (lta ba) realized
- **Chapter 19**: Deity yoga—the **meditation** (sgom pa) method
- **Chapter 20**: Thögel—the **fruition display** ('bras bu'i snang ba)

This mirrors the classic Buddhist structure:
<list> **View**: How things are (emptiness, primordial purity)
<list> **Meditation**: How to realize it (deity yoga, recognition)
<list> **Conduct**: How to manifest it (post-meditation pure perception)
<list> **Fruition**: Complete awakening (the three bodies)

**The Unity of the Path:**
<tantra> "In the Great Perfection, there is no contradiction between deity yoga and direct recognition. The deity IS the self-display of awareness. To visualize the deity is to recognize one's own nature in form. To recognize the nature is to see the deity in emptiness. Form and emptiness are inseparable like a rainbow and the sky."

For the practitioner who has recognized the ground in Chapter 18, this chapter provides the **methodology** for stabilizing that recognition while accumulating the necessary causes for complete awakening. The deity becomes the **support** (rten) for recognition, the **expression** of compassion (thugs rje), and the **manifestation** of wisdom (ye shes). Through this practice, ordinary perception is transformed into pure perception, and the practitioner progresses toward the spontaneous visions of Thögel presented in Chapter 20.
