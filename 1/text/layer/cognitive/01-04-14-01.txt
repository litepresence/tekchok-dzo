Detailed section enumeration: eighteen tantras of mind section, twenty-one of space section, hundred and nineteen of instruction section. Specific titles: Klong drug, Sgra thal 'gyur, Rig pa rang shar, etc. Enumeration establishes comprehensiveness. Not necessary to memorize; preserve sense of vast textual basis.

Four kāyas exposition: dharmakāya, sambhogakāya, nirmāṇakāya, svabhāvikakāya. Buddha form dimensions as complete manifestation structure. Dharmakāya as essence, sambhogakāya as enjoyment, nirmāṇakāya as emanation, svabhāvikakāya as nature. Not sequential stages but simultaneous aspects. Translate *sku bzhi* as four kāyas consistently. Risk of reifying as separate entities; maintain aspect-nature relationship.

View analysis: emptiness-luminosity union without separation. Non-dual understanding free from extremes. Not emptiness alone, not luminosity alone, but inseparable. *Stong gsal dbyer med* as technical formulation requiring precise rendering. Beyond existence and non-existence extremes. Dzogchen view register distinct from Madhyamaka negation. Preserve positive formulation of unity.

[3411-3450]
Buddha qualities enumeration: ten powers, four fearlessnesses, eighteen distinct qualities. Standard Mahāyāna list of enlightened attributes. Ten powers: knowing appropriateness, ripeness, faculties, inclinations, paths, dhyānas, deaths-births, exhaustion, origins, cessation. Four fearlessnesses: claim of enlightenment, claim of cessation of obscurations, claim of teaching obstacles, claim of teaching path. Eighteen distinct: six unmixed, six unconfused, six unsurpassed. Enumeration preserves traditional structure without requiring verification.

[3451-3490]
Meditation analysis: non-meditation and non-distraction. Natural abiding without contrivance. Beyond meditation and non-meditation duality. Not "not meditating" but meditation without meditator. *Sgom pa* negated as effortful, *ma yengs* affirmed as natural. Dzogchen meditation register distinct from gradual path techniques. Risk of nihilistic interpretation; preserve naturalness.

[3491-3530]
Conduct exposition: beyond acceptance and rejection. Spontaneous activity without contrivance. *Spyod pa* as natural expression of view, not behavioral code. *Blang dor med*: nothing to adopt, nothing to reject. *Lhun grub*: spontaneous, not effortful. Three kāyas conduct: dharmakāya conduct as view, sambhogakāya as meditation, nirmāṇakāya as activity. Preserve spontaneity against ritualization.

[3531-3570]
Result analysis: self-liberation and primordial purity. Spontaneous perfection already complete. Not attainment but recognition. *Rang grol*: liberated by itself, not by effort. *Ka dag*: pure from beginning, not purified. Result as ground revealed, not produced. Fruition fivefold: dharmakāya free from elaboration, sambhogakāya five-certainties, nirmāṇakāya benefit-beings, svabhāvikakāya indivisible, mahāsvabhāvikakāya uncompounded. Translate technical terms consistently across text.

[3571-3610]
Samaya vows exposition: root and branch commitments. Dzogchen samaya distinct from lower vehicles: not external observance but recognition. Root: not losing view. Branch: compassion, devotion, activity. Violation as losing recognition, not breaking rules. Self-liberated in view means samaya self-maintained. Risk of license; preserve seriousness without ritualism.

[3611-3650]
Path of unity: view, meditation, conduct, result as one. Not sequential but simultaneous. Ground, path, result inseparable. Trekcho and thogal as aspects, not stages. Atiyoga as complete in itself, not requiring other vehicles. Self-sufficient, self-liberating. Dzogchen as peak vehicle, yet equally available. Maintain both superiority and accessibility.

Lineage transmission: mind transmission of buddhas, sign transmission of vidyādharas, oral transmission of individuals. Three streams converging. Masters of India and Tibet enumerated: Garab Dorje, Mañjuśrīmitra, Śrī Siṃha, Jñānasūtra, Vimalamitra, Vairocana, Nyima Özer, Nyima Döndrup, Zhonnu Özer. Specific names require accurate rendering. Geographical origins: Uḍḍiyāna, India, Tibet. Lineage as authority warrant.
