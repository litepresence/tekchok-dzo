[10722] <ornament> First: If one asserts that the ground is singular like the variety of appearances, there is the fault of that singular basis arising outwardly as variety. For if the ground were truly one, how could it manifest as the ten thousandfold display? The singular cannot become manifold—this is the logical consequence of such assertion.
[10723] <ornament> Second: If one asserts that the ground is multiple like the variety of appearances, there is the fault of that manifold ground failing to account for singular experience. For if the ground were truly many, how could there be unified consciousness? The manifold cannot become singular—this too has logical consequences.
