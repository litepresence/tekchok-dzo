[998-998] Third: gods perpetually intoxicated with fragrant nectar. Nectar distilled from merit. Cup always full, never emptying. This mountain dweller has seen such nectar—in the cup of meditation, in the wine of samadhi. But cups break. Nectar spills.

[998-998] Perpetual intoxication—what is this but avoidance? The sweet taste masks the bitter truth. The gods drink to forget: forget what? That even nectar ends.

[998-998] See what is. The cup is empty. The drinker is empty. The drinking is empty. Yet the nature of emptiness—how intoxicating!
