First: All phenomena gathered within samsara and nirvana are gathered into the self-nature of the two truths.

[7-13]
The Root Wisdom of the Middle Way states: "Buddhas taught the Dharma, relying correctly upon the two truths: the conventional truth of the world, and the ultimate truth of meaning." From the Magical Illusion Net Tantra: "One abides in the manner of the two truths."

[1]
Samsara and nirvana—two words A mirror shows your face—the glass is not the person. one nature. All phenomena: the hell realms and pure lands, the confused mind and the awakened mind, your suffering and your joy. Gathered into two truths. Not two realities but two perspectives.

[8-13]
This old philosopher spent decades parsing these lines. Nagarjuna's precision fascinated him. Each word measured, each syllable weighed. Then he recognized: the precision was not in the text but in the mind meeting the text. The two truths were not concepts but the nature of conceptuality itself.
