PRACTICAL SYNTHESIS:

The nine vehicles present a complete path from bondage to liberation:

1-3: Outer vehicles (Sravaka, Pratyekabuddha, Mahayana) purify grasping at self and phenomena.
4-6: Kriya, Upa, Yoga purify vision and establish pure perception.
7-9: Mahayoga, Anuyoga, Atiyoga reveal the self-perfected nature.

But these are pedagogical stages, not mandatory curriculum. Some recognize immediately (Atiyoga capacity). Some need structure (Mahayoga). Some need methodical progression (lower vehicles). All are valid. All lead to the same recognition.

The test is not which vehicle you practice but whether recognition dawns. A Dzogchen practitioner without recognition is a blind person with a bright lamp. A Sravaka with recognition is a sighted person walking in daylight.

Patrul Rinpoche's parting words: Practice the vehicle that suits you. Practice it fully. Don't mix vehicles like mixing medicines without knowledge. When recognition stabilizes, vehicle becomes irrelevant. Until then, respect all vehicles, practice your own sincerely, and may recognition dawn swiftly.

This completes the comprehensive commentary on the nine vehicles.

ESSENCE OF NINE VEHICLES:

Sravaka: There is no self.
Pratyekabuddha: There is no external cause.
Cittamatra: There is no external object.
Madhyamaka: There are no inherent characteristics.
Kriya: Vision can be pure.
Upa: Method and wisdom united.
Yoga: Deity and self are one.
Mahayoga: Generation and completion non-dual.
Anuyoga: Instantaneous perfection.
Atiyoga: Nothing was ever other than perfect.

Each statement is complete. Each contains the others. The progression is for minds that progress. The recognition is immediate for minds that recognize.
