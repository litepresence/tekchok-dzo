[1671-1680]
The five aggregates. Form, feeling, perception, formations, consciousness. This is not a list to memorize. This is the prison. This is also the key.

[1672-1680]
A practitioner once spent twelve years analyzing each aggregate separately. He became very knowledgeable. Very precise. He could explain form aggregate in twelve sub-categories. He could diagram consciousness in charts. On the day he died, he was still analyzing. The aggregates analyzed him.

[1673-1677]
Form aggregate arises from four elements. Yes. But what are elements? What is arising? This is not beginner's material. This is the essence. The form you perceive right now—reading these words—is aggregate.

Longchenpa points to what cannot be pointed to; the finger and moon are one in recognition. The eye that reads is aggregate. The consciousness that understands is aggregate. Where is the perceiver?

[1678-1680]
Devotional recognition cuts through analysis. Not rejecting analysis. Going deeper. Longchenpa writes these words from vast compassion. This practitioner feels that compassion now. The aggregates are not enemies to be destroyed. They are display to be recognized.

[1681-1700]
Feeling aggregate. Pleasant, unpleasant, neutral. Three poisons in disguise. This old fool spent decades chasing pleasant feelings. Avoiding unpleasant. Ignoring neutral. Finally he saw: the chase itself was feeling aggregate. The avoidance was feeling aggregate. There was no one feeling—only feeling itself, arising and liberating.

[1685-1690]
The five doors of perception. Eye, ear, nose, tongue, body. Each with its object. Each with its consciousness. Fifteen factors. Plus the mind door. Eighteen. This is the Abhidharma analysis. Precise. Useful. But do not stop there. Where is the perceiver of these eighteen?

[1691-1700]
Single-pointed knowing. Clear knowing. Discerning knowing. These are not philosophical concepts. This practitioner once tried to cultivate them. Ridiculous! They are already present. The cultivation itself is what obscures. Stop cultivating. Start recognizing.

[1701-1731]
Perception aggregate. The grasper and the grasped. This is where confusion accelerates. Form appears. Consciousness contacts. Perception grasps. Suddenly: "I like this. I don't like that." The entire samsara spins from this moment. But recognition also happens here. Form appears. Consciousness contacts. Perception recognizes: this is display. Liberated in the same moment.

[1710-1720]
Formations aggregate. The volitional factors. Karmic creation. This is where you build your future prison. Each choice. Each intention. Each subtle movement of mind. This practitioner watches this carefully now. Not to control it—that is more formation. To recognize it. Formation itself is empty. The one who forms is empty. What remains?

[1721-1731]
Consciousness aggregate. The all-ground. The basis of everything. Vast. Subtle. This is not something to be eliminated. This is rigpa's own nature when unrecognized. Do not fear consciousness. Recognize it. The one who fears and the fear itself—both consciousness. Rest there.

[1671-1671]
Five aggregates. Five fingers of one hand. Five waves of one ocean. Do not analyze them to death. Do not meditate on them as objects. Recognize them as your own display. Then even the recognition is seen as display. Rest in that. This is what Longchenpa points to.

[1671-1671]
This old practitioner has nothing more to say. The aggregates have spoken. The elements have spoken. Everything is teaching. Everything is recognition. Rest in that.

[1680-1700]
An old yogi wandering through autumn valleys once collected fallen leaves, trying to preserve their colors. Each day the pile grew smaller, more brittle, until one morning he found only dust. The aggregates are like those leaves—beautiful, impermanent, self-liberating. The one who gathers and the dust gathered: same nature.

[1725-1731]
See what is.
 The recognition that liberates is always immediate, never gradual.

What is called delusion here is wisdom's play of hide-and-seek, spontaneously self-liberating.

The apparent complexity resolves in simplicity when recognition dawns—before analysis, already complete.
