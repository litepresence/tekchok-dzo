Second: seeing. Not ordinary visual perception, but the seeing that is
inseparable from what is seen. Like a mirror reflecting—a reflection
without reflector, seen without seer.

This hermit once thought seeing was something he did. Then he recognized:
seeing happens. The seen and the seeing—simultaneous, not sequential.
No one sees. Seeing occurs.
