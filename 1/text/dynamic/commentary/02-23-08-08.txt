[1-1] Fourth—self-appearance realized, true power obtained. When appearances are recognized as self-appearance, when the display is known as one's own display, then true power is obtained. Not power over others. Not power to manipulate. The power of recognition—the power that needs no demonstration.

[1-1] A wandering practitioner... once sought power through force, through dominance, through control. He gained nothing but exhaustion. When he recognized self-appearance—when he saw that all phenomena were his own mind's display—then true power dawned. Not the power to change things, but the power to rest unchanged.
