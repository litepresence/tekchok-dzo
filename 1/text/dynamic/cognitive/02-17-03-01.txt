The conduct-progressive-yogin encompasses four dimensions: conduct that transforms the power of appearances, oral instruction for fixing appearances directly, the intention regarding appearance-existence as the arising of the ground, and the result of transforming the basis of afflictions. This framework establishes how continuous engagement with conduct serves as a complete path in itself.

[2056-2059]
The first category subdivides into three aspects: general definition of conduct briefly indicated, detailed explanation of definite secret great conduct, and establishing duration through sequential conduct. This tripartite structure provides both theoretical understanding and practical application guidelines for conduct practice.

[2060-2063]
Three types of conduct are presented: conduct without attachment or fixation, conduct without negation or proof, and conduct without attachment despite engagement. These categories demonstrate the non-dual character of authentic Great Perfection conduct, which operates without the limitations of ordinary acceptance and rejection. The practitioner engages with appearances while remaining free from the binding quality of fixation.
