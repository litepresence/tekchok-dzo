First simile: Buddha and beings as shell and horn. Dang po—first—gzhi rig pa gcig pa—single ground awareness—rtogs pa sangs rgyas—realized Buddha—gzhi thog tu rang grol—self-liberated from ground-beginning—mgo 'dra—like head/crest. Ma rtogs sems can—unrealized sentient being—'khrul pas rwa lta bu—like horn through delusion. Rang shar las citation: dpe'i ngo sprid—simile introduction—sangs rgyas dang sems can gyi dpe—Buddha and sentient being simile—mgo dang rwa'i tshul—manner of head and horn. Gcig las du mar byung—many arisen from one. Structural function: establish primordial unity despite apparent diversity. Head represents self-recognition, horn represents deluded projection from same basis.

Second simile: water and drawing. Dbyings gcig—single expanse—rig dus rang sar grol—liberated at own place through recognition—chu la ri mo bris pa lta bar—like drawing on water—gzhi thog tu grol—liberated from ground. Ma rig pas chu mer ba ltar—like water in fire through ignorance—'khrul rkyen gyi byung khungs—delusion-condition source. Rang shar las citation: rig pa dang ma rig pa'i dpe—awareness and ignorance simile—chu dang ri mo'i tshul—manner of water and drawing—rkyen la rag lus—dependent on conditions. Water as awareness: drawing appears but doesn't persist. Fire as ignorance: water appears disturbed.

Fourth simile: gold and fool's gold. Rig pa gcig—single awareness—shes rab—wisdom—rang rtsal phyin ci ma log—own display not mistaken—gzung 'dzin las grol—free from grasping—gser 'dra—like gold. Yid—mind—rtsal phyin ci log—mistaken display—gzung 'dzin du skyes—born as grasping—ra gan lta bu—like fool's gold. Rang shar las citation: shes rab dang yid kyi dpe—wisdom and mind simile—gser dang ra gan gyi tshul—manner of gold and fool's gold—kha dog mi phyed—color not different. Same substance, different recognition: gold knows itself, fool's gold requires external validation.
