First simile: Buddha and beings as shell and horn. Single ground awareness—realized Buddha—self-liberated from ground-beginning—like head/crest. Unrealized sentient being—like horn through delusion. Rang shar las citation: simile introduction—Buddha and sentient being simile—manner of head and horn. Many arisen from one. Structural function: establish primordial unity despite apparent diversity. Head represents self-recognition, horn represents deluded projection from same basis.

Second simile: water and drawing. Single expanse—liberated at own place through recognition—like drawing on water—liberated from ground. Like water in fire through ignorance—delusion-condition source. Rang shar las citation: awareness and ignorance simile—manner of water and drawing—dependent on conditions. Water as awareness: drawing appears but doesn't persist. Fire as ignorance: water appears disturbed.

Fourth simile: gold and fool's gold. Single awareness—wisdom—own display not mistaken—free from grasping—like gold. Mind—mistaken display—born as grasping—like fool's gold. Rang shar las citation: wisdom and mind simile—manner of gold and fool's gold—color not different. Same substance, different recognition: gold knows itself, fool's gold requires external validation.

Fifth simile: ocean and boat. Single expanse—all-ground like boat. Like Dharmakāya ocean—floating in nature. Dharmakāya's obscured portion—or path—or manner of display—arising. Rang shar las citation: all-ground and Dharmakāya simile—manner of ocean and boat—path arising. All-ground floats on Dharmakāya; both are water, one moves, one doesn't.

Sixth simile: sleeping and waking. Delusion and liberation two—single in ground-awareness nature. One person—gone to sleep—and awakened—like. Rang shar las citation: delusion and liberation simile—manner of falling asleep and waking—sense faculties differ. Same person, different states: waking knows what sleeping forgot.

Seventh simile: darkness and light. Delusion-appearance and ground-appearance two—equal appearance in awareness nature. Self-luminosity—daytime—reverse-luminosity—night—clear and unclear—difference exists. Rang shar las citation: delusion appearance and bardo simile—manner of darkness and light—pure and impure difference.

Eighth simile: woman and touch. Realized and unrealized two—single in awareness—delusion's grasping fixation—purified or not—difference exists. Maiden's touch—nature not meeting condition—unrealized—like. When meeting sun condition—purified—realization simile. Rang shar las citation: realized and unrealized simile—appearance overwhelming and not overwhelming.

Ninth simile: wisdom and imprints. Wisdom and imprints two—similar in awareness potency—difference of abandoning and antidote. Rang shar las citation: wisdom and imprints simile—manner of firebrand and fire—burning afflictions.

Tenth simile: mother's lap. Awareness wisdom-appearance—entering—simile meaning—awareness not similar—like wisdom. Liberation through dissolving into ground—like child entering mother's lap. For confidence. Structural function: provide accessible analogies for profound distinctions.

Eleventh simile: gold and gold color. Single in awareness-luminosity—mere turning from ground and arising from it—divided into wisdom and light. Rang shar las citation: wisdom and light simile—like gold and gold color. Because name and color not different. Same essence, different designation based on function.

Twelfth simile: beer and intoxication. On single awareness—pointing-out instruction—beer and—intoxication like. Rang shar las citation: instruction and intoxication simile—like beer and intoxication. For entering knowledge. Instruction causes recognition like beer causes intoxication: cause and effect, yet same nature.

Thirteenth simile: sky and rainbow. Outer clarity wisdom—dissolved into inner light—gathered into crystal light—or rainbow dissolved into expanse—without distinction. Rang shar las citation: wisdom dissolving into light simile—like sky and rainbow. Because without entity. Rainbow appears in sky, has no separate existence, naturally dissolves.

Fourteenth and fifteenth similes. Within expanse ocean—when wisdom stream dissolves—no difference in own face. Rang shar las citation: wisdom dissolving into expanse simile—like streams gathering in ocean. Because non-dual. Fifteenth: not returning after dissolving into expanse. Like powerful arrow. Because without obstruction. Arrow shot into space doesn't return; awareness dissolved into expanse doesn't revert to delusion.
