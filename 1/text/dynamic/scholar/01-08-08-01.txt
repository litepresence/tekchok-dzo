**Doxographical Placement:** This marker appears within Chapter 8, which presents the seven views on the basis (གཞི་ལ་ལྟ་བ་བདུན་ gzhi la lta ba bdun) according to the རྫོགས་ཆེན་ (Great Perfection) system.

**The Seven Views:** The seven views are typically enumerated as:
1. གཞི་ཁ་དག་ (gzhi ka dag): basis as primordially pure
2. གཞི་ལྷུན་གྱིས་གྲུབ་ (gzhi lhun gyis grub): basis as spontaneously present
3. གཞི་མི་ངེས་ (gzhi mi nges): basis as uncertain
4. གཞི་ངེས་ (gzhi nges): basis as certain
5. གཞི་འགྱུར་བ་ (gzhi 'gyur ba): basis as transformable
6. གཞི་བླང་བ་ (gzhi blang ba): basis as accepting
7. གཞི་སྣ་ཚོགས་ (gzhi sna tshogs): basis as manifold

**Philosophical Framework:** The seven views on the basis represent seven limited (ཐ་དད་ tha dad) perspectives on the single basis (གཞི་གཅིག་ gzhi gcig), all of which are ultimately partial (ཕྱེད་པ་ shyed pa).

**Each View's Limitation:**
1. **First View (Primordial Purity):** Captures emptiness (སྟོང་པ་ stong pa) but may miss spontaneous presence (ལྷུན་གྱིས་གྲུབ་)
2. **Second View (Spontaneous Presence):** Captures luminosity (གསལ་བ་ gsal ba) but may miss primordial purity (ཁ་དག་)
3. **Third-Seventh Views:** Each captures partial aspects while missing others

**Complete Realization:** The complete realization requires transcending all seven partial views to recognize the གཞི་མཆོག་ (gzhi mchog, "supreme basis") beyond conceptual extremes—neither pure nor impure, neither one nor many.

**Analytical Phase:** This marker initiates the དཔྱད་པ་ (dpyad pa, "analytical") phase of the chapter wherein each view is examined and its limitations demonstrated, ultimately leading to the recognition of the viewless view (ལྟ་བ་མི་ལྟ་བ་ lta ba mi lta ba).
