# First Aspect: Practice Chamber Preparation
**Architectural Context:** This passage presents the first (དང་པོ་ dang po) of four aspects regarding the practice chamber (སྒ໲ོད་ཁང་ sgrub khang). The structure establishes the material foundation for ཐོད་རྒ໱་ (thod rgal) practice, progressing from spatial orientation through light manipulation to blessing transmission. The passage comprises:

1. **Directional determination** (ཕྱོགས་ཕྱེད་པ་ phyogs phyed pa): Orientation of the practice space
2. **Solar apertures** (ཉི་མའི་ཟེར་ nyi ma'i zer): Sunbeams entering through openings
3. **Pure crystal preparation** (ཤེལ་དཀར་དྲི་མ་མེད་པ་ shel dkar dri ma med pa): Pure crystal medium
4. **Mirror blessing** (མེ་ལོང་བྱིན་གྱིས་བརླབས་ me long byin rlabs): Consecration of the reflecting surface

**Citation Structure:** The passage cites from the མངོན་པར་བཤད་པའི་རྒ໱ུད་ (mNgon par bshad pa'i rgyud, "Tantra of Clear Introduction"), establishing scriptural authority for these material preparations.

**Key Terminology:**
- སྒ໲ོད་ཁང་ (sgrub khang): "practice chamber, accomplishment house"—a specially prepared space for meditation practice, distinct from ordinary rooms
- ཕྱོགས་ (phyogs): "direction, region"—the cardinal directions
- ཕྱེད་པ་ (phyed pa): "half, divided"—indicates the practice chamber is oriented toward a specific direction
- ཉི་མའི་ཟེར་ (nyi ma'i zer): "sun's rays"—the beams of sunlight entering through openings
- ཤེལ་ (shel): "crystal"—the clear medium used for light manipulation
- དཀར་ (dkar): "white, pure"—indicating clarity and purity
- དྲི་མ་ (dri ma): "defilement, impurity"—with negative མེད་ (med) indicating purity
- མེ་ལོང་ (me long): "mirror"—the reflecting surface
- བྱིན་གྱིས་བརླབས་ (byin rlabs): "blessing, consecration"—the empowerment transmitted to the objects

**Sentence Structure:** The passage uses the quotative ཅེས་ (ces) introducing scriptural citation, followed by explanatory prose (lines 9407-9410) elaborating on the four aspects.

[9404-9412] <context>
**Doxographical Placement:** This passage belongs to Chapter 20's practical instructions for ཐོད་རྒ໱་ (thod rgal) practice, specifically addressing the external material supports necessary for this highest yoga tantra practice.

**Scriptural Authority:** The citation from མངོན་པར་བཤད་པའི་རྒ໱ུད་ (mNgon par bshad pa'i rgyud, "Tantra of Clear Introduction"—also known as the འཇིག་རྟེན་འཛིན་པའི་རྒ໱ུད་ or similar Nyingma tantra) establishes authority for these material preparations within the རྙིང་མ་ (Nyingma) tradition.

**External Support (རྟེན་ rten):** The practice chamber represents the external support (རྟེན་ rten) that enables internal realization (གོང་འཕེད་ gong 'phel). In tantric practice, external conditions are not separate from internal realization—the external environment is a reflection of internal clarity.

**Light as Medium:** The specification of sunbeams (ཉི་ཟེར་ nyi zer) reflects the རྙིང་མ་ (Nyingma) understanding that natural light serves as the medium for visionary experience. The thödgal practitioner works with actual light, not imagination.

**Mirror Function:** The "mirror that clarifies" (གསལ་བར་བྱེད་པའི་མེ་ལོང་ gsal byed me long) refers to the crystal or mirror used to concentrate and direct light, serving as an aid to concentration and a support for generating luminous visions.

[9404-9412] <concept>
**Philosophical Framework:** This passage reflects the རྫོགས་ཆེན་ (Dzegchen) integration of external conditions and internal realization. The practice chamber is not merely a physical space but functions as:

1. **Field of Merit (བྱེད་སྤྱི་ཞིང་ bsod nams zhing):** Where merit accumulates through practice
2. **External Support (རྟེན་ rten):** Mirrors the internal channels and energies
3. **Primordial Purity (ཁ་དག་ ka dag):** The emphasis on purity (དྲི་མ་མེད་ dri ma med) indicates the practice environment mirrors the primordial purity to be recognized

**Blessing (བྱིན་གྱིས་ byin rlabs):** The blessing of རྡོ་རྗེ་སེམས་དཔའ་ (rdo rje sems dpa', Vajrasattva) indicates that material preparations must be consecrated to become effective supports for realization. Without empowerment, external objects remain mere objects; with empowerment, they become supports for awareness.

**Vajrasattva Blessing:** The Vajrasattva (རྡོ་རྗེ་སེམས་དཔའ་) is the sambhogakāya Buddha of purification. His blessing cleanses the practice space of obstructions and empowers it for realization. This reflects the tantric principle that all phenomena can be transformed through sacred perception (ཡིད་ལྟོས་ yid ltos) and consecration (བྱིན་གྱིས་བརླབས་ byin rlabs).
