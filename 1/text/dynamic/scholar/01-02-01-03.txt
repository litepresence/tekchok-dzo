<structure>
**Philological Analysis:** Second tier (*bang rim gnyis pa*) of the Heaven of the Thirty-Three, occupied by gods adorned with flower garlands (*me tog gi phreng ba*).

<philology>
**Key Terms:**
- *gnyis pa* (second) — ordinal numeral with *-pa* suffix
- *me tog* (flower) + *phreng ba* (garland) — compound describing ornamentation
- *thogs pa* (holding) — participle indicating characteristic activity

<context>
**Doctrinal Framework:** The three tiers of Trayastrimsa heaven represent progressively refined desire-realm pleasures: protection → aesthetics → intoxication. Flower garlands symbolize merit manifesting as beauty.

<concept>
**Symbolic Significance:** Floral adornment represents the aesthetic dimension of heavenly existence—accumulated merit as sensory refinement, yet still within cyclic existence.
