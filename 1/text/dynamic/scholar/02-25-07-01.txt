[16972-16995] <structure>
**Fivefold Fruition Architecture:** This passage presents the twenty-fifth and final major section (rim khang nyi shu rtsa lnga pa) of the Treasury, systematically delineating the five Buddha-qualities (*sku gsum yon tan 'phrin las*) as spontaneous results ('bras bu lhun grub). Lines 16972-16995 establish the three-kāya taxonomy through essence (*ngo bo*), definitive terminology (*nges tshig*), and divisions (*dbye ba*)—the standard scholastic *sa bcad* pattern. The section serves as textual culmination, completing the architectural arc from homage to fruition through Body (*sku*), Speech (*gsung*), Mind (*thugs*), Qualities (*yon tan*), and Activities (*'phrin las*).

[16972-16995] <philology>
**Tripartite Terminology:** *Chos sku* (Dharma-kāya): "Free from elaboration" (*spros pa dang bral ba*)—empty of eight extremes. *Longs spyod rdzogs sku* (Saṃbhoga-kāya): "Free from marks" (*mtshan ma dang bral ba*)—spontaneously present (*lhun gyis grub pa*) for bodhisattvas. *Sprul sku* (Emanation-kāya): "Free from determination" (*nges pa dang bral ba*)—appearing variously (*sna tshogs su ston*) according to disciples' needs. **Tantra Citation:** *Rtsal rdzogs* provides definitional verses (lines 16988-16994) with parallel structure: *sku yi ngo bo 'gyur ba med* (unchanging essence), *gsal 'tsher* (clear-blazing terminology), *du* enumeration marking threefold division. **Particles:** *kyis* (16976) instrumental; *las* (16987) ablative source; *dang* (16984-16986) enumerative; *ste* (16986) emphatic.

[16972-16995] <context>
**Nyingma Synthesis:** Integrates *Ratnagotravibhāga* (Rgyud bla ma) Buddha-nature theory with tantric three-kāya structure. Dzogchen distinctiveness emphasizes *lhun grub* (spontaneous presence): activities occur "until saṃsāra is empty" without effort—like moon-in-water reflection. Unlike Sarma sequential attainment, Nyingma presents three-kāya as primordially complete (*ka nas rdzogs pa*) to be recognized rather than achieved. Citations: *Dkon mchog sgron me* (16979-16982) establishes Dharma-kāya as Buddha-body; *Rtsal rdzogs* (16987-16995) provides three-kāya definitions.

[16972-16995] <concept>
**Three Kāya Ontology:** Dharma-kāya transcends eight extremes (*mtha' brgyad*)—neither existent nor non-existent, not both, not neither. Saṃbhoga-kāya manifests five certainties (*nges pa lnga*) in pure lands. Emanation-kāya appears as any being (*'gro drug*) responsive to karma and merit. Ultimately indivisible (*dbyer med*): emptiness (basis) = enjoyment (expression) = activity (responsiveness). The asymmetry of defining characteristics—free from elaboration, marks, determination—maps onto the three aspects: essence (*ngo bo*), nature (*rang bzhin*), and compassion (*thugs rje*).

[16996-17033] <structure>
**Sixfold Speech Taxonomy:** Progressive refinement through: (1) Great Deed Speech (*mdzad pa chen po'i gsung*)—primary teaching; (2) Brahma Speech (*tshangs pa'i gsung*)—sixty melodic branches; (3) Six Migrations Speech (*'gro drug rang snang*)—realm-conforming languages; (4) Beings' Faith Speech (*sems can mos pa'i gsung*); (5) Symbolic Speech (*brda'i gsung*); (6) Word Speech (*tshig gi gsung*). Ontological hierarchy: Emanation-kāya (single-essence distinctions) → Saṃbhoga-kāya (symbolic, *brda tsam*) → Dharma-kāya (inexpressible meaning, *brjod med don*).

[16996-17033] <philology>
**Echo Metaphor (*brag ca*):** *Rgyud bla ma* citation (17011-17022) presents five characteristics: (1) without letters (*yi ge med*); (2) arising from others' cognition (*gzhan gyi rnam rig las byung*); (3) without conception (*rnam rtog med*); (4) without fabrication (*bzo med*); (5) neither outer nor inner (*phyi dang nang na gnas ma yin*). **Key Terms:** *Gang la gang 'dul* (16998)—appearing appropriately for whoever; *Yan lag drug cu* (16999)—sixty branches; *Dbang po tshim pa* (17001)—satisfying faculties; *Brda bstan pa* (17002)—demonstrating through symbols.

[16996-17033] <context>
**Buddhist Linguistic Philosophy:** Sūtra framework: Nirmāṇakāya speech (conventional, audible); Saṃbhogakāya speech (symbolic, supramundane); Dharmakāya speech (inexpressible). **Dzogchen perspective:** All speech is *rang snang* (self-appearing display). The six modes represent pedagogical adaptation: ordinary beings hear Word Speech only; bodhisattvas understand Symbolic and Faith-Responsive; Buddhas communicate through all six simultaneously. **Scriptural sources:** *Rgyud bla ma* (17010-17022); *Rtsal rdzogs* (17023-17026).

[16996-17033] <concept>
**Phenomenology of Buddha-Speech:** Echo arises from four conditions: sound-source, reflecting surface, air medium, cognizing ear. Similarly, Buddha-speech requires: Buddha's compassion, disciples' receptivity, teaching situation, cognitive apprehension. No echo exists independently—neither in sound, wall, air, nor ear. Yet the echo undeniably "occurs." This is the Middle Way: neither inherently existent nor non-existent. The six speech modes resolve into "speech of awareness" (*rig pa'i gsung*)—not produced by vocal cords, not heard by physical ears, direct transmission mind-to-mind.

[17034-17046] <structure>
**Threefold Mind Classification:** (1) Mindfulness-Ceased Mind (*dran pa sangs pa'i thugs*, 17036)—complete abandonment; (2) Vajra Unchanging Mind (*'gyur med rdo rje'i thugs*, 17037)—complete realization; (3) Various-Knowing Mind (*mkhyen pa sna tshogs pa'i thugs*, 17038)—complete deeds. Categorical logic: Abandonment (*spangs pa*) = removal of obstacles; Realization (*rtogs pa*) = direct knowledge; Deeds (*mdzad pa*) = compassionate activity. Parallel to three Buddha-bodies: Dharmakāya = Abandonment/emptiness; Saṃbhogakāya = Realization/enjoyment; Nirmāṇakāya = Deeds/activity.

[17034-17046] <philology>
**Mind Terminology:** *'Khrul pa med pa* (17034)—without delusion, indicating stainless awareness. *Rig pa rang sa na gsal ba* (17035)—awareness clearly abiding in own place. *Dran pa sangs pa* (17036)—mindfulness-ceased, beyond deliberate recollection. *'Gyur med rdo rje* (17037)—unchanging vajra, immutable suchness. *Rtsal rdzogs* citation (17040-17046): *Thugs kyi ngo bo 'khrul pa med* (mind-essence without delusion); *Thugs kyi nges tshig bsam par snang* (appears as thought); threefold division (*gsum du bstan*). Multiple genitives: *Ye shes dran pa sangs pa'i thugs* (awareness-wisdom of mindfulness-ceased mind).

[17034-17046] <context>
**Abhidharma to Dzogchen:** Standard Abhidharma: mind (*citta*) = six consciousnesses + mental factors. Mahāyāna transformation: *Tathāgatagarbha* (primordially pure), *Ālayavijñāna* (ground consciousness), *Prajñāpāramitā* (mind as emptiness). **Dzogchen fruition:** Dharmakāya mind = beyond deliberate mindfulness; Saṃbhogakāya mind = unchanging vajra (indestructible, luminous, empty); Nirmāṇakāya mind = various-knowing (all beings' minds individually). Distinctive Nyingma view: primordially complete (*ye nas rdzogs pa*), already present as ground (*gzhi*), recognition (*ngo shes*) is path.

[17034-17046] <concept>
**Fruition States:** Mindfulness-ceased transcends deliberate attention—no need to "remember" awareness; recognition (*ngo shes*) is spontaneous, like mirror reflecting without trying. Vajra Unchanging: not subject to birth/death, unaffected by conditions, yet spontaneously manifests all forms. Various-Knowing: not accumulating facts but direct simultaneous knowing of all knowables. Integration: empty (dharmakāya) + luminous (saṃbhogakāya) + responsive (nirmāṇakāya) = one mind. Recognition of this is Buddhahood.

[17047-17060] <structure>
**Three-Level Qualities:** (1) Ground-qualities (*gzhi la gnas pa'i yon tan*, 17050)—Dharmakāya abiding, self-benefit complete (*rang don mthar phyin*); (2) Path-qualities (*lam la snang ba'i yon tan*, 17051)—Saṃbhoga-kāya clear, other-benefit arising (*gzhan don mdzad pa 'char*); (3) Fruition-qualities (*mtha' la skyol ba'i yon tan*, 17052)—Emanation-kāya guiding, conducting to ultimate. **Jewel Simile:** *Nor bu lta bu* (like a jewel) indicates spontaneous perfection (*lhun grub tu 'byung*)—wish-fulfilling gem (*yid bzhin nor bu*) providing all needs effortlessly.

[17047-17060] <philology>
**Quality Terminology:** *Legs dgu ma lus pa* (17048)—all nine perfections without exception. *Lhun grub tu 'byung ba* (17048)—spontaneously arising. *Rang sa zin pa* (17050)—abiding in own place. *'Khar la gsar ba* (17051)—clear in vase/container (Saṃbhoga-kāya appears within dharmadhātu-vase). *Mtha' la skyol ba* (17052)—conducting to ultimate. *Rtsal rdzogs* citation (17054-17060): parallel threefold enumeration with *yin pas* (since) causal connective establishing ground-path-fruition relationship.

[17047-17060] <context>
**Buddha-Qualities Framework:** Sūtra: thirty-two marks (*mtshan sum cu rtsa gnyis*), eighty beauties (*dpe brgyad cu*). Tantra: innumerable qualities, spontaneously present (*lhun grub*), not fabricated. **Nyingma synthesis:** Sūtra (gradual accumulation) + Tantra (swift transformation) + Dzogchen (primordial completion). **Sarma view:** qualities must be attained (*thob bya*) through path practice. **Nyingma view:** qualities primordially complete (*ye rdzogs*), already present as ground, recognition is path. **Jewel symbolism:** *Cintāmaṇi* (wish-fulfilling gem), *Maṇi* (jewel in lotus), clarity/value/indestructibility/wish-fulfillment.

[17047-17060] <concept>
**Spontaneous Perfection:** Ordinary understanding: qualities must be acquired through effort and practice over time. Dzogchen correction: qualities already present as nature of awareness; recognition, not accumulation. Ground-qualities: what needs to be done for oneself is done—not "achieved" but "recognized" (like realizing you already have the jewel). Path-qualities: appearance is spontaneous expression, doesn't require departure from ground (like gem's light naturally illuminating). Fruition-qualities: guiding beings doesn't exhaust the guide; activity continues "until saṃsāra is empty" (like gem fulfilling wishes without depletion). If qualities are primordially present: no essential difference between saṃsāra and nirvāṇa; defilements are adventitious (*glo bur*); qualities are intrinsic (*rang bzhin*); recognition is awakening.

[17061-17109] <structure>
**Four Enlightened Activities:** (1) Pacifying (*zhi ba*, 17085-17086)—purifying obscurations, quelling suffering; (2) Increasing (*rgyas pa*, 17087-17088)—developing qualities, fulfilling aspirations; (3) Magnetizing (*dbang*, 17089-17090)—gathering beings, maturing through empowerment; (4) Wrathful (*drag po*, 17091-17094)—subduing malevolence, crushing obstacles. **Structural Formula:** Activity name (instrumental case) → Object (*'gro ba*) → Effect → Result (inexhaustible wheel of ornamentation). Culmination (17095-17096): "Body, speech, mind, qualities, activities—the inexhaustible wheel of ornamentation's Buddha-field completely perfected, greatly expanded."

[17061-17109] <philology>
**Activity Terms:** *Zhi ba* (17085)—pacifying, root *zhi* (peace). *Rgyas pa* (17087)—increasing/expanding. *Dbang* (17089)—magnetizing/power, short for *dbang du byed pa* (bringing under power). *Drag po* (17091)—wrathful/violent, *drag* (fierce), not cruelty but powerful subjugation. *Seng ge rtsal rdzogs* citation (17098-17109): *'Phrin las ngo bo 'grub par byed* (activity essence accomplishes); *'Phrin las nges tshig bya ba byed* (definitive terminology performs actions); *Dbye na rnam pa bzhis bstan pa'o* (divisions: four aspects). Simile: *Zla gzugs chu nang du ston pa lta bu* (17074)—like showing moon in water.

[17061-17109] <context>
**Maṇḍala Correspondence:** East (Pacifying): Vairocana, Buddha family, white. South (Increasing): Ratnasambhava, Jewel family, yellow. West (Magnetizing): Amitābha, Lotus family, red. North (Wrathful): Amoghasiddhi, Karma family, green. **Tantric ritual application:** Pacifying (*śānti*)—white, water, remove obstacles; Increasing (*pauṣṭika*)—yellow, earth, longevity/merit; Magnetizing (*vaśīkaraṇa*)—red, fire, influence/attraction; Wrathful (*raudra*)—black/dark blue, air, destruction of obstacles. **Scriptural sources:** *Mngon rtogs rgyan* (17066-17069)—activity continuity; *Rgyud bla ma* (17075-17079)—effortless engagement; *Seng ge rtsal rdzogs* (17081-17109); *Rig pa rang shar*.

[17061-17109] <concept>
**Effortless Accomplishment:** Ordinary causality: Cause → Effect (sequential); Agent → Action (intentional); Means → End (instrumental). Buddha-Activity: No sequential causation; no intentional agent; no instrumental means. Yet benefit occurs "until saṃsāra is empty." **Moon-in-water simile:** Moon (Buddha's compassion) exists independently, shines naturally, no intention to reflect. Water (disciples' minds) must be clear and still, provides reflective surface, conditioned and impermanent. Reflection appears when conditions meet—neither merely moon nor merely water, empty of inherent existence, yet functionally efficacious. **Dzogchen fruition:** Activities are display (*rol pa*) of awareness; not "done by" Buddha but natural expression; like sun shining—no deliberate intention, just nature.
