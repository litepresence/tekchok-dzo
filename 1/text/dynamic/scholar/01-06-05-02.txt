[8674] <structure>
Second: Feeling
Enumeration marker identifying feeling (*tshor ba*) as the second category in a phenomenological classification.

[8674] <philology>
*gnyis pa tshor ba ste myong ba'i dngos rjes la spyod* — "Second: feeling, engaging with the object of experiencing." *tshor ba* (feeling/sensation, Sanskrit *vedanā*) as the bridge between consciousness and objects.

[8674] <context>
Analysis of feeling as the fourth aggregate. Feeling functions as affective coloring—pleasant (*sukha*), unpleasant (*dukkha*), or neutral (*upeksā*)—conditioning subsequent responses.

[8674] <concept>
In Dzogchen, feelings are displays of awareness (*rig pa'i rnam pa*). Recognizing their empty nature transforms feeling from bondage to awakening—neither suppressing nor grasping, but seeing as empty yet appearing.
