## Two Truths Framework

**Core Teaching:** All phenomena gathered within samsara and nirvana are gathered into the self-nature of the two truths.

**Citations:**
- **Root Wisdom (Mūlamadhyamakakārikā):** "Buddhas taught the Dharma, relying correctly upon the two truths: the conventional truth of the world, and the ultimate truth of meaning."
- **Magical Illusion Net Tantra:** "One abides in the manner of the two truths."

**Structure:**
1. General proposition: phenomena gathered into two truths
2. Scriptural authority: citations from root tantras
3. Definition: conventional truth divided into false and true conventional

[2847-2848] <philology>
**Key Terms:**
- bden pa gnyis: two truths
- kun rdzob: conventional truth
- don dam: ultimate truth
- bden pa: truth/reality
- snang srid: appearance-existence
- 'khor 'das: samsara and nirvana
- rang bzhin: self-nature

**Definitions:**
- Conventional truth (kun rdzob bden pa): phenomena marked by characteristics appearing as objects of intellect
- False conventional: appearances that deceive—as if inherently real
- True conventional: same appearances recognized as dependently arisen, functional, yet empty
