# First: The Nature of Bardo
**Architectural Context:** This passage introduces the bardo (བར་དོ་) topic with systematic classification and analysis. The structure follows the standard Tibetan scholastic exposition format: establishing the nature (ངོ་བོ་ ngo bo), etymology (ངེས་ཚིག་ nges tshig), divisions (དབྱེ་བ་ bye ba), appearance mode (སྣང་ལུགས་ snang lugs), and boundary/limit (ས་མཚམས་ sa mtshams).

**General to Specific:** The passage first establishes the general definition of bardo as the state where external phenomena appear to internal mind (lines 12003-12004), then presents the two-fold typology: pure dharmatā bardo (line 12006) vs. impure delusion bardo (line 12007).

**Section Transition:** The text marks major transition with section colophon at 11991-11993, then begins the SECOND MAJOR TOPIC: bardo detailed exposition for intermediate practitioners (འབུད་པ་ rnams "intermediate practitioners").

**Fourfold Bardo Typology:** This passage introduces the classic fourfold bardo classification:
1. བར་དོ་སྤྱི་ (bardo spyi): natural existence bardo
2. འཆི་ཁའི་བར་དོ་ ('chi kha'i bardo): death-moment bardo
3. ཆོས་ཉིད་བར་དོ་ (chos nyid bardo): dharmatā bardo
4. སྤྱོད་ཡུལ་བར་དོ་ (spyod yul bardo): existence bardo

**Ontological Foundation:** Bardo is defined as "the state/condition where external phenomena appear to internal mind" (ཕྱི་ཡུལ་གྱི་ཆོས་ནང་གི་བློ་ལ་སྣང་བའི་གནས་སྐབས་). This establishes the དྲང་པོ་ (Yogācāra) epistemological basis—bardo is fundamentally a mode of cognition, not an external location.

**Two-Fold Typology:** The fundamental distinction is between:
1. **Pure Dharmatā Bardo (དག་པ་ཆོས་ཉིད་བར་དོ་):** Appearance as empty essence (ངོ་བོ་) and wisdom (ཡེ་ཤེས་)—the ground's self-manifestation as luminosity
2. **Impure Delusion Bardo (འཁྲུལ་པའི་བར་དོ་):** Appearance of the six realms (འཁོར་དྲུག་) as confused—ordinary samsaric experience

**rDzogs chen Distinction:** This reflects the fundamental རྫོགས་ཆེན་ distinction between:
- ཁ་དག་ (ka dag): "original purity"—the ground's essence
- ལྷུན་གྱིས་གྲུབ་པ་ (lhun gyis grub pa): "spontaneous presence"—the ground's manifestation

These are modes of recognition vs. delusion—the same appearances can be recognized as wisdom or experienced as confusion.

**Path Implication:** The passage establishes that both pure and impure bardos exist "within the space of primordial purity" (ཀ་དག་གི་དབྱིངས་), and that the impure can be "returned to purity" (ཀ་དག་ཏུ་ནུབ་) through recognition practice.
