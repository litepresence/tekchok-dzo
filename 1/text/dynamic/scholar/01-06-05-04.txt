[8676] <structure>
Fourth: Experience
Enumeration marker for the fourth category addressing the indivisibility of mind and wisdom.

[8676] <philology>
*bzhi pa myong ba ste sems dang ye shes gnyis dbyer med pa'i dus* â€” "Fourth: experience, the time when mind and wisdom are indivisible." *myong ba* (experience) marks culmination; *dbyer med* (indivisible/non-dual) indicates essential identity.

[8676] <context>
Completion stage context where experience reveals mind-wisdom non-duality. Culmination of the fourfold enumeration moving from joy to feeling to bliss to non-dual experience.

[8676] <concept>
Indivisibility (*dbyer med*) of mind (*sems*) and wisdom (*ye shes*) represents ultimate Dzogchen view. Not separate entities to unite, but inherently non-dual nature recognized through practice culmination.
