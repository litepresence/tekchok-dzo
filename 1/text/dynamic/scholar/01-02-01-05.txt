[1134-1174] <concept>
## The Formless Trap: Subtle Fixation

**Progressive Withdrawal:**

**Infinite Space:**
Object: Space (absence of form)
Fixation: On vastness, openness
Problem: Still objectifying space

**Infinite Consciousness:**
Object: Consciousness itself
Fixation: On knowing, luminosity
Problem: Reifying consciousness

**Nothingness:**
Object: Absence, void
Fixation: On emptiness as thing
Problem: Substantializing nothingness

**Neither-Nor:**
Object: Neither perception nor non-perception
Fixation: On transcendence itself
Problem: Cognitive exhaustion, confusion

**The Dzogchen Diagnosis:**

All four are:
- **Grasping at extreme**: Space, consciousness, void, neither
- **Missing the middle**: Non-dual awareness
- **Saṃsāric refinement**: Better saṃsāra, but still saṃsāra
- **Obstacle to liberation**: Can become fixation points

**The Buddha's Story:**
Siddhartha mastered all four under his early teachers:
- Āḷāra Kālāma: Taught infinite space
- Uddaka Rāmaputta: Taught infinite consciousness/neither-nor
- Buddha: Rejected them as insufficient
- Found "deathless" beyond formless

**Dzogchen Resolution:**
*Ka-dag* (primordial purity) transcends:
- Not space (has no location)
- Not consciousness (has no subject)
- Not nothingness (has no absence)
- Not neither-nor (has no paradox)

Natural awareness (*rig pa*) = spontaneous liberation from all four.
