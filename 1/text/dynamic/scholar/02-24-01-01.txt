**Nyingma Pure Land Framework:** This section integrates Dzogchen with pure land (*zhing*) traditions—distinct from Pure Land Buddhism (Amitabha devotion) but sharing goal of rebirth in buddha-field. **Distinction:** Self-nature emanation (*rang bzhin sprul pa*) = pure land as projection of mind's nature, not external location. **Sources:** *A ti bkod pa chen po* (Great Ati Display tantra)—cited at 15356-15403 with detailed pure land descriptions. **Comparison:** Sarma (new schools) emphasize single pure land (Sukhavati); Nyingma emphasizes five buddha-fields corresponding to five families (*rigs lnga*). **Historical:** Pure land visualization developed in Tibet (11th-14th c.) combining Indian *sukhāvatīvyūha* sūtras with Dzogchen mind-nature theory.
