[1] [10548] དང་པོ་ནས་མྱང་འདས་ལྷུན་གྱིས་གྲུབ་པའི་ཕྱིར་རོ།
[1] [10548] <ornament> First: regarding the purpose of demonstrating that mere unceasing display cannot constitute the true recognition of liberation from saṃsāra and nirvāṇa.

[2] [10549] །དེ་གཉིས་ཀྱི་གཞི་ལྷུན་གྲུབ་ཡིན་ནོ་ཞེས་པའང་མི་རིགས་ཏེ།
[2] [10549] <prose> The position holding that the ground of those two—saṃsāra and nirvāṇa—is mere unceasing display is thus also unreasonable upon analysis.

[3] [10550] ལྷུན་གྲུབ་ཡིན་ན་མི་འགྱུར་བས་འཁོར་འདས་འབྱུང་བར་མི་རིགས་སོ།
[3] [10550] <prose> If rigpa were merely unceasing display without recognition, it would be unreasonable for saṃsāra and nirvāṇa to arise and cease without any recognition of its own face.

[4] [10551] །ཡང་ན་སུ་ཡང་འབད་མེད་དུ་གྲོལ་བར་འགྱུར་ཏེ།
[4] [10551] <prose> Or alternatively, who among sentient beings would ever become liberated without recognizing their own nature whatsoever?

[5] [10552] རྒྱུ་འབྲས་ཡེ་ནས་ལྷུན་་གྲུབ་ཡིན་པའི་ཕྱིར་རོ།
[5] [10552] <prose> If cause and effect were both primordially spontaneously accomplished from the very beginning without any dependence on recognition,

[6] [10553] །དེ་ལྟ་ན་རྒྱུ་འབྲས་དུས་མཉམ་པར་ཐལ་ལ།
[6] [10553] <prose> Then cause and effect would necessarily exist simultaneously as one essence, rendering all distinction meaningless.

[7] [10554] འདོད་ན་རྒྱུ་འཁོར་བ་ཡིན་དུས།
[7] [10554] <prose> If one accepts this position, then when the cause is established as saṃsāra,

[8] [10555] འབྲས་བུ་མྱང་ངན་ལས་འདས་པ་མ་ཡིན་པས་གསལ་ལ།
[8] [10555] <prose> The effect would clearly not be able to manifest as nirvāṇa, for they would be identical.

[9] [10556] དེ་གཉིས་གཅིག་ན་འཁོར་བ་མྱང་འདས་སུ་ཐལ་ལ།
[9] [10556] <prose> If those two—saṃsāra and nirvāṇa—were truly one essence, then saṃsāra would necessarily follow wherever nirvāṇa is present.

[10] [10557] མྱང་འདས་འཁོར་བར་ཐལ་ཏེ་གཉིས་གཅིག་ཡིན་པའི་ཕྱིར་རོ།
[10] [10557] <prose> And nirvāṇa following upon saṃsāra would cause those two to be one without distinction—an absurd consequence. || 10 ||

[11] [10558] །འདོད་ན་ཐ་དད་པར་མི་རུང་ལ།
[11] [10558] <prose> If one accepts this identity, they cannot be separate in any meaningful way.

[12] [10559] དག་མ་དག་འདུག་པས་གསལ་ལོ།
[12] [10559] <prose> Clearly, pure and impure cannot exist simultaneously as cause and effect if they are one substance.

[13] [10560] །དེ་དག་དང་གཞན་ཡང་སྐྱོན་དུ་མ་གནས་ཏེ།
[13] [10560] <prose> Because of these and many other logical faults, the position of mere unceasing display as the ground does not abide under scrutiny.

[14] [10561] ཀློང་དྲུག་པ་ལས།
[14] [10561] <tantra> From the Klongdrukpa, treasury of the Great Perfection:

[15] [10562] ལྷུན་གྲུབ་སྣ་ཚོགས་གཞི་དེ་ཡང༌།
[15] [10562] <tantra> Mere spontaneous display being posited as the ground of all variety,

[16] [10563] །ཡང་དག་གཞི་ཉིད་མ་ལགས་ཏེ།
[16] [10563] <tantra> Is truly not the correct recognition of liberation.

[17] [10564] །གལ་ཏེ་ཡིན་ན་འདི་ལྟར་འགྱུར།
[17] [10564] <tantra> If it were, it would necessarily become like this:

[18] [10565] །འདི་དག་སེམས་ཅན་འབད་པས་ནི།
[18] [10565] <tantra> These sentient beings, without any effort or practice,

[19] [10566] །གྲོལ་བ་ཉིད་དུ་མི་འགྱུར་ཏེ།
[19] [10566] <tantra> Would never attain liberation itself from confusion.

[20] [10567] །མ་རིག་ཉིད་ཀྱིས་སྤགས་པའི་ཕྱིར།
[20] [10567] <tantra> Non-recognition of one's own face becomes the cause of continued obscuration. || 20 ||

[21] [10568] །རིག་པ་ཡོད་པས་གྲོལ་གསུངས་ན།
[21] [10568] <tantra> If mere awareness existing were said to be sufficient cause for liberation,

[22] [10569] །འབྲས་བུའང་གྲོལ་ཉིད་མི་འགྱུར་རམ།
[22] [10569] <tantra> Then the effect—liberation—also would not become distinct from ordinary existence.

[23] [10570] །དཔེར་ན་སོལ་བ་ནག་པོ་ལ།
[23] [10570] <tantra> For example, just as charcoal piled upon black earth,

[24] [10571] །བྱི་དོར་དག་གིས་དཀར་པོ་ཡི།
[24] [10571] <tantra> Cannot be cleaned to appear as pure white by any effort.

[25] [10572] །མདོག་ཏུ་བསྒྱུར་བར་མི་ནུས་བཞིན།
[25] [10572] <tantra> Like color that cannot change its fundamental nature,

[26] [10573] །རྒྱུ་དང་འབྲས་བུ་ཐ་དད་པས།
[26] [10573] <tantra> Cause and effect would remain forever separate without connection.

[27] [10574] །ཡང་ན་ལྷུན་གྲུབ་མ་ལགས་སོ།
[27] [10574] <prose> Again, mere unceasing display cannot truly constitute the authentic recognition of realization,

[28] [10575] །དེ་བཞིན་རྒྱུ་དང་འབྲས་གཅིག་ན།
[28] [10575] <prose> For if cause and effect were established as truly one without distinction,

[29] [10576] །འབད་པ་རྣམས་ལ་དགོས་མེད་འགྱུར།
[29] [10576] <prose> Then recognition and resting in one's own nature would become entirely unnecessary, rendering the direct path meaningless.

[30] [10577] །དེ་ཕྱིར་ལྷུན་གྲུབ་ཅེས་པའི་གཞི།
[30] [10577] <prose> Therefore, to merely name unceasing display as the ground without understanding its true relation to primordial purity || 30 ||

[31] [10578] །དོན་ངོ་བཏུབ་པ་ལགས་མ་ལགས།
[31] [10578] <prose> Is not a meaning that can be genuinely understood or established through direct recognition.

[32] [10579] །ཀུན་ཏུ་བཟང་པོས་བདག་ལ་གསུངས།
[32] [10579] <prose> Samantabhadra, the Primordial Buddha, spoke these words to me:

[33] [10580] །སྟོན་པས་གསུངས་པ་སེམས་དཔའ་ཆེ།
[33] [10580] <tantra> "O great bodhisattva, thus has the Teacher declared to the assembly,

[34] [10581] །ཇི་ལྟར་ཁྱོད་ཀྱིས་གོ་བ་བཞིན།
[34] [10581] <tantra> According to your own understanding and realization,

[35] [10582] །འདི་ཉིད་ཤེས་བྱའི་ཆོས་ཙམ་ལས།
[35] [10582] <tantra> This teaching of unceasing display as the sole ground exists merely among the conventional dharmas that can be known by the intellect,

[36] [10583] །ཡང་དག་ངོ་བོ་མ་ཡིན་ནོ།
[36] [10583] <tantra> And is not itself the correct essence of the ultimate recognition."

[37] [10584] །ཞེས་སོ།
[37] [10584] <prose> Thus it is declared.

[38] [10585] །གཉིས་པ་ལའང་འདོད་པ་དགོད་པ་ནི།
[38] [10585] <prose> The second position is also established and refuted in this manner:

[39] [10586] གཤིས་མ་གྲུབ་པས་མ་ངེས་ལ།
[39] [10586] <prose> Because the nature of awareness is not established as any definite entity, it remains uncertain and indeterminate;

[40] [10587] རྐྱེན་གྱིས་བསྒྱུར་བས་མ་ངེས་ཏེ།
[40] [10587] <prose> Because conditions transform and alter it in every moment, it remains uncertain without fixed nature. || 40 ||

[41] [10588] སེམས་སྡེ་ལས།
[41] [10588] <tantra> From the Semde teachings, the Mind Section of the Great Perfection, it is said:

[42] [10589] ངེས་མེད་མིང་གི་སྣ་ཚོགས་ལས།
[42] [10589] <tantra> "From the manifold names and designations given to uncertainty,

[43] [10590] །དོན་ཀྱང་བཏགས་པ་ཙམ་གྱིས་སྟོང༌།
[43] [10590] <tantra> The meaning too is merely labels, empty in themselves like echoes.

[44] [10591] །རང་བཞིན་གཅིག་ཏུ་མ་ངེས་པས།
[44] [10591] <tantra> Because the nature is not determined as singular or fixed,

[45] [10592] །གང་ལྟར་བཏགས་པ་དེ་ལྟར་སྣང༌།
[45] [10592] <tantra> Whatever label is applied through convention, appears accordingly without inherent reality."

[46] [10593] །ཞེས་སོ།
[46] [10593] <prose> Thus it is taught.

[47] [10594] །གཉིས་པ་དེ་དགག་པ་ནི།
[47] [10594] <prose> Second, the thorough refutation of that position holding uncertainty as the basis:

[48] [10595] གཅིག་ཡིན་ན་གཅིག་མ་ཡིན་པ་དངོས་འགལ་རྫས་ཐ་དད་པའི་རིགས་པས་གནོད་དེ།
[48] [10595] <prose> If the basis were truly one essence, it could not simultaneously be non-one—a direct logical contradiction; harm comes through reasoning about the impossibility of separate substances being one.

[49] [10596] གཞི་མ་ངེས་པ་དེ་འཁོར་བའི་འབྱུང་གཞི་ཡིན་ན་དེ་ཉིད་དུ་ངེས་པའི་ཕྱིར་རང་ཚིག་གིས་གསལ་ལ།
[49] [10596] <prose> If that uncertain basis were truly the cause-base from which saṃsāra arises, it would be clearly determined in itself as the cause of confusion through its own assertion.

[50] [10597] མྱང་འདས་ཀྱི་གཞི་ཡི་ནའང་གསལ་ལོ།
[50] [10597] <prose> Conversely, if it were truly the basis of nirvāṇa, this too would be clearly established as determined in a contradictory manner. || 50 ||

[51] [10598] །ཡང་གཞི་དེ་དག་པ་ཡིན་དུས་མ་དག་པ་མ་ཡིན་པས་རེས་འཇོག་མ་ངེས་པར་འདོད་པ་དང་འགལ་ལ།
[51] [10598] <prose> Moreover, if that basis were established as inherently pure, impurity could not possibly arise from it; thus accepting both alternation between purity and impurity and fundamental uncertainty causes direct logical contradiction.

[52] [10599] ཡང་དྲི་མ་དག་ནས་ཀྱང་མ་དག་པར་ལྡོག་པར་ཐལ་ཏེ།
[52] [10599] <prose> Moreover, proceeding logically from purity, impurity would also necessarily revert to purity, eliminating all distinction.

[53] [10600] གཞི་ལ་མ་ངེས་ཟིན་ས་མེད་པའི་ཕྱིར་རོ།
[53] [10600] <prose> Because the basis would remain uncertain without any determinate ground or established nature.

[54] [10601] །ཡང་འཁོར་འདས་ཕན་ཚུན་འཆོལ་བར་ཐལ་ཏེ།
[54] [10601] <prose> Also, saṃsāra and nirvāṇa would mutually mix together without distinction, destroying the path and fruition.

[55] [10602] གཞི་མ་ངེས་པས་གང་དུའང་འགྱུར་བ་ཡིན་པའི་ཕྱིར་རོ།
[55] [10602] <prose> The basis being fundamentally uncertain means it changes in every regard without any stability;

[56] [10603] །འདོད་ན་སེམས་ཅན་འབད་མེད་དུ་གྲོལ་ལ།
[56] [10603] <prose> Consequently, if one accepts this position, all sentient beings would be liberated without any effort or practice whatsoever,

[57] [10604] སངས་རྒྱས་མ་དག་པར་ལོག་ནས་སྡུག་བསྔལ་ལ་སྤྱོད་པ་ཐལ་ལོ།
[57] [10604] <prose> While conversely, fully awakened Buddhas would revert to impure confusion, once again engaging with suffering.

[58] [10605] །ཡང་གཞི་དེ་སེམས་པ་ཅན་དུ་ཐལ་ཏེ་མ་ངེས་པའོ་ཞེས་གཅིག་ཏུ་དམ་འཆའ་བར་ངེས་པའི་ཕྱིར་རོ།
[58] [10605] <prose> Moreover, that basis would necessarily follow as a possession of mind rather than mind's true nature; thus asserting only uncertainty would itself constitute a form of determination, contradicting the original claim.

[59] [10606] །དེ་དག་ལ་སོགས་པའི་སྐྱོན་དུ་མ་ཡོད་དེ།
[59] [10606] <prose> Many faults such as these and more would necessarily arise from this position.

[60] [10607] ཀློང་དྲུག་པ་ལས།
[60] [10607] <tantra> From the Klong drug pa, profound treasury of Great Perfection teachings: || 60 ||

[61] [10608] མ་ངེས་པ་ཡི་གཞི་གསུངས་པ།
[61] [10608] <tantra> "The basis taught as fundamentally uncertain and indeterminate—

[62] [10609] །འདི་ཡང་གཞི་ཉིད་མི་བཏུབ་པར།
[62] [10609] <tantra> This too cannot truly constitute the authentic basis of reality.

[63] [10610] །བདག་གིས་བློ་རིམ་འདི་ལྟར་གོ
[63] [10610] <break> <tantra> I understand at this level of intelligence and analysis

[64] [10611] །འབྲས་བུ་རེས་འཇོག་ཅན་ཉིད་དམ།
[64] [10611] <break> <tantra> That effects must necessarily possess alternation and change,

[65] [10612] <tantra> Or become endowed with reversion to their cause.

[66] [10613] །མ་ངེས་ངེས་པ་ཅན་འགྱུར་ཞིང༌།
[66] [10613] <tantra> The uncertain itself becomes determined through its own existence,

[67] [10614] །མ་ངེས་པ་ནི་ངེས་ཅན་འགྱུར།

[68] [10615] <tantra> Because uncertainty must exist in itself to be asserted,

[70] [10617] །འདི་ཀུན་ཤེས་བྱ་མ་ལགས་སམ།
[70] [10617] <tantra> Are not all these knowable dharmas clear to the intelligent? || 70 ||

[71] [10618] །སྟོན་པས་གསུངས་པ་དེ་བཞིན་ནོ།
[71] [10618] <tantra> The Teacher has spoken thus regarding this fault."

[72] [10619] །ཞེས་སོ།
[72] [10619] <prose> Thus it is definitively established.

[73] [10620] །གསུམ་པ་ལའང་འདོད་པ་བརྗོད་པ་ནི།
[73] [10620] <prose> The third position is also expressed in this manner by proponents of philosophical views:

[74] [10621] །ངོ་བོ་ལ་འགྱུར་བ་མེད་པས་ནམ་མཁའ་ལྟ་བུ།
[74] [10621] <prose> Because the essence of awareness does not change in its nature, it is like space itself—pervasive and unchanging;

[75] [10622] སྣང་ཚུལ་བསྒྱུར་དུ་མི་བཏུབ་པ་མེ་དང་ཆུ་ལྟ་བུར་ངེས་པར་འདོད་དོ།
[75] [10622] <prose> Appearances, being unable to change in their fundamental nature, are accepted as determined and fixed, like the nature of fire and water.

[76] [10623] །གཉིས་པ་དེ་དགག་པ་ནི།
[76] [10623] <prose> Second, the thorough logical refutation of that position:

[77] [10624] ངོ་བོ་ལ་མེད་སྣང་ཚུལ་བསྒྱུར་དུ་མི་བཏུབ་པས།
[77] [10624] <prose> If the essence of awareness does not change in any way and appearances likewise cannot change,

[78] [10625] གཞི་དེ་ལས་འཁོར་བ་འབྱུང་བར་མི་འཐད་ཅིང༌།
[78] [10625] <prose> Then saṃsāra arising from that unchanging basis is not logically valid,

[79] [10626] སུ་ཡང་གྲོལ་བར་མི་རུང་ངོ༌།
[79] [10626] <prose> And consequently no one could possibly attain liberation from what never arose.

[80] [10627] །མྱ་འདས་ཐོག་མ་ནས་ཡོད་ན་མུ་སྟེགས་རྟག་པ་དང་ཁྱད་མེད་ལ་རྒྱུ་རྐྱེན་ལས་གྲོལ་བར་འདོད་པ་དང་འགལ་ལོ།
[80] [10627] <prose> If nirvāṇa existed primordially as an unchanging essence, there would be no difference from the eternalists' view of permanence maintained by non-Buddhist philosophers; accepting liberation arising from causes and conditions would directly contradict this position. || 80 ||

[81] [10628] །འཁོར་བའི་ངོ་བོ་མྱང་འདས་ཡིན་པས་མི་འགྱུར་ཅེས་འདོད་ནའང༌།
[81] [10628] <prose> Furthermore, if one accepts that the essence of saṃsāra, already being nirvāṇa in itself, does not change,

[82] [10629] ངོ་བོ་མྱ་འདས་ཡིན་ན་གཟོད་འགྲོལ་མི་དགོས་ཏེ།
[82] [10629] <prose> Then if the essence itself is already nirvāṇa without change, liberation through practice would not be necessary at all—

[83] [10630] མེ་ཚ་བར་ད་གཟོད་བསྒྱུར་མི་དགོས་པ་བཞིན་ནོ།
[83] [10630] <prose> Just as fire, being hot by its very nature, requires no change or effort to become hot.

[84] [10631] །བློ་སྣང་ལ་དགོས་སོ་ཞེ་ན།
[84] [10631] <prose> If one says this analysis is necessary only regarding mental appearances and not the basis itself,

[85] [10632] སྣང་ཚུལ་བསྒྱུར་མི་བཏུབ་པ་དང་འགལ་ལོ།
[85] [10632] <prose> This contradicts the fundamental position that appearances cannot change if the basis is unchanging.

[86] [10633] །འཁོར་བའི་སྣ་ཚུལ་ཡིན་གྱི།
[86] [10633] <prose> Being that the appearance of saṃsāra itself manifests from the basis,

[87] [10634] གཞིའི་སྣང་ཚུལ་མིན་ནོ་ཞེ་ན།
[87] [10634] <prose> If one says it is not the appearance of the basis but something separate,

[88] [10635] གཞི་དེ་གཉིས་བཅས་སུ་ཐལ་ཏེ།
[88] [10635] <prose> It necessarily follows that that basis would possess duality—appearing and not appearing—which is absurd.

[89] [10636] གཞི་ལས་ལོགས་ན་འཁོར་བའི་སྣང་ཚུལ་ཡོད་པའི་ཕྱིར་རོ།
[89] [10636] <prose> Because the appearance of saṃsāra exists separate from the basis itself in this view, creating duality where none should exist.

[90] [10637] །ཡང་གཞི་དེ་རྟོགས་ནས་ངེས་པར་འདོད་ན་གཞིར་མི་རུང་སྟེ།
[90] [10637] <prose> Moreover, if one realizes that basis and accepts determination, it cannot constitute the authentic basis that transcends all extremes. || 90 ||

[91] [10638] རྟོགས་པའི་སྔོན་དུ་རྟོགས་གཞི་ཡོད་ལ།
[91] [10638] <prose> If a basis of realization were to exist inherently before realization itself occurs,

[92] [10639] དེ་ནས་རྟོག་པའི་བློ་བྱུང་བའི་ཕྱིར་རྟོགས་པ་ལ་གཞི་མི་ལྟོས།
[92] [10639] <prose> Then a merely cognizing mind arises independently, and thus realization does not depend upon realizing the basis—making realization meaningless.

[93] [10640] གཞི་ལ་རྟོགས་པའི་བློ་ལྟོས་ཀྱང༌།
[93] [10640] <prose> Yet the basis depends upon the mind of realization to be known, creating circular dependence.

[94] [10641] རྟོགས་པའི་སྔོན་ནས་ཡོད་དགོས་པས་ཁྱབ་བོ།
[94] [10641] <prose> Because realization must exist beforehand to realize the basis, this logical error pervades the entire position.

[95] [10642] །མ་རྟོགས་ཀྱང་གཞི་ཡིན་ན་གཞི་ཡིན་པར་གང་གིས་ཤེས།
[95] [10642] <prose> If what is not realized is also claimed to be the basis, what knows it to be the basis?

[96] [10643] བློས་ཤེས་ན་མ་རྟོགས་ཀྱང་གཞི་ཡིན་པ་བུད་དོ།
[96] [10643] <prose> Wherein, if ordinary mind knows it, then not realizing it also becomes the basis—rendering all distinction meaningless.

[97] [10644] །དེ་དག་ལ་སོགས་པའི་སྐྱོན་དམིགས་པ་ཡང༌།
[97] [10644] <prose> Perceive also the many faults that follow logically from this untenable position.

[98] [10645] ཀློང་དྲུག་ལས།
[98] [10645] <tantra> From the Klong drug pa, treasury of Dzogchen instructions:

[99] [10646] །ངེས་པའི་གཞི་ཞེས་བསྟན་པ་ལ།
[99] [10646] <tantra> "Regarding the determined basis that is taught by some proponents,

[100] [10647] །འདི་ཡང་ཡང་དག་སྙིང་པོ་མིན།
[100] [10647] <tantra> This too is not the correct essence of the authentic basis. || 100 ||

[101] [10648] །དཔེར་ན་མིག་སེར་ཅན་དག་ལ།
[101] [10648] <tantra> Just as for those with the eye disease of yellowing vision,

[102] [10649] དུང་ལས་སེར་པོ་སྣང་བ་བཞིན།
[102] [10649] <tantra> A pure white conch appears mistakenly as yellow,

[103] [10650] །རང་ལ་ལྡོག་པའི་ཁྱད་ཀྱིས་ནི།
[103] [10650] <tantra> Through the distinction of reversion to oneself and other,

[104] [10651] །རང་གཞན་གཉིས་མེད་ངེས་མཐའ་བྲལ།
[104] [10651] <tantra> Self and other are free of the two extremes of determination.

[105] [10652] །དེ་ཕྱིར་དེ་ཡང་ཡང་དག་མིན།
[105] [10652] <tantra> Therefore that view too is not correct upon analysis.

[106] [10653] །ཡང་ན་རྒྱུར་ངེས་འབྲས་ངེས་པས།
[106] [10653] <tantra> Or, because cause is determined and effect is determined as identical,

[107] [10654] །བདག་རྟག་པ་དང་ཁྱད་མེད་འགྱུར།
[107] [10654] <tantra> The self becomes no different from the eternalist view of permanence.

[108] [10655] །འདི་ཡང་ཤེས་བྱ་མ་ལགས་སམ།
[108] [10655] <tantra> Are not these knowable dharmas apparent to the wise?

[109] [10656] །གསུངས་པ་དེ་ཡང་གོང་དང་མཚུངས།
[109] [10656] <tantra> What was said before accords with what has been shown here."

[110] [10657] །ཞེས་སོ།
[110] [10657] <prose> Thus it is definitively established by reasoning. || 110 ||

[111] [10658] །བཞི་པ་ལའང་འདོད་པ་བརྗོད་པ་ནི།
[111] [10658] <prose> The fourth position is also expressed by proponents of this view:

[112] [10659] ངོ་བོ་ཕྱོགས་དང་རིས་སུ་མ་ཆད་ལ།
[112] [10659] <prose> The essence is not divided into directions or categories in its true nature,

[113] [10660] །སྣང་ཚུལ་ཅིར་ཡང་བསྒྱུར་དུ་བཏུབ་པའོ།
[113] [10660] <prose> While appearances are changeable in whatever manner through conditions.

[114] [10661] །གཉིས་པ་དེ་དགག་པ་ནི།
[114] [10661] <prose> Second, the logical refutation of that untenable position:

[115] [10662] གཞི་བསྒྱུར་དུ་བཏུབ་ན་དག་པ་སངས་རྒྱས་མ་དག་པ་འཁྲུལ་པར་འགྱུར་ལ།
[115] [10662] <prose> If the basis were truly changeable in its essence, pure enlightened Buddhas would devolve into impure confusion.

[116] [10663] སུས་ཀྱང་མ་འབད་པར་སེམས་ཅན་སངས་རྒྱས་སུ་བསྒྱུར་ནས་གྲོལ་བར་ཐལ་ལོ།
[116] [10663] <prose> Furthermore, whoever accepts this unacceptable consequence follows that sentient beings, without any effort or practice, spontaneously change into Buddhas and are liberated.

[117] [10664] །ཡང་འབྲས་བུ་རྒྱུར་ལྡོག་པར་ཐལ་ཏེ།
[117] [10664] <prose> Also, effect would illogically revert to cause, destroying temporal sequence.

[118] [10665] འགྱུར་བའི་གཞི་ཡིན་པའི་ཕྱིར་རོ།
[118] [10665] <prose> Because it would be a basis of fundamental change rather than stability.

[119] [10666] །ཡངས་སོགས་བེམས་པོའང་རིག་པར་འགྱུར་ལ།
[119] [10666] <prose> Earth and other material elements would become awareness, and

[120] [10667] རིག་པའང་བེམས་པོར་འགྱུར་པར་ཐལ་ལོ།
[120] [10667] <prose> Awareness would also devolve into matter—absurd consequences. || 120 ||

[121] [10668] །ཡང་ངོ་བོ་རིས་སུ་མ་ཆེད་པ་དེ་བསྒྱུར་རམ།
[121] [10668] <prose> Moreover, if that which is not divided into categories of essence in the basis itself changes,

[122] [10669] །གཞན་བསྒྱུར།
[122] [10669] <prose> Or if appearances other than essence change,

[123] [10670] ངོ་བོ་བསྒྱུར་ན་ངོ་བོ་གཞིར་མི་རུང་སྟེ།
[123] [10670] <prose> If the essence itself changes, essence cannot coherently be called the basis.

[124] [10671] གཞི་ལས་གཞན་པའི་ཆོས་སུ་བསྒྱུར་བའི་ཕྱིར་རོ།
[124] [10671] <prose> Because it would change into a dharma other than the authentic basis.

[125] [10672] །གཞན་བསྒྱུར་ན་ངོ་བོ་གང་དུ་ཡང་བསྒྱུར་བའི་དམ་བཅའ་ཉམས་སོ།
[125] [10672] <prose> If appearances other than essence change, the essence itself would be damaged by changing somewhere, compromising its integrity.

[126] [10673] །ཡང་རྟག་པ་བསྒྱུར་རམ།
[126] [10673] <prose> Also, one must ask: does the permanent change, or

[127] [10674] མི་རྟག་པ་བསྒྱུར།
[127] [10674] <prose> Does the impermanent change in this view?

[128] [10675] རྟག་པ་བསྒྱུར་དུ་མི་རུང་སྟེ་ནམ་མཁའ་བཞིན།
[128] [10675] <prose> The permanent cannot change by definition, like space which remains unaltered.

[129] [10676] མི་རྟག་པ་བསྒྱུར་མི་དགོས་ཏེ་འགྱུར་ཟིན་པའི་ཕྱིར།
[129] [10676] <prose> The impermanent does not need to change through effort, for it has already changed by its very nature.

[130] [10677] ཡང་དུས་གསུམ་གྱི་རིགས་པས་འདས་མ་འོངས་བསྒྱུར་རྒྱུར་མེད་དེ་དངོས་མེད་ཡིན་ལ།
[130] [10677] <prose> Also, through the reasoning of the three times—past, present, and future—past and future cannot be changed, being non-existent in the present moment. || 130 ||

[131] [10678] ད་ལྟའི་སྔ་ཆགས་འགགས།
[131] [10678] <prose> The present has already arisen and immediately ceased, so it cannot be changed.

[132] [10679] ཕྱི་ཆ་མ་སྐྱེས་པས་མེད་པའི་ཕྱིར།
[132] [10679] <prose> The later part of a moment is not yet born, being non-existent, so there is nothing to change.

[133] [10680] བསྒྱུར་རྒྱུ་མ་དམིགས་པས་གཞི་ཅིར་ཡང་བསྒྱུར་དུ་བཏུབ་པ་དེ་མི་འཐད་དེ།
[133] [10680] <prose> Because change cannot be perceived in any of the three times, that the basis is changeable in whatever way is not logically valid.

[134] [10681] དེ་ཉིད་ལས།
[134] [10681] <tantra> From that very source of reasoning,

[135] [10682] བསྒྱུར་དུ་བཏུབ་ཅེས་སྔར་གསུངས་པས།
[135] [10682] <prose> As was said before by the Teacher regarding what is called changeable:

[136] [10683] །རང་གི་གནས་ལུགས་བསྒྱུར་བཏུབ་ན།
[136] [10683] <tantra> "If one's own state of the basis is held to be changeable,

[137] [10684] འདི་ཡང་ངེས་པ་མེད་པར་མཚུངས།
[137] [10684] <tantra> This too is no different from being fundamentally undetermined.
