<directional-reification> <cardinal-substantialism>
**Misreading:**
The twenty-five stacked fields (*nyi shu rtsa lnga brtshegs ma*) arranged in directional patterns (southeast, southwest, etc.) interpreted as actual cosmic geography—literal locations one could navigate to.

**Why it arises:**
Cardinal directions suggest spatial arrangement. "Stacked" implies vertical structure. The elaborate descriptions sound like cosmic maps.

**Primary consequence:**
The fields are substantialized into destinations. Practitioners attempt to visualize or travel to these locations. The metaphorical nature (mandala of mind) is lost.

**Secondary consequences:**
Meditation becomes spatial navigation. The text's topology is literalized. The recognition that these are modes of awareness-display is missed.

**Cascade effects:**
Triggers <spatial-navigation> (traveling to fields), <directional-obsession> (cardinal fixation), <cosmic-tourism> (visiting realms), <topological-literalism> (maps as reality).

**Cascade effects:**
Triggers <pure-land-aspiration> (seeking rebirth), <deity-externalization> (worshipping field-guardians), <thal-ba-fetishism> (obsession with specific fields), <location-spirituality> (where matters).
