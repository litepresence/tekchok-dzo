[997-997] [AESTHETIC MERIT REIFICATION] [BEAUTY AS ACHIEVEMENT]
**Misreading:**
The flower-garland gods interpreted as showing that spiritual advancement brings beauty, pleasure, and lovely surroundings—like heaven's reward for good behavior.

**Why it arises:**
Flowers are universally appealing. The garland sounds like a prize. The progression from screens to flowers suggests improvement.

**Primary consequence:**
Spiritual path congeals into beautification project. Meditation aims at pleasant experiences. Recognition is confused with enjoyable states.

**Secondary consequences:**
Practitioners seek blissful meditation. Aesthetic preferences determine practice choices. Suffering is seen as failure rather than path.

**Cascade effects:**
<beauty obsession> → seeking pleasant states
<merit materialism> → goodness as currency
<aesthetic spirituality> → pretty = profound
<comfort addiction> → avoiding difficulty and [GARLAND GRASPING] (wearing the flowers).
