<!-- BLOCK 001: SPONTANEOUS COMPLETION WAITING -->
<!-- ================================================================ -->
<spontaneous-completion-waiting id="scw-001" severity="critical" tibetan-terms="ལྷུན་གྲུབ">
  <trigger>Waiting for *lhun grub* to happen</trigger>
  <delusion>
    The practitioner believes that *lhun grub* (spontaneous completion) is a future event 
    that will occur when conditions are ripe, not recognizing that *ka dag* (primordial purity) 
    is already complete. The error lies in temporalizing the timeless—treating spontaneous 
    completion as something to be achieved rather than recognized.
  </delusion>
  <tibetan-source>
    ཐོག་མའི་ཀ་དག་ལ་རིག་པ་རང་ངོ་ཤེས་ནས་ད་རྒྱུར་མི་ལྡོག་པས་འབྲས་བུ་ཞེས་བྱ་བར་མིང་འགྱུར་བའོ།
  </tibetan-source>
  <correction>
    *Lhun grub* is not a destination but the nature of rigpa itself. The recognition 
    that there is nothing to complete is the completion.
  </correction>
  <related-terms>
    ka dag (ཀ་དག), dzogpa (རྫོགས་པ), rangdrol (རང་གྲོལ)
  </related-terms>
</spontaneous-completion-waiting>

<!-- BLOCK 002: LIGHT BODY ACHIEVEMENT -->
<!-- ================================================================ -->
<light-body-achievement id="lba-002" severity="critical" tibetan-terms="འོད་ལུས">
  <trigger>Striving to achieve *od lus* (light body)</trigger>
  <delusion>
    The practitioner grasps at the *od lus* (light body of great transference) as a goal, 
    accumulating practices to transform the physical body into rainbow light. This misses 
    that *od lus* is the natural display of rigpa's luminosity, not a bodily transformation 
    to be accomplished through effort.
  </delusion>
  <tibetan-source>
    ལུས་འདི་བོར་ནས་འོད་ཀྱི་སྣང་བ་འཆར།
  </tibetan-source>
  <correction>
    The *od lus* is already present as the luminous nature of mind. Grasping at its 
    manifestation creates the very obscurations that prevent its recognition.
  </correction>
  <related-terms>
    od lus (འོད་ལུས), rang snang (རང་སྣང), ye shes (ཡེ་ཤེས), gsal ba (གསལ་བ)
  </related-terms>
</light-body-achievement>

<!-- BLOCK 003: RAINBOW BODY CHASING -->
<!-- ================================================================ -->
<rainbow-body-chasing id="rbc-003" severity="critical" tibetan-terms="འཇའ་ལུས">
  <delusion>
    The practitioner pursues *ja lus* (rainbow body) as the ultimate attainment, 
    believing the body must shrink and dissolve into light. This creates a subtle 
    materialism—treating the body as something to be transformed rather than recognizing 
    that all phenomena are already self-liberated (*rangdrol*).
  </delusion>
  <tibetan-source>
    ཕྲ་རགས་དེངས་པ་ཡིས། ཕུང་པོ་ལྔ་ཡང་མཐར་གྱིས་ཕྲ།
  </tibetan-source>
  <correction>
    *Ja lus* is the recognition that form is emptiness and emptiness is form. 
    The chase itself is the obstacle.
  </correction>
  <related-terms>
    ja lus (འཇའ་ལུས), stong pa (སྟོང་པ), rangdrol (རང་གྲོལ), snang srid (སྣང་སྲིད)
  </related-terms>
</rainbow-body-chasing>

<!-- BLOCK 004: TREKCHO COMPLETION PRIDE -->
<!-- ================================================================ -->
<trekcho-completion-pride id="tcp-004" severity="high" tibetan-terms="ཁྲེགས་ཆོད">
  <delusion>
    The practitioner believes *khregs chod* (cutting through) has been completed, 
    resting in the conviction that all thoughts are self-liberated. This pride of 
    completion (*rtogs pa'i nga rgyal*) becomes a new fixation, preventing the natural 
    unfolding of *thod rgal*.
  </delusion>
  <tibetan-source>
    བག་ཆགས་སྦུབས་ཀྱིས་ཡེ་ཤེས་སྒྱུ་ལུས་བསྒྲིབས།
  </tibetan-source>
  <correction>
    There is no one to complete trekcho and nothing to complete. The recognition 
    of this is itself the completion beyond completion.
  </correction>
  <related-terms>
    khregs chod (ཁྲེགས་ཆོད), thod rgal (ཐོད་རྒལ), nga rgyal (ང་རྒྱལ), rtogs pa (རྟོགས་པ)
  </related-terms>
</trekcho-completion-pride>

<!-- ================================================================ -->
<thogal-vision-completion id="tvc-005" severity="high" tibetan-terms="ཐོད་རྒལ">
  <delusion>
    The practitioner awaits *thod rgal* visions as signs of progress, interpreting 
    lights and deities as indicators of approaching completion. This turns the direct 
    crossing into a gradual path, creating dependence on appearances.
  </delusion>
  <tibetan-source>
    འོད་མེད་ཁ་དོག་རྣམས་དང་བྲལ། གང་གི་ངོ་བོ་གྲུབ་མེད་པས།
  </tibetan-source>
  <correction>
    *Thod rgal* is not about visions but the direct recognition of rigpa's own face. 
    Visions are spontaneous self-display (*rang snang*), neither to be pursued nor rejected.
  </correction>
  <related-terms>
    thod rgal (ཐོད་རྒལ), rang snang (རང་སྣང), ye shes (ཡེ་ཤེས), gtig gsal (གཏིང་གསལ)
  </related-terms>
</thogal-vision-completion>

<!-- BLOCK 006: FIVE-KAYA ACCUMULATION -->
<!-- ================================================================ -->
<five-kaya-accumulation id="fka-006" severity="high" tibetan-terms="སྐུ་ལྔ">
  <delusion>
    The practitioner attempts to accumulate or develop the five kayas (*sku lnga*) 
    through practice, missing that the five kayas are spontaneously present (*lhun grub*) 
    as the natural expression of *chos sku* (dharmakaya).
  </delusion>
  <tibetan-source>
    ཆོས་སྐུའི་ངང་ན་གཏིང་གསལ་ཀྱི་སྐུ་ལྔ་གཏིང་གསལ་དུ་གནས་པས།
  </tibetan-source>
  <correction>
    The five kayas are not developed but recognized. They are the innate radiance 
    (*rang gdangs*) of dharmakaya, never separate from it.
  </correction>
  <related-terms>
    sku lnga (སྐུ་ལྔ), chos sku (ཆོས་སྐུ), longs sku (ལོངས་སྐུ), sprul sku (སྤྲུལ་སྐུ), rang gdangs (རང་གདངས)
  </related-terms>
</five-kaya-accumulation>

<!-- BLOCK 007: THREE-BODY SEPARATION -->
<!-- ================================================================ -->
<three-body-separation id="tbs-007" severity="critical" tibetan-terms="སྐུ་གསུམ">
  <delusion>
    The practitioner conceives of *sku gsum* (three kayas) as separate entities—
    dharmakaya as emptiness, sambhogakaya as clarity, nirmanakaya as compassion. 
    This reification destroys their non-dual nature (*dbyer med*).
  </delusion>
  <tibetan-source>
    ཡེ་ཤེས་དབྱིངས་སུ་ཐིམ་ཡང་སྐུ་གསུམ་འདུ་འབྲལ་མེད་པར་ཕྲ་བའི་ཡེ་ཤེས་ནང་གསལ་དུ་གནས་པའོ།
  </tibetan-source>
  <correction>
    The three kayas are indivisible (*'du 'bral med pa*). They are three aspects 
    of a single reality, not three separate bodies.
  </correction>
  <related-terms>
    sku gsum (སྐུ་གསུམ), dbyer med (དབྱེར་མེད), 'du 'bral med (འདུ་འབྲལ་མེད), nang gsal (ནང་གསལ)
  </related-terms>
</three-body-separation>

<!-- BLOCK 008: DHARMAKAYA DESTINATION -->
<!-- ================================================================ -->
<dharmakaya-destination id="dd-008" severity="critical" tibetan-terms="ཆོས་སྐུ">
  <delusion>
    The practitioner aims to reach *chos sku* (dharmakaya) as a final destination, 
    believing it to be a state beyond all phenomena. This makes dharmakaya into an 
    object of attainment, missing that it is the nature of all experience.
  </delusion>
  <tibetan-source>
    ཆོས་སྐུ་བརྗོད་བྲལ་དེ་ཉིད་དུ་གཅིག་པས་ངོ་མཉམ་ཐ་དད་མེད་པའི་རང་བཞིན་ཐིག་ལེ་ཉག་གཅིག་ཏུ་སངས་རྒྱས་ཏེ།
  </tibetan-source>
  <correction>
    Dharmakaya is not reached but recognized as the ground (*gzhi*) of all experience. 
    It is the very basis from which one has never departed.
  </correction>
  <related-terms>
    chos sku (ཆོས་སྐུ), gzhi (གཞི), brjod bral (བརྗོད་བྲལ), ngo mnyam (ངོ་མཉམ), thig le (ཐིག་ལེ)
  </related-terms>
</dharmakaya-destination>

<!-- ================================================================ -->
<sambhogakaya-pure-land id="spl-009" severity="high" tibetan-terms="ལོངས་སྐུ">
  <delusion>
    The practitioner aspires to be reborn in the *longs sku* (sambhogakaya) pure lands, 
    treating them as literal locations where enlightenment is easier to attain. This 
    misses that sambhogakaya is the luminous display of one's own mind.
  </delusion>
  <tibetan-source>
    ཕྱག་རྒྱ་མཆོག་གི་ལོངས་སྤྱོད་ཆེན་པོར་རང་སྣང་བའོ།
  </tibetan-source>
  <correction>
    The pure lands are not locations but the natural expression (*rang snang*) of 
    wisdom. To seek them elsewhere is to overlook their presence here and now.
  </correction>
  <related-terms>
    longs sku (ལོངས་སྐུ), longs spyod (ལོངས་སྤྱོད), zhing khams (ཞིང་ཁམས), phyag rgya mchog (ཕྱག་རྒྱ་མཆོག)
  </related-terms>
</sambhogakaya-pure-land>

  </correction>
  <related-terms>
    sprul sku (སྤྲུལ་སྐུ), sprul pa (སྤྲུལ་པ), thugs rje (ཐུགས་རྗེ), 'phrin las (འཕྲིན་ལས)

<self-liberation-complacency id="slc-011" severity="high" tibetan-terms="རང་གྲོལ">
  <delusion>
    The practitioner adopts the view that everything is *rangdrol* (self-liberated) 
    and therefore no practice or discipline is needed. This intellectual understanding 
    without direct recognition becomes spiritual bypassing.
  </delusion>
  <tibetan-source>

  </tibetan-source>
  <correction>
    True self-liberation is not a belief but the direct recognition of rigpa. 
    Without this recognition, "everything is liberated" is just another concept.
  </correction>
  <related-terms>
    rangdrol (རང་གྲོལ), rang zad (རང་ཟད), rig pa (རིག་པ), rtogs pa (རྟོགས་པ)
  </related-terms>
</self-liberation-complacency>

<spontaneity-effort-paradox id="sep-015" severity="critical" tibetan-terms="ལྷུན་གྲུབ">
  <delusion>
    The practitioner creates an impossible paradox: trying to make spontaneity (*lhun grub*) 
    happen through effort. The more they try to rest without contrivance, the more 
    contrived the effort becomes.
  </delusion>
  <tibetan-source>
    ལྷུན་གྱིས་གྲུབ་པའི་ཡེ་ཤེས་སྐུ་གསུང་ཐུགས་མི་ཟད་པ་རྒྱན་གྱི་འཁོར་ལོའི་འབྱུང་གནས་སུ་ཕྲ་བའི་ཆར་གཏིང་གསལ་དུ་གནས་ཏེ།
  </tibetan-source>
  <correction>
    Spontaneity cannot be achieved. The moment of recognizing that effort itself 
    is spontaneously present is the resolution of the paradox.
  </correction>
  <related-terms>
    lhun grub (ལྷུན་གྲུབ), rtsol ba (རྩོལ་བ), gtig gsal (གཏིང་གསལ), rang byung (རང་བྱུང)
  </related-terms>
</spontaneity-effort-paradox>
