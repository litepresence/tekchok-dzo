[4848-4857] <methodological-error> [FIVE EXPLANATIONS AS REQUIREMENTS]
**Misreading:**
The five types of explanation (root meaning, yoga meaning, purpose meaning, word meaning, history meaning) are interpreted as mandatory structure that must be present for teaching to be valid, creating rigid formalism.

**Why it arises:**
Formalism habit. Enumerated structure suggests requirement. Missing any element seems like defect.

**Primary consequence:**
Rigid teaching format. Teachers feel compelled to include all five types; students expect all five as proof of authenticity.

**Secondary consequences:**
Formulaic presentations; formalism over substance; anxiety about "complete" explanation.

**Cascade effects:**
<ritual-formalism> through rigid structure.
<pedagogical-rigidity> through mandatory format.

Anxiety-driven learning. Students fear not receiving "complete" teaching; teachers fear not giving "proper" explanation.

Perfectionism paralysis; obsessive completion-seeking; missing that these are pedagogical pointers, not threats.

**Cascade effects:**
<anxiety-amplification> through defect-fear.
<perfectionism-paralysis> through completeness-obsession.

[4880-4884] <sectarian-error> [VIEW-PROTECTION AS POLEMIC]
**Misreading:**
The purpose statement ("to protect one's own view and refute others'") is interpreted as establishing Dzogchen as sectarian project of proving superiority over other traditions.

**Why it arises:**
Competitive religious mindset. "Refute others" sounds like polemic. Historical context of Tibetan school rivalries.

**Primary consequence:**
Sectarian defensiveness. The practitioner approaches Dzogchen as "correct view" to defend against "wrong views."

**Secondary consequences:**
Inter-school hostility; intellectual combat; missing that "refutation" is release of fixation, not winning debates.

**Cascade effects:**
<sectarianism> through competitive interpretation.
<intellectual-aggression> through view-combat.
