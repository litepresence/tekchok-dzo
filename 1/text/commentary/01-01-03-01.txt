The teaching here is whispered not spoken, written not in books but in the arrangement of stones pilgrims place one by one over centuries. Delightful arrangement of what was never arranged. Thal-ba-'dzin below the six worlds—wheel-shaped, completely pure like a prayer wheel spun by wind without hand. I dreamed of flying through these realms last winter. Passed through the wheel and saw my own face in every spoke, every realm, every Buddha-field. The teaching there is Truth itself, they say. What does that mean? It means no one is lying to you. No one is teaching you. The wheel turns itself. The wind prays itself.

[606-618]
Thalwa Brdal above—lotus-blossom shaped, extremely expansive like a glacier calving into turquoise lake. White and red shimmering without coarse form, the way dawn light touches snow peaks without touching. This is where abhidharma expands to fill the aeon. Like a flower opening so slowly that watching it, you become the opening. The skull-garlands at Thalwa's Drum are not ornaments of death. They are memories of heads that once thought they were separate, now strung together as one recognition.

Northeast intermediate—Thalwa's Current, stupa-shaped, five arrangements, various sounds emerging like hot spring water bubbling from earth's deep warmth. Dependent arising taught here not as dry philosophy but as the actual mechanism of how worlds emerge from attention—your attention, right now, creating this field. At twenty-one, the utterly pervasive: no inside, no outside. Just pervading, like sky pervades without effort. The causal condition pervading action—your next breath is already there, waiting for you to notice. The stone cairn at the mountain pass has been waiting centuries for you to add your stone.
