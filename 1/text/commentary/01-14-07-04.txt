Therefore. Thus. Precise analysis: Second, light ray thread appear, that from subtle and, move and, flicker and, stable and, fire also appear. Third, bindu rim arise. That from upward-stand and, side arise etc. also arise.

[19421-19430]
This old practitioner watched candle flames in a dark room. The flame flickered, moved, yet was always the same flame. The bindu appearances are like this—moving, changing, yet always the same nature. The movement is not the bindu changing; it is the display becoming more clear.

[19430-19440]
Voice 20 proclaims: Third, fruit self-ripen bindu three: from Sun-Moon Union—fruit self-ripen bindu three: light ripen bindu, wisdom ripen bindu, awareness ripen bindu. From that: light ripen bindu upright abide, very clear not-mixed rainbow like, this familiarized appearance also see.

[19435-19445]
Three words. Stop. Recognize. The fruit bindu is the completion of the path—not something new appearing, but the recognition of what has always been. Like realizing you've been wearing glasses your whole life. The glasses don't appear; you simply recognize what was already there.

[19440-19450]
Therefore. Thus. Precise analysis concludes: The bindu stages—upright, side-arise, etc.—are natural unfoldings, not achievements. Like a flower opening to the sun—it doesn't try to open; it opens naturally when conditions are right. Your recognition is the sun; let the bindu open naturally.

[19445-19455]
This old hermit offers final teaching on bindu: Don't be attached to any particular bindu appearance. Some see spheres, some see lights, some see nothing—all can be advanced or deluded. The measure is recognition, not appearance. Rest in that, and all appearances become wisdom's play.
