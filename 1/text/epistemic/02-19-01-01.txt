However, within Dzogchen hermeneutics this remains provisional (drang don) because operating through tantric methodology. The ngo bo gcig (same nature) principle is crucial: all nine vehicles point to same ground (gzhi) but approach through different capacities (dbang po) and methods (thabs). This prevents hierarchical elitism: Dzogchen is not "better" but more direct—suitable for those with sharp faculties (dbang po rab). Outer tantra remains valid and necessary for those requiring gradual approach (dbang po 'bring). Longchenpa's presentation honors diversity of practitioner capacities while establishing unity of the goal.

The definitive view recognizes all vehicles are upāya—expedient means for liberation—and none are ultimate in themselves. This is the dzogchen view beyond vehicles: not rejecting lower vehicles but recognizing their provisional status from perspective of ground that transcends all vehicles.>
