<Wisdom-space (*ye shes kyi klong*) threefold: (1) wisdom-emptiness obstacle-free (*ye shes stong pa gegs med kyi gnad*), (2) play-emptiness without interruption (*rol pa stong pa bar chad med pa'i gnad*), (3) indemonstrable essential-point (*bstan du med pa'i gnad*). The phrase *chos nyid 'gyur med gegs su med pa* (dharmatā unchanging obstacle-free) indicates *ye shes* as *rig pa*'s knowing quality—not separate from emptiness but its luminous clarity.>

<Space-space (*klong gi klong*) threefold: (1) essence as uncompounded (*ngo bo 'dus ma byas pa'i gnad*), (2) nature as birthless (*rang bzhin skye med kyi gnad*), (3) characteristics as unceasing play (*mtshan nyid 'gag med rol pa'i gnad*). This completes the *gnad drug* (six essential points) structure. The threefold pattern—essence (*ngo bo*), nature (*rang bzhin*), characteristics (*mtshan nyid*)—mirrors *sku gsum* (three bodies) topology applied to recognition itself.>

<Nature (*rang bzhin*) free of conceptual grasping (*rnam rtog gzung zad pa*), characteristics (*mtshan nyid*) free of dualistic identification (*gnyis med ngos gzung dang bral ba*)—this describes *klong* (space/expanse) as unobstructed openness. The phrase *chos nyid khungs nas gdon pa* (extracting dharmatā from source) is pointing-out instruction, not philosophical extraction.>
