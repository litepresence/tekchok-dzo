[2983] <ornament> First: Appearing to meaning, the aspect of one's own consciousness arises only outwardly.
[2984] <prose> As for example: When the image of a golden jewel hung upon a tree branch on the pond's bank appears in the water, the aspect of the jewel appears outwardly—just so.
[2985] <prose> In this, there is division.
[2986] <prose> The number of aspects is asserted to be the same as the number of consciousnesses.
[2987] <prose> Or asserting aspects and consciousnesses as split like an egg.
[2988] <prose> Or asserting aspects and consciousnesses as various and non-dual—these three.
