Seven aspects enumerated: (1) basis primordially pure from beginning (ka dag); (2) spontaneously present with qualities complete (lhun grub); (3) uncertain and indeterminate (ma nges); (4) certain and established as actual basis (nges pa); (5) transformable into anything (bsgyur rung); (6) accepting anything that arises (blangs rung); (7) various manifold multiplicity (sna tshogs). These seven represent different ways of recognizing single primordial purity, each perceiver accepting own recognition as complete.

Extended recognition of each aspect: Great sphere free from elaborations represents primordially pure. Unceasing display represents natural expression spontaneously possessing qualities like sun and rays. Uncertain represents nature not established singularly, freedom from extremes. Certain represents abiding without change. Transformable represents naturally appears as whatever manifests. Accepting represents all phenomena arise equally from awareness. Multiplicity represents appears without ceasing, naturally variegated.
