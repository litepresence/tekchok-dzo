[2539-2542] <structure>
Śrāvakayāna Introduction
**Analysis:** Longchenpa begins the presentation of the Śrāvakayāna (*nyan thos kyi theg pa*) with the fivefold structure standard in Tibetan doxography:

1. **Ngo bo** (Essence): The nature of the vehicle
2. **Nges tshig** (Etymology): Why it is so named
3. **Dgos pa** (Purpose): What it accomplishes
4. **'Bras bu** (Result): The fruition attained
5. **Dbye ba** (Divisions): Sub-schools

The structure reflects the *Grub mtha'* literature's analytical framework.

[2540-2541] <concept>
Essence of Śrāvaka Vehicle
**Key Definition:**
*'Khor ba las rang nyid gcig pur thar bar 'dod pa'i blo gang zag gi bdag med rtogs pa'i thabs su gyur pa'o*—"The method for realizing selflessness of persons for those whose minds desire liberation for themselves alone from saṃsāra."

**Analysis:**
This definition captures the distinctive feature of the Śrāvakayāna: the realization of *pudgalanairātmya* (gang zag gi bdag med) but not *dharmanairātmya* (chos kyi bdag med). The limitation is soteriological, not philosophical—"themselves alone" (*rang nyid gcig pu*) indicates the absence of the bodhisattva's altruistic motivation.

**Etymology** [2542]:
*Ston pa la chos nyan nas gzhan la sgrogs shing*—"Having heard the Dharma from the Teacher, proclaiming to others."

The term *śrāvaka* derives from Sanskrit *śru* (to hear): they are "hearers" who receive the teaching and transmit it.

[2547-2561] <philology>
Vaibhāṣika and Sautrāntika Sub-schools
**Analysis:**

**Vaibhāṣika** (*bye brag tu smra ba*) [2549-2561]:
- **Ontology**: Coarse conventionally (*rag kun rdzob*), ultimately atomic (*rdul phran*)
- **Cognition**: Continuous mentally (*shes rgyun kun rdzob*), momentary ultimately (*skad cig don dam*)
- **Simile**: Like a yak-tail fan and its hairs (*g.yag rnga ne'u gsing*)

**Key Assertion** [2556]: *Gang zag gi bdag med par khas blangs kyang chos kyi bdag rdul phran dang skad cig yod par khas blangs*—"While asserting selflessness of persons, they assert dharmas as truly existent atoms and moments."

This is the hallmark of the Vaibhāṣika: *pudgalanairātmya* without *dharmanairātmya*.

**Sautrāntika** (*mdo sde pa*) [2580-2584]:
- **Epistemology**: Twelvefold cognition (*shes pa bcu gnyis*) apprehending four truths
- **Purification**: Abandoning eighty-eight afflictions (*nyon mangs phra rgyas brgyad cu rtsa brgyad*)
- **Result**: Realization of sublime wisdom (*ye shes dam pa*)

[2557-2561] <context>
The Four Truths and Path Structure
**Analysis:**

The Vaibhāṣika path follows the classical structure:

**Four Truths** (*bden pa bzhi*):
1. *Sdug bsngal* — Suffering (to be known)
2. *Kun 'byung* — Origin (to be abandoned)
3. *Lam* — Path (to be cultivated)
4. *'Gog pa* — Cessation (to be attained)

**Twelve aspects** (*rnam pa bcu gnyis*):
- Three per truth (impermanence, suffering, emptiness, selflessness)

**Five paths** (*lam lnga*):
1. *Tshogs lam* — Path of accumulation
2. *Sbyor lam* — Path of preparation
3. *Mthong lam* — Path of seeing
4. *Sgom lam* — Path of meditation
5. *Mthar phyin lam* — Path of completion

**Four results** (*'bras bu bzhi*):
1. *Rgyun du zhugs pa* — Stream-enterer
2. *Lan gcig phyir 'ong ba* — Once-returner
3. *Phyir mi 'ong ba* — Non-returner
4. *Dgra bcom pa* — Arhat

This structure is common to all Buddhist vehicles, but the Śrāvaka achieves them through realizing only *pudgalanairātmya*.

[2690-2702] <concept>
Results and Stages
**Analysis:**

**Progressive Realization:**
The text details how the four results are attained through meditation on the four truths:

- Meditation on cessation (*'gog pa*) → Stream-enterer (s ābhadra to saptamī)
- Meditation on origin (*kun 'byung*) → Once-returner (up to aṣṭamī)
- Meditation on path (*lam*) → Arhat (completed daśamī)

**Bodhisattvas on Śrāvaka grounds** [2702-2708]:
*Sa bdun man chad sems dpa' ni rang rang dpon la nyan pa'o*—"Bodhisattvas below the eighth ground listen to their own masters."

*Sa brgyad man chad sems dpa' ni sprul pa'i sku la nyan par 'dod*—"Below the eighth ground, bodhisattvas listen to the nirmāṇakāya."

*Dngos por mthong med do*—"Not seen as entities."

This indicates that from the eighth ground (*sa brgyad*) upward, bodhisattvas receive teachings from the saṃbhogakāya, not the nirmāṇakāya.

[2710-2748] <context>
Pratyekabuddhayāna
**Analysis:**

**Essence** [2711-2712]: *Rang don phyogs gcig pas byang chub slob dpon la ma brten par*—"Self-interest partially, without depending on a teacher for enlightenment."

**Two Types** [2717-2718]:
1. **Like a rhinoceros** (*bse ru lta bu*): Solitary practitioners
2. **Like a cuckoo** (*ne tso ltar*): Living in groups

**Distinctive Realization** [2724-2726]:
*Gzung don rtogs pa spong phyir dang / 'dzin pa'i mi spong phyir dang ni / rten gyi bse ru lta bu'i lam*—"Abandoning grasping at objects, not abandoning grasping itself—the rhinoceros-like path."

This verse from the *Abhisamayālaṃkāra* distinguishes the Pratyekabuddha: they realize the emptiness of external objects (*bāhyārtha-śūnyatā*) but not the emptiness of the apprehending consciousness.

**Duration** [2739-2741]:
*Bsé ru bskal pa brgya yi rgyus*—"Rhino [liberation] through a hundred eons' cause."

This refers to the time required for accumulating the causes as a Pratyekabuddha.
