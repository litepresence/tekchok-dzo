. Five perfections: generosity, discipline, patience, diligence, concentration.

[3307-3308] <philology>
The term pha rol tu phyin pa (perfection) carries its technical Mahāyāna sense of transcendent virtues. Instrumental particles (-kyis, -gis) indicate the means by which perfections become transcendent. Causative constructions establish relationships between practice and result.

[3307-3316] <structure>
The passage demonstrates Longchenpa's systematic presentation. Structural markers rim pa organize the five perfections: generosity countering miserliness, discipline countering immorality, patience countering aggression, diligence countering laziness, concentration countering distraction.

[3309-3323] <context>
Citations from Thal 'gyur, Rang shar, and Nyi zla kha sbyor establish doctrinal validity. The zhes so formula indicates transmission lineage. The five perfections represent the common Mahāyāna foundation.
