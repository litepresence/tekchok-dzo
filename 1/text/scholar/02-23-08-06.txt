**Analysis:** The progression to "seeing" as a distinct stage indicates the shift from conceptual recognition to direct perception. In Buddhist epistemology (*tshad ma*), *mthong* (seeing) typically refers to direct perception (*mngon sum tshad ma*), while *rtogs* (realization) may include inferential understanding (*rjes dpag*). Here in Dzogchen fruition, "seeing" likely indicates the direct perception of awareness's nature without mediation of concept. The second stage of seeing suggests the practitioner has moved beyond initial recognition to stable direct perception—what was understood intellectually is now seen directly. This corresponds to the *thod rgal* (direct transcendence) stage where visions (*snang ba*) are not merely imagined or visualized but directly perceived as the natural luminosity (*rang gsal*) of awareness. The absence of qualification (*mthong* rather than *X mthong*) suggests the seeing has become all-inclusive—seeing without object, awareness seeing itself.
