# The Four Lamps: Water Lamp of Far-Casting
**Architectural Context:** This passage presents the detailed exposition of the Four Lamps (སྒྲོན་མ་བཞི་ sgron ma bzhi) in the ཐོད་རྒ໱་ (thod rgal) practice system. The text transitions from the preceding discussion of the Three Movements of Mind-Turning (ཡིད་འཁོར་གསུམ་) to this systematic presentation of the four visionary lamps that serve as supports for recognition practice. The structural placement reflects the རྫོགས་ཆེན་ architectural principle that ཐོད་རྒ໱་ practice presupposes preliminary mind-turning (ཡིད་འཁོར་).

**Foundation:** The passage begins with the analysis of winds and wisdom (རླུང་དང་ཡེ་ཤེས་), establishing the energetic basis for the visionary lamps.

[16025-16059] <philology>
**Key Terminology:**
- རླུང་ (rlung): "wind, breath, vital energy"—the subtle energy moving in channels
- ཡེ་ཤེས་ (ye shes): "wisdom, pristine awareness"—the gnostic aspect
- རྒྱང་ཞགས་ (rgya mtshams): "far-casting, extending to distance"—the expansive aspect
- སྡེ (sde): "division, category"—the classification of manifestations

**Particle Analysis:**
- ལས་ (las): ablative "from"
- ལ་ (la): dative/locative "to, in"
- འི་ (i): genitive "of"

[16060-16159] <philology>
**The Four Lamps Detailed:**
1. རྒྱང་ཞགས་ཆུའི་སྒྲོན་མ་ (rgya mtshams chu'i sgron ma): Water Lamp of Far-Casting
2. ཐིག་ལེ་སྟོང་པའི་སྒྲོན་མ་ (thig le stong pa'i sgron ma): Lamp of Empty Bindus
3. དབྱིངས་རྣམ་དག་པའི་སྒྲོན་མ་ (dbyings rnam par dag pa'i sgron ma): Lamp of Completely Pure Sphere
4. ཤེས་རབ་རང་བྱུང་གི་སྒྲོན་མ་ (shes rab rang byung gi sgron ma): Lamp of Self-Arising Wisdom

**Additional Terms:**
- ཐིག་ལེ་ (thig le): "bindu, drop, essence"—the subtle essence
- དབྱིངས་ (dbyings): "sphere, expanse, dhātu"—the ground of manifestation
- ཤེས་རབ་ (shes rab): "wisdom, prajñā"—discriminating awareness
- རང་བྱུང་ (rang byung): "self-arisen"—spontaneous manifestation

[16060-16159] <context>
**Thödgal Context:** Longchenpa introduces the foundational ཐོད་རྒ໱་ terminology following the སྒྲོན་མ་འབར་བ་ (sgron ma 'bar ba, "Lamp Blazing") tantric tradition, also known as པྲ་ཏི་པྲ་དོ་ (Pradīpodyotana).

**Tantric Sources:** This presentation derives from the གསང་བ་སྙིང་པོ་ (Guhyagarbha, "Secret Essence") and related རྙིང་མ་ (Nyingma) tantras that systematize visionary practice.

[16060-16159] <concept>
**Philosophical Framework:** The Four Lamps represent the progressive refinement of visionary experience in ཐོད་རྒ໱་ practice:

1. **Water Lamp (ཆུའི་སྒྲོན་མ་):** Holds both saṃsāra and nirvāṇa appearances—the initial expansive vision

2. **Empty Bindus Lamp (ཐིག་ལེ་སྟོང་པའི་):** The essential nature as empty yet luminous—the essence

3. **Pure Sphere Lamp (དབྱིངས་རྣམ་དག་པའི་):** The completely pure expanse—the nature

4. **Self-Arising Wisdom Lamp (ཤེས་རབ་རང་བྱུང་གི་):** Wisdom that arises from itself—the compassion

This fourfold structure reflects the རྫོགས་ཆེན་ teaching that the ground (གཞི་) possesses all qualities from the beginning—these lamps are not created but recognized.

# SA BCAD: Enumeration of the Four Lamps

Longchenpa lists the four lamps following the completion of the Three Movements of Mind-Turning in the preceding section. This structural placement reflects the རྫོགས་ཆེན་ architectural principle that ཐོད་རྒ໱་ practice presupposes preliminary mind-turning (ཡིད་འཁོར་).

**Tantric Source:** The passage cites the སྒྲོན་མ་འབར་བ་ (sgron ma 'bar ba) tantric tradition.

# Architectural Scheme of the Water Lamp Analysis
**Architectural Context:** Longchenpa establishes a ninefold analytical framework (དགོས་ dgu) for the Water Lamp, following the comprehensive མཛད་པ་ (mdzod, "treasury") genre's encyclopedic approach.

**Ninefold Schema:**
1. ངོ་བོ་ (ngo bo)—Essence
2. ངེས་ཚིག་ (nges tshig)—Definition
3. དབྱེ་བ་ (bye ba)—Classification
4. མཚན་ཉིད་ (mtshan nyid)—Characteristic
5. གནས་ (gnas)—Abode
6. གང་གིས་སྐྱེ་ཚུལ་ (gang gis skyed tshul)—Manner of arising
7. འཆར་སྒོའི་སྣང་བ་ ('char sgo'i snang ba)—Appearance of arising door
8. མཐུད་དཔེ་ (mthun dpe)—Simile
9. ཚད་ངེས་པ་ (tshad nges pa)—Definite measure

This ninefold schema reflects the མཛད་པ་ genre's encyclopedic impulse and mirrors structural patterns found in Longchenpa's ཡིད་བྱེད་མཛད་པ་ (Yid bzhin mdzod).
