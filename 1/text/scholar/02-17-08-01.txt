. Refuge and bodhicitta: foundation of all practice.

[3057-3058] <context>
The passage cites authoritative tantric sources establishing doctrinal validity for refuge (skyabs 'gro) and bodhicitta (byang chub kyi sems). The zhes so formula indicates transmission lineage. These practices represent the indispensable foundation that all Buddhist paths, including Dzogchen, require.

[3057-3058] <structure>
The passage demonstrates systematic presentation. Structural markers rim pa organize the progressive stages: taking refuge in the Three Jewels, generating relative bodhicitta (aspiration and engagement), then absolute bodhicitta (recognition of mind nature).

[3059-3123] <concept>
The operational logic—cause (ordinary samsaric state), method (refuge and bodhicitta), result (liberation and enlightenment)—integrates standard Mahāyāna practice within Great Perfection. Refuge is not rejected but recognized as the recognition that awareness itself is the ultimate refuge. Bodhicitta transforms from deliberate generation to spontaneous recognition of buddha-nature.

[3059-3073] <philology>
The term skyabs 'gro (refuge) carries its technical sense of seeking protection from the Three Jewels. The compound byang chub kyi sems (bodhicitta) distinguishes relative (sems kyi) from absolute (rig pa'i). Instrumental particles indicate the means of generating and stabilizing bodhicitta. Causative constructions establish relationships between practice and realization.
