Second Category: Feeling as Object of Experiential Contact
**Analysis:** This passage marks the second category (*gnyis pa*) in a classification system concerning phenomenological dimensions of experience. The structure presents a systematic analysis of how consciousness engages with objects, specifically focusing on the role of feeling (*tshor ba*) in experiential contact.

**Tibetan: གཉིས་པ་ལ་ཚོར་མིག་མེད་ཀྱི་དངོས་དོན་ལ་**

The structure enumerates categories of experience, with this entry specifically analyzing the mechanism by which feeling functions as the bridge between consciousness and objects.

[8674] <philology>
Philological Analysis
**Analysis:**

**Key Tibetan Terms:**
- *Gnyis pa* (གཉིས་པ་) - second (ordinal)
- *Tshor ba* (ཚོར་པ་) - feeling, sensation (Sanskrit: *vedanā*)
- *Myong ba'i* (མོང་པའི་) - of experiencing (verbal noun from *myong ba*)
- *Dngos rjes* (དངོས་རྗེས་) - object/result (genitive construction)
- *La* (ལ་) - locative marker
- *Pho ba* (ཕྱོག་པ་) - engagement, proceeding toward

**Compound Analysis:**
*tshor ba myong ba'i dngos rjes la pho ba* = "engagement toward the object of feeling's experiencing"

The genitive construction *yi* indicates the object of feeling's experience - the target toward which the experiential process is directed.

**Grammatical Structure:**
- Locative *la* marks the direction/goal
- Verbal noun *myong ba'i* indicates the experiential quality
- *Dngos rjes* indicates the result or object of that experience

Philosophical Context: Feeling in Buddhist Phenomenology
**Analysis:**

**Five Aggregates Framework:**
- Feeling (*tshor ba*) is the fourth of five aggregates (*phung po lnga*)
- It is distinct from:
  - Form (*gzugs*) - physical form
  - Perception (*'du ses*) - cognitive identification  
  - Formation (*'du byed*) - mental formations
  - Consciousness (*rnam par she sa*) - knowing consciousness

**Affective Dimension:**
- Feeling (*vedanā*) refers to the bare affective tone:
  - Pleasant (*sukha*)
  - Unpleasant (*dukkha*)
  - Neutral (*upeksā*)

**Function in Experience:**
Feeling serves as the bridge between consciousness and objects:
1. Consciousness contacts an object
2. Feeling arises as the affective coloring
3. This feeling conditions subsequent responses

This enumeration analyzes the mechanics of how feeling functions as this bridge.

Doctrinal Significance: Feeling and Liberation
**Analysis:**

**Ordinary Function:**
- Feeling creates attachment through pleasant sensations
- Aversion arises from unpleasant sensations
- Neutral feelings create ignorance

**Liberation Path:**
- Recognizing the empty nature of feeling liberates its binding power
- The "experiential contact" (*tshor ba myong ba*) becomes an opportunity for recognition
- This transforms feeling from bondage to awakening

**Dzogchen View:**
- All experiences, including feelings, are displays of awareness (*rig pa'i rnam pa*)
- The object of feeling's experience is empty of inherent existence (*rang bzhin stong pa*)
- Recognizing this, feeling transforms from suffering to wisdom

**Practical Application:**
- Not suppressing or grasping at feelings
- Seeing feelings as empty yet appearing
- This is the basis for transforming all experiences into awakening
