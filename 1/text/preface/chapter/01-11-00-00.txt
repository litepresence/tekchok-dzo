## CHAPTER 11: Channels, Winds, and Bindus

**Tibetan:** རིམ་ཁང་བཅུ་གཅིག་པ། (Eleventh Lecture Hall)  
**Location:** Volume 1, Sections 01-11-01-01 through 01-11-02-01  
**Tibetan Lines:** 13104-13830  
**Total Liturgical Lines:** ~330

This chapter presents the **subtle body anatomy**: channels (*rtsa*), winds (*rlung*), and bindus (*thig le*) that serve as the interface between mind and world. Longchenpa explains how consciousness enters the embryo, the formation of the three main channels (central, right, left), and the circulation of vital energies.

**The Three Main Channels:**
- **Central channel** (*dbu ma*): Path of wisdom, clear light
- **Right channel** (*ro ma*): Solar, method, white bindu  
- **Left channel** (*rkyang ma*): Lunar, prajñā, red bindu

**The Five Vital Winds:**
Life-bearing, downward-clearing, fire-dwelling, upward-moving, pervasive winds.

**Key Contribution:** Establishes the subtle body physiology essential for completion stage practices.
