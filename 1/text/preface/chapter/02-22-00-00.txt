## CHAPTER 22: Phowa Transference

**Tibetan:** རིམ་ཁང་ཉི་ཤུ་རྩ་གཉིས་པ། (Twenty-second Lecture Hall)  
**Location:** Volume 2, Sections 02-22-01-01 through 02-22-07-01  
**Tibetan Lines:** —  
**Total Liturgical Lines:** ~1,800

This chapter presents **consciousness transference** (*'pho ba*)—the method of directing consciousness to a pure field at death. Longchenpa distinguishes between transference for practitioners of three capacities and provides detailed techniques.

**Three Types of Phowa:**
1. **Dharmakāya phowa**: For supreme practitioners—liberation into expanse
2. **Saṃbhogakāya phowa**: For middling—transference to pure field  
3. **Nirmāṇakāya phowa**: For ordinary—rebirth as human with Dharma

**Signs of Success:**
Specific physical signs indicating consciousness has left through crown aperture: body warmth, complexion, posture.

**Key Contribution:** The "insurance policy" for practitioners—ensuring favorable circumstances if recognition is not complete.
