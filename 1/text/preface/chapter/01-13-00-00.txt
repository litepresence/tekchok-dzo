## CHAPTER 13: Four Lamps

**Tibetan:** རིམ་ཁང་བཅུ་གསུམ་པ། (Thirteenth Lecture Hall)  
**Location:** Volume 1, Sections 01-13-01-01 through 01-13-06-01  
**Tibetan Lines:** 16025-17360  
**Total Liturgical Lines:** ~1,050

This chapter presents the **four lamps** (*sgron ma bzhi*)—the interfaces through which primordial purity becomes manifest appearance. Longchenpa explains how the crystal tube (*shel gyi bu ga*), far-reaching lasso water lamp (*rgyang zhags chu'i sgron ma*), lamp of the hollow place (*rtsa ba sbubs kyi sgron ma*), and lamp of self-arisen wisdom (*rang byung ye shes kyi sgron ma*) function as conduits for light and awareness.

**The Four Lamps:**
1. **Far-reaching water lamp**: Outer, reflection medium
2. **Lamp of the hollow place**: Inner, channels and winds  
3. **Lamp of self-arisen wisdom**: Secret, bindu and light
4. **Pure lamp of empty bindus**: Suchness, ultimate nature

**Key Contribution:** Establishes the four interfaces through which recognition of the natural state becomes possible.
