## CHAPTER 8: Primordial Ground

**Tibetan:** རིམ་ཁང་བརྒྱད་པ། (Eighth Lecture Hall)  
**Location:** Volume 1, Sections 01-08-01-01 through 01-08-08-01  
**Tibetan Lines:** 10472-11334  
**Total Liturgical Lines:** ~860

This chapter establishes the **ground** (*gzhi*)—the dimension of primordial purity that precedes both saṃsāra and nirvāṇa. Longchenpa presents the sevenfold nature of the ground: essence (*ngo bo*), nature (*rang bzhin*), compassion (*thugs rje*), and their interdependence.

**Key Topics:**
- **Primordial purity** (*ka dag*): Unstained from the beginning
- **Spontaneous presence** (*lhun grub*): Self-manifesting display
- **Ground vs. basis of delusion**: Recognition vs. non-recognition
- **Two truths**: From Dzogchen perspective

The chapter dismantles reification of philosophical systems while establishing the unique Dzogchen view.

**Key Contribution:** Establishes the ground of Dzogchen view—the basis for all subsequent recognition.
