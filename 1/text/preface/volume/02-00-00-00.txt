# VOLUME 2: Recognition and Liberation

## Path, Bardo, and Fruition

**Tibetan:** ཐེག་པའི་མཆོག་རིན་པོ་ཆེའི་མཛོད་ཀྱི་འགྲེལ་པ་ཕྱི་མ། (Theg mchog rin po che'i mdzod kyi 'grel pa phyi ma)  
**Full Title:** Treasury of the Supreme Vehicle: Autocommentary (Latter Volume)  
**Chapter Range:** Chapters 15-25 (The Latter Volume - phyi ma)  
**Sections:** 107  
**Approximate Lines:** 17,330  
**Author:** Longchen Rabjampa (1308-1364)  
**Translation Period:** 2024-2026

---

## Overview

This second volume moves from **cosmological description to direct liberation**. Where Volume 1 established the ground (*gzhi*)—pure vision, cyclic existence structure, subtle body anatomy—Volume 2 presents the **path** (*lam*) of recognition and the **fruition** (*'bras bu*) of complete awakening.

The volume's architecture mirrors the **completion phase** (*rdzogs rim*): cutting through conceptual mind (*Trekchö*), crossing through visionary experience (*Thögal*), navigating intermediate states (*bardo*), and ultimately dissolving all phenomena into the expanse of primordial purity. Volume 1 prepared the vessel; Volume 2 fills it with nectar of realization.

---

## Volume Character

Volume 2 is both **prescriptive** and **descriptive**: offering practices for those who need them while constantly pointing beyond practice to the natural state already perfect. It is the practical application of Volume 1's theoretical foundations, yet repeatedly demonstrates that theory and practice ultimately dissolve into direct recognition.

Longchenpa's pedagogical genius appears in the progression: beginning with the five elements (same as ordinary existence), demonstrating they are in essence wisdom's display. He presents progressive path instructions, advanced completion-stage practices, detailed bardo navigation, and finally reveals the **Great Liberation**—recognition that nothing needed doing, for fruition has always been complete.

---

## The Fruition Complete

Volume 2 stands as **practical completion**. Where Volume 1 established "what is," this volume demonstrates "how to recognize what is." The bookend structure—Chapter 1's pure vision answered by Chapter 25's spontaneous fruition—reveals Longchenpa's ultimate intent: **the ground has never been separate from the result.**

May this volume serve as the **path of recognition** for all who engage with it.

May all beings realize the supreme vehicle.

**Sarva mangalam.**
