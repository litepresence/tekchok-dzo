[314-389] <nihilistic-error> <emptiness-misunderstanding>

**Misreading:**

**Why it arises:**
"Merely imputed" (btags pa tsam) triggers nihilistic reading common in Western emptiness interpretations. The idealist-sounding language (worlds complete in mind) invites solipsistic interpretation. Modern constructivism and "reality is perception" philosophies provide ready framework. The denial of external reality seems confirmed by "outer-arising is inner-arising's play."

**Primary consequence:**
The profound middle way between existence and non-existence collapses into denial. Practitioners develop subtle nihilism—believing "it's all just mind" means experiences lack validity or significance. The rich display of phenomena is dismissed as "mere imputation," generating spiritual bypassing and disengagement.

**Secondary consequences:**
- Using "emptiness" to dismiss conventional reality and ethical engagement
- Believing recognition means seeing through the "illusion" of phenomena rather than recognizing their nature
- Missing that "imputed" refers to conceptual overlay, not the display itself
- Reducing the magnificent correspondences (sun-moon as method-wisdom, etc.) to "just symbols"

**Cascade effects:**
<nihilistic-error> → <detachment-error> (disengaging from life as "unreal") → <pedagogical-error> (teaching others to "see through" phenomena) → <experience-denial-error> (suppressing genuine responses as "imputed").
