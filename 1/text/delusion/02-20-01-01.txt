[8083-8125] [PRACTICE ERROR] [PHOWA AS ESCAPE]
MISREADING:
Phowa (transference) is an escape from death or physical relocation.

WHY IT ARISES:
"Transference" suggests spatial movement.

PRIMARY CONSEQUENCE:
Fear of death driving practice.

SECONDARY CONSEQUENCES:
Missing the deathless nature always present.

CASCADE EFFECTS:
[PRACTICE ERROR].

[8126-8164] [ONTOLOGICAL ERROR] [GREAT TRANSFERENCE AS MAGIC]
MISREADING:
Great transference ('pho ba chen po) is a magical disappearance or ascension.

WHY IT ARISES:
Hagiographic stories of masters disappearing.

PRIMARY CONSEQUENCE:
Literal belief in magical feats.

SECONDARY CONSEQUENCES:
Disappointment with ordinary reality.

CASCADE EFFECTS:
[REIFICATION ERROR].
