<Masterful analysis:

Risk of nihilism: readers may interpret zad med and 'phel med as denying conventional progress, leading to chos sku phyogs lhung (deviation into dharmakāya nihilism). Longchenpa's drang nges hermeneutics: the nges don (definitive meaning) is that the ground is already perfect, but the drang don (provisional meaning) is that path is necessary for obscured beings. These are not contradictory but complementary—addressing different dimensions of the teaching. The ka dag principle does not negate the need for purification (sbyang ba) but reveals that purification reveals what was never impure. Similarly, accumulation ('tshogs) is necessary conventionally but ultimately reveals that nothing was ever lacking.
The ngo bo (essence) is emptiness, the rang bzhin (nature) is clarity, the thugs rje (compassion) is unobstructed—three aspects of single awareness. Longchenpa's spros bral (freedom from elaborations) prevents both nihilism (chad lta) and eternalism (rtag lta).>
