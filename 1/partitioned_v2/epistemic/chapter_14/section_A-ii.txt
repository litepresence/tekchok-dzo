[636-653] 
[DZOGCHEN VIEW – FIVE SPACES AS GROUND-DISPLAY]
[DECLARATIVE FINALITY]
[RISK: SPATIALIZATION]
Longchenpa presents five spaces (*nam mkha' lnga*) from spontaneous presence: pervading-all-common space, objectless clear-radiance space, unmixed pure space, indicating-meaning space, and liberating-to-pure-field space.
[CONTEXT] DOXOGRAPHICAL PLACEMENT: This fivefold classification of space (*dbyings*) distinguishes Nyingma Dzogchen from Sarma traditions. While Gelug (*dge lugs*) emphasizes dharmadhātu as emptiness-of-inherent-existence (*rang bzhin gyis stong pa nyid*), and Sakya (*sa skya*) treats dharmadhātu as object-of-void-cognition (*stong pa nyid kyi yul*), Longchenpa's five spaces prevent reification of any single definition. The "indicating-meaning" (*don mtshon byed*) space specifically refers to the Nyingthig (*snying thig*) tradition of pointing-out instructions (*ngo sprod*).
Epistemic precision: These are not progressively refined "levels" of space but simultaneous aspects of expanse. "Pervading" indicates omnipresence without extension; "objectless" indicates clarity without grasped-object; "unmixed" indicates purity without contrast; "indicating" refers to the pointing-out function central to *snying thig* practice; "liberating" indicates soteriological efficacy. Risk: Spatialization—reading these as nested containers or hierarchical dimensions rather than nondual descriptions of the same expanse.
[654-664] 
[STRUCTURE] CHAPTER TRANSITION: From Elements to Supported-Knowledge
[CONTEXT] MDZOD GENRE CONVENTION: This transition from Chapter XV (*rim khang bco lnga pa*) on dependent arising (*rten 'byung*) to the presentation of supported-knowledge (*brten pa shes pa*) follows the classical *mdzod* (treasury) genre structure. Longchenpa here employs the standard Nyingma hermeneutical progression from ground-ontology (*gzhi'i gnas lugs*) to path-practice (*lam gyi sbyor ba*), mirroring the structure of his own *Yid bzhin mdzod* and *Grub mtha' mdzod*. The supported/supporter (*brten pa/rtem*) distinction derives from *Yogācāra* (*rnal 'byor spyod pa*) sources but is reinterpreted Dzogchenically to prevent reifying wisdom as entity while maintaining functional efficacy (*don byed nus pa*).
Longchenpa summarizes: five elements' awareness-aspect gathered are great elements; their support is minor elements. Citation marks end of Chapter XV. Transition: Having established dependent arising (*rten 'byung*), the text now analyzes supported-knowledge in four parts: supported kaya (*rtem sku*), resonance-light (*gdangs 'od*), supported wisdom (*brten pa ye shes*), and activity classification (*mdzad pa 'phrin las*).
[685–704]
[DZOGCHEN VIEW – SPONTANEOUS PRESENCE]
[DECLARATIVE FINALITY]
[RISK: GRADUALISM]
Longchenpa establishes completion (*rdzogs pa*) through the Self-Arisen (*rang shar*) tantra as the spontaneous presence of the three kayas' pure vision. The epistemic structure here is not progressive achievement but immediate recognition of what is already complete (*g.rdzogs*).
The text presents seven aspects of completion through mirror-like wisdom (*me long lta bu'i ye shes*): one complete, two complete, all complete—wisdom vision unceased clear; pure wisdom light-rays radiate; concept-free pure awareness; five light-rays unceased clear; grasping-free great self-liberation; outer inner secret dharmas clear; deed-abandoned awareness; doer-deed dharmas simultaneous complete; mind-free awareness; various vision simultaneous complete; self-vision clear pure great; delusion vision simultaneous complete; self-liberated self-vision great; other-arisen dharmas simultaneous complete; three kayas completion manner thus!
[RISK: GRADUALISM] The enumeration (seven completions) must not be read as sequential stages. The Wylie *g.rdzogs* appears repeatedly to emphasize simultaneity (*dus gcig*), not accumulation.
[DECLARATIVE FINALITY] The refrain "simultaneous complete" (*dus gcig rdzogs*) functions as epistemic closure—there is nothing to add, nothing to cultivate.
[751-756] 
[DZOGCHEN VIEW – DHARMAKĀYA AS SELF-ABIDING GREATNESS]
[DECLARATIVE FINALITY]
[RISK: SUBSTANTIALIZATION]
Longchenpa defines dharmakāya as "nature spontaneous presence luminosity body self-abiding greatness" (*rang bzhin lhun grub 'od gsal gyi sku rang gnas chen po*). Citation establishes dharmakāya's definitive term (*nges tshig*): unchanging, empty, clear, all deeds complete. Epistemic precision: The compound strings essence (*rang bzhin*), nature (*lhun grub*), and compassion (*'od gsal*) into dharmakāya's definition. "Self-abiding" (*rang gnas*) prevents any movement toward or away—dharmakāya neither comes from elsewhere nor goes elsewhere. It is not "in" the practitioner to be discovered. Risk: Substantialization—reading "unchanging empty clear" as attributes of a metaphysical entity rather than the non-attribute of ground.
[757-762] 
[DZOGCHEN VIEW – OWN-BODY AS BEYOND ELABORATION]
[DECLARATIVE FINALITY]
[RISK: VIEW COLLAPSE]
Own-body (*rang lus*) is free from all elaboration extremes—nothing established anywhere. Citation: "No outer no inner no expanse—this is dharmakāya's own-body." Epistemic precision: The negation pattern "no X no Y no Z" operates at apophatic extreme. This is not merely Madhyamaka's absence of svabhāva but Dzogchen's positive assertion of ground-beyond-negation. Risk: View collapse—reading this as standard Madhyamaka emptiness, missing that Dzogchen's "no expanse" (*dbyings su med*) refers to freedom even from dharmadhātu as reified category.
[763-775] 
[DZOGCHEN VIEW – SIMILES AS PEDAGOGICAL INDICATION]
[INSTRUCTIONAL PROVISIONALITY]
[RISK: SIMILE REIFICATION]
Similes: awareness-luminosity like sun, depth like ocean, unstained by faults like lotus, unchanging like mountain, vast without center-edge like space, possessing all-vanquishing confidence and primordial lion. Citation: "Sun ocean lotus mountain space lion—these are dharmakāya's similes." Epistemic function: Similes indicate without defining. Sun indicates luminous self-illumination; ocean indicates unfathomable depth; lotus indicates purity-within-samsara; mountain indicates unmoving stability; space indicates unobstructed pervasion; lion indicates fearless confidence. Risk: Simile reification—treating sun/ocean/lotus as literally present in dharmakāya rather than as pedagogical pointing devices.

[DZOGCHEN VIEW – RIGPA SIDE]
[DECLARATIVE FINALITY]
<Masterful analysis:
The seven aspects of Dharmakaya fruition—spontaneous presence (*lhun grub*) as great primordial liberation (*ye grol chen po*), primordially established (*ye grub*) activity completing all meanings, qualities abiding in oneself without seeking—are not achievements but recognition-descriptions. The verse citation presents the non-action of Dharmakaya: conduct without ceasing, fixation, or attachment; meditation spontaneous, immediate, without wavering; view complete without seeing or having-seen; fruition without fabrication, arising, or production; activity without cultivation, yet cultivation complete; qualities arising as one, two, all. Each line denies conventional action (*byed pa*) while asserting completion (*rdzogs*). This is Dzogchen's paradox: nothing to do, yet everything complete; no practice, yet recognition perfect. The Wylie phrases *byas med, byung med, 'byung ba med* (without fabrication, without arising, without production) negate *samskrta* (conditioned) categories, while *bsgrubs zin* (already established) points to *nisprapanca* (unelaborated) nature. The practitioner does not "accomplish" these—recognition reveals they were never absent.>

[STRUCTURAL TRANSITION]
[UPĀYA STATEMENT]
<Masterful analysis:
The structural marker *gnyis pa* (second) transitions from Dharmakaya to Sambhogakaya (*longs sku*), from emptiness-form to enjoyment-form. The fivefold analysis—essence (*ngo bo*), definitive terminology (*nges tshig*), characteristic (*mtshan nyid*), mode of appearance (*snang lugs*), mode of abiding (*gnas lugs*)—replicates scholastic methodology while preparing for Dzogchen transformation. The *Rang Shar* citation describes Sambhogakaya essence: birth existing yet possessing deathless vajra; five lights, bodies unobscured, individually clear; possessing five fathers, five objects, five aggregates; five innate aspects, five wisdoms, five realizations; five bases, five paths, five fruitions. This operates as *upāya*: the apparent multiplicity of Sambhogakaya's qualities serves as recognition-support for practitioners who cognize through differentiation. The Dzogchen integration recognizes that these "fives" are not categories to accumulate but simultaneous dimensions of single awareness-display. The transition from Dharmakaya's non-action to Sambhogakaya's apparent multiplicity marks pedagogical progression: from the simplicity of emptiness to the richness of spontaneous presence (*lhun grub*).>
[866–933]
[DZOGCHEN VIEW – SPONTANEOUS PRESENCE]
[DECLARATIVE FINALITY]
[RISK: ENUMERATION FATIGUE]
Longchenpa presents a comprehensive taxonomy of "fives" (*lnga*) that structure the Dzogchen path from ground to fruit. The epistemic strategy is enumerative completeness—establishing that all aspects of the path are already spontaneously perfect (*ye rdzogs, ye grub, ye shar, ye gdangs*).
**The Fivefold Structure:**
- **Five Mothers** (*ma lnga*): Buddha Eyes, Mamaki, Pandara, Samaya Tara—the female buddhas representing the five wisdoms
- **Five Groups** (*tshom bu lnga*): Akshobhya etc., each surrounded by five, adorned with five-colored light walls
- **Five Primordial Completions** (*ye lnga*): Paths complete, bodies complete, awareness self-dawned, lights self-resonant, obscurations self-pure
- **Five Knowledges** (*shes lnga*): Knowing the five bodies, five wisdoms, five lights, self-vision, and great extreme-free non-duality
- **Five Realizations** (*rtogs pa lnga*): Realizing object as dharmadhatu, beings as bodies, vision as light, dawn-liberation as limitless, discriminating awareness as awareness itself
[RISK: ENUMERATION FATIGUE] The extensive listing must not be read as a checklist to complete. The prefix *ye-* (primordial/self-) on each completion indicates this is recognition, not achievement.
[DECLARATIVE FINALITY] "Thus in groups of five appearing" (*de ltar lnga tshan du snang ba*)—the epistemic closure emphasizes that these are aspects of spontaneous presence, not constructed categories.
[934-943] 
[DZOGCHEN VIEW – SAMBHOGAKĀYA AS UNIFIED DISPLAY]
[DECLARATIVE FINALITY]
[RISK: KĀYA TRINITARIANISM]
Longchenpa establishes Sambhogakāya (*longs sku*) through five aspects unified: some sphere (*yul*), some method (*thabs*), some appearance (*snang ba*), some prajna (*shes rab*)—thus explained as unified Enjoyment Body. Definitive term: perfecting virtues (*yon tan du longs pa*), dwelling in luminosity-purity (*'od gsal dag pa la spyod pa*), both purposes spontaneously complete (*don gnyis lhun gyis rdzogs pa*), wisdom reaching full measure (*ye shes tshad du phyin pa*), mark-simile light-ray appearing body (*mtshan dpe 'od zer snang ba'i sku*). Five "longs" (*longs*) make "complete enjoyment" (*longs spyod rdzogs pa*).
Epistemic precision: The five aspects are not components but simultaneous descriptions. "Sphere" indicates object-pole; "method" indicates subject-pole; "appearance" indicates display; "prajna" indicates recognition; "light-ray body" indicates form. The unification prevents trinitarian reading (three separate kayas) by showing Sambhogakāya as dharmakāya's self-recognition. Risk: Trinitarianism—treating Sambhogakāya as ontologically distinct from Dharmakāya rather than wisdom-display aspect.
[944-958] 
[DZOGCHEN VIEW – FIVE PERFECTS AS SAMBHOGAKĀYA'S DEFINITION]
[DECLARATIVE FINALITY]
[RISK: SUBSTANTIALIZATION]
Citation from spontaneous presence: "Dharmatā emptiness's mode dwells—perfect; clear unmixed all-pervading—perfect; five wisdoms unseparated dwelling—perfect; all-unequaled individual marks possessed—perfect; method-prajna unseparated dwelling—perfect." Five appearances markless dwelling—enjoyment; without grasping self-clear all-enjoying—enjoyment; completely clear equal-dwelling—enjoyment; naturally naturally dwelling in sphere emanating—enjoyment; two kayas intended ultimate enjoyment—enjoyment.
Epistemic precision: The repetition of "perfect" (*longs*) and "enjoyment" (*spyod*) establishes Sambhogakāya not as location but as activity—dwelling, pervading, enjoying. The "five appearances markless" indicates that form-display is empty-form, not substantial form. Risk: Substantialization—reading "light-ray body" as physically luminous entity rather than wisdom-radiance without material substrate.
[979-985] 
[DZOGCHEN VIEW – SAMBHOGAKĀYA'S FIVE-FAMILY DISPLAY]
[DECLARATIVE FINALITY]
[RISK: REIFICATION]
Longchenpa presents five families' (*rigs lnga*) individual mandalas as non-mixed, clear resonance. From Self-Arisen: marks and signs, radiant light rays, father-mother-body's characteristics held, Sambhogakāya body's marks.
[CONTEXT] NYINGMA-SARMA POLEMICS: The five-family (*rigs lnga*) mandala structure distinguishes Nyingma presentation from Sarma (*gsar ma*) tantric systems. While Gelug (*dge lugs*) and Sakya (*sa skya*) emphasize the five Buddha-families as purified aggregates (*phung po lnga*) following *Guhyasamāja* (*gsang 'dus*) exegesis, Longchenpa's *snying thig* tradition treats the five families as spontaneous display (*rang shar*) of awareness without transformation (*sgyur med*). This reflects the fundamental Nyingma distinction between causal (*rgyu*) and result (*'bras bu*) vehicles—the five families are not achieved through purification but recognized as always-already present. The "father-mother-body" (*yab yum gyi sku*) refers to method-wisdom (*thabs shes*) inseparability, distinct from lower tantra's gradual generation-stage (*bskyed rim*) visualization.
Epistemic precision: This is not cosmological geography but direct pointing to awareness-display. Risk: Reification—reading five-family mandalas as metaphysical locations rather than wisdom-aspect recognition.
[986-998] 
[DZOGCHEN VIEW – THREE MODES OF SAMBHOGAKĀYA]
[DECLARATIVE FINALITY]
[RISK: TEMPORALIZATION / NIHILISM]
Appearance-mode (*snang tshul*): from one nature rigpa, Dharmakāya body sends light-body to ten directions benefiting. Rigpa body unmoving from dharmadhātu, without change or transformation, self-appearance clear light field—two perfections inside-outside. Citation: "One nature two essences, Sambhogakāya's appearance."
[CONTEXT] DOXOGRAPHICAL DISTINCTION: The three-mode (*tshul gsum*) presentation—appearance, abiding, and emanation—derives from *snying thig* tantras, particularly *Rig pa rang shar* and *Sgra thal 'gyur*. This structure contrasts with standard Yogācāra three-kāya theory by asserting Sambhogakāya as Dharmakāya's self-recognition (*rang ngo shes*) rather than intermediate state between empty Dharmakāya and form Nirmāṇakāya. Longchenpa here follows the *man ngag sde* (Instruction Class) view that Sambhogakāya is immediate (*cig car*) display, not developmental (*rim gyis*) achievement.
Abiding-mode (*gnas tshul*): acceptance-rejection free dharmadhātu, non-dual space, five lights' perfected expanse, emanation's flowing great ring. Risks: Temporalization (appearance as process); Nihilism (non-dual as mere absence rather than clarity-emptiness inseparable).
[1036-1041] 
[DZOGCHEN VIEW – NIRMĀṆAKĀYA AS BENEFIT-PERFECTION]
[DECLARATIVE FINALITY]
[RISK: FUNCTIONALISM]
Longchenpa presents Nirmāṇakāya (*sprul sku*) as "tamed's two benefits perfected accomplishing" (*gdul bya'i don gnyis rdzogs par byed pa*). Citation: "Nirmāṇakāya body's marks: accomplishing completing deeds, two forms desired."
[CONTEXT] KĀYA TRIAD COMPLETION: The three-kāya structure concludes with Nirmāṇakāya as spontaneous benefit-activity. Unlike Sarma (*gsar ma*) presentations where Nirmāṇakāya represents "form body" (*gzugs sku*) as distinct from "truth body" (*chos sku*), Longchenpa's *snying thig* view asserts Nirmāṇakāya as Dharmakāya's self-recognition appearing as other-benefit (*gzhan don*). The "two benefits" (*don gnyis*)—self-benefit (*rang don*) and other-benefit (*gzhan don*)—are not sequential achievements but simultaneous perfections. This reflects the *'bras bu'i theg pa* (Result Vehicle) view that fruition is already-complete, not produced.
Epistemic precision: Nirmāṇakāya is not "vehicle for benefit" but spontaneous expression of perfected activity. Risk: Functionalism—viewing Nirmāṇakāya merely as method for benefiting beings, rather than recognizing inseparability from Dharmakāya's nature.
[1042-1065] 
[DZOGCHEN VIEW – SELF-APPEARANCE EMANATION]
[DECLARATIVE FINALITY]
[RISK: VIEW COLLAPSE / SOTERIOLOGICAL CONFUSION]
Generally divisions: nature-emanation (*rang bzhin sprul pa*), taming-emanation (*'gro 'dul sprul pa*), but here relying on two-benefits' perfection: completing-deeds Nirmāṇakāya (*bya ba rdzogs pa'i sprul sku*) and doing-deeds Nirmāṇakāya (*bya ba byed pa'i sprul sku*). Self-benefit spontaneously accomplished completing-deeds Nirmāṇakāya—this life or bardo self-appearance spontaneous gate perfected, from impure gate six beings self-appearance dawned, three conjunctions' (*sbyor gsum*) emanations benefit done. Six families self-appearance merely recognized, dharmadhātu peace. Self-appearance's samsāra must be emptied because confused-appearance must be emptied.
[CONTEXT] BARDO DOCTRINE: The reference to "this life or bardo" (*tshe 'dam bar do*) invokes the *snying thig* teaching on recognizing self-appearance during the intermediate state (*bar do*). "Three conjunctions" refers to the simultaneous presence of location (*gnas*), teacher (*ston pa*), and retinue (*'khor*) in pure vision. The instruction that "self-appearance's samsāra must be emptied" distinguishes Dzogchen from eternalist views—samsāra is not ultimately real but must be recognized as empty display.
Generally: "May exhausted and emptied samsāra three lower realms"; generating mind to liberate all beings. Particularly in bardo, emanation able-unable and having emanated becoming Buddha—all depends on self-appearance spontaneous gate when six beings' locations appear, there self-appearance's emptiness self-appearance, teachings and so on benefit done. Therefore samsāra-appearance all emptied, spontaneous gate purified, nirvāṇa dharmadhātu dissolved—called "Buddha's parinirvāṇa." This is extremely important vital-point for all with prajna.
Risks: View collapse—reducing to lower vehicle gradual accomplishment; Soteriological confusion—misunderstanding "emptying samsāra" as nihilistic destruction rather than recognition of never-having-been-established.
