[DZOGCHEN VIEW – PRIMORDIAL PURITY]
[DECLARATIVE FINALITY]
[RISK: SUBSTANTIALISM]
<The text establishes kadag (primordial purity) as the nature of all phenomena. This is not a quality added to things but their original condition. The danger is substantializing purity as a thing—it is rather the absence of inherent nature.>
---
[DZOGCHEN VIEW – NON-DUAL AWARENESS]
[DECLARATIVE FINALITY]
<Rigpa (awareness) is presented as the ground of being. Not an object to be known but the knowing itself. The practitioner is warned against grasping at awareness as a possession.>
