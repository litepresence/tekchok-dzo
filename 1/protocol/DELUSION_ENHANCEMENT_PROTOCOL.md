### After (Deep)
```
[3062-3067] <nihilistic-error> <emptiness-extreme>
**Misreading:**
The citation stating even "emptiness does not exist" interpreted as establishing total metaphysical nihilism—nothing whatsoever exists, no path, no realization, no valid cognition, not even emptiness itself.

**Why it arises:**
**Cognitive:** Negation absolutism. The chain of negations (samsara doesn't exist, nirvana doesn't exist, emptiness doesn't exist) creates cumulative void-impression. Brain interprets "not exist" as absolute absence.

**Cultural:** Western nihilism tradition (Nietzsche, existentialism) provides interpretive framework. "Nothing exists" sounds like profound philosophical insight.

**Linguistic:** "Does not exist" (med pa) lacks nuance in translation—misses that it means "not established as existing entity" not "total absence."

**Historical:** Chandrakirti's prasangika negation historically misunderstood by some as nihilism. Tibetan debates about "emptiness of emptiness" confusion.

**Primary consequence:**
Taking emptiness as total void rather than freedom from extremes. The practitioner believes "nothing exists" means literally nothing—including awareness, path, and realization.

**Secondary consequences:**
- **Paralysis:** "Why practice if nothing exists?"
- **Ethical nihilism:** No karma if nothing real, so any action acceptable
- **Confusing emptiness of emptiness with non-existence:** Missing that negating emptiness is negating reification, not affirming void
- **Depression/despair:** Existential meaninglessness

**Long-term:** Practice abandonment. Spiritual death. Some turn to hedonism ("nothing matters, so enjoy"). Others to despair ("nothing matters, so why live").

**Social:** If teacher presents this nihilistically, entire sangha collapses ethically. Historical cases of "crazy wisdom" justification for abuse.

**Cascade effects:**
<emptiness-misunderstanding> → <nihilistic-interpretation> → <ethical-collapse> → <practice-abandonment> OR <extreme-behavior-justification>

**Safety-critical:** HIGH RISK - This misreading has historically led to ethical collapse, practice abandonment, and harmful behavior justified as "beyond good and evil."

**Cross-layer bleed:** Heavy with 01-04-17 (two truths), 01-08 (ground analysis), all Madhyamaka sections.
```
