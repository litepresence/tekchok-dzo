## Critical Issues Identified

### 1. Generic/Repetitive Classification Statements
**Severity:** HIGH
**Affected Files:** ~50+ files

**Example of problematic pattern:**
```

<view>dzogchen-rigpa
<pedagogy>declarative-finality
<classification> Hermeneutical strategy maintaining apparent vehicle hierarchy while dissolving distinctions through recognition of awareness as single ground. Definitive meaning transcends yet incorporates provisional meaning.
```

This exact phrasing (or minor variations) appears in dozens of files across chapters 6, 8, 13, 14, and Volume 2.

**Impact:** Provides no actual analysis of the specific Tibetan content. Fails to identify key terms, concepts, or doctrinal points.

- 01-06-12-01.txt

## Conclusion

The epistemic layer demonstrates **capability for excellence** (exemplified by 01-01-01-01, 01-04-01-01, 01-09-01-01) but is **inconsistently applied**. The primary issues are:

1. **Batch generation artifacts:** Template language replicated across multiple files without customization
2. **Insufficient engagement:** Many files fail to identify specific Tibetan terms or doctrinal points
3. **Format errors:** Some files have technical errors requiring immediate correction
4. **Variable depth:** Byte ratios vary wildly from excellent (~45%) to inadequate (~5%)

**Estimated effort to bring all files to A- standard:**
- 10 files: Immediate critical fixes (1 week)
- 40-50 files: Rewrite template-generated content (2-3 weeks)
- 50-60 files: Enhance coverage and terminology (2-4 weeks)
- **Total: 100-120 files need significant work (~3 months with dedicated effort)**

**Priority chapters for immediate attention:**
1. Chapter 8 (01-08-*): Trekcho instructions - foundation of practice
2. Chapter 14 (01-14-*): Spontaneous liberation - fruition aspect
3. Chapter 24 (02-24-*): Bardo - practical application
4. Chapter 25 (02-25-*): Final fruition - conclusion of text

These four chapters represent the heart of the Treasury's instructions and currently have the poorest coverage.
