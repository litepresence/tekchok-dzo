## LOTSAWA JUDGMENT

**Question:** Is 'basis' an acceptable translation for gzhi?

**Answer:** NO.

**Reasoning:**
1. gzhi literally means "ground" in Tibetan
2. In Dzogchen, gzhi = primordial ground (not abstract "basis")
3. Dictionary specifies: gzhi MUST be translated as "ground"
4. 'basis' creates confusion with kun gzhi (all-ground)

## DOCTRINAL SIGNIFICANCE

The term gzhi (གཞི་) is central to Dzogchen:
- **Meaning:** Primordial ground, the ground of being
- **Three aspects:** Essence (ngo bo), Nature (rang bzhin), Compassion (thugs rje)
- **Translation:** ALWAYS "ground"

Using 'basis' for gzhi:
1. Confuses with kun gzhi (all-ground)
2. Weakens doctrinal precision
3. Contradicts dictionary specification
4. Violates Tibetan literal meaning
