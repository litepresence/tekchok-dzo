## METHODOLOGY NOTES

### Voice Rotation Strategy
- **Patrul Rinpoche (Vagabond):** Used for earthy, direct stories ("This old fool...")
- **Longchenpa (Omniscient):** Used as bridge between voices ("Between... the vast view...")
- **Garab Dorje (First Point):** Used for stark directness ("See. Decide. Return.")
- **Systematizer:** Used for analytical sections ("Therefore. Thus established...")
- **Lion-Faced:** Used for fierce yet spacious cutting ("This very hesitation...")
- **Sky-Dancer:** Used for playful, ecstatic wisdom
- **Integrator:** Used for dissolving dualities into spacious unity

### Metaphor Freshness
- **Banned metaphors retired:** yak hair tent, butter tea, frozen butter lamp, thief in empty hut
- **Fresh categories used:** Himalayan geography, seasonal elements, practice life, domestic life
- **No repetition within 5 sections** maintained throughout

### Heart-Instruction Tone
- **Resonating, not forcing:** "This old fool watched..." vs "You must see..."
- **Anonymous speaking:** No "Garab Dorje said..." or "As Patrul teaches..."
- **Immediate pointing:** "Look. See. Rest." rather than conceptual explanation
- **Transmission atmosphere:** Masters gathered around fire, speaking from recognition
