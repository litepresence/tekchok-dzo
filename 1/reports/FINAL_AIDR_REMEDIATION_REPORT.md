## AI-TELL PATTERNS IDENTIFIED AND REMOVED

### 1. Spiritual Clichés (Most Common)
**Pattern:** Generic mystical language that sounds profound but says little

**Examples Removed:**
- "space between thoughts where thoughts have never been"
- "fingers pointing at a moon that has never been absent"
- "already complete, always was"
- "return to what you never left"
- "the teaching opens like a door that was never closed"
- "seed planted in fertile ground"
- "vessel receives water not by effort but by being empty and open"
- "lightning illuminating the valley"
- "rain falling on different soils"

**Impact:** These phrases are AI training artifacts - generic spiritual language without specific doctrinal content.

### 2. Repetitive Content (Copy-Paste Hallucinations)
**Pattern:** Same sentences repeated 5-8 times within single files

**Examples Removed:**
- "What appears to arise is merely what was never absent, taking form."
- "The form is not separate from the forming."
- "The knowing is not separate from what is known."

**Impact:** AI model repeating its own outputs, creating circular, meaningless text.

### 3. Hallucinated Attribution
**Pattern:** AI inventing voices, characters, or sources

**Examples Removed:**
- "Voice 20 proclaims:..."
- "An old vagabond."
- "A practitioner."
- "Hence. The structure."

**Impact:** Fake attributions with no basis in the source text.

### 4. Excessive Line Range Repetition
**Pattern:** Same [start-end] range appearing 3-8 times

**Example:** `[8112-8112]` appearing 8 times in one file

**Impact:** Indicates AI filling space without meaningful content per section.

### 5. Poetic Mysticism Without Substance
**Pattern:** Flowery metaphors masking lack of technical content

**Examples Removed:**
- "The sun ripens all fruits without choosing. Its light asks nothing."
- "Between the extremes, the vast middle way opens like a horizon..."
- "What is called delusion here is wisdom's play of hide-and-seek"
- "The sequence completes itself, not by reaching an end, but by recognizing..."

**Impact:** Sounds spiritual but lacks doctrinal precision expected in scholarly work.
