## PURPOSE

Each exposition file serves as the "Editorial Conventions" section that appears in the front of the published text, explaining to readers:

1. **What this layer is** - Name, Tibetan term, definition
2. **Why it exists** - Purpose within the multi-layer architecture
3. **How it was created** - Methodology and voice
4. **How to use it** - Reading guidance for different audiences

## LAYER-SPECIFIC GUIDANCE

### For Tibetan, Wylie, Literal Layers (Source)

These are the foundation layers - emphasize:
- Authority and immutability
- Philological precision
- Relationship to source manuscripts
- Verification methods

### For Liturgical Layer

Emphasize:
- The transformation from literal to elegant
- The voice of Vairotsana Lotsawa
- Metrical classification (PROSE/VERSE/ORNAMENTAL/MANTRA)
- Relationship to Tibetan oral transmission tradition

### For Commentary Layer

Emphasize:
- The voice of Patrul Rinpoche
- Line-by-line engagement
- Recognition catalysis vs. academic explanation
- Relationship to terma tradition

### For Scholar Layer

Emphasize:
- Four Pillars methodology
- Academic rigor with practitioner accessibility
- Philological precision
- Relationship to Western scholarship

### For Epistemic Layer

Emphasize:
- View stratification (nine yanas)
- Hermeneutical positioning
- Risk analysis and uncertainty
- Relationship to drang nges

### For Delusion Layer

Emphasize:
- Error classification taxonomy
- Failure-mode cartography
- Cascade effects
- Protective function

### For Cognitive Layer

Emphasize:
- Translator's log methodology
- Pre-verbal recognition documentation
- Uncertainty and alternative readings
- Translator's personal engagement

### For Introduction Layer

Emphasize:
- Chapter contextual framing
- Bridge between technical and experiential
- Structural preview
- Practice relevance

### For Meter Layer

Emphasize:
- PROSE/VERSE/ORNAMENTAL/MANTRA classification
- Liturgical formatting guidance
- Relationship to oral performance
