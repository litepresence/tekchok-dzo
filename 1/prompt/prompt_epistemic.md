## QUALITY STANDARDS (A++ Exemplar)

**⚠️ QUALITATIVE EXCELLENCE IS MANDATORY. Byte ratios are diagnostic tools only.**

### Six Criteria for A++ Quality:

**1. Comprehensive Coverage**
- Every major doctrinal point from Tibetan source must be classified
- View shifts (sutric → tantric → Dzogchen) must be identified
- Technical terms must be explicitly named and contextualized

**2. Specific Terminology**
Use precise Tibetan terms where they appear:
- ka-dag (primordial purity), lhun-grub (spontaneous presence)
- kun gzhi (all-ground), chos sku (Dharmakaya)
- rig pa (awareness), sems (mind)
- gzhi snang (ground-appearance), ye shes (wisdom)

**3. Zero Generic Content (ANTI-FLUFF)**
❌ **FORBIDDEN PHRASES:**
- "This passage presents [topic] within Dzogchen context..."
- "Sophisticated hermeneutical strategy maintaining apparent vehicle hierarchy..."
- "Unlike sutric or tantric presentations, this operates from direct view..."
- "Ground is way things are—empty yet manifest, pure yet appearing..."
- "Fivefold analytical framework structures presentation while transcending it..."

✅ **REQUIRED:** Specific analysis that could ONLY apply to this passage

**4. View Precision**
Specific view-register identification:
- `<view>dzogchen-rigpa` - Direct primordial awareness
- `<view>dzogchen-sems` - Mind-based pedagogical entry
- `<view>tantric-transformative` - Result methods, subtle body
- `<view>sutric-provisional` - Causal vehicle reasoning
- `<view>ordinary-cognition` - Empirical, conventional

**5. Citation Integration**
Root text citations must be:
- Named (e.g., "Kuntu Zangpo Klong drug pa")
- Contextualized (what function does this citation serve?)
- Classified (provisional authority vs definitive authority)

**6. Risk Contextualization**
Risk flags must specify exact doctrinal tension:
❌ Generic: "<risk>reification</risk>"
✅ Specific: "Risk of reifying compassion as emotional state rather than natural responsiveness"
❌ Generic: "<risk>view-collapse</risk>"
✅ Specific: "Risk of collapsing kun gzhi into chos sku or vice versa"
