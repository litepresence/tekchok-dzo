### STRUCTURE REQUIREMENTS

Each volume introduction MUST include:

**1. HEADER SECTION**
- Volume number and title (English and Tibetan)
- Chapter range covered
- Total sections and approximate line count
- Historical context (Longchenpa, 14th century)

**2. OVERVIEW PARAGRAPH**
- The volume's unique contribution to the whole work
- How it builds on or prepares for the other volume
- The progression of thought from beginning to end
- Key turning points or transitions within the volume

**3. CHAPTER BREAKDOWN**
- Brief summary of each chapter (2-3 sentences)
- Highlight chapter's unique contribution
- Note any chapter pairs or sequences
- Mark pivotal chapters

**4. KEY THEMES**
- Major doctrinal developments
- Technical terminology introduced
- Practical instructions provided
- Unique Dzogchen perspectives

**5. READING GUIDANCE**

**For Practitioners:**
- How to approach the volume as a practice manual
- Key points to recognize and apply
- Common pitfalls to avoid
- Integration with other volumes

**For Scholars:**
- Historical and doctrinal context
- Relationship to other Tibetan Buddhist texts
- Unique contributions to Dzogchen literature
- Critical points for academic analysis

**6. VOLUME RELATIONSHIPS**
- How this volume connects to the other volume
- Prerequisites or foundations laid
- Fruition or completion achieved
- The overall arc across both volumes

**7. QUALITY NOTES**
- Translation approach and standards
- Special considerations
- Acknowledgment of collaborators
