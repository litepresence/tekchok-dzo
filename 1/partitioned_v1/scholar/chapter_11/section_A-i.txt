[STRUCTURE] Seventh Week: The Wind Element and Subtle Body Architecture
This page continues the detailed exposition of the seventh week of fetal development, focusing on the wind element (རླུང་) as the activating force within the subtle body channels. The text presents a tripartite structure: (1) the movement of wind-channel pulsation (རླུང་གི་རྩ་འཕར་ཞིང་འགྱུ་བ་), (2) the four syllabic abodes (ཡཾ་, སྤུངས་འབྱེད་, ཡེ་འབྱེད་སྡུད་, ཨོཾ་), and (3) the five aggregates established as supports (ཕུང་པོ་ལྔའི་གཞི་). [PHILOLOGY] Technical Terminology of the Subtle Body
Key terms require precise analysis: རྩ་འཕར་ (channel-pulsation) combines རྩ་ (nāḍī/channel) with འཕར་ (to quiver/pulsate), indicating vibratory rather than gross movement. འགྱུ་ཞིང་རྒྱུ་པ་ (creeping and moving) describes the serpentine, kundalinī-like quality of subtle wind. The four syllables mark progressive stages: ཡཾ་ (yaṃ, air element) quivers; སྤུངས་འབྱེད་ indicates gathering and separating; ཡེ་འབྱེད་སྡུད་ suggests projection and retraction; ཨོཾ་ (oṃ) rolls, folds, and abides—describing the spiral movement of prāṇa. [CONTEXT] Tathāgatagarbha Sūtra Influences and Dzogchen Integration
This passage integrates Tathāgatagarbha theory (the five aggregates as naturally pure) with Dzogchen's emphasis on direct recognition. The five Buddha families (རིགས་ལྔ་) appearing on the heads of the five supports—ཛྲཱིཾ་ (Vajra), ཁཾ་ (Ratna), ཏཱཾ་ (Padma), མཾ་ (Karma), བྱཾ་ (Buddha)—correspond to the classical tantric maṇḍala structure but are here naturalized as pre-existing conditions. [CONCEPT] The Ontology of Natural Transformation (Rang Gnas Sgyur Ba)
The core philosophical assertion—གནས་པ་བསྒྱུར་བ་ཙམ་ལས་རིག་ངེས་པའི་སངས་རྒྱས་སུ་འགྱུར་ངེས་པ་ (through merely changing the abode, one certainly becomes a Buddha of certain awareness)—articulates the Dzogchen doctrine of spontaneous liberation (རང་གྲོལ་). Unlike gradualist paths requiring accumulation of merit and wisdom over aeons, this view holds that buddhahood is already present (རང་གནས་, self-abiding) and requires only recognition, not construction. The "change of abode" refers to a shift in perspective—from identifying with aggregates as solid to recognizing them as five wisdoms. The three basis wisdoms (གཞི་གནས་ཀྱི་ཡེ་ཤེས་གསུམ་) and five characteristic-holding wisdoms (མཚན་ཉིད་འཛིན་པའི་ཡེ་ཤེས་ལྔ་) represent the gnoseological infrastructure of this recognition, where what appears as ordinary physiology is re-cognized as enlightened manifestation.

The passage establishes precise correspondences between aggregates (ཕུང་པོ་), supports (གཞི་), and elements: form aggregate (གཟུགས་ཀྱི་ཕུང་པོ་) = subtle atoms (རྡུལ་ཕྲ་རབ་), perception (འདུ་ཤེས་) = heat on horse-drum, feeling (ཚོར་བ་) = three-cubit thread, consciousness (རྣམ་ཤེས་) = color-mind movement, formations (འདུ་བྱེད་) = feeling-door opening. [CONCEPT] Basis Wisdom as Pre-Enlightened Gnosis
The distinction between gzhi gnas kyi ye shes (basis wisdom) and mtshan nyid 'dzin pa'i ye shes (characteristic-holding wisdom) reveals a sophisticated epistemology. The five-colored thigles (ཐིག་ལེ་) represent condensed luminosity, like droplets of pure awareness that will expand into the full manifestation of the five wisdom Buddhas at the time of recognition.
