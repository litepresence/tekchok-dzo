[ONTOLOGICAL ERROR] [REIFICATION ERROR]
MISREADING:
Samantabhadra as a "primordial Buddha" suggests a historical or mythological figure existing in time and space, rather than the ground of being itself.
WHY IT ARISES:
Western religious frameworks unconsciously impose theistic interpretations. The term "Buddha" triggers associations with Shakyamuni-type figures. The Sanskrit title and ornate homage structure resemble theistic liturgy, activating familiar devotional patterns.
PRIMARY CONSEQUENCE:
Ground (gzhi) is reified into a metaphysical entity or god-substitute. The five perfections become attributes of a supreme being rather than dimensions of awareness.
SECONDARY CONSEQUENCES:
Dzogchen becomes a theistic religion rather than recognition of mind's nature. Practice shifts to worship rather than direct investigation. The supreme vehicle becomes a path to reach this "Buddha" rather than recognition of what already is.
CASCADE EFFECTS:
Triggers [ETERNALISTIC ERROR] (seeking permanent refuge in a being), [MEDITATIONISM ERROR] (attempting to "contact" Samantabhadra through effort), and [HIERARCHICAL ERROR] (viewing the fivefold homage as climbing toward a distant goal rather than recognizing simultaneous aspects).

[PERCEPTUAL LEVEL CONFUSION] [REALM SUBJECTIVISM]
MISREADING:
The different realms (hell, heaven, Buddha-fields) interpreted as different subjective perceptions of the same objective reality—like different people seeing the same cloud as different shapes.
WHY IT ARISES:
The text describes how pure and impure perception see differently (*las dag pa'i snang ba* vs. *ma dag pa*). Buddhist teachings often emphasize perception-dependence. This invites subjective idealist interpretation.
PRIMARY CONSEQUENCE:
Dzogchen is reduced to subjective idealism—reality is mind-dependent projection. The objective pole is lost; everything dissolves into personal perception.
SECONDARY CONSEQUENCES:
If realms are "just perception," then changing perception changes reality. This supports magical thinking—"if I think positively, hell becomes heaven." The actual meaning—perception and perceived are non-dual—is missed.
CASCADE EFFECTS:
Triggers [SUBJECTIVE IDEALISM] (mind-only philosophy), [MAGICAL THINKING] (thinking changes reality), [SOLIPSISM RISK] (only my perception exists), [PERCEPTUAL RELATIVISM] (all views equally valid), and [YOGACARA REDUCTION] (reducing Dzogchen to mind-only).

SECONDARY CONSEQUENCES:

CASCADE EFFECTS:
