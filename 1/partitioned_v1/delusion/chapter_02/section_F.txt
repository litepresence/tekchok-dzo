ERROR TYPE 1: [SENSE BASE SUBSTANTIALISM] [ONTOLOGICAL ERROR]
================================================================================
MISREADING:
The twelve sense bases (skye mched bcu gnyis)—the six objects (forms, sounds, smells, tastes, tactile objects, phenomena) and six consciousnesses—are understood as substantial existents that mechanically produce perception through causal contact, like billiard balls colliding.
WHY IT ARISES:
Materialist conditioning assumes sense organs are physical receptors and objects are external stimuli. The language of "arising and expanding" (skye zhing mched) suggests a causal production model. Western science treats perception as information processing between discrete entities.
PRIMARY CONSEQUENCE:
Perception is reified as a mechanical process occurring between truly existent sense organs and objects. The empty, illusory nature of the entire perceptual display is missed. The practitioner attempts to "purify" perception by manipulating these mechanical interactions rather than recognizing their dream-like nature.
SECONDARY CONSEQUENCES:
Subject-object dualism becomes entrenched. The practitioner sees themselves as a consciousness separate from the world of objects. Meditation becomes an attempt to control or transcend this mechanical perception rather than recognizing its non-arising nature.
CASCADE EFFECTS:
This produces [DIRECT RECOGNITION DELAY] where the practitioner perpetually works on "refining perception" without recognizing perception's empty nature. It generates [CAUSAL/RESULT CONFUSION] where understanding sense bases is believed to mechanically produce liberation.

The Samkhya concept of Self (bdag) as possessing the three qualities (dkar po, snum pa, 'dril ba)—pure, oily, coiling—and being permanent, indestructible, and self-cognizant is interpreted as a philosophical error to be refuted, rather than recognizing that any reified self, including the Buddhist "correct" self, is equally empty.
WHY IT ARISES:
Comparative religious study emphasizes doctrinal differences. The practitioner assumes Buddhist selflessness is simply the opposite of Hindu selfhood. Western philosophy distinguishes between different self-concepts. The refutation of Samkhya invites comparison.
PRIMARY CONSEQUENCE:
The practitioner understands "no-self" as the absence of Samkhya-type self while reifying other concepts. The subtle grasping to Buddhist identity as "selfless person" is missed. The critique remains superficial, targeting only obvious targets.
SECONDARY CONSEQUENCES:
Intellectual pride develops. "We Buddhists have no-self; Hindus have self" becomes identity-marker. The deeper emptiness of all concepts of self and no-self is never accessed. Practice remains at the level of doctrinal correctness.
CASCADE EFFECTS:
This feeds [VEHICLE HIERARCHY ELITISM] where Buddhist selflessness is considered superior to Hindu selfhood. It produces [SUTRA DISMISSAL] when practitioners dismiss non-Buddhist traditions without recognizing the universality of pointing-out instructions.

ERROR TYPE 3: [ATOMISTIC REDUCTIONISM] [ONTOLOGICAL ERROR]
================================================================================
MISREADING:
The Samkhya presentation of dust-particles (rdul mun pa), potency (snying stobs), and the three qualities as ultimate constituents of reality is interpreted as demonstrating the error of reductionism, while failing to recognize that Buddhist dharmas are equally reductionistic and equally empty.
WHY IT ARISES:
Scientific materialism uses similar reductionistic language. The practitioner assumes Buddhist analysis is different in kind from Samkhya analysis. Western education conditions reductionistic thinking. Both systems appear to analyze reality into constituents.
PRIMARY CONSEQUENCE:
The practitioner believes Buddhist dharmas are "truly real" constituents while Samkhya qualities are "false." The equality of all conceptual analysis in emptiness is missed. One reductionism is replaced by another.
SECONDARY CONSEQUENCES:
Dharmas are reified as ultimate existents. Buddhist atomism replaces Samkhya atomism. The practitioner memorizes lists of dharmas as "true description of reality" rather than conventional designations.
CASCADE EFFECTS:
This produces [AGGREGATE REIFICATION] where Buddhist dharmas are treated as real building blocks. It generates [VIEW COLLAPSE] where the practitioner mistakes conceptual dharmic analysis for direct recognition of emptiness.

ERROR TYPE 4: [ETERNALIST LIBERATION FANTASY] [PRACTICE ERROR]
================================================================================
MISREADING:
The Samkhya view of liberation as abiding in the nature of Self without distraction, leading to the Lord's realm (dbang phyug gi gnas) with perfect retinue and enjoyments, is dismissed as wrong without recognizing that Buddhist liberation-fantasies are equally fantastical and equally empty.
WHY IT ARISES:
The practitioner wants to feel superior to "wrong views." The critique of others' fantasies blinds one to one's own. Buddhist heavens and pure lands seem different from Hindu heavens. The practitioner assumes Buddhist goals are not fantasies.
PRIMARY CONSEQUENCE:
The practitioner pursues Buddhist liberation while critiquing Samkhya liberation. Both are grasping at future states. The recognition that all liberation-concepts are conventional is missed.
SECONDARY CONSEQUENCES:
Spiritual materialism develops around "correct" Buddhist goals. Pure land visions are taken literally. The practitioner accumulates merit for future enjoyment in Buddha-fields, exactly paralleling the criticized Samkhya view.
CASCADE EFFECTS:
This produces [CAUSAL/RESULT CONFUSION] where Buddhist practice is believed to mechanically produce Buddhist results. It generates [PROGRESSION MYTH] where Buddhist enlightenment is conceived as future achievement.

ERROR TYPE 5: [DOCTRINAL ONE-UPMANSHIP] [PEDAGOGICAL ERROR]
================================================================================
MISREADING:
The detailed refutation of non-Buddhist views is interpreted as establishing Buddhism's intellectual superiority, encouraging the practitioner to study comparative philosophy primarily to win debates and prove Buddhist dominance, rather than as skillful means pointing to recognition.
WHY IT ARISES:
Competitive education rewards winning arguments. Religious identity requires feeling superior to "others." The practitioner wants intellectual validation. The systematic refutation invites comparative assessment.
PRIMARY CONSEQUENCE:
The practitioner becomes a Buddhist apologist. Study is directed toward refuting others rather than recognizing mind nature. The transformative purpose of studying wrong views—recognizing similar errors in oneself—is lost.
SECONDARY CONSEQUENCES:
Interfaith hostility develops. The practitioner cannot learn from non-Buddhist wisdom traditions. Intellectual pride obscures genuine humility. The universality of pointing-out instructions across traditions is missed.
CASCADE EFFECTS:
This feeds [VEHICLE HIERARCHY ELITISM] where Buddhist views are considered absolutely superior. It produces [SUTRA DISMISSAL] when practitioners arrogantly dismiss all non-Buddhist teachings as "mere wrong view."

ERROR TYPE 1: [NIHILISM CONFIRMATION BIAS] [ONTOLOGICAL ERROR]
================================================================================
MISREADING:
The presentation of Lokayata/Charvaka nihilist views denying past and future lives, karma, causality, and liberation is interpreted as demonstrating the opposite error from eternalism, rather than recognizing that both extremes arise from the same fundamental error—grasping to concepts of existence and non-existence.
WHY IT ARISES:
Dualistic thinking positions nihilism as opposite of eternalism. The practitioner assumes if eternalism is wrong, nihilism must be equally wrong in the opposite direction. The refutation of extremes invites taking a middle position between them.
PRIMARY CONSEQUENCE:
The practitioner believes Buddhism avoids both "existence" and "non-existence" to arrive at a third position. The deeper teaching—that all positions including the middle are empty—is missed. The practitioner is stuck in subtle metaphysics.
SECONDARY CONSEQUENCES:
Conceptual understanding replaces direct recognition. The practitioner can explain why both eternalism and nihilism are wrong without recognizing mind nature. Scholarly mastery of refutations masks lack of realization.
CASCADE EFFECTS:
This produces [VIEW COLLAPSE] where intellectual understanding of emptiness replaces direct recognition. It generates [PEDAGOGICAL ERROR] where the practitioner teaches others to "avoid extremes" without embodying that avoidance.

ERROR TYPE 2: [MATERIALIST REDUCTION DISMISSAL] [EPISTEMIC ERROR]
================================================================================
MISREADING:
The Lokayata view that mind emerges from the four elements is refuted as wrong without recognizing that Buddhist views of mind-dependent origination are equally conventional and equally unable to capture the nature of mind.
WHY IT ARISES:
The practitioner wants to feel superior to "materialists." Buddhist teachings about consciousness seem more sophisticated than element-based theories. Western science is often materialist, creating antagonism. The practitioner assumes Buddhist view is "right answer."
PRIMARY CONSEQUENCE:
The practitioner believes Buddhist consciousness theory is ultimately true. The emptiness of all theories of mind is missed. One conceptual framework replaces another without recognition of their equal conventionality.
SECONDARY CONSEQUENCES:
Spiritual pride develops. "Buddhism correctly understands consciousness" becomes identity-marker. The practitioner cannot engage materialist science with humility. Dialogue becomes debate.
CASCADE EFFECTS:
This feeds [VEHICLE HIERARCHY ELITISM] where Buddhist psychology is considered superior to Western psychology. It produces [SUTRA DISMISSAL] when practitioners dismiss scientific materialism without recognizing its conventional validity.

ERROR TYPE 3: [SPONTANEOUS GENERATION CONFUSION] [CAUSAL/RESULT CONFUSION]
================================================================================
MISREADING:
The Lokayata examples of spontaneous generation (insects from dust, plants from moisture) are interpreted as demonstrating the error of denying causality, without recognizing that Buddhist dependent origination is equally mysterious and equally unable to be fully conceptually grasped.
WHY IT ARISES:
The examples seem obviously wrong to educated practitioners. Science refutes spontaneous generation. The practitioner assumes Buddhist causality is "correct" causality. The refutation invites complacency.
PRIMARY CONSEQUENCE:
The practitioner believes they understand causality correctly while Lokayatas don't. The profound mystery of dependent origination is reduced to textbook formula. The recognition that causality is ultimately inexpressible is lost.
SECONDARY CONSEQUENCES:
Intellectual arrogance develops. "I know how causality works" replaces wonder. The practitioner becomes a Buddhist scientist, confident in their understanding. The miracle of experience arising is taken for granted.
CASCADE EFFECTS:
This produces [CAUSAL/RESULT CONFUSION] where understanding dependent origination conceptually is believed to be sufficient. It generates [PROGRESSION MYTH] where the practitioner believes they are "learning correct causality" on the path.

ERROR TYPE 4: [DEATH AS ANNIHILATION COMFORT] [VIEW COLLAPSE]
================================================================================
MISREADING:
The Lokayata view that death is final annihilation with no future result is refuted as wrong, but the practitioner fails to recognize that Buddhist views of continuation are equally unable to capture the nature of death and that both views miss the empty nature of the one who dies.
WHY IT ARISES:
Fear of death drives desire for continuation. The practitioner wants to believe in rebirth. Nihilism seems depressing. Buddhist teachings about bardo and future lives offer comfort. The refutation of annihilation validates hope.
PRIMARY CONSEQUENCE:
The practitioner believes Buddhist continuation is "correct" while annihilation is "wrong." The recognition that both are conceptual imputations on the ungraspable is missed. Death becomes a philosophical problem to solve.
SECONDARY CONSEQUENCES:
The practitioner fears death less but still fears it. The recognition that there is no one to die is replaced by confidence in rebirth. Death remains an obstacle rather than teacher.
CASCADE EFFECTS:
This produces [CAUSAL/RESULT CONFUSION] where good karma is accumulated to ensure good rebirth. It generates [PROGRESSION MYTH] where enlightenment is deferred to future lives when conditions will be better.

MISREADING:

SECONDARY CONSEQUENCES:
Compassion for those holding wrong views is replaced by pity or contempt. Dialogue with non-Buddhists becomes conversion attempt. The practitioner cannot learn from other traditions. Openness is lost.
CASCADE EFFECTS:
This feeds [VEHICLE HIERARCHY ELITISM] where Buddhist practitioners consider themselves superior to all others. It produces [SUTRA DISMISSAL] when practitioners arrogantly dismiss all "wrong views" without recognizing their own limitations.
