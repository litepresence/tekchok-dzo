ERROR TYPE 1: [360 WRONG VIEWS OBSESSION] [PEDAGOGICAL ERROR]
================================================================================
MISREADING:
The enumeration of 360 wrong views (sum brgya drug cu) derived from the basis (dbyings) is interpreted as establishing a comprehensive taxonomy of errors that the practitioner must study, memorize, and avoid, rather than recognizing that all wrong views share the same root—grasping to extremes.
WHY IT ARISES:
Academic mind seeks comprehensive catalogs. The practitioner assumes detailed knowledge protects against error. The extensive enumeration invites systematic study. Fear of wrong view drives comprehensive analysis.
PRIMARY CONSEQUENCE:
The practitioner becomes a wrong-view expert, memorizing all 360 variations without recognizing their common root. Time is spent cataloging errors rather than recognizing mind nature. Study replaces practice.
SECONDARY CONSEQUENCES:
Scholastic arrogance develops. The practitioner can enumerate all wrong views while holding subtle versions themselves. Others are judged by their doctrinal correctness. The transformative purpose is lost in pedantic mastery.
CASCADE EFFECTS:
This feeds [PREPARATION PARALYSIS] where genuine practice is deferred until "all wrong views are understood." It generates [VEHICLE HIERARCHY ELITISM] where the practitioner feels superior for knowing the complete taxonomy.

ERROR TYPE 2: [BASIS-DERIVED DELUSION CONFUSION] [ONTOLOGICAL ERROR]
================================================================================
MISREADING:
The presentation that all beings arise from, abide in, and dissolve back into the basis/elemental sphere ('byung ba'i dbyings) is interpreted as establishing a metaphysical substrate or ground of being from which phenomena emerge, rather than recognizing the basis as a conventional designation for the ungraspable nature of mind.
WHY IT ARISES:
Metaphysical thinking seeks ultimate ground. The language of "arising from" suggests causal substrate. The practitioner wants an ontological foundation. Both science and religion posit fundamental grounds.
PRIMARY CONSEQUENCE:
The basis becomes a new metaphysical absolute. The practitioner grasps to "elemental sphere" as ultimate reality. The emptiness of the basis itself is missed. One ground replaces another.
SECONDARY CONSEQUENCES:
The practitioner believes they have found "the source of everything." Spiritual materialism develops around basis-realization. Others are judged by their recognition or non-recognition of this ground.
CASCADE EFFECTS:
This produces [VIEW COLLAPSE] where conceptual understanding of basis replaces direct recognition. It generates [CAUSAL/RESULT CONFUSION] where contacting the basis is believed to mechanically produce liberation.
================================================================================
ERROR TYPE 3: [LIBERATION AS DISSOLUTION FETISH] [PRACTICE ERROR]
================================================================================
MISREADING:
The view that liberation consists in dissolving back into the basis ('byung ba'i dbyings su yal), with no return to samsara, is interpreted as a metaphysical claim about the nature of liberation, rather than recognizing that liberation is not a state of dissolution but freedom from all concepts including dissolution.
WHY IT ARISES:
Spiritual escapism seeks final rest. The language of "dissolving" suggests peaceful end. The practitioner wants permanent escape from samsara. Death and dissolution are associated with rest.
PRIMARY CONSEQUENCE:
Liberation becomes a state of cosmic dissolution. The practitioner pursues disappearance into the basis as goal. The dynamic, responsive nature of enlightened activity is lost in favor of static absorption.
SECONDARY CONSEQUENCES:
Fear of engagement develops. The practitioner avoids relationships and responsibilities. "Just dissolving into the basis" becomes spiritual bypassing. Compassionate activity is rejected as "samsaric return."
CASCADE EFFECTS:
This produces [NIHILISTIC ERROR] where liberation is confused with annihilation. It generates [SUTRA DISMISSAL] when the practitioner rejects Mahayana emphasis on compassionate return as inferior to "final dissolution."

ERROR TYPE 4: [WRONG VIEW CATEGORIZATION PRIDE] [PEDAGOGICAL ERROR]
================================================================================
MISREADING:
The detailed categorization of wrong views into eternalist types (20), nihilist types (20), and sub-variations (Samkhya, Vaisesika, Lokayata, etc.) is interpreted as demonstrating the superiority of Buddhist taxonomy, encouraging the practitioner to master these classifications as evidence of spiritual development.
WHY IT ARISES:
Intellectual achievement signals competence. The practitioner wants measurable progress. Educational systems reward categorical mastery. Religious identity requires distinction through knowledge.
PRIMARY CONSEQUENCE:
The practitioner becomes a comparative religion scholar rather than Dharma practitioner. Time is invested in learning Indian philosophical systems. Recognition of mind nature is deferred indefinitely.
SECONDARY CONSEQUENCES:
Academic pride develops. "I know all six systems of Indian philosophy" becomes identity-marker. The practitioner looks down on "simple practitioners" who haven't studied wrong views. Authentic humility is lost.
CASCADE EFFECTS:
This feeds [VEHICLE HIERARCHY ELITISM] where scholarly mastery is equated with spiritual attainment. It produces [DIRECT RECOGNITION DELAY] where recognition is deferred until "all philosophical study is complete."

ERROR TYPE 5: [EXTREME VIEW BINARY TRAP] [EPISTEMIC ERROR]
================================================================================
MISREADING:
The clear division between eternalist views (rtag par smra ba) and nihilist views (chad par smra ba) is interpreted as establishing a binary classification system where Buddhist middle way occupies a third position between them, rather than recognizing that the middle way transcends the very framework of extremes.
WHY IT ARISES:
Binary thinking seeks clear categories. The middle appears as compromise between opposites. Western logic emphasizes non-contradiction. The practitioner assumes truth lies between extremes.
PRIMARY CONSEQUENCE:
The middle way becomes a new extreme—the "correct" position between wrong positions. The freedom from all positions is missed. Subtle attachment to "Buddhist correctness" replaces gross attachment to extremes.
SECONDARY CONSEQUENCES:
The practitioner defends "middle way" as doctrinal position. Debates with eternalists and nihilists become about proving the middle correct. The inexpressible nature of wisdom is lost in defense of concept.
CASCADE EFFECTS:
This produces [VIEW COLLAPSE] where conceptual middle way replaces direct recognition. It generates [PEDAGOGICAL ERROR] where the practitioner teaches others to "avoid extremes" while remaining trapped in subtle conceptualization.

================================================================================
MISREADING:
The detailed refutation of 360 wrong views is interpreted as establishing that liberation requires the extinction or elimination of all wrong views through systematic refutation, rather than recognizing that wrong views self-liberate when their empty nature is recognized.

SECONDARY CONSEQUENCES:
Doctrinal fundamentalism develops. The practitioner judges others by their view-correctness. Debate about fine points replaces practice. The transformative purpose of teachings is lost in scholastic wrangling.

ERROR TYPE 4: [VEHICLE PROGRESSION LADDER] [PROGRESSION MYTH]
================================================================================
MISREADING:
The transition from wrong views to Sravaka vehicle is interpreted as the beginning of a progressive ladder where each vehicle is higher than the last, establishing a hierarchy from worldly to transcendent, Hinayana to Mahayana, sutra to tantra.
WHY IT ARISES:
Developmental psychology expects progression. The text's structure suggests ascent. The practitioner wants to know "where am I on the path." Spiritual marketing emphasizes advanced teachings.
PRIMARY CONSEQUENCE:
The practitioner becomes obsessed with vehicle-identification. "I am a Mahayana practitioner" becomes ego-identity. Lower vehicles are looked down upon. The recognition that all vehicles are skillful means is lost.
SECONDARY CONSEQUENCES:
The practitioner rushes to "higher" vehicles without foundation. Sravaka and Pratyekabuddha teachings are dismissed as "too basic." The gradual accumulation of wisdom is replaced by ambition for advanced status.
CASCADE EFFECTS:
This produces [VEHICLE HIERARCHY ELITISM] where higher-numbered vehicles are pursued for status. It generates [TANTRA SUBSTITUTION] where advanced practices are taken up without proper foundation.

ERROR TYPE 5: [TRANSCENDENT/WORLDLY DUALISM] [ONTOLOGICAL ERROR]

MISREADING:
The distinction between worldly vehicles (jig rten pa'i theg pa) and transcendent vehicles ('jig rten las 'das pa'i theg pa) is interpreted as establishing an ontological dualism between ordinary and sacred, samsara and nirvana as distinct realms, rather than recognizing their inseparability.

Dualistic thinking habitually creates sacred/profane distinctions. The practitioner wants to escape the worldly. Religious traditions often posit transcendent realms. The language of "beyond world" suggests separate domain.
PRIMARY CONSEQUENCE:
The practitioner pursues transcendence as escape from world. Ordinary life is devalued. "Transcendent practice" becomes special activity separate from daily life. The natural perfection of all experience is denied.
SECONDARY CONSEQUENCES:
Spiritual escapism develops. The practitioner neglects worldly responsibilities. Relationships and work are seen as obstacles. The integration of practice and daily life collapses into dualistic separation.
CASCADE EFFECTS:
This produces [SUTRA DISMISSAL] where worldly Dharma is rejected in favor of transcendent. It generates [NIHILISTIC ERROR] where the world is dismissed as worthless illusion.

MISREADING:
The Sravaka vehicle is understood as a preliminary or inferior path to be transcended by superior practitioners, rather than recognizing it as a complete path appropriate for certain capacities.
WHY IT ARISES:
Hierarchical thinking assumes later = better. The practitioner wants to be 'advanced.' Vehicle numbering suggests ranking.
PRIMARY CONSEQUENCE:
The practitioner dismisses Sravaka teachings as 'too basic' without having understood their profound purpose.
SECONDARY CONSEQUENCES:
Foundation is skipped in rush to 'higher' vehicles. The practitioner becomes a Mahayana snob who lacks basic virtue.
CASCADE EFFECTS:
[VEHICLE HIERARCHY ELITISM] and [SUTRA DISMISSAL]
ERROR TYPE 2: [ONTOLOGICAL ERROR] [VIEW COLLAPSE]
================================================================================
MISREADING:
The Hinayana/Sravaka path is interpreted as requiring aeons of solitary practice, making it impractical for modern practitioners who therefore must skip to faster methods.
WHY IT ARISES:
Impatience and spiritual materialism. The practitioner wants quick results. 'Three countless aeons' seems impossibly long.
PRIMARY CONSEQUENCE:
The practitioner rejects gradual paths in favor of 'direct' methods without proper foundation.
SECONDARY CONSEQUENCES:
Practice becomes superficial. The depth developed through gradual training is missing. 'Direct' methods fail due to lack of preparation.
CASCADE EFFECTS:
[TANTRA SUBSTITUTION] and [DIRECT RECOGNITION DELAY]
ERROR TYPE 3: [CAUSAL/RESULT CONFUSION] [VIEW COLLAPSE]
================================================================================
MISREADING:
The distinction between Sravaka and Pratyekabuddha vehicles is interpreted as establishing rigid personality types, rather than recognizing fluid categories based on capacity and inclination.
WHY IT ARISES:
Typology fascination. The practitioner wants to identify 'which one am I?' Personality tests are popular.
PRIMARY CONSEQUENCE:
The practitioner becomes fixated on vehicle-identity. 'I am a Pratyekabuddha type' becomes self-limiting belief.
SECONDARY CONSEQUENCES:
Flexibility is lost. The practitioner follows a rigid path based on initial assessment rather than responding to circumstances.
CASCADE EFFECTS:
[VEHICLE HIERARCHY ELITISM] and [PREPARATION PARALYSIS]

================================================================================
MISREADING:
The 'result' of the Sravaka path—Arhatship—is understood as final extinction (nirvana with remainder) that represents a lower goal than Buddhahood.
WHY IT ARISES:
Mahayana superiority complex. The practitioner believes bigger goal = better. Compassion for all beings seems superior to personal liberation.
PRIMARY CONSEQUENCE:
The practitioner looks down on Arhats as 'selfish' while lacking their actual attainment. Pride without substance.
SECONDARY CONSEQUENCES:
The profound attainment of Sravaka path is dismissed. The practitioner criticizes what they have not understood.
CASCADE EFFECTS:
[VEHICLE HIERARCHY ELITISM] and [VIEW COLLAPSE]

WHY IT ARISES:
Educational conditioning. The practitioner assumes structured progression. Fear of missing prerequisites.
PRIMARY CONSEQUENCE:
The practitioner defers genuine recognition while working through vehicle-stages. The path becomes endless preparation.
SECONDARY CONSEQUENCES:
Each vehicle becomes another box to check. Recognition is perpetually deferred until 'completion' of current vehicle.
