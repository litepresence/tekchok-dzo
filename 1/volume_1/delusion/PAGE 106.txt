[3915-3917]
[ONTOLOGICAL ERROR] [NIHILISTIC ERROR]

MISREADING:
"Even the name of buddha does not exist" and "view and meditation are without measure" means there is no path, no practice, and no need for the Buddhist teachings at all.

WHY IT ARISES:
The explicit negation of buddha-name existence and the assertion that view/meditation lack measure appears to dissolve all conventional frameworks. The practitioner seeking liberation from effort seizes on this as validation.

PRIMARY CONSEQUENCE:
Rejection of refuge, bodhicitta, precepts, and guru relationship under the banner of "beyond buddha." The distinction between negating inherent existence and negating conventional functionality collapses.

SECONDARY CONSEQUENCES:
Dismissal of all gradual paths. Justification of unethical behavior through "beyond good and evil." Isolation from sangha and lineage.

CASCADE EFFECTS:
Spiritual bypassing → accumulation of negative karma → obstacles to recognition → eventual crisis and blame directed at Dzogchen itself.

[3919-3921]
[PRACTICE ERROR] [MEDITATIONISM ERROR]

MISREADING:
"Afflictions are self-liberated" and "acceptance and rejection do not exist" means one should simply allow whatever arises without intervention, making no effort to transform mind.

WHY IT ARISES:
The phrase "self-liberated" suggests automatic liberation without recognition. The negation of acceptance/rejection appears to validate passive non-engagement with experience.

PRIMARY CONSEQUENCE:
Mistaking non-conceptual ignorance for self-liberated wisdom. The practitioner becomes passive, allowing habitual patterns to continue unchecked under the guise of "self-liberation."

SECONDARY CONSEQUENCES:
Entrenchment of afflictive patterns. False confidence in "crazy wisdom." Deterioration of ethical conduct and relational capacity.

CASCADE EFFECTS:
Habitual patterns strengthen → negative karma accumulates → deepening confusion → rejection of Dharma as ineffective.

[3922-3925]
[REIFICATION ERROR] [EPISTEMIC ERROR]

MISREADING:
"Gtar-ka's obstacle-clearing" and "confusion is self-pacified" refer to a specific technique or stage where obscurations are removed, revealing a pre-existing pure ground.

WHY IT ARISES:
The term "gtar-ka" (membrane/veil) suggests something covering something else. The language of clearing and pacification implies a process of removal to reveal what was hidden.

PRIMARY CONSEQUENCE:
Ground-grasping: belief in a pure basis obscured by adventitious defilements. This mirrors tathāgatagarbha heresy—substantializing buddha-nature as an essence to be revealed.

SECONDARY CONSEQUENCES:
Meditation becomes excavation—trying to peel away layers to reach the pure ground beneath. Eternalist fixation on "original purity" as an ontological state.

CASCADE EFFECTS:
Effort to clear obstacles → frustration when obstacles persist → either forceful suppression or abandonment of practice.

[3927-3940]
[PRACTICE ERROR] [MEDITATIONISM ERROR]

MISREADING:
The detailed descriptions of "heat" (gtum mo) in view, meditation, and conduct represent progressive stages of attainment that practitioners should sequentially master through specific techniques.

WHY IT ARISES:
The structured presentation (movement, attainment, stability) with vivid metaphors (fire blazing, army conquering, treasure-house entering) suggests a curriculum of meditative development.

PRIMARY CONSEQUENCE:
Dzogchen is reduced to a graduated path of energetic cultivation. The practitioner pursues specific phenomenological states described in the metaphors.

SECONDARY CONSEQUENCES:
Fixation on achieving "heat" in body and speech. Measuring progress by intensity of experience. Comparison with other practitioners.

CASCADE EFFECTS:
Obsession with signs of attainment → manipulation of subtle body → physical/psychological imbalance → spiritual materialism → jealousy and competition.

[3930-3931]
[EPISTEMIC ERROR] [VIEW COLLAPSE]

MISREADING:
"The heat of view is the connection between saṃsāra and nirvāṇa" means the view is a special understanding or realization that unifies these two realms into one.

WHY IT ARISES:
"Connection" suggests bridging two separate things. The term "heat" implies energetic activation. The simile of thief recognition suggests a cognitive achievement.

PRIMARY CONSEQUENCE:
View becomes an intellectual understanding or meditative experience rather than the natural self-clarity of mind. The practitioner "holds" a view.

SECONDARY CONSEQUENCES:
Attachment to view as possession. Debates about correct view. Spiritual pride in "having" the view.

CASCADE EFFECTS:
View as ideology → sectarianism → philosophical fixation → loss of direct recognition.

[3941-3945]
[ETERNALISTIC ERROR] [REIFICATION ERROR]

MISREADING:
The similes of garuḍa flying, Brahmā's absorption, and royal empowerment describe actual attainments of power and status that the accomplished Dzogchen practitioner achieves.

WHY IT ARISES:
The imagery suggests majesty, freedom, and sovereignty. Terms like "strength of awareness" and "three bodies" imply acquisition of powerful qualities.

PRIMARY CONSEQUENCE:
Spiritual materialism focused on attaining powers and status. The path becomes accumulation of attainments rather than recognition of nature.

SECONDARY CONSEQUENCES:
Seeking signs of accomplishment. Display of spiritual attainment. Competition for recognition.

CASCADE EFFECTS:
Pride in attainments → manipulation of disciples → abuse of power → corruption of lineage.

[3946-3949]
[ONTOLOGICAL ERROR] [PRACTICE ERROR]

MISREADING:
"Without path of effort" and "mind does not conceptualize" means the practitioner should abandon all method and simply rest without engaging phenomena or thought.

WHY IT ARISES:
The explicit negation of paths and effort suggests doing nothing. The statement that mind doesn't conceptualize appears to instruct non-conceptuality.

PRIMARY CONSEQUENCE:
Mistaking cessation of thought for liberation. The practitioner withdraws from engagement, confusing passivity for naturalness.

SECONDARY CONSEQUENCES:
Mental dullness mistaken for meditation. Avoidance of challenging situations. Quietist withdrawal.

CASCADE EFFECTS:
Suppression of thought → dullness → depression → abandonment of practice.

[3933-3940]
[SCHOLARLY COLLAPSE ERROR] [PEDAGOGICAL ERROR]

MISREADING:
The similes (fire blazing, wild boar tamed, treasure-house) should be decoded and analyzed to extract specific technical instructions for practice.

WHY IT ARISES:
The vivid, specific imagery invites interpretation. Traditional commentaries often decode similes systematically.

PRIMARY CONSEQUENCE:
Endless hermeneutic speculation. The text becomes a puzzle to solve rather than pith instructions for recognition.

SECONDARY CONSEQUENCES:
Preference for scholastic analysis over direct instruction. Attachment to one's interpretative framework.

CASCADE EFFECTS:
Intellectual mastery without realization → pride in scholarship → dismissal of simple practitioners → loss of oral lineage connection.
