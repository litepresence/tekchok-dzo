ERROR TYPE 1: [TRANSCENDENTAL AGGREGATE REIFICATION] [ONTOLOGICAL ERROR]
================================================================================

MISREADING:
The "aggregates without conceptual thought" (rtog pa med pa'i phung po)—including the samadhi aggregate, wisdom aggregate, discernment aggregate, morality aggregate, and liberation aggregate—are understood as actual existent qualities that the practitioner must acquire, possess, and accumulate through effortful practice.

WHY IT ARISES:
Positive terminology triggers acquisitive mind. The language of "wisdom aggregate" (ye shes kyi phung po) and "morality aggregate" (tshul khrims kyi phung po) suggests spiritual attainments to be gained. The practitioner conflates conventional descriptions of enlightened qualities with substantial entities to be possessed.

PRIMARY CONSEQUENCE:
Enlightenment becomes a state of having accumulated sufficient quantities of these "transcendent aggregates." The practitioner pursues spiritual acquisitions—more samadhi, more wisdom, better morality—treating the path as a shopping expedition for enlightened qualities rather than recognition of innate perfection.

SECONDARY CONSEQUENCES:
Comparison mind develops. The practitioner evaluates themselves and others based on apparent possession of these aggregates. Spiritual materialism takes hold—"I have achieved the samadhi aggregate" becomes a claim of status. The fundamental equality of all beings in buddha-nature is obscured by hierarchical assessments of aggregate-possession.

CASCADE EFFECTS:
This leads to [VEHICLE HIERARCHY ELITISM] where the practitioner believes possessing these transcendent aggregates makes them superior to ordinary beings. It generates [PROGRESSION MYTH] where enlightenment is conceived as the gradual accumulation of more and better aggregates over time.

ERROR TYPE 2: [LEARNING PATH FIXATION] [PEDAGOGICAL ERROR]
================================================================================

MISREADING:
The distinction between "learning path" (slob lam) qualities to be abandoned and "beyond learning" (mi slob pa'i dus) qualities is interpreted as establishing a rigid developmental model where certain qualities are exclusively associated with specific stages, creating stage-fixation.

WHY IT ARISES:
Developmental psychology and educational progression models condition the mind to expect hierarchical stages. The text's presentation appears to offer a clear map from learner to beyond-learner. The practitioner wants to know "which stage am I at" and "what comes next."

PRIMARY CONSEQUENCE:
The practitioner becomes obsessed with stage-identification. "I am on the learning path" becomes a self-limiting identity. The spontaneous nature of recognition is forced into a developmental framework. The practitioner defers full engagement until reaching the "beyond learning" stage.

SECONDARY CONSEQUENCES:
Discrimination between practitioners develops. Those "on the path" are seen as inferior to those "beyond learning." The practitioner may fake or claim advanced attainments to escape the "learner" stigma. Authentic humility is replaced by stage-anxiety.

CASCADE EFFECTS:
This feeds [PREPARATION PARALYSIS] where the practitioner believes they must complete all learning-path requirements before genuine practice. It produces [DIRECT RECOGNITION DELAY] where recognition is perpetually deferred until the "appropriate stage" is reached.

ERROR TYPE 3: [LIBERATION TERMINOLOGY OBSESSION] [VIEW COLLAPSE]
================================================================================

MISREADING:
The statement that "at the time of liberation, the name 'aggregates' does not even remain" (grol ba'i tshe phung po zhes pa'i ming du'ang mi gnas) is interpreted literally as meaning that liberation requires the complete destruction or transcendence of all aggregates, as if buddhahood is a state of total absence.

WHY IT ARISES:
Nihilistic tendencies interpret "not remaining" as annihilation. The practitioner conflates "empty of inherent existence" with "total non-existence." Ascetic traditions worldwide valorize transcendence of body and mind as the ultimate goal.

PRIMARY CONSEQUENCE:
Liberation becomes a state of absence—no aggregates, no experience, no cognition. The practitioner pursues blankness or void as the goal. The luminous, cognizant nature of buddhahood is lost in favor of a nihilistic "freedom from all."

SECONDARY CONSEQUENCES:
Fear of ordinary experience develops. The practitioner views daily life as "still having aggregates" and therefore inferior. Meditation becomes an attempt to enter a state where nothing remains. The natural radiance of awareness is suppressed in favor of emptiness-misunderstood-as-void.

CASCADE EFFECTS:
This produces [NIHILISTIC ERROR] where the practitioner denies the validity of all conventional experience. It generates [SUTRA DISMISSAL] when the practitioner concludes that sutra teachings about aggregates are "merely provisional" obstacles to be transcended.

ERROR TYPE 4: [SCRIPTURAL CITATION AUTHORITY] [EPISTEMIC ERROR]
================================================================================

MISREADING:
The citation from the Sun and Moon Sutra (nyi zla kha yor) is taken as establishing absolute authority for the two-fold aggregate classification. The practitioner believes that because this division appears in scripture, it represents an ontologically real distinction rather than a pedagogical device.

WHY IT ARISES:
Scriptural literalism and fundamentalist tendencies seek certainty in textual authority. The practitioner assumes that scriptural citations prove metaphysical truths. Western religious traditions often treat sacred texts as repositories of factual information rather than skillful means.

PRIMARY CONSEQUENCE:
The citation becomes a weapon for doctrinal fundamentalism. "The Buddha said so" ends inquiry rather than opening it. The living experience of recognition is subordinated to textual authority. The practitioner becomes a "bibliolater"—worshipping the book rather than realizing the meaning.

SECONDARY CONSEQUENCES:
Sectarianism develops. The practitioner uses scriptural citations to prove the superiority of their understanding. Debate replaces practice. The transformative intent of the teaching is lost in scholastic wrangling over scriptural interpretation.

CASCADE EFFECTS:
This feeds [VEHICLE HIERARCHY ELITISM] where different scriptural citations are weaponized to prove one vehicle superior to another. It produces [CAUSAL/RESULT CONFUSION] where scriptural study is believed to causally produce realization.

ERROR TYPE 5: [CONCEPTUAL/ NON-CONCEPTUAL DUALISM] [PRACTICE ERROR]
================================================================================

MISREADING:
The distinction between "aggregates tainted by conceptuality" (rtog pas sbags pa'i phung po) and "aggregates free from conceptuality" (rtog pa rnams rang bzhin gyis med pa'i phung po) is interpreted as a rigid dualism where the goal is to completely eliminate all conceptual aggregates and exist solely in non-conceptual aggregates.

WHY IT ARISES:
Dualistic thinking habitually creates exclusive categories. The practitioner assumes "non-conceptual = good" and "conceptual = bad." Meditation traditions often emphasize stopping thought as the primary method, reinforcing this binary.

PRIMARY CONSEQUENCE:
The practitioner develops an aggressive stance toward conceptual mind. All thought is treated as obstacle. The discriminating wisdom (shes rab) that distinguishes without grasping is lost in favor of blank non-conceptuality. The practitioner becomes "zoned out" rather than luminously aware.

SECONDARY CONSEQUENCES:
Inability to function in daily life. The practitioner cannot think clearly when needed. Important distinctions are missed because "thinking is bad." The integration of wisdom and skillful means collapses into one-sided emphasis on emptiness.

CASCADE EFFECTS:
This produces [TANTRA SUBSTITUTION] where the practitioner seeks "faster" methods that promise to bypass conceptual mind entirely. It generates [PROGRESSION MYTH] where the practitioner believes they are "progressing" from conceptual to non-conceptual, not recognizing that both are equally empty.
