ERROR TYPE 1: [NIHILISM CONFIRMATION BIAS] [ONTOLOGICAL ERROR]
================================================================================

MISREADING:
The presentation of Lokayata/Charvaka nihilist views denying past and future lives, karma, causality, and liberation is interpreted as demonstrating the opposite error from eternalism, rather than recognizing that both extremes arise from the same fundamental error—grasping to concepts of existence and non-existence.

WHY IT ARISES:
Dualistic thinking positions nihilism as opposite of eternalism. The practitioner assumes if eternalism is wrong, nihilism must be equally wrong in the opposite direction. The refutation of extremes invites taking a middle position between them.

PRIMARY CONSEQUENCE:
The practitioner believes Buddhism avoids both "existence" and "non-existence" to arrive at a third position. The deeper teaching—that all positions including the middle are empty—is missed. The practitioner is stuck in subtle metaphysics.

SECONDARY CONSEQUENCES:
Conceptual understanding replaces direct recognition. The practitioner can explain why both eternalism and nihilism are wrong without recognizing mind nature. Scholarly mastery of refutations masks lack of realization.

CASCADE EFFECTS:
This produces [VIEW COLLAPSE] where intellectual understanding of emptiness replaces direct recognition. It generates [PEDAGOGICAL ERROR] where the practitioner teaches others to "avoid extremes" without embodying that avoidance.

ERROR TYPE 2: [MATERIALIST REDUCTION DISMISSAL] [EPISTEMIC ERROR]
================================================================================

MISREADING:
The Lokayata view that mind emerges from the four elements is refuted as wrong without recognizing that Buddhist views of mind-dependent origination are equally conventional and equally unable to capture the nature of mind.

WHY IT ARISES:
The practitioner wants to feel superior to "materialists." Buddhist teachings about consciousness seem more sophisticated than element-based theories. Western science is often materialist, creating antagonism. The practitioner assumes Buddhist view is "right answer."

PRIMARY CONSEQUENCE:
The practitioner believes Buddhist consciousness theory is ultimately true. The emptiness of all theories of mind is missed. One conceptual framework replaces another without recognition of their equal conventionality.

SECONDARY CONSEQUENCES:
Spiritual pride develops. "Buddhism correctly understands consciousness" becomes identity-marker. The practitioner cannot engage materialist science with humility. Dialogue becomes debate.

CASCADE EFFECTS:
This feeds [VEHICLE HIERARCHY ELITISM] where Buddhist psychology is considered superior to Western psychology. It produces [SUTRA DISMISSAL] when practitioners dismiss scientific materialism without recognizing its conventional validity.

ERROR TYPE 3: [SPONTANEOUS GENERATION CONFUSION] [CAUSAL/RESULT CONFUSION]
================================================================================

MISREADING:
The Lokayata examples of spontaneous generation (insects from dust, plants from moisture) are interpreted as demonstrating the error of denying causality, without recognizing that Buddhist dependent origination is equally mysterious and equally unable to be fully conceptually grasped.

WHY IT ARISES:
The examples seem obviously wrong to educated practitioners. Science refutes spontaneous generation. The practitioner assumes Buddhist causality is "correct" causality. The refutation invites complacency.

PRIMARY CONSEQUENCE:
The practitioner believes they understand causality correctly while Lokayatas don't. The profound mystery of dependent origination is reduced to textbook formula. The recognition that causality is ultimately inexpressible is lost.

SECONDARY CONSEQUENCES:
Intellectual arrogance develops. "I know how causality works" replaces wonder. The practitioner becomes a Buddhist scientist, confident in their understanding. The miracle of experience arising is taken for granted.

CASCADE EFFECTS:
This produces [CAUSAL/RESULT CONFUSION] where understanding dependent origination conceptually is believed to be sufficient. It generates [PROGRESSION MYTH] where the practitioner believes they are "learning correct causality" on the path.

ERROR TYPE 4: [DEATH AS ANNIHILATION COMFORT] [VIEW COLLAPSE]
================================================================================

MISREADING:
The Lokayata view that death is final annihilation with no future result is refuted as wrong, but the practitioner fails to recognize that Buddhist views of continuation are equally unable to capture the nature of death and that both views miss the empty nature of the one who dies.

WHY IT ARISES:
Fear of death drives desire for continuation. The practitioner wants to believe in rebirth. Nihilism seems depressing. Buddhist teachings about bardo and future lives offer comfort. The refutation of annihilation validates hope.

PRIMARY CONSEQUENCE:
The practitioner believes Buddhist continuation is "correct" while annihilation is "wrong." The recognition that both are conceptual imputations on the ungraspable is missed. Death becomes a philosophical problem to solve.

SECONDARY CONSEQUENCES:
The practitioner fears death less but still fears it. The recognition that there is no one to die is replaced by confidence in rebirth. Death remains an obstacle rather than teacher.

CASCADE EFFECTS:
This produces [CAUSAL/RESULT CONFUSION] where good karma is accumulated to ensure good rebirth. It generates [PROGRESSION MYTH] where enlightenment is deferred to future lives when conditions will be better.

ERROR TYPE 5: [WRONG VIEW SUPERIORITY COMPLEX] [PEDAGOGICAL ERROR]
================================================================================

MISREADING:
The detailed analysis of wrong views is interpreted as establishing a hierarchy from worst (nihilism) to best (Buddhism), encouraging the practitioner to look down on those who hold wrong views without recognizing that all views are equally empty and that the practitioner likely holds subtle versions of these "obvious" errors.

WHY IT ARISES:
Educational systems rank answers from wrong to right. The practitioner wants to be on the "right" side. Clear categorization invites hierarchical thinking. Religious identity requires distinction from "others."

PRIMARY CONSEQUENCE:
The practitioner develops spiritual arrogance. "At least I'm not a nihilist" becomes subtle pride. The recognition that one's own Buddhist views are equally conventional is blocked. Others are judged by their views.

SECONDARY CONSEQUENCES:
Compassion for those holding wrong views is replaced by pity or contempt. Dialogue with non-Buddhists becomes conversion attempt. The practitioner cannot learn from other traditions. Openness is lost.

CASCADE EFFECTS:
This feeds [VEHICLE HIERARCHY ELITISM] where Buddhist practitioners consider themselves superior to all others. It produces [SUTRA DISMISSAL] when practitioners arrogantly dismiss all "wrong views" without recognizing their own limitations.
