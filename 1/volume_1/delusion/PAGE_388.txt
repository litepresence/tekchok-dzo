[16331-16350]
[PRACTICE ERROR] [RECOGNITION AS EVENT]

MISREADING:
Recognition (ngo shes) is a particular experience or insight that occurs in time.

WHY IT ARISES:
"Moment of recognition" suggests temporal event. Spiritual autobiographies describe breakthroughs.

PRIMARY CONSEQUENCE:
Waiting for or chasing recognition experiences.

SECONDARY CONSEQUENCES:
Disappointment with ordinary awareness.

CASCADE EFFECTS:
[PSYCHOLOGIZATION ERROR] [MEDITATIONISM ERROR].

[16351-16370]
[EPISTEMIC ERROR] [FRUITION AS ATTAINMENT]

MISREADING:
The fruition ('bras bu) is a state to be achieved in the future through practice.

WHY IT ARISES:
Goal-oriented mentality. Progressive view of spiritual development.

PRIMARY CONSEQUENCE:
Deferring present satisfaction to future attainment.

SECONDARY CONSEQUENCES:
Accumulation of practices for distant goal.

CASCADE EFFECTS:
[SUTRIC PROVISIONAL VIEW] temporal displacement.

[16371-16390]
[ONTOLOGICAL ERROR] [GROUND AS FOUNDATION]

MISREADING:
The ground (gzhi) is a foundational basis upon which everything depends.

WHY IT ARISES:
"Ground" metaphor suggests support. Western metaphysics seeks foundations.

PRIMARY CONSEQUENCE:
Treating ground as metaphysical substrate.

SECONDARY CONSEQUENCES:
Searching for or trying to access the ground.

CASCADE EFFECTS:
[ETERNALISTIC ERROR] [REIFICATION ERROR].
