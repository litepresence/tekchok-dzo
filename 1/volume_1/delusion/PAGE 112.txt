[4122-4126]
[SCHOLARLY COLLAPSE ERROR] [PEDAGOGICAL ERROR]

MISREADING:
The catalog structure (4,000 distinctions, 500,000 summaries, 87 sharp nails) presents Dzogchen as an encyclopedic system where progress is measured by number of items cataloged.

WHY IT ARISES:
The large precise numbers invite quantification. The structure suggests comprehensive coverage.

PRIMARY CONSEQUENCE:
Dzogchen becomes a data collection project. The practitioner accumulates distinctions and summaries as knowledge.

SECONDARY CONSEQUENCES:
Catalog obsession. Database Buddhism. Spreadsheet spirituality.

CASCADE EFFECTS:
Data accumulation → information overload → inability to practice → confusion.

[4127]
[ONTOLOGICAL ERROR] [VIEW COLLAPSE]

MISREADING:
"Essential-point seeing" and "essential-point knowing's emanation three" establish essential-point (gnad) as something to be seen or known through a threefold emanation process.

WHY IT ARISES:
The term "essential-point" suggests something crucial. The emanation structure implies production.

PRIMARY CONSEQUENCE:
Essential-point is reified as an object of vision or knowledge. The practitioner seeks to see or know it.

SECONDARY CONSEQUENCES:
Gnad-fixation. Seeking special points. Missing that all is equally essential.

CASCADE EFFECTS:
Point-chasing → fragmentation → missing the whole → never satisfied.

[4129-4132]
[ETERNALISTIC ERROR] [REIFICATION ERROR]

MISREADING:
The hierarchy (tantra supreme, scripture secondary, instruction accessible) establishes a value-ranking of texts where tantras contain the highest truth.

WHY IT ARISES:
The implicit hierarchy suggests comparative value. The tantra emphasis implies esoteric superiority.

PRIMARY CONSEQUENCE:
Tantra fetishization. The practitioner seeks only tantric texts, dismissing others.

SECONDARY CONSEQUENCES:
Elitism about tantras. Rejection of sutras. Guru shopping for tantric empowerments.

CASCADE EFFECTS:
Tantra elitism → sectarianism → dismissal of gradual paths → premature advanced practice.
