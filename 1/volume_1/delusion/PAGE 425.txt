[CAUSAL ERROR] [GENEALOGICAL ERROR]

MISREADING:
Lines 17931-17944: The text describes a causal chain: "From non-awareness arises non-awareness's display; from that display arises mind; from mind's embellishment arises mentation..." This sequential presentation might be read as temporal genesis.

WHY IT ARISES:
The grammatical structure (X las byung = "arises from X") naturally suggests temporal succession. Without understanding dependent origination as non-temporal, readers impose sequential causality.

PRIMARY CONSEQUENCE:
Samsara appears as a real historical development rather than a timeless confusion. The co-emergent nature of ground and ignorance is missed.

SECONDARY CONSEQUENCES:
Practitioners may seek the "beginning" of ignorance or try to trace back through the causal chain, as if finding the first cause would enable liberation.

CASCADE EFFECTS:
Triggers [HISTORICAL ERROR] where timeless confusion is temporalized, and [SEARCH FOR ORIGIN ERROR] where practitioners seek the "first moment" of delusion.
