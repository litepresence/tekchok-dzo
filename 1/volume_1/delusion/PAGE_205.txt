[PRACTICE ERROR] [THIGLE MANIPULATION]

MISREADING:
The bindu/thigle practices are interpreted as physical manipulations of actual substances within the body, like moving fluids or energy through hydraulic mechanics.

WHY IT ARISES:
Physiological reductionism projects onto subtle body. The liquid-like descriptions suggest material substances. Desire for tangible, controllable practice supports hydraulic interpretation.

PRIMARY CONSEQUENCE:
The teaching that thigle is the luminous nature of mind, not a substance to move, is entirely lost. Practice becomes body-manipulation rather than recognition-cultivation.

SECONDARY CONSEQUENCES:
Practitioners force physical movements seeking "thigle flow," causing injury. The wisdom aspect of bliss is abandoned for sensation-seeking. Technique-accumulation replaces view-grounded practice.

CASCADE EFFECTS:
Triggers [EXPERIENCE SUBSTANTIALISM]. Subsequent pages on four joys will be approached as thigle-management techniques.
