[ONTOLOGICAL ERROR] [ACTIVITIES REIFICATION]

MISREADING:
The thirty-six or twelve activities describe actual things a Buddha does or has done.

WHY IT ARISES:
Enumeration suggests checklist of accomplishments. Active verbs suggest agency.

PRIMARY CONSEQUENCE:
Buddhahood understood as achievement of specific powers and actions.

SECONDARY CONSEQUENCES:
Seeking siddhis and powers as signs of realization.

CASCADE EFFECTS:
[MEDITATIONISM ERROR] and [ETERNALISTIC ERROR].
