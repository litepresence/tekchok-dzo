[398-410] [TANTRIC TRANSFORMIVE VIEW] [UPĀYA STATEMENT] Describes the manifestations of the Vajra holder in various locations with protective capabilities, demonstrating the upāya principle where enlightened activity manifests in forms appropriate to specific contexts and needs of beings.

[411-425] [SŪTRIC PROVISIONAL VIEW] [INSTRUCTIONAL PROVISIONALITY] Provides detailed enumerations of the twelve abodes and the Buddha manifestations appearing in each, presenting this structure as a pedagogical framework for understanding the variety of enlightened activities rather than as literal geographical specifications.

[426-435] [DZOGCHEN VIEW – RIGPA SIDE] [DECLARATIVE FINALITY] <RISK: HIERARCHICAL MISUNDERSTANDING> The enumeration of five complete-abidings with different Buddha manifestations could be misunderstood as hierarchical progression, whereas the view presents them as simultaneous expressions of the same primordial wisdom appearing according to different karmic dispositions.

[436-460] [TANTRIC TRANSFORMIVE VIEW] [INSTRUCTIONAL PROVISIONALITY] Details the specific teachings associated with each abode, showing how the transformative view adapts its methods to the capacities and circumstances of different beings, maintaining the principle that all teachings are skillful means calibrated to disciple's capacities.
