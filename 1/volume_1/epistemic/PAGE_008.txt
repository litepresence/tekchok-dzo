[219-228]
[DZOGCHEN VIEW – BINDU MANIFESTATION]
[PRIMORDIAL POINT DISPLAY]

<From "abiding bindu of light" (*gnas pa 'od kyi thig le*), sixty perfect abidings (*phun sum tshogs pa drug cur gnas*) emerge—hundreds of thousands of Buddha-bodies (*sangs rgyas kyi sku 'bum phrag*) self-appearing (*rang snang*). Five-family Buddhas (*rigs lnga'i sangs rgyas*) radiating immeasurable light-rays (*'od zer dpag tu med pa*) from tongue-faculty (*ljags kyi dbang po*) proclaimed as self-arisen six syllables' sound (*rang byung gi yi ge drug gi sgra*)—compassion (*thugs rje*) radiating without activity (*mdzad pa med pa*). This describes *thig le* as point-instant of recognition, not physical particle.>

[222-228]
[DZOGCHEN VIEW – FIVE FAMILY MAṆḌALA]
[WISDOM BUDDHA DISPLAY]

<Vairocana (*rnam par snang mdzad chen po*) with countless principal and retinue (*gtsu 'khor dang bcas pa*) gathered into Samantabhadra's enjoyment (*kun tu bzang po'i longs spyod*), blazing as Great Perfection body (*rdzogs pa chen po'i skur 'od 'bar*)—likewise Akṣobhya, Ratnasaṃbhava, Amitābha, Amoghasiddhi with individual maṇḍalas. Each "gathering into own enjoyment, blazing as Great Perfection body" indicates *ye shes lnga* (five wisdoms) as display-modes of single recognition.>

[229-237]
[DZOGCHEN VIEW – VAIRĀCANA EMERGENCE]
[TEACHER AS AWARENESS-KING]

<At that time in Light-House completely-pure palace (*'od kyi khang bu... gnas khang*), teacher Vairocana "Self-Knowing Awareness King" (*rang shes rig gi rgyal po*) becomes "lord of all maṇḍalas" (*dkyil 'khor thams cad kyi mangs bdag*)—teaching "unmoving, non-arising, non-abiding, non-taking, non-rejecting, non-moving" (*mi g.yo, mi 'byung, mi gnas, mi len, mi 'dor, mi g.yo*) as self-arisen (*rang byung*). The six negations describe *chos sku* (Dharmakāya) as transcendence of all categories—*mi g.yo* repeated for emphasis on ground-stability.>

[238-248]
[DZOGCHEN VIEW – TAMER OF UNTAMABLE]
[NON-DUAL COMPASSION]

<"Tamer of those not to be tamed" (*gdul bya ma yin 'dul byed*) and precious beautiful retinue (*rin po che mdzes pa'i 'khor*) emanating from and dissolving into body, speech, mind, qualities, activities (*sku gsung thugs yon tan 'phrin las*)—radiating and gathering (*'phro zhing 'dus*). "All appearances, upper and lower, ten directions' worlds, not being... self-arisen Buddha-field clearly became"—this is *chos dbyings* as pure appearance (*rnam dag snang ba*), Vajradhara qualities-complete body (*rdo rje 'chang yon tan rdzogs pa'i sku*).>
