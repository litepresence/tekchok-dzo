[249-252]
[DZOGCHEN VIEW – SAṂBHOGAKĀYA BASIS]
[MERIT AS PURIFICATION]

<From "offering vajra as basis for generating bodhicitta" (*sems bskyed pa'i rten du rdo rje phul ba*), Saṃbhogakāya arises—merit purified (*bsod nams rnam par dag pas*) so body appears without nature (*sku snang la rang bzhin med pa*), accumulation purified (*bsags pa rnam par dag pas*), great mind wisdom (*thugs kyi shes rab chen po*) knowing all aspects (*rnam pa thams cad mkhyen pa*). This describes *rnam par dag pa* (complete purification) as recognition, not moral merit. The "offering" is recognition-act, not transactional exchange.>

[253-268]
[DZOGCHEN VIEW – THIRTY-SIX ACTIVITIES]
[CLASSIFICATION OF DISPLAY]

<Dharmakāya time (*chos sku'i dus*) with pure object-thought Dharmadhātu (*chos nyid 'od gsal gyi gzhi*), Samantabhadra completing all natural activities (*rang bzhin gyi mdzad pa ma lus pa rdzogs par mdzad*)—countless Buddhas on single atom's peak with teaching actual nature Dzogchen (*ston pa dngos rang bzhin rdzogs pa chen po*), uncompounded (*'dus ma byas pa*) and pure view of Dharmatā (*chos nyid la mthong ba rnam par dag pa*) dwelling in five perfections (*phun sum tshogs pa lnga*). The thirty-six activities classified into outer/inner/secret sets of four for body/speech/mind describe enlightened activity (*'phrin las*) as display-structure, not behavioral checklist.>

[DZOGCHEN VIEW – ACTIVITY ATTRIBUTES]
[DESCRIPTIVE RATHER THAN PRESCRIPTIVE]

<Technical terms describing activities: *lhang nge ba* (clear-open), *lhan ne ba* (even/smooth), *lhag ge ba* (loose/free), *thal le ba* (direct/piercing-through); for speech: *brjod du med pa* (inexpressible), *bstan du med pa* (unable to be shown), *bltar med pa* (unseeable), *cung zad med pa* (without slight/trace); for mind: stainless samādhi (*dri ma med pa'i ting nge 'dzin*), lion-display samādhi (*seng ge rnam par bsgyings pa*), hero-going samādhi (*dpa' bar 'gro ba*), wish-fulfilling jewel pinnacle (*yid bzhin gyi nor bu'i tog*)—these are recognition-descriptors, not meditative states to achieve.>
