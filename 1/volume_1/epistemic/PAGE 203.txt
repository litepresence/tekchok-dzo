- Risk explanation: [RISK: LITERALISM] — Treating nose measurements as literal anatomical requirements rather than energetic indicators. [RISK: REIFICATION] — Believing that blood mixing genuinely reveals compatibility rather than serving as conventional indicator (*tha snyad tsam*).>

- Hermeneutical precision: Substance preparation for *byang sems* (bodhicitta/semen) retention and expansion using animal organs (ram intestine, bird claw) and botanicals (dates, sugar, *pashanabheda*, myrrh, colocynth). These substances represent elemental *'byung ba* (earth, water, fire, wind) convergence to support *rlung* (energy) stability. The "five roots" (*rtsa ba lnga*) likely refer to *sa rtsa* (earth channels) or botanical roots—symbolizing grounded stability.
- Temporal warnings: The consumption of these substances is timed with practice phases, not ordinary dietary supplementation.
- Ontological warnings: The substances have no inherent power—they function as *rten* (supports) for *rlung* stabilization. To believe they causally produce realization is *rgyu 'bras* (cause-result) thinking appropriate to lower vehicles, not Dzogchen's *lhun grub* (spontaneous perfection).
- Pedagogical purpose: The substance preparation teaches careful attention to energetic conditions. However, the ultimate "substance" is *rig pa* (awareness) itself—no external substance required for recognition of *ka dag*.
- Risk explanation: [RISK: SUBSTANTIALISM] — Believing that physical substances causally produce spiritual realization. [RISK: HEDONISM] — Confusing bliss (*bde ba*) techniques with sensual indulgence. The bliss here is *ye shes kyi bde ba* (wisdom bliss), not ordinary pleasure—requires recognition of *stong pa* (emptiness) as essence.>
