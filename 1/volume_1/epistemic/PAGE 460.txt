[DZOGCHEN VIEW – RIGPA SIDE]
[DECLARATIVE FINALITY]
[RISK: REIFICATION]

<The ground (*gzhi*) as primordial purity (*ka dag*): not the basis of delusion but the basis of liberation. Not a cause that produces an effect, but the nature that is recognized. The ground is not something to find but to recognize as always present.>

[20013-20037]
[DZOGCHEN VIEW – RIGPA SIDE]
[DECLARATIVE FINALITY]
[RISK: TEMPORALIZATION]

<The path (*lam*) as spontaneous perfection (*lhun grub*): not something to travel but something to allow. Recognition deepens through familiarity, not through effort. The path is not from here to there but from not-recognizing to recognizing the same ground.>

[20038-20062]
[DZOGCHEN VIEW – RIGPA SIDE]
[DECLARATIVE FINALITY]
[RISK: REIFICATION]

<The fruition (*'bras bu*) as direct crossing (*thod rgal*): not a destination arrived at but full manifestation of what was always present. Fruition is not different from ground, only fully recognized. This is the meaning of "ground and fruition are not two.">
