[DZOGCHEN VIEW – RIGPA SIDE]
[DECLARATIVE FINALITY]
[RISK: TEMPORALIZATION]

<The three kāyas are simultaneous, not sequential. Ground: all three present. Path: all three recognized. Fruition: all three stable. There is no time when dharmakāya exists but saṃbhogakāya does not—just as there is no time when the sun exists but its light does not.>

[DZOGCHEN VIEW – RIGPA SIDE]
[DECLARATIVE FINALITY]
[RISK: REIFICATION]

<Integration with five wisdoms: dharmadhātu wisdom as dharmakāya, mirror-like as saṃbhogakāya's clarity, the other three as nirmāṇakāya's activity. Or: all five wisdoms present in each kāya, each kāya containing all five. The interpenetration is total.>

<From *Thal 'gyur*: Rigpa wisdom path itself, on one's own channel wheel, kāya-speech self-perfect abiding. The three kāyas are not somewhere else—they are the nature of one's own rigpa, the structure of awareness itself.>
