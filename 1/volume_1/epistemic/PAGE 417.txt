[DZOGCHEN VIEW – RIGPA SIDE]
[DECLARATIVE FINALITY]
[RISK: REIFICATION]

<From *Thal 'gyur*: Wisdom is not conceptual thought. Essence self-luminous, core meaning. Divisions: three. Definition: abiding from beginning's meaning. Thus knowing is wisdom. Wisdom is not a special state but the natural knowing of awareness when not covered by mind.>

[INSTRUCTIONAL PROVISIONALITY]
[RISK: REIFICATION]

<First, mind explained: characterized by ignorance, equal abiding. From *Mu tig phreng ba*: Mind is accumulation-arising, also contaminated with defilement. Alaya-holding mind and others become defilement's number. This is mind's nature—grasping, accumulating, obscuring.>

[RISK: VIEW COLLAPSE]

<Mind as accumulation—gathering karma and habitual tendencies, performing samsara's work. Wisdom as natural knowing—self-aware, self-luminous, free from accumulation. The difference is like a cloudy sky versus clear sky—the sky itself unchanged, only the obscuration differs.>
