[2316-2326] The text identifies those who assert permanence regarding conduct as believing cause and result are impermanent while conduct is permanent for liberation. They view external houses and homes as permanent, all appearances as permanent. This is viewing what lacks permanent meaning as nihilistic, yet conducting themselves in eternalist philosophical positions. They perform fierce actions emphasizing black mantras, speak in black unbroken continuity, desiring results through strong attachment to this. The view register here reveals the complexity of actual practitioner positions that do not fit neat philosophical categories. Preservation requires maintaining the distinction between asserted view and actual conduct.

[2327-2340] Some emphasize divination and prophecy, looking only to this world's pleasure, saying there is nothing beyond. Some emphasize subtle miraculous powers, believing this world has repelled armies through miracles, and in the future through miracles being suppressed, they will be born in fortune or power's retinue. This view strongly takes up debate, putting forward their own truth while refuting others, desiring meaning. Some also desire meaning from mistaken austerity. Attachment to this world is called worldly Lokayata, engaging in this world's activities. Some, understanding their own truth, also kill others to elevate themselves.

[2341-2349] Some seek meaning through manner or custom. Some make leaf-huts from hair to seek meaning. Some wrap themselves in single cloth saying inner heat has arisen. Some holding khatvanga and damaru as implements claim to be emanation bodies. Some say whatever virtue you do benefits me, desiring meaning from this. Some defeat others' reasoning with their own reasoning, claiming this is my action, desiring meaning from this. Some say cause is cut off like space, completely cut off. Some say this complete cut-off makes their own body empty. Those who say both cause and result are cut off...
