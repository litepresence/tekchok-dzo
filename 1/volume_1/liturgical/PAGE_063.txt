[2213] Permanence and impermanence.
[2214] The meaning of the view of impermanence is as follows:
[2215] The great tirthika emptiness of sudden cutoff.
[2216] Like the emptiness of space in continuous cutoff.
[2217] Viewing the nature of cutoff as nonexistent.
[2218] The cutoff of results as emptiness.
[2219] The permanence of the nonexistent emptiness.
[2220] The tenets of the permanent vehicle.
[2221] The tenets of eternal permanence.
[2222] As follows regarding permanent things:
[2223] From one's own citta mandala,
[2224] Not arising from father causes,
[2225] Not depending on mother conditions,
[2226] As large as beans,
[2227] As small as mustard seeds,
[2228] As tiny as hair tips,
[2229] Oily, smooth, white, and clear.
[2230] Thus, that nature of causes,
[2231] Exists permanently without change from the beginning.
[2232] This and habitual tendencies, aggregates,
[2233] Abiding inseparably as one,
[2234] Is held as the tenet of permanent causes.
[2235] As follows regarding permanent results:
[2236] The great lord of fortune itself,
[2237] Through virtues of reliance and worship,
[2238] Although abandoning this first body,
[2239] Produces births on one's own ground.
[2240] By the great lord of fortune,
[2241] Results are held as permanent.
[2242] The two natures of permanence and impermanence,
[2243] Do not accord with their own meanings.
