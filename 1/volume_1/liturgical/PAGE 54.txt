[1740] The faculties are two:
[1741] The faculty arising from the portion of form, and
[1742] The faculty arising from the portion of wisdom.
[1743] The faculty arising from the portion of form is six:
[1744] The eye faculty, holding the coarse earth portion.
[1745] The ear faculty, holding the moving wind portion.
[1746] The nose faculty, holding the gathering water portion.
[1747] The tongue faculty, holding the ripening fire portion.
[1748] The mind faculty, holding the pervading space portion.
[1749] The body faculty, holding the gathering of various portions.
[1750] The faculty arising from the portion of wisdom is six:
[1751] The faculty of faith, which afflicts the mind completely.
[1752] The faculty of effort, which sharpens the mind.
[1753] The faculty of mindfulness, which holds word and meaning without interruption.
[1754] The faculty of concentration, which manifests the emptiness of dharmata and produces engagement.
[1755] The faculty of wisdom, which engages the meaning of view.
[1756] The faculty of mental peace, which produces the non-conceptual meaning. Thus it is taught.
[1757] Fifth: The aspects of objects and possessors of objects.
[1758] The aspects arise from grasping and the grasper.
[1759] If divided: The portion of phenomenon appearing as the grasped object entity, and the portion of dharmata, the emptiness of that nature.
[1760] The cognition that is object and not object-possessor.
[1761] That, although appearing as object, is free from all extreme grasping.
[1762] The supreme cognition beyond expression that does not appear as object.
[1763] From the previous:
[1764] Objects are two:
[1765] The object of entities, and
[1766] The object of emptiness.
[1767] The object of entities is:
[1768] Form, sound, smell, taste, dharma,
[1769] The appearance of touch, the appearance of the four great elements,
[1770] And the various appearances of entering conditions.
[1771] The object of emptiness is:
[1772] All emptiness, free from all cognition of extreme grasping.
