[15408-15444] [STRUCTURE] Three Kāyas Fruition
The text presents the three kāyas as the complete fruition. The structure establishes: (1) dharmakāya as emptiness and clarity, (2) saṃbhogakāya as the five Buddha families, and (3) nirmāṇakāya as the various forms benefiting beings.

[15408-15444] [PHILOLOGY] Three Kāyas Terminology
The term chos sku (dharmakāya) identifies the truth body. The compound longs sku (saṃbhogakāya) establishes the enjoyment body. The phrase sprul sku (nirmāṇakāya) identifies the emanation body.

[15408-15444] [CONTEXT] Trikāya Doctrine
The text presents the classical Buddhist trikāya doctrine in Dzogchen context. The three bodies represent three aspects of enlightened manifestation. This reflects the complete Buddhist understanding of enlightenment as multidimensional.

[15408-15444] [CONCEPT] Kāyas as Innate Qualities
The text conceptualizes the three kāyas as innate qualities of awareness, not achievements. Dharmakāya is awareness's emptiness; saṃbhogakāya is its clarity; nirmāṇakāya is its compassion. These are always present; practice reveals what is already so.
