[4319-4327] [STRUCTURE] Technical Tantra Definitions

**Analysis:** Lines 4319-4327 establish the definition of "tantra" (*rgyud*) through three aspects: indicated meaning, indicator, and continuum.

**Verse Definition [4319-4326]:**

| Line | Tibetan | Translation | Function |
|------|---------|-------------|----------|
| 4319 | *lus ngag dal bas sdug bsngal dbral* | Body speech relaxed separates from suffering | Method |
| 4320 | *rig pa dal bas sku lnga shar* | Awareness relaxed, five kāyas arise | Result |
| 4321 | *rig pa shar bas 'byung rkyen 'gags* | Awareness arises, conditions cease | Causality |
| 4322 | *dbyings rig shar bas yul sems 'gags* | Expanse-awareness arises, object-mind cease | Non-duality |
| 4323 | *de ltar mtshon byed thabs kyi rgyud* | Thus indicator-method tantra | Definition |
| 4324 | *rang gi rgyud la ma bskyed rdzogs* | In own continuum, unproduced perfect | Nature |
| 4325 | *mtshon par byed pas thabs yin te* | Because indicating, it is method | Etymology |
| 4326 | *rgyun du gnas pas rgyud ces bya* | Abiding as continuum, called tantra | Continuity |

**Threefold Definition:**

1. **Indicated Meaning** (*mtshon bya*): Liberation from suffering, five kāyas
2. **Indicator** (*mtshon byed*): The tantra text itself as method (*thabs*)
3. **Continuum** (*rgyud*): Abiding continuity (*rgyun du gnas pa*) in practitioner

[4328-4346] [PHILOLOGY] Nature Tantra Etymology

**Analysis:** Lines 4328-4346 present the etymological analysis of Nature Tantra (*rang bzhin gyi rgyud*).

**Verse Quotation [4331-4332]:**
*gzhi dang lam dang 'bras bu yis / gzung bar nges pas rang bzhin rgyud* — "Ground, path, and result — definite as held, nature tantra"

**Essence [4334-4336]:**
- *ngo bo ni stong gsal rig pa'i ngo bo* — Essence: empty-clear awareness essence
- *gzhi lam 'bras bu bsdus pa* — Ground-path-result gathered
- Pervading all (*kun la khyab pa*), Buddha-accomplishing nature (*sangs rgyas bsgrub pa'i rang bzhin*)

**Etymology:**
*rang bzhin gyi rgyud* — "Nature tantra" because it pervades all and is the nature of Buddha-accomplishment.

**Three Divisions [4337-4340]:**

| # | Division | Tibetan | Domain |
|---|----------|---------|--------|
| 1 | Ground | *gzhi rang bzhin gyi rgyud* | Primordial basis |
| 2 | Essence | *snying po rang bzhin gyi rgyud* | Core realization |
| 3 | Essence | *bcud rang bzhin gyi rgyud* | Distilled result |

**Ground Nature Tantra [4341-4346]:**

| Aspect | Tibetan | Translation | Characteristic |
|--------|---------|-------------|----------------|
| Essence | *ngo bo* | Primordially pure | No ignorance name |
| Nature | *rang bzhin* | Spontaneously accomplished | Awareness clear as light |
| Compassion | *thugs rje* | Pervading all | Arising-source unceasing |

[4347-4357] [CONTEXT] Essence and Essence-Nature Tantras

**Analysis:** Lines 4347-4357 present the Essence (*snying po*) and Essence-Nature (*bcud*) tantras.

**Essence Nature Tantra [4347-4349]:**
- *ye shes sum brtsegs snying gar snying po'i tshul du byung ba* — Wisdom three-tiered, heart-essence manner arising
- Ground nature tantra's potency (*rtsal*) arises as four lamps (*sgron ma bzhi*) from the basis-appearing eye (*gzhi snang mig*)
- Path four appearances (*lam snang ba bzhi*) actually seen

**Four Lamps (*sgron ma bzhi*):**
1. Far-reaching water lamp (*rgyang zhags chu'i sgron ma*)
2. Existence lamp (*sgron ma srid pa*)
3. Diurnal-nighttime lamp (*nyin mtshan sgron ma*)
4. Naturally-pure lamp (*rang dag sgron ma*)

**Essence-Nature Tantra [4350-4357]:**

[4358-4387] [CONCEPT] Ground-Path-Result Integration

**Analysis:** Lines 4358-4387 elaborate the three clarities and other triadic classifications that complete the Nature Tantra framework.

**Three Clarities (*gsal ba gsum*) [4358-4366, 4381-4387]:**

| # | Clarity | Tibetan | Description |
|---|---------|---------|-------------|
| 1 | External | *phyi yul snang ba'i dus na* | Object appearing time |
| 2 | Awareness | *rig pa las skur gsal* | Awareness clear as body |
| 3 | Expanse | *dbyings las kha dog tu gsal* | Expanse clear as color |

**Three Gdings (*gdings gsum*) [4367-4370]:**
- View confidence (*lta ba'i gding*)
- Object confidence (*yul gyi gding*)
- Delusion-ceased certainty confidence (*'khrul pa 'gags pa thag chod pa'i gding*)

**Three Non-Reversals (*mi ldog pa gsum*) [4371-4376]:**
1. Samsara primordially Buddha — awareness no cause
2. Awareness body appears, delusion-pure appears — appearances non-reversing
3. Dharmatā appears variously — obtained realization confidence

**Liberation Dynamics [4381-4387]:**

| Aspect | Mechanism | Result |
|--------|-----------|--------|
| Delusion appearance | Self-pure (*rang dag pa*) | Samsara cause non-existent |
| Purity appearance | Self-arisen (*rang shar ba*) | Conditions self-cease |
| Bardo appearance | Dharmatā appearance | Samsara result self-dissolved |

**Philosophical Integration:**

The Nature Tantra establishes that ground (*gzhi*), path (*lam*), and result (*'bras bu*) are not sequential but three aspects of the same nature (*rang bzhin*):
- **Ground** = primordial purity (*ka dag*)
- **Path** = four appearances (*snang ba bzhi*)
- **Result** = three clarities (*dangs ma gsum*)

This represents the characteristic Dzogchen view of "primordial enlightenment" (*ye nas sangs rgyas*) where the result is already present in the ground.
