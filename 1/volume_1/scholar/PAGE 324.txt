## PAGE 324 [13332-13356]

### [STRUCTURE]
The passage presents **channel wheel elaboration** and the **twelve sense bases** formation. Lines 13332-13356 establish:

1. **Fourth day development** (13332-13335): Eight channels generating twelve sense bases
2. **Channel wheel architecture** (13335-13339): Ro 'dus 'khor lo (taste-gathering wheel) as navel chakra
3. **Thirty-two to forty-six day development** (13340-13356): Elemental stabilization and crown wheel (rtse mo) formation

The structure demonstrates **systematic physiological cosmology**—the body as mandala.

### [PHILOLOGY]
**Technical terminology:**

- **རོ་རྣམས་འདུས་པའི་འཁོར་ལོ།** (13334): "Wheel of gathered tastes"—technical term for **navel chakra** (maṇipūra). Ro (taste) indicates the **digestive/gustatory function**; 'dus pa (gathered) marks the **centralizing** nature of this chakra.

- **སྐར་མའི་འོད་འདྲ་བ་འཚེར་བའི་གདངས།** (13334): "Resonance radiating like starlight"—gdangs (resonance/tone) establishes the **sonic dimension** of channel wheels. The compound suggests **luminous vibration**.

- **འདུས་པ་ནི་རོ་དྲུག་འདུས་པ་སྟེ།** (13377): "Gathering is the gathering of six tastes"—defines the navel wheel through **six taste-categories** (ro drug), linking Ayurvedic physiology to tantric anatomy.

- **འགྲོ་དྲུག་གི་ཆ་མི་ལ་ཚང་བའི་གནད་དོ།** (13379): "The crucial point where the six realm-elements are complete in humans"—gnad (crucial point/vital point) identifies this as **essential instruction**.

**Particle constructions:**
The **purposive** དུ། appears at 13332 ('phel ba yan lag gi rtsa bzhi ste brgyad du nges la), establishing **teleological development**—channels develop "toward" specific numbers. The **locative-inclusive** ན།...སུ། structures mark **spatial containment**.

### [CONTEXT]
**Dzogchen physiology:**
The passage presents **Dzogchen** integration of **Guhyagarbha** tantra embryology. The twelve sense bases (skye mched bcu gnyis) developing from channels reflects the **interdependence** of physical and mental in Buddhist phenomenology.

**Yogic anatomy:**
The **four wheels** ('khor lo bzhi) correspond to:
1. Navel (ro 'dus): Taste/gathering
2. Heart (dran pa 'dus): Memory/consciousness
3. Throat (ro myong): Taste-experience/speech
4. Crown (rtse mo rnam bkod): Apex/complete manifestation

This structure parallels **Kālacakra** and **Hevajra** systems.

**Numerical symbolism:**
The numbers (32, 36, 60, 96) reflect **Vedic-Tantric numerology**:
- 32 = completion of major channels
- 36 = 12 sense bases × 3 dimensions
- 60 = lunar-solar correspondence
- 96 = four wheels × 24 (solar hours)

### [CONCEPT]
**The six tastes and six realms:**
Line 13379 establishes a **microcosm-macrocosm correspondence**: the six tastes in human digestion mirror the six realms of existence. This reflects the **Hindu-Buddhist** principle that **the body contains the universe**.

**Taste-yoga and vital extraction:**
Lines 13377-13384 present **rasāyana** (bcud len) theory: the six tastes gathered (ro drug 'dus pa) through "taste-yoga" (zas kyi rnal 'byor) can be refined over three years (lo gsum) to achieve vital extraction. This is the **tantric alchemical** dimension of physiology.

**The four behaviors and four wheels:**
The text specifies (13339-13340) that the four wheels support four behaviors ('gro nyal 'dug sdod spyod lam rnam bzhi): going, lying, sitting, staying. This establishes **postural practice** (gnas pa bzhi) as integral to channel work.
