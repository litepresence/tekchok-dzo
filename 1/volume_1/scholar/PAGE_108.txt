[3979-3992] [PHILOLOGY] Three Error Reversal Systems

**Analysis:** Lines 3979-3992 present the detailed terminology of three approaches to reversing fundamental ignorance.

**First Approach: Root Cut (*rtsad gcod*) [3979-3992]:**

**Essence [3979-3981]:**
- Delusion root = non-awareness (*ma rig pa*)
- Basis of three realm samsara (*khams gsum 'khor ba'i gzhi*)
- When driven to self = ignorance cut at root (*ma rig pa rtsad bcad*)
- Called: self-awareness (*rang rig pa*)

**Key Term:** *rtsad bcad* — "cut at root" — definitive severing, not gradual elimination.

**Nature Analysis [3982-3983]:**
- Nature arises variously (*rang bzhin sna tshogs su byung bas*)
- Therefore causal-conditional searching (*rgyu rkyen gyi ba ded pa*)
- Object's vital point cut (*dmigs pa'i gdar sha chod*)
- Samsara's delusion basis exhausted (*'khor ba 'khrul pa'i ltos zad*)
- Result: causeless-empty primordial purity (*rgyu med stong par ye dag pa*)

**Second Approach: Self-Liberated Placing [3993-3999]:**

| Aspect | Tibetan | Function |
|--------|---------|----------|
| Essence | *dang po 'khrul ma myong bas ma rig pa'i rgyud dag* | First not experienced delusion, ignorance continuum pure |
| Present | *da lta 'khrul pa la mi gnas pa* | Now not abiding in delusion |
| Future | *phyis 'khrul mi srid pa* | Future delusion impossible |
| Nature | *rang bzhin la dbyer med pas 'khor 'das gzhi gcig* | Without difference, samsara-nirvana ground one |

**Third Approach: Basis Confidence [4000-4004]:**

| Aspect | Tibetan | Significance |
|--------|---------|------------|
| Essence | *ltung byed gzhi stongs pas byas pas ma rnyed pa'i gzhi 'chos pa* | Falling-doer basis empty, unfound fabricated ground confidence |
| Nature | *rang bzhin rtsis las 'das pas 'khrul ma 'khrul tsam du yang grub pa med pa* | Beyond calculation, delusion-non-delusion not established |
| Characteristic | *mtshan nyid la gnyis ma grub pas 'khrul ma 'khrul gyi zla dang bral ba* | Two non-established, delusion-non-delusion companion free |

**Scriptural Context:**
These approaches are drawn from the *Man ngag gi sde* (Instruction Section) of Atiyoga, representing the pinnacle of the nine vehicles. The emphasis on *rang grol* (self-liberation) distinguishes Atiyoga from lower vehicles requiring antidotes (*gnyen po*).

[4028-4100] [CONCEPT] Philosophical Architecture of Error Reversal

**Analysis:** The three approaches present distinct philosophical methodologies for achieving liberation.

**Comparative Framework:**

| Approach | Method | Target | Result |
|----------|--------|--------|--------|
| Root Cut | Immediate recognition | Ignorance root | Primordial purity |
| Self-Liberated Placing | Natural resolution | Continuum of delusion | Basis empty |
| Basis Confidence | Calculative transcending | Establishment/non-establishment | Beyond both |

**Key Philosophical Distinctions:**

1. **Root Cut:** Emphasizes *ye nas* (primordial) — delusion never truly existed
2. **Self-Liberated:** Emphasizes *rang grol* (self-liberated) — delusion liberates itself
3. **Basis Confidence:** Emphasizes *gnad la phebs* (striking crucial point) — decisive breakthrough

**Soteriological Implications:**

The three approaches accommodate different cognitive styles:
- **Direct perceivers** → Root Cut
- **Sequential practitioners** → Self-Liberated Placing  
- **Analytical minds** → Basis Confidence

All three achieve the same result: recognition that *'khrul pa* (delusion) and *ma 'khrul pa* (non-delusion) are not ultimately distinct (*zla dang bral ba* — without companion/opposite).

**Technical Terminology:**

- *gzhis gtad* (line 3980): "driving to ground" — redirecting delusion to its basis
- *rtsad bcad* (line 3980): "cut at root" — definitive severing
- *ltos zad* (line 3983): "basis exhausted" — fundamental resolution
- *gdar sha chod* (line 3983): "vital point cut" — decisive intervention
