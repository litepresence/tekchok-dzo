**Vowel Distribution Rule [5476-5490]:**
For a single letter like KA with multiple vowel signs:
- Two na-ro (*na ro gnyis*)
- Two gu-gu (*gu gu gnyis*)
- Two 'greng bu (*'greng bu gnyis*)
- Two zhabs kyu (*zhabs kyu gnyis*)
- Two ya-btags (*ya btags gnyis*)
- Two ra-btags (*ra btags gnyis*)
- One vowel L (*'dogs kyi la gcig*)
- One klad kor (*klad kor*) and one ring cha (*ring cha gcig*)

The rule: upper and lower na-ro, etc. are given half each to base KA and vowel L; klad kor given to L; ring cha benefits both.

**Example Forms [5489-5490]:**
KĪLĪ KŌLŌ KĒLĒ KŪLŪ KYŪLŪ YA KRA LĀ RA LAṀ

When LA and RA are not compatible in vital force (*srog mi mthun*), the acquired L (*la thob*) and ra-btags are inserted in the sequence.

**Equal Distribution [5491-5493]:**
If upper and lower branches have more or less, give the remaining equally to the vowel letter.

**Klad Kor Authority [5493]:**
The klad kor has authority over the vowel.

**Four Ornamental Letters [5504]:**
Na-ro, gu-gu, 'greng bu, and klad kor are called the four ornamental letters (*rgyan gyi yi ge bzhi*).

**Four Support Letters [5505-5506]:**
Ya-btags, ra-btags, and zhabs kyu (three) are called support letters (*rten gyi yi ge bzhi*) or end-support (*mthar rten*).
