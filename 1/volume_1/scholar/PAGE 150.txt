[5935-5988] [CONTEXT] Three Kayas Teaching Manifestation
**Analysis:** Lines 5935-5988 analyze how the three kayas manifest teachings:

**Citation from *Thal 'gyur* [5936]:**
"Gzhi is five-fold, place is considered three" — the meaning of this.

**Citation from *Rin po che spungs pa* [5939-5969]:**
Extensive verses explaining how:
- Dharmakaya's unmarked speech
- Unproliferated self-pure palace
- Non-dual with self, appearing to retinue
- Dharma from unmarked speech
- Self-ceased non-abiding from mind arises
- Not spoken, without speaking, in great equality demonstrated
- Self-clear pure Sambhogakaya
- From five pure light palace
- Unproliferated great bliss speech
- Five kayas, retinues of families
- Non-existent pure dharmas
- Five wisdoms gathered from mind arise
- Pure light rays tongue spread
- Self-arisen letters six
- Not spoken, self-arisen greatly demonstrated

**Nirmanakaya [5957-5969]:**
Without fixation, from beings' own places, awakening six families, endowed with proliferation words' speech, whichever faculties' dharma, memory self-clear from mind, faculties without desire spread on tongue, taught as numbered vehicle dharma.

**Preamble Meaning Establishment [5970-5988]:**
Thus explaining two preambles' meaning and purpose: "Thus I heard at one time" — Vajrapani's promise to retinue to teach what he heard from Samantabhadra without addition or omission. Or, Vajrapani himself accepted as tantra teacher, said "I taught to retinue."

**Citation from *Thal 'gyur* [5975]:**
"Teacher's speech taught to retinue."

**Vajrapani as Buddha [5977-5988]:**
Vajradhara is Buddha, as *Dpal mchog dang po* says: "Before all Buddhas, Vajradhara, King Vajra, Vajra Sattva." And from *Sbyong rgyud*: "Before all Buddhas, destroying all unknowing obscurations, Vajra, Vajra, Vajra holder, Vajra, Vajra, Vajra holder, Vajrapani, great hand Vajra."
