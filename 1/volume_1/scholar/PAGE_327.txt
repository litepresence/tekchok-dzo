## PAGE 327 [13411-13451]

### [STRUCTURE]
The passage presents **consciousness wheel elaboration** and **mind distinctions**. Lines 13411-13451 establish:

1. **Sixteen-channel wheel** (13412-13428): Dran pa 'dus pa'i 'khor lo with sixteen minds
2. **Mind-wind correspondence** (13428-13436): Sixteen winds carrying minds
3. **Mind itself analysis** (13436-13441): Four aspects of mind-essence
4. **Sixfold mind-memory** (13441-13451): Yid kyi dran pa drug with rebirth destinations

The structure demonstrates **systematic psychology**—mental factors mapped onto physiological structures.

### [PHILOLOGY]
**Technical terminology:**

- **དྲན་པ་འདུས་པའི་འཁོར་ལོ་རྩ་བཅུ་དྲུག་གི་ནང་ན་སེམས་བཅུ་དྲུག་བརྟེན་ནས་གནས་པའོ།** (13428): "In the sixteen channels of the memory-gathering wheel, sixteen minds abide dependent"—dran pa 'dus (memory-gathering) identifies the **heart chakra** as the site where consciousness aggregates.

- **སྐྱེ་མེད་དོན་གྱི་སེམས་ཉིད།** (13437): "Mind-itself of unborn meaning"—skye med (unborn) marks the **ultimate nature** of mind free from origination.

- **འགག་མེད་དྲན་པའི་སེམས་ཉིད།** (13438): "Mind-itself of non-ceasing memory"—'gag med (non-ceasing) establishes **continuity without permanence**.

- **འགྱུར་མེད་དཔེ་དོན་འདྲེས་པའི་སེམས་ཉིད།** (13439): "Mind-itself of unchanging example-meaning mixture"—dpe don (example-meaning) refers to the **two truths** integrated.

- **རང་རྫོགས་དངོས་དོན་གང་དུ་ཡང་མ་ཕྱེ་བའི་སེམས་ཉིད་དོ།** (13440): "Mind-itself of self-perfected actual-meaning not divided anywhere"—rang rdzogs (self-perfected) is **Dzogchen** technical terminology.

**Particle constructions:**
The **ablative of source** ལས། appears extensively (13446, 13448, 13450), establishing **karmic causality** for rebirth destinations. The **locative of abiding** ན། marks **spatial-mental correspondence**.

### [CONTEXT]
**Yogācāra psychology:**
The sixfold yid kyi dran pa (mind-memories) correspond to **Yogācāra** analysis of manas and mano-vijñāna. The passage integrates **consciousness-only** theory with tantric physiology.

**Rebirth cosmology:**
The destinations (lha, lha min, mi, byol song, yi dwags, dmyal ba) follow standard **six realms** ( 'gro drug) cosmology. The text presents **how mental factors determine rebirth**.

**Dzogchen mind-essence:**
Lines 13436-13441 present the **four mind-itself aspects**, a **Dzogchen** classification establishing mind as:
1. Unborn (skye med)
2. Non-ceasing ('gag med)
3. Unchanging ('gyur med)
4. Self-perfected (rang rdzogs)

### [CONCEPT]
**The sixteen minds and their winds:**
The text presents a **sophisticated psychosomatic theory**: each of sixteen minds in the heart wheel has a corresponding wind-horse (rlung rta). The mind rides the wind like a rider on a horse—**consciousness requires energy** to function.

**Mind-memory and rebirth mechanism:**
Lines 13444-13451 establish **how mental factors create rebirth**:
- **Kun tu tshol byed** (searching-everywhere) → clean/unclean/perverted destination
- **Gtan la 'bebs pa** (definitive-establishing) → vidyādhara/god/animal destination
- **Nges par 'jog pa** (certain-establishing) → khechara/human/desire-realm destination

This demonstrates **karmic intentionality**: the **mode** of mind determines the **destination** of rebirth.

**Mind-itself beyond elaboration:**
The fourfold analysis (13436-13441) presents **mind-itself** (sems nyid) as:
- Beyond birth and cessation
- Beyond example and meaning
- Beyond division
- Self-perfected

This is the **Dzogchen view**: mind is not something to be achieved but **already complete**.
