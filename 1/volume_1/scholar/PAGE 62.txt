[2100-2164] [CONTEXT] The Hundred Teachers
**Analysis:**

Longchenpa presents the "three hundred views of space" (*dbyings kyi lta ba sum brgya*), organized around teachers across the three times.

**Structure:**
- 100 past teachers (*'das pa'i ston pa*)
- 100 future teachers (*ma 'ongs pa*)
- 100 present teachers (*da ltar ba*)

**Ten Teachers Enumerated:**

2. **Grasper of the nonexistent** (*med pa 'dzin pa*)
3. **Life** (*srog*)
4. **Leaf-possessor** (*lo ma can*)
5. **Purity-possessor** (*gtsang ma can*)
6. **Vishnu** (*khyab 'jug*)
7. **Mind of Vishnu** (*khyab 'jug sems*)
8. **Three sticks** (*khur bu gsum*)
9. **Kolapaka** (*'bru rdzas can*)
10. **Equal conduct** (*spyod pa mnyam pa*)

These represent the diverse teachers and lineages of non-Buddhist India, each propounding a particular view of space or cosmic principle.

[2165-2245] [STRUCTURE] Additional Enumerations
**Analysis:**

The text continues listing the remaining teachers, including:

**Ethical and Social Categories:**
- Thinking of dharma (*chos su sems pa*)
- Respecting mothers (*ma gus pa*)
- Respecting fathers (*pha gus pa*)

**Celestial Observers:**
- Sixty moon-dawners (*zla ba shar ba drug cu*)
- Sun-dawners (*nyi ma shar ba*)

**Ascetic Practices:**
- Hand-eaters (*lag za*)
- Heart-livers (*snying za*)
- Those who become passed/cease (*'das par 'gyur*)
- Those who do not become passed (*mi 'das*)

**Samkhya-related:**
- Possessors of three aggregates (*phung po gsum pa*)
- Living by two or three (*gnyis gsum gyis 'tsho*)
- Entering Samkhya yogins (*grangs can rnal 'byor du zhugs pa*)

**Nature Dwellers:**
- Seventy owls (*'ug pa bdun cu*)
- Forest dwellers (*nags gnas*)

**Named Teachers:**
- Bhakhanapa (*bha kha na pa*)
- Bhala (*bha la*) and dog-possessors (*khyi can*)
- Brahmin conduct (*bram ze'i spyod pa*)
- Lords of animals (*srog chags gi bdag po*)
- Gautama (*gau ta ma*)

These 100 teachers represent the diverse philosophical and religious movements of ancient India, providing a comprehensive doxographic survey.
