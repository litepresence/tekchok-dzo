[5150-5186] [PHILOLOGY] Root and Branch Vowel Systems
**Analysis:** Lines 5150-5186 detail the phonetic analysis of Sanskrit-Tibetan correspondences in the letter system:

**Root Letter Analysis:**
- *rtsa ba'i yi ge sum cu rtsa bzhi'i dbang du byas te* — referring to the thirty-four root letters as the basis
- *nya dang wa gnyis srog gcig pas 'dir gcig tu byas te* — NYA and WA, sharing one vital force (*srog*), are counted as one
- The vowel system (*'dogs*) places NYA, while simple letters (*rkyang pa*) place WA

**Technical Terms:**
- *ring cha'i a* — long A (vowel length)
- *mi gcig pa nyis brtsegs* — non-identical double-stacked
- *rkyang par snang ba* — appearing as simple/isolated
- *don gyis nyis brtsegs su gnas* — established as double-stacked by meaning

**Sanskrit Phonetic Grid [5160-5165]:**
The text presents the complete Sanskrit alphabet with Tibetan phonetic renderings:
- A KṢA KHA GA GHA (vowels and gutturals)
- CA CHA JA JHA ÑA (palatals)
- ṬA ṬHA ḌA ḌHA ṆA (cerebrals)
- TA THA DA DHA NA (dentals)
- PA PHA BA BHA MA (labials)
- YA RA LA VA ŚA ṢA SA HA (semi-vowels and sibilants)

**Dharmatological Application [5166-5176]:**
When combined with vowels (*'dogs dang bcas pas*), applied to dharmatā:
- *snang ba dang stong pa* — appearance and emptiness
- *thabs dang shes rab* — method and wisdom
- *yab dang yum* — father and mother
- *dbyings dang ye shes* — expanse and wisdom
- *ka dag dang lhun grub* — primordial purity and spontaneous presence

The citation from *Rang shar* [5172-5176] validates this hermeneutical approach.

[5177-5206] [STRUCTURE] The Five Branch Vowels
**Analysis:** Lines 5177-5206 analyze the five appendages (*btags*) that modify root letters:

**Five Appendages (*rnam pa lnga btags*) [5183]:**
1. *na ro* — vowel sign above (for i, ī)
2. *gu gu* — u-vowel sign
3. *'greng bu* — vertical stroke (for e, ai)
4. *ya btags* — ya-appendage
5. *ra btags* — ra-appendage

When these five are appended to root letters, they symbolize (*mtshon pa*) the five wisdoms' own light (*ye shes lnga'i rang 'od*) in awareness (*rig pa*). The text provides visual examples [5186] of complex letter forms.

**Appendix of Appendix (*'dogs kyi 'dogs*) [5189-5206]:**
The text distinguishes:
- Root vowels (*rtsa ba'i 'dogs*) — the thirty-four consonants with vowel marks
- Branch vowels (*yan lag*) — *na ro*, etc. attached to these
- Appendix of appendix — vowels on vowelled letters

**Hermeneutical Note [5207-5227]:**
The text presents two modes of explanation (*bshad lugs rnam pa gnyis*):
- Samsara explanation — letters without vowels as samsara's dharmas
- Nirvana explanation — vowels and branches as nirvana

This reflects the Nyingma hermeneutical principle of contextual interpretation (*skabs kyis go ba*).
