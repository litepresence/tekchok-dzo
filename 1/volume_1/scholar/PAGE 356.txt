[14488-14524] [STRUCTURE] Empty Thigle Lamp
The text presents the empty thigle lamp (thig le stong pa'i sgron ma). The structure establishes: (1) essence as the self-light of awareness, (2) position in the heart center, and (3) the five lights as its display.

[14488-14524] [PHILOLOGY] Thigle Lamp Terminology
The term thig le (bindu/drop) describes luminous spheres of light. The compound stong pa'i sgron ma (empty lamp) identifies its emptiness nature. The phrase 'od lnga (five lights) describes its colorful display.

[14488-14524] [CONTEXT] Thigle Mysticism
The text presents thigle as luminous spheres that appear in advanced practice. They represent the bindu or essence of awareness made visible. This reflects the tantric and Dzogchen understanding of light as the nature of mind.

[14488-14524] [CONCEPT] Thigle as Awareness Essence
The text conceptualizes thigle as the visible form of awareness's essence. Empty yet luminous, they represent the union of emptiness and clarity. Seeing thigle is seeing the nature of mind directly.
