[15482-15518] [STRUCTURE] Wind Element and Wisdom Result
The text presents the wind element (rlung gi 'byung ba) and its result as all-accomplishing wisdom. The structure establishes: (1) wind channels as the basis for activity, (2) wind as the mount of consciousness, and (3) purified wind becoming the wisdom of enlightened activity.

[15482-15518] [PHILOLOGY] Wind Wisdom Terminology
The term rlung gi 'byung ba (wind element) identifies the air element. The compound bya grub ye shes (all-accomplishing wisdom) establishes its enlightened form. The phrase phrin las (enlightened activity) describes the result.

[15482-15518] [CONTEXT] Wind as Activity
The text presents wind as the element of activity and movement. All actions, both physical and mental, depend on wind. When purified, wind becomes the capacity for enlightened activity that benefits all beings.

[15482-15518] [CONCEPT] Activity as Wind Function
The text conceptualizes all activity as the function of wind. Physical movement, speech, and thought all require wind. Purified wind is the energy of enlightenment; it is what enables Buddhas to act without obstruction.
