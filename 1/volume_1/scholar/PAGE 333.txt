[13566-13600] [STRUCTURE] Taste Wheel and Completion Stage
The text presents the wheel of gathered tastes (ro rnams 'dus pa'i 'khor lo) and completion stage processes. The structure establishes: (1) the navel chakra with sixty-two petals, (2) fire element as the primary transformative agent, and (3) three years of training for nectar extraction.

[13566-13600] [PHILOLOGY] Alchemical Terminology
The term ro rnams 'dus pa'i 'khor lo (wheel of gathered tastes) identifies the navel chakra. The compound bcud len (nectar extraction/amṛta) describes the completion stage practice. The phrase bskyed rim bsgoms pas (through meditation of generation stage) establishes the progression to completion.

[13566-13600] [CONTEXT] Rasāyana Practice
The text presents rasāyana (bcud len) practice where subtle essences are extracted and transmuted. The six tastes correspond to different energetic effects. This reflects Indian alchemical traditions adapted to Buddhist completion stage practice.

[13566-13600] [CONCEPT] Taste as Transformation
The text conceptualizes taste as a vehicle for transformation. Sweet produces radiance; sour produces strength; salty produces bodhicitta. The physical process of digestion becomes an alchemical process of spiritual refinement.
