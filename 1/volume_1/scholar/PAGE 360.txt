[14636-14672] [STRUCTURE] Thödgal Practice Integration
The text presents the integration of thödgal practice with the four lamps. The structure establishes: (1) gaze positions for each lamp, (2) progressive stages of vision development, and (3) the fruition as the dissolution of vision into wisdom.

[14636-14672] [PHILOLOGY] Practice Terminology
The term lta stangs (gaze position) describes the physical technique. The compound snang ba mched pa (vision development) identifies the progressive unfolding. The phrase ye shes su yal (dissolve into wisdom) describes the fruition.

[14636-14672] [CONTEXT] Thödgal Technique
The text presents specific techniques for thödgal practice involving gaze, posture, and attention. The four lamps appear in sequence as practice deepens. This reflects the practical dimension of Dzogchen instruction.

[14636-14672] [CONCEPT] Vision Dissolving into Wisdom
The text conceptualizes the fruition of thödgal as the dissolution of vision into wisdom. The lamps are not the goal but the path; the goal is recognition beyond vision. Eventually even the most sublime visions are transcended.
