## PAGE 336 [13751-13787]

### [STRUCTURE]
The passage presents **birth preparation** and **elemental-psychological formation**. Lines 13751-13787 establish:

1. **Sex determination and position** (13752-13758): Male right-facing, female left-facing
2. **Nine-month birth** (13756-13758): Male 10th month, female 9th month
3. **Avadhūti activation** (13758-13760): Wind enters nostrils, afflictions coarse
4. **Element-aggregate formation** (13761-13787): Four elements → four aggregates

The structure completes **gestation** with birth mechanics.

### [PHILOLOGY]
**Technical terminology:**

- **བུ་ཁྱེའུ་རྣམས་ནི་མའི་གློ་གཡས་ནས་ཁ་ནང་དུ་ལྟ་ལ།** (13752): "Males from mother's right side face inward"—embryonic **orientation** determines sex and birth position.

- **རླུང་ཨ་ཝ་དྷཱུ་ཏཱི་ནས་སྣ་སྦུག་གཡས་གཡོན་ལ་དངོས་སུ་རྒྱུ་བས།** (13758): "Wind from avadhūti actually moves to right-left nostrils"—avadhūti (central channel) **bifurcation** at nostrils.

- **ས་ལས་ཤ་དངོས་སུ་གྲུབ་པར་བྱས།** (13762): "From earth, flesh actually established"—element-aggregate **correspondence**.

- **ཤ་ལས་གཏི་མུག** (13766): "From flesh, delusion"—**material-mental causation**.

- **ཁྲག་ལས་འདོད་ཆགས།** (13767): "From blood, desire"—**sanguine psychology**.

**Particle constructions:**
The **ablative of origin** ལས། appears extensively for **element-derivation**. The **causative-final** བྱས་པས། marks **productive causation**.

### [CONTEXT]
**Embryological sex determination:**
The text presents **ancient embryology**: males form on right, face inward; females on left, face outward. This reflects **Ayurvedic** theory (male = solar/right, female = lunar/left).

**Avadhūti physiology:**
The central channel (avadhūti) opening to nostrils (13758) establishes **prāṇa** flow. When this opens at birth, **afflictions coarsen**—the fall from womb-bliss into conditioned existence.

**Element psychology:**
The element-aggregate-affliction correspondences follow **classical Indian** physiology:
- Earth → flesh → form (gzugs) → delusion (gti mug)
- Water → blood → feeling (tshor ba) → desire ('dod chags)
- Fire → heat → perception ('du shes) → anger (zhe sdang)
- Wind → breath → formations ('du byed) → pride (nga rgyal)

### [CONCEPT]
**Birth as spiritual fall:**
The text presents **soteriological embryology**: when avadhūti wind enters nostrils (13758), afflictions "become coarse" (je rags). Birth is **forgetting**—the infant loses womb-samādhi and enters conditioned consciousness.

**Elemental psychology:**
The correspondences (13761-13770) establish **material basis of mind**:
- Physical elements produce psychological aggregates
- Aggregates produce afflictions
- Yet the same elements can produce **wisdom** when recognized

This is **non-duality**: saṃsāra and nirvāṇa share the same substrate.

**Wind as affliction-vehicle:**
Line 13777 specifies that **three poisons ride the wind-horse** (dug gsum gyi rtog pa th dad 'char ba). The wind that animates the body also **carries karma**. This establishes **prāṇāyāma** as ethical practice—purify wind, purify mind.
