## PAGE 338 [13830-13876]

### [STRUCTURE]
The passage presents **three main channels** and **four knots** exposition. Lines 13830-13876 establish:

1. **Three channels from Mu Tig Phreng Ba** (13831-13847): Ro ma, rkyang ma, kun 'dar ma
2. **Ro ma analysis** (13847-13854): Left channel, desire-based, thigle transformation
3. **Rkyang ma analysis** (13854-13862): Right channel, unchanging, ultimate-relative integration
4. **Kun 'dar ma analysis** (13862-13876): Central channel, all-gathering, bliss-source

The structure provides **definitive three-channel physiology**.

### [PHILOLOGY]
**Critical terminology:**

- **རྩ་རྣམས་ནས་ནི་ཐིག་ལེ་སྟེ།** (13836): "From channels, indeed bindus"—from **Guhyasamāja** or similar tantra. Channels produce bindus as **fruits**.

- **རོ་མ།** (13844): "Taste/Female"—left channel associated with:
  - Taste-experience (ro myong)
  - Feminine principle (mo)
  - Desire ('dod chags)
  - Means (thabs)

- **རྐྱང་མ།** (13854): "Single/Isolate"—right channel as:
  - Unbranching ('khyog med, yal ga med)
  - Unchanging (mi 'gyur ba)
  - Absolute thigle (don dam thig le)

- **ཀུན་འདར་མ།** (13862): "All-shaking"—central channel as:
  - All-gathering (kun 'dus pa)
  - Universal pervasion (spyi la khyab pa)
  - Source of all bliss (bde ba kun skye)

- **ལྟེ་བའི་གཡོན་ནས་འཁྱིལ་རྣམས་གཡས་ནས་རྒྱུ་ལ།** (13869): "From left of navel, coiling, from right moves"—ro ma's **helical path**.

**Particle constructions:**
The **defining** ཅེས་བྱ། appears extensively for **etymology**. The **causal** ལས། establishes **channel functions**.

### [CONTEXT]
**Guhyasamāja source:**
The citation (13831-13841) from **Mu Tig Phreng Ba** (Pearl Garland) establishes **father tantra** authority for three-channel theory.

**Hatha yoga parallels:**
The three channels correspond to:
- Iḍā (left, moon, feminine)
- Piṅgalā (right, sun, masculine)
- Suṣumnā (central, fire, union)

**Dzogchen integration:**
The channels are presented as **already wisdom**:
- Ro ma = thabs (means)
- Rkyang ma = shes rab (wisdom)
- Kun 'dar ma = their union

### [CONCEPT]
**Ro ma as desire-transformer:**
The text presents **sublimation physiology** (13847-13854): ro ma channel transforms desire ('dod chags) into buddhahood. Same substance, different direction—desire **downward** creates saṃsāra; desire **upward** creates enlightenment.

**Rkyang ma as unchanging wisdom:**
The right channel (13854-13862) contains **both ultimate and relative bindus** but remains **unchanging** (mi 'gyur ba). This establishes **wisdom-stability** amidst phenomenal flux.

**Kun 'dar ma as universal ground:**
The central channel (13862-13876) is **source of all bliss** (kun gyi bde ba). It "shakes" ('dar) because it **vibrates with all energies** yet remains stable. This is **ground of being**.
