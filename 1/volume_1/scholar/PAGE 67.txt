[2392-2427] [STRUCTURE] Synthesis of Views
**Analysis:**

**Fourfold Classification:**

Longchenpa synthesizes the 360 views into four categories:

1. **Extreme View** (*mtha' lta*):
   - Eternal permanence (*rtag pa ther zug*)
   - Complete eternalism

2. **Extreme End** (*mtha' mtha'*):
   - Permanent is permanent, impermanent is impermanent—both
   - Half-permanent, half-interrupted

3. **Distant Benefit** (*ring phan*):
   - Half permanent, half interrupted
   - Effect severed

4. **Indifference** (*btang snyoms*):
   - Completely interrupted
   - Complete severance

**Reduction to Two:**

All four reduce to:
- Permanence (*rtag pa*)
- Interruption/severance (*chad pa*)

**The 360 Components:**
- 300 spaces (*dbyings*) + 60 views (*lta ba*)
- All gathered into four types
- These gathered into permanence and interruption

**Purpose of Teaching:**

*phyin log lam du mi zhugs par bya ba'i phyir* — "so that one does not enter through recognizing wrong paths"

Longchenpa emphasizes that this extensive doxography serves a practical purpose: preventing practitioners from mistakenly following erroneous paths.

[2428-2429] [CONTEXT] Transition to Transcendent Vehicles
**Analysis:**

The Buddha's statement: *rigs kyi bu khyod phyin log gi lam du zhugs par 'jigs pas na / 'jig tshogs kyi lta ba mang po 'di dag bsdus nas bshad do* — "Son of the clan, fearing you enter wrong paths, I gathered and taught these many views of aggregates."

This marks the completion of the outsider (*phyi rol pa*) doxography and transitions to the transcendent vehicles (*'jig rten las 'das pa'i theg pa*).

**Three-part Structure:**

1. Method superior to outsiders (*phyi rol pa las khyad par du 'phags pa'i thabs*)
2. General explanation (*spyi bshad pa*)
3. Extensive particular explanation (*bye brag tu rgyas par bshad pa*)
