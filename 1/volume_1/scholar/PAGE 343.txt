[14035-14077] [STRUCTURE] Five Classes and Channel Characteristics
The text presents the five classes of females based on channel characteristics. The structure establishes: (1) deer-nosed type with desire, (2) lotus type with openness, (3) conch-shell type with coiling, (4) picture type with flexibility, and (5) linear type with directness.

[14035-14077] [PHILOLOGY] Female Type Terminology
The term ri dags can (deer-nosed type) describes specific anatomical characteristics. The compound dung can ma (conch-shell bearer) identifies another channel configuration. The phrase rigs can lnga'i 'du 'chod (union of five classes) establishes the complete system.

[14035-14077] [CONTEXT] Tantric Classification
The text presents a tantric classification system for understanding energetic compatibility. The five types are not merely physical but describe subtle energy patterns. This reflects the tantric emphasis on direct knowledge of subtle phenomena.

[14035-14077] [CONCEPT] Anatomical Spirituality
The text conceptualizes anatomy as spiritual information. Physical characteristics indicate channel configurations; channel configurations indicate spiritual capacities. The body is thus a map of spiritual potential.
