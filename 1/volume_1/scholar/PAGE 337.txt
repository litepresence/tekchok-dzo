[13716-13751] [STRUCTURE] Syllable Sound and Secret Mantra
The text presents the syllable sounds (yi ge) and secret mantra (gsang sngags) as self-sound. The structure establishes: (1) body as three kāyas mandala, (2) speech as secret mantra words, and (3) meditation as the natural state of mind.

[13716-13751] [PHILOLOGY] Mantra Terminology
The term sgra tshigs gsang sngags kyi rang sgra (secret mantra's self-sound of word arrangement) identifies mantra as spontaneous sound. The compound sku gsum dkyil 'khor (mandala of three kāyas) establishes the body's sacred geometry. The phrase bsam gtan (meditation/samādhi) describes the natural mind state.

[13716-13751] [CONTEXT] Mantra Yoga
The text presents mantra yoga where sound is not merely symbolic but the actual vibration of reality. The Mu tig 'phreng rgyud citation establishes scriptural authority. This reflects the tantric understanding of sound as creative power.

[13716-13751] [CONCEPT] Sound as Wisdom Expression
The text conceptualizes sound as the expression of wisdom. Mantra is not invented but discovered—the natural sound of reality. To speak mantra is to align with the vibrational nature of awakening.
