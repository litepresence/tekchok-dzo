[15038-15074] [STRUCTURE] Twenty-Four Gathering Channels
The text presents the twenty-four gathering channels ('du ba'i rtsa) of the crown wheel. The structure establishes: (1) twenty-four channels as the basis of gathering, (2) six channels for each of the four elements, and (3) their connection to the twenty-four sacred places.

[15038-15074] [PHILOLOGY] Gathering Channel Terminology
The term 'du ba'i rtsa nyi shu bzhi (twenty-four gathering channels) identifies a specific group. The compound ma rig 'dus pa'i rtsa (channels of gathered ignorance) shows the dual nature. The phrase 'dod chags bd kan rtsa (channels of desire-phlegm) establishes afflictive correspondences.

[15038-15074] [CONTEXT] Sacred Geography
The text integrates the twenty-four channels with the twenty-four sacred places (pīṭha) of tantric geography. The body contains the entire sacred universe. This reflects the tantric understanding of the body as the mandala of all sacred places.

[15038-15074] [CONCEPT] Body as Sacred Geography
The text conceptualizes the body as containing sacred geography. The twenty-four channels correspond to the twenty-four sacred places; purifying the channels purifies the places. The body is the pilgrimage site; realization is the fruit.
