## PAGE 322 [13279-13307]

### [STRUCTURE]
The passage presents **second seven-day period** (bdun tshan gnyis pa) embryology with systematic channel development. Lines 13279-13307 establish:

1. **Completion of three kāyas** (13279-13280): Lhun grub dissolution into ka dag
2. **Water channel generation** (13282-13290): Eight water channels producing eight consciousnesses
3. **Wind-sound manifestation** (13291-13296): Khra spungs (gathered sound) wind creating elemental wheels
4. **Embryonic nāḍī formation** (13296-13307): Twenty-one-day completion of srin bu (subtle channels)

The architectural progression follows **elemental emergence**: from wisdom dissolution (space) → water channels → wind-sound → earth-solidification.

### [PHILOLOGY]
**Technical terminology:**

- **ཀ་དག་རང་སོར་གནས་པ།** (13279): "Primordial purity abiding in its own place" - rang sor (self-place) indicates **non-contrived naturalness**. The རང་ prefix emphasizes **self-originated** rather than achieved.

- **བདུན་ཚན་གཉིས་པ།** (13281): "Second seven-day [period]" - tshan marks **collective/group** timing. The enumeration establishes **tantric gestational cycles**.

- **སྐྱེད་བྱེད་དང་པོའི་འཁོར་ལོ།** (13282): "First producing wheel" - technical term for the **navel chakra** as generative center. Skyed byed (producer/generator) identifies the **manipūra** function.

- **ཁ་སྤུངས།** (13291): "Gathered sound/mouth-heap" - onomatopoeic term for the **primordial sound** of wind in channels. The wind-sound (rlung gi rang sgra) transforms into seed-syllables (yi ge).

- **དབང་བསྒྱུར་གྱི་ལུས།** (13305): "Body of transformation/control" - dbang bsgyur indicates **empowerment capacity**. The phrase specifies this is **not physical form** (dngos gzugs ma yin) but energetic body.

**Grammatical structures:**
The **causal-connective** ལས། appears extensively (13287, 13289, 13291), establishing **karmic causality** in embryonic development. The **conditional-consequential** ན...བྱུང་། structures (13286, 13296) mark **developmental thresholds**.

### [CONTEXT]
**Tantric embryology:**
The passage aligns with **Nyingma** subtle body physiology as presented in the **Nyi Zla Kha Sbyor** tantra (cited at 13307). The eight consciousnesses (rnam shes brgyad) from eight channels reflect **Yogācāra** psychology integrated into **Dzogchen** physiology.

**Mantric cosmology:**
The **seed-syllables** (yi ge) and **light-columns** ('od) with **Samantabhadra parents** (kun bzang yab yum) establish the **adhiṣṭhāna** (blessing) dimension of embryology—each channel contains enlightened potential.

**Karmic theory integration:**
Line 13306 specifies that Buddha-bodies do not abide in **dual parents** (gnyis gnyis), establishing the **non-dual** nature of enlightened form. This contrasts with ordinary embodiment which requires dual conditions.

### [CONCEPT]
**Wind as the animator of consciousness:**
Lines 13286-13287 present the **wind-consciousness relationship**: when karmic wind (las kyi rlung) moves through channels, they **vibrate and coil** ('gul zhing 'phril) producing eight consciousnesses. This establishes **vāyu** as the bridge between physical and mental.

**The four earth channels and gathering wind:**
The text describes a sophisticated **energetic architecture** (13291-13296): four earth channels form wheels ascending upward, while **khra spungs wind** holds the space between them like **mustard seeds not touching** (yungs 'bru tsam gyis ma reg par). This creates the **luminous gap** where awareness resides.

**Twenty-one days as karmic completion:**
Line 13328 establishes **zhag nyer gcig** (twenty-one days) as the completion of all mantra activities (sngags kun gyi las mtha'). This reflects the **tantric principle** that energetic processes complete in three seven-day cycles—gestation follows the same pattern as spiritual practice.
