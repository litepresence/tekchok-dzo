## PAGE 323 [13308-13331]

### [STRUCTURE]
The passage presents **citation and third seven-day period** with aggregate formation. Lines 13308-13331 divide into:

1. **Tantric citation** (13308-13314): Rdo rje rtse mo rol pa las—on form and emptiness
2. **Karmic completion** (13315-13317): Fourteen days marking karmic relationships
3. **Third seven-day development** (13317-13331): Elemental completion and aggregate manifestation

The structure demonstrates the **integration of scripture, karma, and physiology**.

### [PHILOLOGY]
**Technical terminology:**

- **གཟུགས་གཅིག་ལས་ནི་ལུས་ཀྱི་མཐའ།** (13308): "From one form, the body's limit"—quotation from **Vajra Pinnacle Play** tantra. Mttha' (limit/extreme) indicates the **boundaries of form** that are ultimately empty.

- **གྲུག་མཐའ་མ་གྲུབ་མཐར་འཛིན་པ།** (13309): "Grasping at extremes where the extreme of destruction is unestablished"—presents **Mādhyamika** emptiness through tantric verse. Gru mtha' (destruction-extreme) refers to **nihilism**.

- **ཆགས་སྤྱོད་ཀྱི་རླུང་།** (13315): "Desire-activity wind"—technical term for **kāma-vāyu**, the wind that drives attachment. The compound establishes the **link between wind-energies and afflictions**.

- **འདོད་ཆགས་ཀྱི་ལས་ཉེ་བར་ལེན་པ།** (13315): "Karma of attachment as near-seizer"—nye bar len pa (upādāna) identifies the **second link** of dependent origination, here applied to embryonic desire.

- **ཀུན་གཞིའི་རྟེན་བཞི།** (13320): "Four supports of ālaya"—the ālaya-vijñāna's fourfold basis, a technical **Yogācāra** term integrated into Dzogchen physiology.

**Grammatical analysis:**
The **quotative** ཞེས་སོ། appears at 13314 and 13330, marking **canonical authority**. The **causal-final** བྱས་པས། (13323, 13324) establishes **resultative processes** in development.

### [CONTEXT]
**Doxographical synthesis:**
The passage integrates three systems:
1. **Madhyamaka**: Emptiness of form extremes (13308-13314)
2. **Yogācāra**: Ālaya-vijñāna and aggregates (13320-13323)
3. **Dzogchen**: Natural dissolution into dharmatā (13329)

**Tantric classification:**
The citation from **Rdo rje rtse mo rol pa** (Vajra Pinnacle Play) establishes the text's **Mahāyoga** affiliation. This tantra focuses on **guhyagarbha** (secret womb) teachings.

**Embryological correlation:**
The twenty-one-day (zhag nyer gcig) completion corresponds to the **three-week formation** in Ayurvedic embryology, but with tantric interpretation—dissolution of ālaya support marks the shift from collective to individual consciousness.

### [CONCEPT]
**Form, destruction-extreme, and emptiness:**
The citation at 13308-13314 presents the **sunyata of form**: form arises from limits, but the limit of destruction is unestablished. This means **form is neither eternal nor annihilated**—it appears dependently without inherent existence.

**Desire-wind and karmic formation:**
Lines 13315-13317 analyze **how desire creates embodiment**: desire-activity wind (chags spyod kyi rlung) in each srin bu (subtle channel) produces attachment-karma. This establishes that **embodiment is driven by karmic winds** that carry the imprints of past attachment.

**The four ālaya-supports and eighteen dhātus:**
The text presents the **physiological basis** for the eighteen elements (khams bco brgyad). At 13320-13322, the four ālaya-supports stabilize as energetic spheres (rlangs kyi dum bu), forming the basis for sense-consciousness development. This demonstrates that **the elements are energetic configurations** rather than solid substances.
