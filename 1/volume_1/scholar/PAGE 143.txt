[5513-5538] [CONTEXT] Mantra Etymology and Meaning
**Analysis:** Lines 5513-5538 decode the profound meanings of Sanskrit mantras:

**Stacked Letter Pronunciation Rule [5513-5519]:**
When similar vital force, etc., pronounce sound. The text references "Stacked Letters 4" (*brtsegs yig 4*).

**Mantra OṂ KO ŚO MI TRĪ ṬAṂ [5520]:**

**Philological Decoding:**
- OṂ — unborn (*skye ba med pa*)
- KO — pure Dharmakaya (*dag pa chos kyi sku*)
- ŚO — wisdom nature (*shes rab kyi bdag nyid*)
- MI — non-duality of all dharmas (*chos thams cad gnyis su med pa*)
- TRĪ — permanent wisdom (*rtag pa'i ye shes*)
- ṬAṂ — emptiness without cessation (*stong pa 'gag pa med pa*)

**Dharmatological Meaning [5525-5528]:**
- Expense arising from expanse (*dbyings las dbyings byung ba*)
- Adorned by non-dual wisdom (*ye shes gnyis med kyis brgyan pa*)
- Therefore: nature of expanse-wisdom triple-stacked (*dbyings ye shes sum brtsegs kyi bdag nyid*)
- Awareness-wisdom of dharmatā equality (*chos nyid mnyam pa nyid las ma 'das pa'i rig pa'i ye shes*)

**Two Types of Light [5529-5530]:**
1. Light of realized persons (*rtogs pa'i gang zag gi 'od*)
2. Light of unrealized sentient beings (*ma rtogs pas sems can gyi 'od*)

**Realization Dynamics [5531-5532]:**
For those with realization, this very appearance dawns as flowing wisdom. For unrealized, though five lights exist, they are not seen — without symbol (*mtshon du med*).
