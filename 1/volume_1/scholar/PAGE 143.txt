[5508-5538] [STRUCTURE] Stacked Letter Pronunciation System

**Analysis:** Lines 5508-5538 present the systematic pronunciation rules for Tibetan stacked letters.

**Stacked Letter Combinations [5508-5512]:**

| Combination | Pronunciation | Example |
|-------------|---------------|---------|
| *gu gu gcig* | One gu-gu | *kī* pronounced with single extension |
| *na ro gnyis brtsegs* | Two na-ro stacked | *stod de smad pa* — "top then bottom" |
| *zhabs kyur gcig* | One zhabs-kyu | *ko'u* |
| *'greng bu gnyis brtsegs* | Two 'greng-bu stacked | *'greng ste bkug pa* — "stretched then curved" |

**Class 3 Stacked Letters [5513-5520]:**

**Rule:** When vital forces (*srog*) are similar, pronounce as sound.

**Example Mantra [5520]:**
*o ko sho mi tri tsaṃ* — OṂ KO ŚO MI TRI ṬAṂ

**Decoding System:**
Each syllable corresponds to specific dharmatological meaning:
- OṂ = unborn (*skye ba med pa*)
- KO = pure Dharmakāya (*dag pa chos kyi sku*)
- ŚO = wisdom nature (*shes rab kyi bdag nyid*)
- MI = all dharmas non-dual (*chos thams cad gnyis su med pa*)
- TRI = permanent wisdom (*rtag pa'i ye shes*)
- ṬAṂ = emptiness without cessation (*stong pa 'gag pa med pa*)
