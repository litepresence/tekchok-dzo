[15815-15851] [STRUCTURE] Four Visions as Conduct Fruition
The text presents the four visions (snang ba bzhi) as the fruition of conduct. The structure establishes: (1) direct perception of dharmatā, (2) development of experience, (3) full maturation of wisdom, and (4) exhaustion of phenomena into dharmatā.

[15815-15851] [PHILOLOGY] Four Visions Terminology
The term snang ba bzhi (four visions) identifies the progressive stages. The compound chos nyid mngon sum (direct perception of dharmatā) identifies the first vision. The phrase nyams gong 'phel (development of experience) describes the second vision.

[15815-15851] [CONTEXT] Thödgal Progression
The text presents the four visions as the progressive stages of thödgal practice. Each vision represents a deepening of recognition. This reflects the Dzogchen path of vision reaching its culmination.

[15815-15851] [CONCEPT] Visions as Recognition Stages
The text conceptualizes the four visions as stages of recognition becoming stable. First one perceives dharmatā directly; then experience develops; then wisdom matures; finally phenomena exhaust into dharmatā. This is the complete path.
