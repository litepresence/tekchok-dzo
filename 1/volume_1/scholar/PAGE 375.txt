[15445-15481] [STRUCTURE] Five Buddha Families Result
The text presents the five Buddha families (rigs lnga) as the result of practice. The structure establishes: (1) Akṣobhya's mirror-like wisdom in the east, (2) Ratnasambhava's equality wisdom in the south, (3) Amitābha's discriminating wisdom in the west, (4) Amoghasiddhi's all-accomplishing wisdom in the north, and (5) Vairocana's dharmadhātu wisdom in the center.

[15445-15481] [PHILOLOGY] Buddha Family Terminology
The term rigs lnga (five families) establishes the classification system. The compound mi bskyod pa (Akṣobhya/unshakable) identifies the eastern Buddha. The phrase 'od dpag med (Amitābha/boundless light) identifies the western Buddha.

[15445-15481] [CONTEXT] Mandala of Result
The text presents the five Buddha families as the mandala of complete enlightenment. Each direction, color, and wisdom represents an aspect of purified consciousness. This reflects the tantric understanding of enlightenment as complete mandala.

[15445-15481] [CONCEPT] Families as Purified Elements
The text conceptualizes the five families as the five elements purified. Earth becomes Ratnasambhava; water becomes Amitābha; fire becomes Amoghasiddhi; wind becomes Akṣobhya; space becomes Vairocana. Purification reveals the Buddha nature of elements.
