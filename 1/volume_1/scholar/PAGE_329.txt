## PAGE 329 [13490-13525]

### [STRUCTURE]
The passage presents **mathematical mandala enumeration** and **disease etiology**. Lines 13490-13525 establish:

1. **Numerological synthesis** (13490-13519): 212 + 112 + 18 + 16 + 5 + 5 + 31 = 399
2. **Srin bu afflictions** (13519-13522): 84,000 srin bu with corresponding letters and kāyas
3. **Hair calculation** (13522-13525): 140,000 body hairs
4. **Wind-functions** (13525): Disease causation when wind doesn't function

The structure creates a **computational physiology** linking mandala, disease, and subtle body.

### [PHILOLOGY]
**Technical terminology:**

- **གྲངས་ཉི་ཤུ་ཉི་ཤུ་སྟེ་ཉིས་བརྒྱ་དང་བཞི་བཅུ་སྟེ་བསྡོམས་པས་དྲུག་བརྒྱ་དང་དྲུག་ཅུ་རྩ་དྲུག** (13400-13411): "Twenty-twenty count makes two hundred forty, totaled as six hundred sixty-six"—complex enumeration establishing **sacred mathematics**.

- **སྲིན་བུ་ཁྲི་ཕྲག་བརྒྱད་དང་སྟོང་ཕྲག་ལྔ།** (13502): "85,000 srin bu"—the classical number of **afflictions** (nyon mongs pa) in Abhidharma.

- **ཁྱབ་འཇུག་རུས་སྦལ་གྱི་རྣམ་པ་དང་འདྲ་བ།** (13521): "Like Vishnu-turtle form"—Hindu cosmological metaphor adapted to **subtle body anatomy**.

- **དབང་པོའི་སྒོ་གསལ་ལ་འགུལ་བའི་རླུང་མེད་པ།** (13522): "No moving wind at the clear sense-gates"—dbang po'i sgo (sense-gates) as **apertures** for consciousness.

**Numerical compounds:**
The passage uses **classical Indian large numbers**:
- 'bum phrag (hundred-thousand)
- khri phrag (ten-thousand)
- stong phrag (thousand)

### [CONTEXT]
**Abhidharma correlation:**
The 84,000 srin bu correspond to the **84,000 afflictions** countered by the **84,000 dharma-gates**. The text **somatizes** this teaching—afflictions are physiological, not merely mental.

**Hindu-Buddhist synthesis:**
The Vishnu-turtle metaphor (13521) reflects **Indian cosmology** where the world rests on a turtle. Here adapted to **subtle body**: the ālaya rests on srin bu like the world on a turtle.

**Medical etiology:**
Lines 13522-13524 present **wind-dysfunction** as root of disease. When wind doesn't move at sense-gates or limbs, disease arises. This parallels **Tibetan medical** (gso ba rig pa) wind-disorder theory.

### [CONCEPT]
**The body as computational mandala:**
The passage presents **sacred mathematics** where:
- 212 = mental factors
- 112 = sense bases  
- 18 = elements
- 16 = channels
- 5 + 5 = sense faculties + objects
- 31 = emotional elaborations

Total: 399 = approximately 400 gates to liberation, matching **Abhidharma** numerology.

**Srin bu as affliction-substrate:**
The 84,000 srin bu (13519-13522) establish that **afflictions are embodied**. Each srin bu contains:
- A letter (yi ge)
- A kāya (sku)
- A wisdom-being (ye shes sems dpa')

This demonstrates **the non-duality of affliction and wisdom**: same substrate, different recognition.

**Wind as physiological governor:**
Line 13522 specifies that **rlung gi 'gyur byed** (wind-transformation) governs health. When wind doesn't function at sense-gates, disease arises. This establishes **prāṇa** as the master physiological regulator.
