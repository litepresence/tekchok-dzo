[1041] [PHILOLOGY] Divine Society and Sensory Culture
**Analysis:**

The social dimension of the Thirty-three gods' realm:
- *lha'i bu dang bu mo* — Gods' sons and daughters
- *glu dang sil snyan* — Songs and cymbals/music
- *'khrol bas gang ba* — Filled with playing/performance

**Grammar Note:** *'khrol ba* (to play music) with instrumental *-s* indicates ongoing activity, suggesting perpetual entertainment as characteristic of this realm.

[1042-1044] [STRUCTURE] The Common City (*spyi'i 'gro khyer*)
**Analysis:**

Longchenpa distinguishes between:
1. The "Common Beautiful-to-see" (*spyi'i 'gro khyer blta na sdug*) — general urban area
2. The outer cyclic existence (*'khor bar*) — peripheral pleasure palaces

**Urban Features:**
- *nang gi sa gzhi* — Interior ground
- *lam srang* — Streets
- *yid du 'ong ba* — Mind-pleasing
- *dus bzhir me tog gi char 'bab pa* — Flower rain falling in four seasons

This reflects the *kalpavṛkṣa* motif where divine environments spontaneously produce sensory delights.

[1043] [CONCEPT] Architectural Dimensions
**Analysis:**

**Jewel Mansions (*rin po che'i khang brtsegs*):**
- Width: 4,500 leagues per side (*ngos re dpag tshad bzhi bzhi stong lnga brgya pa*)
- Height: 3.5 leagues (*dpangs su phyed dang gsum 'phags pa*)

These dimensions exceed physical possibility, indicating symbolic or visionary geography rather than literal measurement.

[1045-1047] [CONTEXT] Urban Hydrology and Flora
**Analysis:**

**Fortification:**
- *rin po che sna bdun gyi ra ba bdun* — Seven walls of seven jewels
- Interval (*bar na*) filled with falling water (*'bab chus yongs su gang ba*)

**Botanical Features:**
- *pad+ma la sogs pa* — Lotus and similar flowers
- *me tog gi tshogs rgyas* — Expanding flower groups
- *bya skad snyan par sgrogs pa* — Birds proclaiming sweetly

**Technology:**
- *rin po che'i gru lding ba* — Flying jewel boats

This represents the fusion of natural beauty with technological marvel in divine realms.

[1048-1049] [PHILOLOGY] The Intermediate Cities (*mtshams gyi grong*)
**Analysis:**

**Location:** At four corners (*mtshams bzhi*) of Meru's peak

**Structure:**
- *khang brtsegs lnga brgya pa* — 500 stacked mansions
- *lha'i grong gis brgyan pa bzhi* — Four cities adorned by gods
- *thang ra ba* — Plain/flat walls
- *dpag tshad lnga pas sbrel ba* — Connected by 5-league walls

**Guardians:**
- *gnod sbyin lag na rdo rje mched bzhi* — Four Vajra-hand yaksha siblings
- *lha rigs* — God race/lineage

These correspond to the Four Great Kings' extended retinue, protecting the periphery of the central divine realm.

[1050-1054] [CONTEXT] The Four Directional Gardens
**Analysis:**

**Dimensions:** 20 leagues in each direction (*phyogs bzhir dpag tshad nyi shu nyi shu*)

**Cardinal Gardens:**

**East (*shar*):**
- *shing rta sna tshogs kyi skyed mos tshal* — Chariot Various park
- Features: ponds, clothes, chariots
- Function: Social/transportation gathering

**South (*lho*):**
- *rtsub 'gyur gyi skyed mos tshal* — Rough-change park
- Feature: Hard armors arise during battle (*g.yul 'gyed pa'i tshe go cha sra ba*)
- Function: Martial preparation

**West (*nub*):**
- *longs spyod 'dres pa'i skyed mos tshal* — Enjoyment-mix park
- Feature: Immeasurable enjoyments (*dpag tu med pa*)
- Function: Sensory pleasure

**North (*byang*):**
- *kun tu dga' ba'i skyed mo tshal* — Everywhere-joy park
- Features: Free from all sorrow (*mya ngan thams cad dang bral*), brilliant joys (*mdangs dga' ba*)
- Function: Meditative bliss

**Doxographical Note:** These four gardens correspond to the four immeasurables (*tshad med bzhi*) in corrupted form—practiced with attachment, they produce god-realm experiences rather than liberation.

[1055-1058] [PHILOLOGY] Detailed Garden Features
**Analysis:**

**Dimensions:**
- Width: 250 leagues (*chu zheng dpag tshad nyis brgya lnga bcu pa*)
- Shape: Beautiful form (*dbyibs legs shing blta na sdug pa*)

**Ground Composition:**
- *gser gyi bye ma* — Gold sand/pebbles
- *rin po che sna tshogs kyi rjod zhing* — Various jewel flower beds
- *mtsho dang rdzing* — Lakes and ponds

**Exterior Ground (*phyi rol na sa gzhi*):**
- Shares names with interior features (*ming mtshungs pa*)
- Decorated with flowers and ponds

**Structural Note:** The recursive naming (*de dag dang ming mtshungs pa*) suggests fractal or holographic cosmology where macrocosm and microcosm mirror each other—a key theme in Dzogchen metaphysics that Longchenpa will develop later in the text.
