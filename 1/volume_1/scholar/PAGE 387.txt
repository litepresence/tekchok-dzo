[15889-15925] [STRUCTURE] Second Vision: Development of Experience
The text presents the second vision (nyams gong 'phel) as development of experience. The structure establishes: (1) increase of visionary appearances, (2) maturation of experience, and (3) progress toward stable recognition.

[15889-15925] [PHILOLOGY] Second Vision Terminology
The term nyams gong 'phel gyi snang ba (vision of developing experience) identifies the second stage. The compound snang ba mched pa (increasing vision) describes the process. The phrase goms pa (familiarization) establishes the method.

[15889-15925] [CONTEXT] Deepening Practice
The text presents the second vision as the deepening and maturation of practice. Experience becomes more stable and extensive. This reflects the Dzogchen path's progression from recognition to stabilization.

[15889-15925] [CONCEPT] Development as Maturation
The text conceptualizes the second vision as the maturation of what was recognized in the first vision. Like a seed growing into a plant, recognition develops into stable experience. Development is natural when recognition is present.
