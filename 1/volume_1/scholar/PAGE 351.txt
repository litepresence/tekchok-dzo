[14303-14339] [STRUCTURE] Heart Wheel: Dharma Center
The text presents the heart wheel (snying ga chos kyi 'khor lo) as the Dharma center. The structure establishes: (1) heart as the fire element center, (2) eight channels of the heart wheel, and (3) heart as the seat of consciousness.

[14303-14339] [CONTEXT] Heart as Consciousness Seat
The text presents the heart as the seat of consciousness and emotional center. The eight channels correspond to the eight consciousnesses. This reflects the Buddhist understanding of heart-mind (sems) as the center of experience.

[14303-14339] [CONCEPT] Heart as Wisdom Source
The text conceptualizes the heart wheel as the source of wisdom. While the head is often associated with intellect in Western traditions, in Buddhism the heart is the seat of both emotion and wisdom. The heart center integrates feeling and knowing.
