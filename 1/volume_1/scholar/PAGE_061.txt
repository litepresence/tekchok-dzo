[2033-2098] [STRUCTURE] Enumeration of the 360 Components (Continued)
**Analysis:**

This section completes the enumeration of the 360 erroneous positions (*'jig tshogs sum brgya drug cu*) constituting the mistaken vehicles (*log pa'i theg pa*).

**Philological Note:** The term *'jig tshogs* (transitory collection) refers to the five aggregates as impermanent, composite phenomena—the primary basis for grasping at self (*bdag 'dzin*).

**Organization from *Rig pa rang shar*:**

**The 300 Spaces (*dbyings*):**
These represent the underlying ontological ground posited by various non-Buddhist schools—the cosmic principles, elemental substrates, and universal selves that serve as the foundation for their metaphysical systems.

**The 60 Views (*lta ba*):**

**Categories of Eternalism (*rtag lta*):**

The text systematically enumerates schools propounding permanence:

1. **Samkhya** (*grangs can*) — enumerationists
2. **Vaisesika** (*bye brag pa*) — particularists  
3. **Kumaralabha** (*ku ma ra la chen po*) — great Kumaralabdha
4. **Ear-followers** (*rna ma phug pa*) — scriptural literalists
5. **Five Fires practitioners** (*gsang ba me lnga*) — fire ascetics
6. **Body-burners** (*me ras til mar lus bsreg*) — cremation-ground practitioners
7. **Spear-tip ascetics** (*mdung rtse'i dka' thub*) — self-mortification
8. **Sacrifice practitioners** (*mchod dang 'tshog pa'i dka' thub*) — ritualists
9. **Flesh-chariot makers** (*sha skor lus la byas 'thog*) — tantric materialists
10. **Dog-conduct** (*khyi yi cha byad spyod pa*) — mimetic ascetics

**Philosophical Variations:**

The 60 views include positions on:
- **Agency** (*byed pa*): as cause, as path
- **Permanence** (*rtag pa*): of cause, result, occasion, path, conduct
- **Meaning** (*don*): permanent meaning as nonexistent (the synthesis view)
- **Activity** (*bya byed*): dependence on actions, refutation of others

**Nihilist Categories (*chad lta*):**

The enumeration includes extreme positions:
- Cause severance (*rgyu chad*)
- Result severance (*'bras bu chad*)
- Path severance (*lam chad*)
- Wisdom severance (*ye shes chad*)
- Great severance of emptiness (*stong pa chen po*)

**Tirthika Typology:**

The text presents nine types of tirthikas (*mu stegs*):
1. Secret Vedas speakers (*rig byed gsang bar smra*)
2. Similar wrongness (*cha mthun gol ba*)
3. Manifest mind (*mngon snang sems kyi*)
4. Confused appearance grasping ('*khrul snang 'dzin pa*)
5. Anger-producing (*zhe sdang skyed pa*)
6. Activity and mind (*bya byed sems kyi*)
7. Grasping meaningless ('*dzin pa don med*)
8. Erroneous speech (*kha tshig gol ba*)
9. Permanent meaning as nonexistent (*rtag par don med*)

**Named Proponents:**

The text lists historical figures:
- Kani (*ka ni*)
- Bhasira (*bha si ra*)
- Kabisha (*ka bhi sha*)
- Sindhara (*sin dha ra*)
- Dhasim (*dha sing*)
- Kati (*ka ti*)
- Samgha (*sang gha*)
- Sankari (*shang ka ri*)
- Dhasaka (*dha sha ka*)

These represent the diverse teachers and lineages of non-Buddhist India during Longchenpa's time.
