[15112-15148] [STRUCTURE] Body Channels and Kāya Manifestation
The text presents the thirty-eight body channels and their manifestation as the nirmāṇakāya. The structure establishes: (1) channels corresponding to the ten directions, (2) channels for the various body aspects, and (3) the complete manifestation form.

[15112-15148] [PHILOLOGY] Body Kāya Terminology
The term sku yi sum cu rtsa brgyad (thirty-eight body channels) establishes the physical dimension. The compound phyogs bcu (ten directions) identifies spatial orientation. The phrase 'gyur med rdo rje'i sku (vajra body of unchange) describes the ultimate form.

[15112-15148] [CONTEXT] Nirmāṇakāya Physiology
The text presents the nirmāṇakāya as manifesting through specific channel groups. The thirty-eight channels produce the thirty-two major marks and eighty minor marks. This reflects the Buddhist understanding of the Buddha's body as the result of complete purification.

[15112-15148] [CONCEPT] Marks as Channel Manifestations
The text conceptualizes the thirty-two marks and eighty signs as manifestations of purified channels. Physical beauty in the Buddha is not mere aesthetics but the visible form of inner purity. The body shows the mind's realization.
