[14562-14598] [STRUCTURE] Self-Arising Wisdom Lamp
The text presents the self-arising wisdom lamp (shes rab rang byung gi sgron ma). The structure establishes: (1) essence as self-arising without cause, (2) recognition of wisdom beyond cultivation, and (3) the fourth lamp completing the set.

[14562-14598] [PHILOLOGY] Self-Arising Terminology
The term rang byung (self-arisen) describes spontaneous manifestation without cause. The compound shes rab (wisdom/prajñā) identifies its cognitive nature. The phrase sgron ma bzhi (four lamps) completes the enumeration.

[14562-14598] [CONTEXT] Spontaneous Wisdom
The text presents wisdom as self-arising rather than cultivated. The fourth lamp represents the culmination of thödgal practice where wisdom appears without effort. This reflects Dzogchen's emphasis on naturalness.

[14562-14598] [CONCEPT] Wisdom as Natural Radiance
The text conceptualizes wisdom as the natural radiance of awareness. Not produced by causes, it appears when conditions allow. The self-arising wisdom lamp represents the effortless perfection of recognition.
