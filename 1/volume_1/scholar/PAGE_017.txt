[510] [CONTEXT] # Citation from Ati-koṭa-chen-po'i-rgyud
**Analysis:** The text cites the *Ati-koṭa-chen-po'i-rgyud* (Great Ati-crown Tantra), also known as the *gYu 'phrul* (Turquoise Illusion) or *gYu 'phrul chen po* (Great Turquoise Illusion), one of the seventeen tantras (*rgyud bcu bdun*) of the *Mengagde* (Upadeśa) section of Dzogchen. The specification *g.yu yig can* (turquoise-script possessing) indicates this text's transmission through symbolic script.

[511-518] [STRUCTURE] # Nirmāṇakāya Body Activities
**Analysis:** The thirty-six activities framework continues, now applied specifically to the Nirmāṇakāya emanation-body. The body's external four activities (*sku'i phyi'i mdzad pa bzhi*) relate to the historical Buddha's life-events (*bde bar gshegs pa'i mdzad pa*):

1. **Birth** (*bltams pa*) — abandoning the "river of birth" (*sku skye ba'i chu bo*)
2. **Marriage** (*phong*) — actually *gyon* (dressing/donning), contextually marriage
3. **Skill competition** (*sgyu rtsal 'gran pa*) — demonstrating mastery over rivals
4. **Renunciation** (*mngon par byung ba*) — leaving the palace
5. **Alms-gathering** (*bsod snyoms la spyod pa*) — the mendicant's round

The "four rivers of suffering" (*sdug bsngal gyi chu bo bzhi*) are the classical formulation: birth, aging, sickness, and death.

[519-525] [STRUCTURE] # Internal Body Activities
**Analysis:** The internal four activities symbolize renunciation and bodhisattva activity:

1. **Abandoning retinue and enjoyment** (*'khor dang longs spyod spang ba*)
2. **Excellent horse** (*rta mchog*) and **chariot abandonment** (*shing rto dor ba*) — royal vehicles renounced
3. **Essence going to bodhi** (*snying po byang chub tu 'gro ba*) — approaching the bodhi-tree
4. **Emanating light-rays** (*'od zer bkaye ba*)
5. **Transforming weapon-rain to flowers** (*mtshon cha'i char pa me tog tu sgyur ba*) — the famous episode of subduing Māra's army

[526-531] [STRUCTURE] # Secret Body Activities
**Analysis:** The secret four activities (*gsang ba'i bzhi*) focus on samādhi mastery:

1. **Austerity performance** (*dka' thub mdzad pa*) — six years of ascetic practice
2. **Meru's golden samādhi sounds** — manifesting 120,000 samādhi sounds (*ting nge 'dzin gyi sgra 'bum phrag brgya nyi shu*) in a single instant
3. **Precious casket samādhi** (*rin po che za ma tog gi ting nge 'dzin*) — constant abiding
4. **Vajra-like samādhi** (*rdo rje lta bu'i ting nge 'dzin*) — achieving bodhi

[532-536] [STRUCTURE] # Speech Activities: The Twelve Divisions
**Analysis:** The speech activities correspond to the standard twelve divisions of Buddhist scripture (*gsung rab yan lag bcu gnyis*):

**Outer four:**
1. *mdo sde* — Sūtra-section
2. *dbyangs su bsnyad pa* — Geya (verse intoned)
3. *lung bstan pa* — Vyākaraṇa (prophecy)
4. *tshigs su bcad pa'i sde* — Gāthā (verse section)

[537-541] [STRUCTURE] # Internal Speech Activities
**Analysis:** The internal four:
1. *shin tu rgyas pa* — Vaipulya (extensive sūtras)
2. *skyes pa rabs* — Jātaka (birth stories)
3. *gleng gzhi brjod pa* — Nidāna (origin stories)
4. *de ltar byung ba* — Itivṛttaka ("thus it was said")

[542-546] [STRUCTURE] # Secret Speech Activities
**Analysis:** The secret four:
1. *ched du brjod pa* — Udāna (inspired utterances)
2. *rtogs pa brjod pa* — Avadāna (narratives of realization)
3. *glegs bam lung* — Nipāta (miscellaneous collections)
4. *rmad du byung ba'i chos* — Adbhūtadharma (marvelous events)

[547-561] [STRUCTURE] # Mind Activities: Samādhi Enumerations
**Analysis:** Mind activities are presented through specific samādhi names:

**Outer four:**
1. *dpal phreng gi ting nge 'dzin* — Śūraṅgama-samādhi
2. *rin po che tog gi ting nge 'dzin* — Ratnolkā (jewel-crest)
3. *rnam par rtog pa'i ting nge 'dzin* — Vicāra-samādhi
4. *za ma tog gi ting nge 'dzin* — Kāraṇḍavyūha (casket)

**Inner four:**
1. *yul thams cad la 'jug pa'i ting nge 'dzin* — Entering all objects
2. *mngon par snang ba'i ting nge 'dzin* — Manifest appearance
3. *gtsug tor rin po che'i ting nge 'dzin* — Uṣṇīṣa-jewel
4. *rgyal mtshan rtse mo'i bkod pa'i ting nge 'dzin* — Banner-peak arranged

**Secret four:**
1. *dag pa'i rnam par 'phrul ba'i ting nge 'dzin* — Pure movement
2. *zab mo'i snang ba'i ting nge 'dzin* — Profound appearance
3. *rin po che sgron ma'i ting nge 'dzin* — Jewel-lamp
4. *bde ba mchog gi ting nge 'dzin* — Supreme good/bliss

[562-564] [STRUCTURE] # Summary: 108 Activities
**Analysis:** The text summarizes that the three kāyas (emanation, enjoyment, and dharma-body) manifest 108 activities (*mdzad pa brgya dang brgyad*). This number (108 = 36 × 3) represents the complete mandala of enlightened activity. The mathematical structure reflects the systematic nature of Nyingma doxography.
