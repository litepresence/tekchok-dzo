## PAGE 331 [13566-13600]

### [STRUCTURE]
The passage presents **taste-wheel yoga** and **fifty-six day fire development**. Lines 13566-13600 establish:

1. **Taste-wheel function** (13566-13584): Six tastes producing aggregates, elements, and sense bases
2. **Digestive alchemy** (13571-13576): Taste-essences creating radiance and vitality
3. **Fifty-six day fire maturation** (13585-13591): Heat activating latent faculties
4. **Numerological synthesis** (13591-13600): 32 + 32 + 64 + 96 = 600

The structure integrates **Ayurvedic rasāyana** with tantric physiology.

### [PHILOLOGY]
**Technical terminology:**

- **རོ་རྣམས་འདུས་པའི་འཁོར་ལོ།** (13568): "Wheel of gathered tastes"—navel chakra as **digestive center**.

- **སྙིགས་མ་དྲི་ཆེན་དང་དྲི་ཆུ་ལ་སོགས་པ་དོར་བར་བྱེད་དོ།** (13570): "Defilements, feces, urine, etc. are eliminated"—snyigs ma (defilement) as **digestive byproducts**.

- **མངར་བས་བཀྲག་བསྐྱེད།** (13571): "Sweet produces radiance"—mngar (sweet) → bkra gs (radiance). **Ayurvedic** physiology: sweet taste nourishes bodily luster.

- **ལན་ཚྭའི་རོས་བྱང་སེམས་བསྐྱེད་པའི་ཕྱིར་རོ་རྣམས་ཞེས་བྱའོ།** (13576): "Called 'tastes' because salty taste produces bodhicitta"—salty taste linked to **bodhicitta generation**, showing **physiological-moral correspondence**.

- **ཟས་ཀྱི་རྣལ་འབྱོར་འགྲུབ་པའོ།** (13578): "Accomplishment of food-yoga"—zas kyi rnal 'byor (food-yoga) = **rasāyana** practice.

**Grammatical structures:**
The **instrumental of means** ཀྱིས། appears extensively for **taste-functions**. The **purposive** པར་བྱེད། marks **intentional production**.

### [CONTEXT]
**Ayurvedic integration:**
The six tastes (ro drug) follow **Ayurvedic** categories:
1. Mngar (sweet)
2. Kha (astringent)
3. Bska (pungent)
4. Skyur (sour)
5. Kleg (bitter)
6. Lan tshva (salty)

Each produces specific physiological effects.

**Rasāyana theory:**
Food-yoga (zas kyi rnal 'byor) and vital extraction (bcud len) are **rasāyana** (rejuvenation) practices. The text integrates **Indian alchemical** physiology with Buddhist soteriology.

**Embryological timing:**
The fifty-six days (zhag lnga bcu) mark **eight weeks** of gestation, when fire-element heat activates the latent faculties (dbang po). This parallels **Western embryology** where week 8 marks major organ development.

### [CONCEPT]
**Taste as path vehicle:**
The text presents **sophisticated taste-yoga**: each of the six tastes produces specific effects:
- Sweet → radiance (bkra)
- Astringent → vitality (mdangs)
- Pungent → splendor (gzi brjid)
- Sour → strength (stobs)
- Bitter → vigor (shed)
- Salty → bodhicitta

This demonstrates **sensory transformation**: ordinary taste becomes **yogic tool**.

**Digestive alchemy:**
The navel wheel functions as **alchemical vessel**: essences (dangs ma) are extracted, defilements (snyigs ma) eliminated. This is **internal rasāyana**—the body refines itself.

**Fire as activating principle:**
The fifty-six day fire-maturation (13585-13591) establishes **agni** (fire) as the **activating principle**. Like digestive fire transforms food, the fire of gestation transforms latent faculties into active capacities.
