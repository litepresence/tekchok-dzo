[3278-3284] [CONCEPT] Three-Level Purification in Yoga Tantra
**Analysis:** Lines 3278-3284 present the threefold purification schema of Yoga Tantra:

**Three Levels:**
1. **Outer** (*phyi*) [3278]: Purification of five aggregates (*phung po lnga*) and five elements (*'byung ba lnga*)
2. **Inner** (*nang*) [3278]: Purification of five actions (*las lnga*) and five afflictions (*nyon mongs pa lnga*)
3. **Secret** (*gsang ba*) [3279]: Purification of five objects (*yul lnga*) and five sense-powers (*dbang po lnga*)

**Result:**
- *rigs lnga'i sangs rgyas su bdag nyid gyur pa* — Self becomes Buddha of five families [3280]
- Through viewing deity and self as siblings/friends (*spun dang grogs ltar*) [3281]
- *dngos grub rang las 'byung* — Siddhis arise from oneself [3283]
- Recognition that deity has no good cause to bestow (*bzang rgyu med par shes*) [3282]

**Superiority:**
- Realizing self has no bad cause (*bdag ngan rgyu med par rtogs*)
- *sgyu lus lha'i skur gsal* — Illusory body clearly as deity body [3284]
- Superior to lower approaches (*'og ma las khyad par du 'phags*)

[3285-3286] [PHILOLOGY] Non-Duality of Deity and Self
**Analysis:** Key doctrinal statement:

**Line 3285:**
- *bskyed rgyu'i lha dang bdag gnyis gnyis su med par rtogs* — Realizing generated-cause deity and self are non-dual

**Line 3286:**
- *don dam dang kun rdzob kyi ming med pa* — No name of ultimate and conventional

This indicates the Yoga Tantra view transcends both the dualistic structure of Kriya (lord-servant) and even the conventional framework itself.

[3287-3310] [CONTEXT] Scriptural Quotation: Rig pa rang shar
**Analysis:** Lines 3288-3309 quote from *Rig pa rang shar* establishing Yoga Tantra view:

**Key Elements:**
- [3289]: "Yoga's established tenet is like this"
- [3290]: Entry door is five enlightenments (*mngon par byang chub lnga*)
- [3291]: View of realization: deity and [yogi as] companions
- [3292]: Protecting samaya: three non-shames (*ma khrel gsum*)
- [3293]: Conduct without rejection-acceptance (*dgag sgrub med pa*)
- [3294-3299]: Enumeration of five enlightenments through seat, body, mudra, generation, letters
- [3300]: "Thus practicing the five enlightenments"
- [3301-3304]: Three non-shames: by yidam deity, by master-disciple, by one's own mind
- [3305-3309]: Deity and yogi as siblings; non-dual mandala; deity arises from and dissolves into self

[3311-3321] [STRUCTURE] Introduction to Mahayoga
**Analysis:** Line 3311 introduces the seventh topic (*don bdun pa*) — Mahayoga Tantra (*rgyud ma ha yo ga'i theg pa*).

**Superiority Over Lower Tantras:**
1. **Outer three** (*phyi pa gsum*) abstain from meat, etc.; practice bathing/cleanliness; endure austerities — causing distress (*gdungs*) [3314-3315]
2. **Inner practitioners** (*nang pa*) consume five meats (*sha lnga*), five nectars (*bdud rtsi lnga*); rely on consorts (*rig ma*) — no acceptance/rejection, no pure/impure [3316-3317]
3. **Lower cannot** (*og ma*) practice deity-face union (*lha zhal sbyor*) [3318-3319]
4. **Outer view** (*phyi pa*) doesn't realize non-dual truth; **inner** realizes it [3320-3321]

**Key Distinction:** Mahayoga transcends both purity (Kriya) and antinomianism through view (*lta ba*), not mere conduct.

[3322-3327] [CONCEPT] Mahayoga: Essence, Etymology, Purpose, Result
**Analysis:** Lines 3322-3327 present the standard fourfold definition:

**1. Essence (*ngo bo*) [3323-3324]:**
- *bd en pa dbyer med dag mnyam ye shes kyi rol pas* — Play of wisdom of non-dual pure equality
- *tshe gcig gis grol ba thob pa'i rang bzhin* — Nature of liberation in one lifetime

**2. Etymology (*nges tshig*) [3325]:**
- *tshegs chung ngu'i thabs kyis don chen po 'grub pa* — Great aim accomplished through small effort
- Therefore called "Inner Method Vehicle" (*nang pa thabs kyi theg pa*)

**3. Purpose (*dgos pa*) [3326]:**
- *tshe gcig gis dngos grub gnyis thob par byed* — Accomplishing two siddhis in one life

**4. Result ('bras bu) [3327]:**
- *tshe 'di nyid du rdo rje 'chang gi sar bgrod* — Proceeding to Vajradhara's level in this life

[3328-3334] [STRUCTURE] Threefold Division of Mahayoga
**Analysis:** Mahayoga divides into three:

1. **Tantra Mahayoga** (*rgyud ma ha yo ga*) [3328]
2. **Scripture Anuyoga** (*lung a nu yo ga*) [3329]
3. **Instruction Atiyoga** (*man ngag rdzogs pa chen po a ti yo ga*) [3330]

**Scriptural Quotation [3331-3334]:** From *Rang shar*:
- "Inner is threefold: Maha, Anu, Ati"

This is the crucial Nyingma classification of Inner Tantras, with Atiyoga (Dzogchen) as the pinnacle.

[3335-3343] [CONCEPT] Tantra Mahayoga: Ground, Path, Fruition
**Analysis:** Tantra Mahayoga practice [3335-3336]:
- *thabs bskyed pa'i rim pa gtso bo* — Generation stage as primary method
- *ting nge 'dzin gsum* — Three samadhis as door
- *bden pa dbyer med chen pos gsal stong 'od gsal gyi ye shes sgom pa* — Meditating on clear-empty luminosity wisdom through great non-dual truth

**Three Approaches to Ground, Path, Fruition [3338-3342]:**
1. **Ground-path-fruition focused** (*gzhi lam 'bras bu gtso bo*):
   - Ground as cause to be made clear (*gsal bar bya ba'i rgyu*)
   - Mind-ultimate free from elaboration (*sems nyid don dam spros bral chen po*)
   - Distinguished by effortlessness (*'bad rtsol byar med*)

2. **Accomplishment-focused** (*sgrub pa gtso bo*) [3345-3346]

3. **Various-path-transform-focused** (*sna tshogs lam 'gyur du 'dod pa*) [3340]

**Line 3343 Simile:**
- Ground-essence (*ngo bo'i gzhi*) and mind are non-dual
- Like gold (*gser*) and its yellowness (*ser po*) — non-dual yet distinguishable
