[15778-15814] [STRUCTURE] Conduct in Daily Life
The text presents Dzogchen conduct in the context of daily activities. The structure establishes: (1) eating as channels sustaining, (2) sleeping as awareness resting, (3) walking as natural movement, and (4) all activities as practice.

[15778-15814] [PHILOLOGY] Daily Activity Terminology
The term zas za ba (eating) identifies ordinary activity. The compound nyal ba (sleeping) establishes rest activity. The phrase 'gro ba (walking) identifies movement activity.

[15778-15814] [CONTEXT] Integration of Practice and Life
The text presents Dzogchen as integrated with all activities, not separate from daily life. There is no distinction between practice time and ordinary time. This reflects Dzogchen's emphasis on continuous practice.

[15778-15814] [CONCEPT] Activities as Practice
The text conceptualizes all activities as practice when recognized. Eating is feeding the channels; sleeping is resting in awareness; walking is natural movement. Recognition transforms the ordinary into the sacred.
