[15852-15888] [STRUCTURE] First Vision: Direct Perception
The text presents the first vision (chos nyid mngon sum) as direct perception of dharmatā. The structure establishes: (1) recognition of awareness's nature, (2) perception of the four lamps, and (3) the beginning of the path of vision.

[15852-15888] [PHILOLOGY] First Vision Terminology
The term chos nyid mngon sum gyi snang ba (vision of direct perception of dharmatā) identifies the first stage. The compound sgron ma bzhi (four lamps) establishes what is perceived. The phrase rig pa'i rang gdangs (awareness's own glow) describes the content.

[15852-15888] [CONTEXT] Entry into Vision
The text presents the first vision as the entry point into thödgal practice. It is the first stable recognition of natural luminosity. This reflects the Dzogchen path's emphasis on direct perception from the beginning.

[15852-15888] [CONCEPT] Direct Perception as Basis
The text conceptualizes direct perception as the basis of the path. Unlike gradual paths that build to perception, Dzogchen begins with it. The first vision is recognition; subsequent visions are its stabilization and deepening.
