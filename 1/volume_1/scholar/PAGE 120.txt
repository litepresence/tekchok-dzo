[4488-4501] [STRUCTURE] Five Names, One Nature

**Analysis:** Lines 4488-4501 establish the unity of five names referring to a single nature.

**Five Names [4488-4489]:**

| # | Name | Tibetan | Aspect |
|---|------|---------|--------|
| 1 | Ground | *gzhi* | Basis |
| 2 | Essence | *snying po* | Core |
| 3 | Essence | *bcud* | Distillation |
| 4 | Method | *thabs* | Skillful means |
| 5 | Nature | *rang bzhin* | Inherent quality |

*ming tha dad pa lnga* — five different names
*don ngo bo gcig las ma 'das* — meaning/nature not transcending one

[4491-4501] [PHILOLOGY] Mañjuśrīnāmasaṃgīti Quotation

**Analysis:** Lines 4491-4501 quote the *Mañjuśrīnāmasaṃgīti* to establish the unity thesis.

**Quotation [4492-4500]:**

| Line | Tibetan | Translation | Significance |
|------|---------|-------------|--------------|
| 4492 | *gzhi gcig ngo bo'i khyad par las* | From ground one essence distinction | Unity in diversity |
| 4493 | *thabs dang rang bzhin rgyud ces ni* | Method and nature tantra | Functional aspects |
| 4494 | *btags pa tsam du de snang yang* | Merely imputed, appearing | Conventional designation |
| 4495 | *ngo bo gzhi gcig rang bzhin stong* | Nature ground one nature empty | Ultimate emptiness |
| 4496 | *tha dad ma yin 'gyur ba med* | Non-different, unchanging | Absolute identity |

**Analogy [4497-4500]:**

*dper na skye bu gang zhig la / ming ni mang po yod gyur na* — "Like a person with many names"
*gang nas bos kyang de ltar snang* — "Called from anywhere, thus appears"
*'gyur med don kyang de bzhin no* — "Unchanging meaning likewise"

**Philological Note:** The *Mañjuśrīnāmasaṃgīti* is a tantra of the Yoga class, quoted here to establish Dzogchen's continuity with earlier tantric traditions.

**Thal 'gyur Quotation [4528-4541]:**

Enumerates the sixteen tantras derived from the *Thal 'gyur* (Direct Transmission) root:
- Self-arisen, self-liberated, self-born, potency perfect
- Beautiful, ornament spread, pearl garland, without letters
- Expanse, heart mirror, sun moon union, introduction ornament
- Body relic blazing, jewel heap, lamp blazing, six great expanses

**Pedagogical Organization:**

| Category | Tantras | Audience |
|----------|---------|----------|
| With elaboration (*spros bcas*) | Ground and essence tantras | All drop sections |
| Middling | Seventeen tantras | Standard practitioners |
| Excellent | Self-arisen seed tantra alone | Advanced practitioners |
| Summit | Three liberations | Highest capacity |

**Philosophical Implications:**

The nineteen tantra system demonstrates:
1. **Comprehensive coverage** — all aspects of Dzogchen path
2. **Progressive subtlety** — from elaborate to direct
3. **Pedagogical flexibility** — accommodating different capacities
4. **Scriptural authority** — grounded in revealed tantras

The unity of five names (ground, essence, essence, method, nature) underpinning all nineteen tantras establishes the fundamental Dzogchen view: all distinctions are conventional (*btags pa tsam*), the nature is singular (*ngo bo gcig*).
