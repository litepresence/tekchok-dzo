[14927-14963] [STRUCTURE] Result: Five Wisdoms in Channels
The text presents the five wisdoms (ye shes lnga) as the result of channel purification. The structure establishes: (1) fifty wisdom channels corresponding to five wisdoms, (2) channels of dharmadhātu, mirror-like, equality, discriminating, and all-accomplishing wisdoms, and (3) the thirty-eight body channels.

[14927-14963] [PHILOLOGY] Five Wisdoms Terminology
The term ye shes rnam pa 'khyil ba (wisdom winding/expanding) describes wisdom manifestation. The compound chos dbyings ye shes (dharmadhātu wisdom) identifies the first wisdom. The phrase me long ye shes (mirror-like wisdom) establishes the second.

[14927-14963] [CONTEXT] Wisdom Physiology
The text presents a wisdom physiology where physical channels embody enlightened cognition. Each wisdom has corresponding channels; purifying channels manifests wisdoms. This reflects the tantric integration of body and enlightenment.

[14927-14963] [CONCEPT] Channels as Wisdom Vessels
The text conceptualizes channels as vessels containing wisdom in latent form. When purified through practice, the channels manifest their wisdom nature. The body is thus the vessel of enlightenment, not its obstacle.
