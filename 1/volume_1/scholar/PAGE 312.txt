The text presents detailed instructions on cutting through practice. The structure establishes: (1) meditation posture and gaze, (2) the object of cutting—conceptual mind, (3) the method of cutting—direct seeing without thought, and (4) the result—resting in naked awareness.

The compound phyag rgya chen po (great seal, mahāmudrā) identifies the ultimate state. The phrase "rtog pa'i rgyu chod" (cutting the cause of concepts) describes the method.

[12983-13006] [CONTEXT] Contemplative Technique Detail
The detailed cutting through instructions provide specific contemplative techniques: posture, gaze, breathing, and mental approach. These technical details reflect the practical orientation of Dzogchen—view must be actualized through specific methods. The instructions bridge philosophical ground and practical application.

[12961-13006] [CONCEPT] Cutting Through as Direct Seeing
The text conceptualizes cutting through as direct seeing beyond conceptual mind. The "cut" is not an act of rejection but of recognition—seeing through concepts to the awareness beneath. The result is not a new state but the disclosure of what was always present. The practice is "effortless effort"—doing nothing while remaining undistracted.
