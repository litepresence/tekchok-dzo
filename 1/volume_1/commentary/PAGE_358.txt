[14754-14761] Listen here, dear practitioner! These channels are not for cutting with knives! Cutting this benefits all liver diseases. In the short rut of that, dwells the kha kha small heat channel—cut it for phlegm disease. At the bile corner of the elbow dwells dza, called the right upper channel—cut it for lung disease. At the left also dwells dza, the left upper channel—cut thus for yellow phlegm.

[14762-14778] At the center back of the knee, ta dwells straight—called the back channel neck completion—cut it for upper body disease. Between the thumb and forefinger of the hand, at the span of one tsen, dwells Ta—called the heart channel white woman—cut it for kidney, bile, and heart diseases. Straight at the center back, tha dwells—called the great back channel—cut it for back taste disease. At the outer corner of the little toe, da dwells—called the liver channel hron bu—cut it for hand disease. Straight at the back of the big toe, dha dwells—called the bile channel ghan dhe—cut it for head disease.

[14779-14793] At the back of the middle finger, na dwells—called the stomach branch—cut it for poison disease. At the back of the ring finger, ta dwells—called the spleen branch—cut it for spleen disease. Straight at the back of the ring finger, tsa dwells—called the kidney crooked channel—cut thus for kidney disease. At the back of the little toe, da letter exists—called the male crooked channel—cut it for male disease. At the center of the palm, dha letter dwells—called the black liver channel—cut it for male liver disease. These are not medical instructions, dear one! They point to where your energy knots! Recognize the knot and it unravels itself! Emaho!

—Patrul Rinpoche, fireside
