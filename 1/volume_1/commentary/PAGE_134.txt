Thus letters' meanings joined—all thus to be known (lines 149-151). Thus it is spoken (line 153).

Do you see the profundity? Each letter contains all dharmas! KA is perfectly complete. A is the limit of all dharmas. This is not mere symbolism. This is the recognition that each sound, each vibration, is the complete expression of reality. When you recite KA, you are expressing perfect completeness. When you recite A, you are expressing the limitlessness of dharmas.

But do not get lost in the letters! The Buddha's intention is clear: words and letters not existing, yet words' meaning greatly liberates (lines 13-19). The letters are like a raft. Use them to cross, then do not carry them on your back.
