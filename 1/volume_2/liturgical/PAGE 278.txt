In short, whatever appears to whoever, appears like the various good and bad dreams of yesterday. Appearing yet not established, they exercise in clear appearance of non-existence.

Second, particular explanation: from four. The appearance of the nature-remaining intermediate state: by the binding of the five aggregates of form, etc., the appearance of the five bodies self-ceases; by the binding of elements and sense-fields, the appearance of light and color self-ceases; by the binding of karma and afflictions, the appearance of wisdom and dharmata self-ceases. To outer appearing objects, inner grasping mind attaches, exercising day and night like a wheel in the depth of the difficult-to-cross water river of four errors, exercising in many karma and latent tendencies.

The four errors: not knowing the nature of samsara's confusion-appearance resembling dream and illusion, grasping impermanent as permanent, untrue as true, suffering cause-result as pleasure, and empty-by-nature as thing and mark.

As antidotes to these, the Conqueror proclaimed the four summaries of Dharma for guiding ordinary individual birth-beings.

The appearance of the death-time intermediate state: elements of body disturbed, outer body's suffering is felt; mental-factor feeling's path lost, inner mind's suffering is felt; latent-tendency grasping's lake overflows, the suffering of separating from this life's appearance though attached is felt; remembering self-harming non-dharma karma, the suffering of fearing to fall into later negative paths' precipice is felt; remembering the cherished body of attachment-grasping, who feels the suffering of fearing the support-aggregate's castle will collapse.
