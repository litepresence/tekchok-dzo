Now ground path! How it connects to view and maturation.

Outer three practices: cause-effect emptiness, five perfections, ten paramitas—these accumulate merit through action, wisdom through study. Seeing dharmas as empty accumulates merit, perfecting ten perfections ripens faculties.

Inner three practices: cause-effect emptiness, five perfections, ten paramitas—these accumulate wisdom through hearing, merit through conduct. Study accumulates wisdom, conduct accumulates merit—supportive for realizing emptiness.

Then show all perfections become conduct: when bodhicitta arises, all six perfections arise naturally—like snow melting into water.

Then three pith teachings—outer: སྐད་གསུམ་བརྒྱུད་པ་དགོང་སྣང་བའཇུག་པ་མཁར་བ།

Middle: གཡེ་དགོང་སྙི་མ་ཡོན་བ་ན་འདི་མ་སྣང་བ་ན་འདི་མ་ཡོན་བ།

Inner: རྒྱོན་དགོང་སྙི་མ་ཡོན་བ་ན་འདི་མ་སྣང་བ་ན་འདི་མ་ཡོན་བ།

Very secret: མཁར་བ།

Dzogchen's supreme advantage: ground path directly accomplishes view and maturation! Outer vehicles need step-by-step accumulation—like building a house brick by brick. Dzogchen accomplishes all at once—like arriving instantly at your destination.

Emaho! What takes lifetimes gradually, happens in an instant!
