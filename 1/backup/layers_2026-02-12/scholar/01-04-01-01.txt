[1910-1942] [CONTEXT]
Samkhya Eternalism
**Analysis:**

Longchenpa identifies the Samkhya (*grangs can pa*) as the foremost (*gzhung phyi mo*) of eternalist traditions. The presentation draws from standard Buddhist doxography.

**The Samkhya Self (*bdag*):**

The self is defined as *rdul mun pa snying stobs gsum cha mnyam pa'i ngo bo* — "the essence of three equal portions of darkness-dust, potency."

**The Three Qualities (*guna*):**

1. **Darkness/Dust** (*rdul mun pa* / *tamas*) — inertia
2. **Potency** (*snying stobs* / *rajas*) — activity
3. **[Implied] Illumination** — clarity (*sattva*)

**Physical Location:**
The self resides in the heart (*snying*) as:
- Large: thumb-sized (*mthe bong tsam*)
- Small: sesame-pod sized (*sran ma'i ga'u*)
- Smaller: sesame-grain sized (*til gyi nyag ma*)

**Qualities of the Self:**
- White (*dkar pa*)
- Oily (*snrum pa*)
- Coiled (*'dril ba*)
- Unbreakable (*gzhig cing gzhom du med*)
- Hidden (*lkog gyur du yod pa*)

**Liberation Theory:**

When the three afflictions are in equilibrium (*gsum cha mnyam pa*), the self knows itself (*rang gis rig*). Upon liberation (*rnam par grol ba*), it appears at the top of the heavens (*mtho ris*) like a white umbrella (*gdugs dkar po*).

**Current State:**
- Without movement (*g.yo ba med pa*)
- Free from action/activity (*bya byed dang bral ba*)
- Deeply knowing objects (*yul rig pas zab po*)
- Eternally existing (*dus rtag par yod pa*)

**Critique:** Not knowing this self-nature causes samsara; knowing it leads to liberation. Ritual practice (*mchod sbyin*) accumulates power for rebirth in divine realms.

**Doxographic Citation:** Longchenpa cites *'Jug pa* (Chandrakirti's *Madhyamakavatara* I.113-114):

```
The self that is not the consumer, the eternal entity, the agent
Examined by the Samkhyas without qualities
Based on slight distinctions,
The traditions of the Tirthikas became different.
```

[1944-1974] [CONTEXT]
Lokayata Materialism
**Analysis:**

Longchenpa presents the *rgyang 'phan pa* (Lokayata/Carvaka) school, representing Indian materialism. The school's fundamental tenet: denial of previous/future lives, karma, cause-effect, and liberation.

**Two Subdivisions:**

1. **Cause-Nihilism** (*rgyu chad par smra ba*)
2. **Result-Nihilism** ('*bras bu chad par smra ba*)

**Cause-Nihilism Position:**

The son of the naked one (*gcer bu pa gnyen gyi bu*) and others propose:
- Mind is not continuous from previous to present
- A "latent mind" (*bag la zhayi sems*) exists inherently in the four elements
- This latent mind, when compressed (*rgyas 'thums*) and meeting conditions of heat/moisture, arises
- New mind enters new body, becoming sentient being

**Pervasive Mind Theory:**

Earth contains latent mind, producing:
- Plants, trees, forests (*rtsi shing dang nags tshal*)
- Water: moistening, movement, sound
- Fire: blazing, burning
- Wind: movement and lifting

**Evidence Cited:**
- Wood and stones striking produce sound
- Trees wither at night, expand by day
- Peeling bark causes drying
- Spontaneous generation: worms from moisture + heat, termites becoming bees, worms becoming frogs

**Critique of Eternal Cause:**
If causes were eternal, wood/stones would have previous lives like sentient beings. Since they don't, causes must be adventitious (*glo bur ba*) like boils arising from accumulated conditions.

Defense Against Scriptural Meaninglessness
**Analysis:**

The text addresses the objection that nihilist views render spiritual practice meaningless.

**Defense of Cause-Nihilism:**
- Even without previous mind, present coarse mind exists
- When it ripens as sentient being, understanding non-existence leads to liberation
- The scripture has purpose: showing that at death, elements dissolve into outer elements
- Consciousness divides and destroys affliction-nature

**Defense of Result-Nihilism:**
- Knowing non-existent nature prevents being "carried away" by other scriptures
- Prevents meaningless effort (*ngal bar byed pa*)
- The scripture of "speaking nonexistence" has meaning for reversing this life's suffering

[2013-2032] [CONCEPT]
The Synthesis Position
**Analysis:**

Longchenpa presents a third position synthesizing cause and result nihilism, attributed to the hunchbacked son of Katika (*ka ta'i bu nog can*).

**Three-Phase Ontology:**

1. **Arising** (*dang po*): All sentient beings first arise from the space of elements (*'byung ba'i dbyings*)
2. **Abiding** (*bar du*): They abide in the space of elements
3. **Dissolution** (*tha ma*): They dissolve into the space of elements without trace (*yang yal te cang med par*)

**Key Doctrines:**

**The Space of Elements as Universal Self:**
- *'byung ba'i dbyings tsam zhig kun gyi bdag nyid du smra* — "they speak of merely the space of arising as the self-nature of all"

**The Ninefold Structure:**

In the space of abiding:
- *mi gnas dgu gnas pa* — "nine abodes of non-abiding" arise as whatever appears

From the space of arising:
- *ma shar dgu shar* — "nine non-arisings arise"

**Appearance Structure:**
- Outer: forms, objects of various colors (*dkar dmar sna tshogs*)
- Inner: sentient beings, mental concepts (*sems kyi rtog pa*)
- Body: nine aspects that do/don't arise differently

**Liberation Mechanism:**

At liberation (*grol ba'i tshe*):
- *ma grol dgu grol* — "nine unliberated become liberated"
- All vessels, contents, embodied forms dissolve themselves (*rang yal*)
- Since there's no unliberation, destroyed beings cannot return to three realms

**Final Position:**
- All beings first arise adventitiously (*glo bur du byung*) from space
- Now they abide adventitiously in space
- Finally liberated adventitiously into space, never returning

Samkhya and Vaisesika Doctrines
**Analysis:**

**Samkhya View:**

The Samkhyas hold three principles as permanent (*rtag pa*):
1. Atoms (*rdul phran*)
2. Darkness (*mun pa*)
3. Three energies (*snying stobs gsum*)

**Causal Theory:**
- Delusion (*ma rig pa*) is the basis of action
- Attachment produces happiness
- Anger turns back armies (*dpung slar log*)

**Vaisesika View:**

The Vaisesikas (*bye brag pa*) view outer aggregates, elements, and ayatanas as minute atoms (*rdul phran*).

**Key Doctrines:**
- Count atoms as "grasped by the first grasper" (*'dzin pa dang po'i 'dzin pa*)
- These are causes and conditions of cessation
- These are causes and conditions of ultimate emptiness
- When body is abandoned, mind and space remain latent (*bag la bab*)
- Latent arising of body (*bag la byung*)
- Abiding at the end of trace emptiness (*thig stong mtha' gnas*)

**Kumaralabha Tenets:**

The school of Kumaralabdha holds:
- Mind is nonexistent; body is nonexistent (*sems med lus med*)
- When mind is nonexistent, body is also nonexistent

This represents an extreme nihilist position within the non-Buddhist traditions.

**Doxographic Method:**

Longchenpa systematically presents each school's:
- Ontological commitments (what they posit as real)
- Causal theory (how things arise)
- Soteriology (path to liberation)

This serves to clearly differentiate Buddhist from non-Buddhist positions.

The transcendent vehicle accepts four seals (*phyag rgya*) that outsiders lack:

1. **All compounded things impermanent** (*'dus byas thams cad mi rtag pa*)
2. **All with defilements suffering** (*zag bcas thams cad sdug bsngal ba*)
3. **All dharmas empty and selfless** (*chos thams cad stong pa bdag med pa*)
4. **Liberation from suffering peaceful** (*sdug bsngal las grol ba zhi ba*)

**Threefold Refuge:**

Going for refuge to the Three Jewels (*dkon mchog gsum*) — another feature absent in outsider paths.

**Fivefold Analysis:**

Longchenpa applies the standard analytical framework:

1. **Essence** (*ngo bo*):
   *'khor ba las nges par 'byin pa'i thabs khyad par can* — "supreme method of definitely exiting samsara"

2. **Definition** (*nges tshig*):
   *zhugs nas bde ba sgrub pa* — "that which, when entered, produces happiness"

3. **Division** (*dbye ba*):
   - Small and great (*theg pa chung dang chen po*)
   - Or three: hearer, self-enlightened, bodhisattva

4. **Purpose** (*dgos pa*):
   *don dang mthar thug sgrub pa* — "accomplishing temporary and ultimate meaning"

5. **Effect** ('*bras bu*):
   *khams gsum las nges par 'byung zhing thar pa thob pa* — "definitely exiting the three realms, obtaining liberation"

**Scriptural Citations:**


[2523-2525] [CONCEPT]
Great Exposition Vehicle as Complete Teaching

**Analysis:** Lines 2523-2525 establish the nine-fold schema as the comprehensive teaching vehicle.

**Key Assertion:**
*shin tu rgyas pa chen po'i theg pa ni* — "The very extensive great vehicle..."

**Completeness Claim:**
- All Word (*bka' thams cad*) gathered in the Three Baskets (*sde snod gsum*)
- All Mantras (*sngags*) are "manifest great" (*mngon par chen po*)

**Philosophical Significance:**

This passage establishes the Nyingma school's distinctive hermeneutic:
1. The nine-vehicle schema is not merely enumerative but hierarchical
2. Atiyoga represents the culmination, not replacement, of lower vehicles
3. Each vehicle corresponds to specific cognitive capacities (*blo yi rim pa*)
4. The "great exposition" (*shin tu rgyas pa chen po*) encompasses all prior vehicles

**Soteriological Implication:**
The progression from Hearer to Atiyoga represents increasingly direct approaches to recognizing the nature of mind, with Atiyoga (*rdzogs pa chen po*) offering the most immediate path through its emphasis on self-liberated awareness (*rig pa rang grol*).

[2526-2538] [STRUCTURE]
Hearer Vehicle Overview
**Analysis:**

Longchenpa presents the hearer vehicle (*nyan thos kyi theg pa*) through five aspects:

**1. Essence (*ngo bo*):**
*'khor ba las rang grol 'dod pa'i sems* — "mind desiring sole self-liberation from samsara"
*gang zag bdag med rtogs pa'i thabs* — "method realizing person-selflessness"

**2. Definition (*nges tshig*):**
- Hearing dharma from teacher (*ston pas chos nyan*)
- Proclaiming to others (*gzhan la sgrog pa*)
- Depending on teachers (*ston pa la rag las*)
- Accomplishing last-existence enlightenment (*tshe tha mar byang chub sgrub pa*)

**3. Purpose (*dgos pa*):**
*'khor ba las nges par 'byung ba* — "definitely exiting samsara"

**4. Effect ('*bras bu*):**
*zag bcas dang zag med kyi dgra bcom* — "foe-destroyer with or without remainder"

**5. Division (*dbye ba*):**
- Sautrantika (*mdo sde pa*)
- Vaibhasika (*bye brag tu smra ba*)

**The Two Truths in Vaibhasika:**

**Relative Truth (*kun rdzob bden pa*):**
- Gross appearances gathered as one
- Like yak-horn and conch (*g.yag ru dang dung lta bu*)

**Ultimate Truth (*don dam bden pa*):**
- Part-possessed dust-atoms (*rdul phran cha can*)
- Separate pieces (*byed pa shas che*)
- Instant (*skad cig ma*) — partless, like pot-water (*bud rdza'i chu lta bu*)

**Person-Selflessness:**
Both schools accept person-selflessness (*gang zag bdag med*), yet accept dharma-self (*chos kyi bdag*) as dust-atoms and instants.
