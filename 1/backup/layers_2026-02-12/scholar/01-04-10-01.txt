[3037-3039] [PHILOLOGY]
**don dam par so so rang gi rig pa'i ye shes de yang rang bzhin gyis ngo bo nyid med pa'o zhes 'dod do**
- **rang bzhin gyis**: By nature, inherently (svabhāva-taḥ)
- **ngo bo nyid med pa**: Without inherent existence (niḥsvabhāva)
- **zhes 'dod do**: "Thus they assert" — introducing the Svātantrika position

[3038-3039] [PHILOLOGY]
**sgyu ma don dam du 'dod pa...ma grub pa rig pa'i ye shes smra bsam brjod med tsam zhig**
- **sgyu ma**: Illusion, magical display (māyā)
- **smra bsam brjod med**: Inexpressible, inconceivable, incommunicable
- **tsam zhig**: Merely, only — limiting particle

[3038-3039] [CONTEXT]
**Illusion-like Ultimate (sgyu ma don dam)**: This branch of Svātantrika (associated with later Bhāvaviveka followers) holds that the ultimate truth is like an illusion—it appears but has no true existence. This preserves the validity of conventional appearance while denying ultimate reality. The meditation on illusion-like appearance becomes the path to realizing emptiness.
