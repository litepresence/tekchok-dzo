[5640-5649] [PRACTICE ERROR] [STABILITY AS PERMANENT STATE]
MISREADING:
Stability in recognition means maintaining a continuous state or experience.

WHY IT ARISES:
"Stable" suggests unchanging condition. Experience-orientation.

PRIMARY CONSEQUENCE:
Trying to hold onto recognition.

SECONDARY CONSEQUENCES:
Tension; fear of losing it.

CASCADE EFFECTS:
[MEDITATIONISM ERROR].

[5774-5801] [EPISTEMIC ERROR] [OBSTACLES AS EXTERNAL]
MISREADING:
Obstacles (laziness, agitation) are personal failings or external forces.

WHY IT ARISES:
Moral judgment of practice difficulties.

PRIMARY CONSEQUENCE:
Self-blame or discouragement.

SECONDARY CONSEQUENCES:
Missing the self-liberated nature of obstacles.

CASCADE EFFECTS:
[PSYCHOLOGIZATION ERROR].
