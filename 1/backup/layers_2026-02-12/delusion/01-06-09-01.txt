[8810-8817] [GROUND-PATH-FRUITION TEMPORALIZATION] [BLISS DEVELOPMENT]
MISREADING:
The cultivation of bliss is interpreted as developing an experience over time through repeated practice, like building muscle or acquiring skill, with bliss as conditioned attainment.

WHY IT ARISES:
Conditioning and training frameworks impose development models. The language of "cultivation" suggests temporal growth. Fear that bliss won't happen without effort drives doing-obsession.

PRIMARY CONSEQUENCE:
The teaching that bliss is the natural expression of mind's nature, always present, never developed, is entirely obscured. Seeking replaces finding.

SECONDARY CONSEQUENCES:
Practitioners chase increasingly intense experiences, becoming bliss-addicted. Disappointment when conditioned experiences fade leads to technique-switching. The unconditioned is abandoned for the conditioned.

CASCADE EFFECTS:
Triggers [PROGRESS TRACKING]. Four joys will be misread as temporal progression rather than simultaneous aspects.

MISREADING:
The specific locations (tip to root, root to navel, etc.) are understood as fixed anatomical points that must be precisely mapped and manipulated, like acupuncture points with guaranteed effects.

WHY IT ARISES:
Cartographic thinking projects spatial certainty onto subtle body. The precise anatomical language suggests objective geography. Scientific materialism supports literalization.

PRIMARY CONSEQUENCE:
The teaching that locations are conceptual designations for awareness-movement, not fixed points, is entirely obscured. The energetic becomes the cartographic.

SECONDARY CONSEQUENCES:
Practitioners carry anatomical maps while missing the awareness that moves. Disappointment when locations don't match descriptions leads to doubt. The fluid becomes fixed.
