[5269-5284] [PEDAGOGICAL ERROR] [POINTING OUT AS MAGIC]
MISREADING:
The pointing-out instruction magically transmits realization from teacher to student.

WHY IT ARISES:
Esoteric expectations. Wish for easy enlightenment.

PRIMARY CONSEQUENCE:
Passive waiting for teacher to "give" realization.

SECONDARY CONSEQUENCES:
Missing the necessity of own recognition.

CASCADE EFFECTS:
[PEDAGOGICAL ERROR].

[5429-5476] [EPISTEMIC ERROR] [RECOGNITION AS ONCE-OFF]

Recognition is a one-time event after which practice is complete.

WHY IT ARISES:
"Recognition" suggests singular achievement.

PRIMARY CONSEQUENCE:
Arrogant claims of completion.

SECONDARY CONSEQUENCES:
No stabilization; falling back into confusion.

[5606-5639] [PRACTICE ERROR] [STABILITY AS PERMANENT STATE]
MISREADING:
Stability in recognition means maintaining a continuous state or experience.

WHY IT ARISES:
"Stable" suggests unchanging condition. Experience-orientation.

PRIMARY CONSEQUENCE:
Trying to hold onto recognition.

SECONDARY CONSEQUENCES:
Tension; fear of losing it.

CASCADE EFFECTS:
[MEDITATIONISM ERROR].
