[4702-4704]
[TAGS: GROUND-PATH-FRUITION TEMPORALIZATION] [TAGS: VEHICLE BYPASS ERROR]

MISREADING:
The botanical metaphor of flowers without fruit ripening establishes a temporal hierarchy where root tantras precede explanatory tantras, which precede branch tantras, creating a developmental sequence that must be completed over time.

WHY IT ARISES:
The sequential presentation of eight tantra categories mirrors educational curricula. The tree metaphor unconsciously triggers growth-phase thinking. "Root" implies foundation before fruit, suggesting temporal priority rather than simultaneous necessity.

PRIMARY CONSEQUENCE:
Practitioner adopts accumulative approach, believing completion requires sequential mastery of all eight categories. Ground becomes future attainment rather than present fact. Path extends indefinitely across tantra categories.

SECONDARY CONSEQUENCES:
Category-hopping between tantra types without penetrating any single one. Temporal anxiety about "which tantra to study first." Completion deferred until all eight categories mastered. Fungibility assumption that each category contributes incrementally.

CASCADE EFFECTS:
Accumulative mindset → spiritual materialism → comparison with others' progress → jealousy and competition → lineage fragmentation.

SECONDARY CONSEQUENCES:
Guru shopping for "authentic" instructions. Disempowerment without access to specific lineages. Commodification of esoteric teachings. Spiritual bypassing through credential accumulation.

CASCADE EFFECTS:
Objectification → dependency on external validation → exploitation by false teachers → loss of discernment → complete spiritual bankruptcy.
