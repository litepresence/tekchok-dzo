ERROR TYPE 1: [VEHICLE CLASSIFICATION OBSESSION] [PEDAGOGICAL ERROR]
================================================================================

MISREADING:
The detailed classification of vehicles—theg pa (vehicles) divided into worldly (jig rten pa) and transcendent ('jig rten las 'das pa), then into subcategories like gods/humans, erroneous views, etc.—is interpreted as establishing a rigid hierarchical system where practitioners must identify their "vehicle type" and progress through fixed stages, rather than recognizing vehicles as skillful means responding to capacities.

WHY IT ARISES:
Educational systems emphasize classification and progression. The practitioner wants clear identity and path. The systematic presentation suggests a taxonomy of spiritual types. Western psychology offers personality typologies that seem analogous.

PRIMARY CONSEQUENCE:
The practitioner becomes obsessed with identifying "which vehicle is mine." Time is wasted analyzing which category fits them best. The recognition that vehicles are provisional designations, not ontological categories, is missed. Practice becomes identity-performance.

SECONDARY CONSEQUENCES:
Sectarian identification develops. "I am a Mahayana practitioner" becomes ego-fortification. Comparisons with other vehicle-practitioners create hierarchy. The fundamental equality of all paths as skillful means is lost.

CASCADE EFFECTS:
This feeds [VEHICLE HIERARCHY ELITISM] where higher-numbered vehicles are considered superior. It produces [PREPARATION PARALYSIS] where the practitioner believes they must master lower vehicles before accessing higher ones.

ERROR TYPE 2: [WORLDLY DHARMA PURSUIT] [PRACTICE ERROR]
================================================================================

MISREADING:
The presentation of worldly vehicles (jig rten pa'i theg pa)—the vehicle of gods and humans (lha dang mi'i theg pa) leading to higher rebirth through virtue—is interpreted as a legitimate long-term path or even a final goal, rather than a provisional preparation for transcendent practice.

WHY IT ARISES:
Fear of lower rebirth drives pursuit of merit. The practitioner wants guaranteed good results. Virtue appears safer than transcendent view. Cultural conditioning emphasizes good behavior for reward.

PRIMARY CONSEQUENCE:
The practitioner remains stuck in worldly dharma pursuit, accumulating merit for better rebirth while never engaging transcendent view. The vehicle meant as preparation becomes permanent residence.

SECONDARY CONSEQUENCES:
Spiritual materialism develops around merit. "I've accumulated enough virtue for good rebirth" becomes the measure of success. Transcendent teachings are postponed indefinitely because "I'm not ready."

CASCADE EFFECTS:
This produces [PROGRESSION MYTH] where enlightenment is conceived as the result of accumulating sufficient worldly merit. It generates [DIRECT RECOGNITION DELAY] where recognition is perpetually deferred until "merit is complete."

ERROR TYPE 3: [DEFINITIONAL OBSESSION] [EPISTEMIC ERROR]
================================================================================

MISREADING:
The fivefold analysis of vehicles—nature (ngo bo), etymology (nges tshig), classification (dbye ba), purpose (dgos pa), and result ('bras bu)—is interpreted as requiring the practitioner to master all definitional details before proceeding, as if enlightenment requires comprehensive scholastic knowledge.

WHY IT ARISES:
Academic training values definitional precision. The practitioner assumes thorough knowledge is prerequisite for practice. The systematic presentation invites scholastic approach. Fear of missing important details drives comprehensive study.

PRIMARY CONSEQUENCE:
Study replaces practice. The practitioner memorizes vehicle definitions, classifications, and etymologies without recognizing their nature. Intellectual mastery is mistaken for spiritual development.

SECONDARY CONSEQUENCES:
Scholastic pride develops. The practitioner becomes a vehicle-classification expert who has never recognized mind nature. Others are judged by their definitional knowledge. The transformative purpose of the teachings is lost in pedantic wrangling.

CASCADE EFFECTS:
This feeds [CAUSAL/RESULT CONFUSION] where definitional study is believed to mechanically produce understanding. It generates [PREPARATION PARALYSIS] where genuine practice is deferred until "definitions are mastered."

================================================================================
ERROR TYPE 4: [ERRONEOUS VEHICLE DISMISSAL] [VIEW COLLAPSE]
================================================================================

MISREADING:
The category of "erroneous vehicles" (phyin ci log gi theg pa) including Lokayata and other non-Buddhist paths is interpreted as establishing absolute superiority of Buddhist paths and encouraging sectarian contempt, rather than demonstrating how all paths including Buddhist ones are provisional.

WHY IT ARISES:
Religious identity requires opposition to "others." The practitioner seeks validation through comparison. Clear categorization of "wrong" paths seems to validate "right" path. Historical religious conflicts condition sectarian attitudes.

PRIMARY CONSEQUENCE:
Sectarian arrogance develops. Non-Buddhist traditions are dismissed without understanding. The practitioner develops superiority complex based on vehicle-classification. Openness to wisdom from any source is blocked.

SECONDARY CONSEQUENCES:
The practitioner becomes a Buddhist fundamentalist. Interfaith dialogue is rejected. The universality of pointing-out instructions is missed. Cultural chauvinism masquerades as Dharma pride.

CASCADE EFFECTS:
This produces [VEHICLE HIERARCHY ELITISM] where Buddhist vehicles are considered absolutely superior to all others. It generates [SUTRA DISMISSAL] when Buddhist practitioners look down on "worldly" practitioners without recognizing their own limitations.

================================================================================
ERROR TYPE 5: [TEMPORAL DEFERMENT SYNDROME] [CAUSAL/RESULT CONFUSION]
================================================================================

MISREADING:
The presentation of vehicles producing their respective results "at some time" (nam zhig na mngon du gyur pa) is interpreted as establishing that results must be deferred to future lifetimes, that enlightenment cannot be accessed now but requires extended temporal progression through vehicle-stages.

WHY IT ARISES:
Temporal thinking assumes future fulfillment. The practitioner cannot imagine immediate enlightenment. Gradual development models condition expectations. "At some time" suggests distant future achievement.

PRIMARY CONSEQUENCE:
Recognition is perpetually deferred. The practitioner always believes enlightenment is "later" after completing current vehicle. The present moment's availability of recognition is never accessed. Future-orientation blocks direct pointing.

SECONDARY CONSEQUENCES:
The practitioner lives in preparation mode, never actualization. "I'm working toward enlightenment" replaces "I am recognizing now." Life becomes endless preparation for a future that never arrives.

CASCADE EFFECTS:
This produces [DIRECT RECOGNITION DELAY] where recognition is always deferred until conditions are right. It generates [PROGRESSION MYTH] where enlightenment is conceived as the endpoint of temporal progression through vehicles.
