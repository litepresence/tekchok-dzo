From one form, the body's limit. Freedom attained in the expanse. Beyond thousands, beyond counting. Even this space-becoming, abandoning the work of containing. Form's limit, particles, space, knowing.

The germs, the winds of attachment and desire arising for each being—this is how samsara begins.

Then the third seven days. From fifteen days, the earth channels expanding—light, syllables, the basis of memory appearing as golden vapor. Within clouds gathering like space, the winds of karmic action moving.

From eighteen days, the eighteen elements complete. The eighteen bases forming, calculated, changing, becoming lumps of eighteen.

The elements pairing, the work of gathering complete, previous power exhausted. The eighteen elements' vapor like egg-white, grasping the elements, the wind of phlegm, bile, etc. Diseases arising as before.

Then from twenty-two to twenty-five days, the elements' power and the body's basis completing.

What is being described here? The detailed anatomy of how you became trapped in this body. How consciousness got mixed with elements, how winds got tangled with desires, how memory became solid.

This isn't philosophy. It's forensic analysis of your imprisonment. Every step is described: how clarity became form, how spaciousness became bound, how freedom became limitation.

The elements didn't trap you. You chose it. Attachment, desire, grasping—these winds moved through the channels and built this cage. Not accidentally. Deliberately. Because somewhere along the way, you thought suffering would be enjoyable.

Now you have a body. Now you have limits. Now you have disease and death.

But the wisdom was never trapped. The awareness was never bound. Only the construction was formed. Only the cage was built.

You are not the cage. You are what built the cage and can recognize that it was never necessary.

The elements will disperse. The winds will calm. The cage will fall. Awareness will remain, never having been confined.

The question is: will you wait for death to recognize this, or will you recognize it now?
