[18172-18176] [CONCEPT] The rten: form aggregate
**Analysis:** Basis (rten) identified as gzugs kyi phung po (form aggregate) in upper body (byang khog stod). Anatomical localization follows tantric subtle body theory.

[18177-18185] [CONCEPT] The gnas: heart-center
**Analysis:** Abode located in snying (heart) to glo ba (lung) connecting region:
- rtsa sog ma'i sbu gu (blade-of-grass channel)
- rlung gi rta (wind-horse)
- rig pa'i gdangs (awareness-resonance)
Analogy: rlung = blind horse; rig gdangs = lame person with eyes. Neither functions alone; together they traverse samsara.

[18186-18192] [PHILOLOGY] De gnyis construction
**Analysis:** De gnyis (those two) refers to rlung and rig gdangs. Gnyis 'dres na (when two mix) establishes condition for conceptual thought ('gyu dgu 'gyu'i rtog pa). Mi 'gyu (does not arise) demonstrates primordial separation (rang chas su gnas pa).

[18193-18195] [CONTEXT] Rig pa rang shar citation
**Analysis:** Classic analogy: sems and rig pa like water (chu) and bubbles (lbu ba). Sems lacks power over rig pa (sems kyi dbang du ma gyur), establishing fundamental asymmetry.

[18196-18203] [CONCEPT] Remaining six aspects
**Analysis:**
- lam: srog rtsa nas 'gyu (moves from life-channel)
- sgo: kha sna gnyis (mouth and nose)
- ngo bo: 'khor ba'i rang bzhin gzung 'dzin (samsaric grasping-grasped nature)
- rtsal: yul la 'dzin zhing nga bdag tu zhen pa (grasping objects, clinging to self)
- byed las: 'khor ba'i bde sdug sna tshogs byed (creates samsaric pleasure/pain)
- 'bras bu: 'khor ba dang ngan song (samsara and lower realms)

[18204-18234] [CONTEXT] Multiple scriptural citations
**Analysis:** Integration of:
1. Rig pa rang shar: impure nature, threefold stacking in heart-lung
2. Rdo rje sems dpa' snying gi me long: ma rig pa, sems, yid gather as samsara basis
3. He badzra: adventitious stains (glo bur dri ma)
4. Rnam 'grel: mind's nature as luminous
5. 'Jam dpal bstod pa: conceptions as great non-awareness

[18235-18241] [CONCEPT] Meeting point of rlung and rig
**Analysis:** Anatomical localization:
- glo ba nas rlung gi rta (wind-horse from lungs)
- snying nas rig gdangs rtsa (awareness-channel from heart)
- gnyis 'dzom pa (meeting point)
Mixing produces bag chags kyi sdud byed sems and yul la rtog pa yid.

[18242-18260] [CONTEXT] Adventitious stains citations
**Analysis:**
- He badzra: beings are buddhas obscured by adventitious stains
- Rnam 'grel: mind's nature luminous, stains adventitious
- 'Jam dpal bstod pa: conceptions as great non-awareness plunging into samsara

[18261-18269] [STRUCTURE] Transition to ye shes
**Analysis:** Don marks transition to detailed explanation of ye shes nature. Thal 'gyur provides framework for fourfold analysis (ngo bo, nges tshig, dbye ba, so so'i don).

[18270-18285] [CONCEPT] Fourfold analysis of ye shes
**Analysis:**
1. ngo bo: so so rang gi rig pa ye nas mkhyen char gnas pa (individual self-awareness as primordial knowing-rain)
2. nges tshig: ye nas gnas pa'i don rtogs shing shes pa (knowing primordially abiding meaning)
3. dbye ba: gzhi gnas, mtshan nyid 'dzin pa, yul la khyab pa
4. so so'i don: Subsequent elaboration

[18286-18293] [CONTEXT] Mu tig phreng ba citation
**Analysis:** Three wisdoms:
- gzhi gnas dag pa'i ye shes
- mtshan nyin 'dzin
- yul khyab
