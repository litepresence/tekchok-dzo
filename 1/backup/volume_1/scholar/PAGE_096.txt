[3539-3547] [CONCEPT] Atiyoga: Spontaneous Accomplishment and Self-Arisen Wisdom
**Analysis:** Lines 3539-3547 present the core view of Atiyoga (Dzogchen):

**Line 3539-3540:**
- *skyon ka nas dag* — Pure from the depths of faults
- *yon tan lhun gyis grub pa* — Qualities spontaneously accomplished
- Object of realization (*rtogs bya'i gzhi*) made definite

**Self-Arisen Meditation [3541-3547]:**
- *sgom pa rang byung gi ye shes* — Meditation is self-arisen wisdom
- *chos nyid kyi rol par shar* — Arising as dharmatā-play
- *gangi byung* — Whatever arises
- No deliberate meditation (*ched du dmigs pa'i sgom pa med*)

**Four Similes of Natural Freedom:**
1. *rang thog tu rang grol ba chu'i dba' rlab lta bu* — Self-liberated on the spot like water waves [3544]
2. *rang dbyings su rang sangs pa nam mkha'i khu rlungs lta bu* — Self-dissolved into own expanse like space vapors [3545]
3. *rang ngang na 'khyams pa rgya mtsho'i klong lta bu* — Wandering in own state like ocean depths [3546]
4. *rang snang la rang gnas pa mar me'i 'od lta bu* — Self-abiding in own appearances like lamplight [3547]

**Four Samayas [3548-3551]:**
- *med pa* — Non-existence
- *phyal ba* — Evenness
- *lhun grub* — Spontaneous accomplishment
- *gcig pu'i dam tshig* — Sole samaya — not transgressing these

[3552-3553] [STRUCTURE] Summit of Vajra Vehicle
**Analysis:** Lines 3552-3553 conclude the Atiyoga introduction:

**Characterization:**
- *rdo rje theg pa'i rtse mo* — Summit of Vajra vehicle
- Object of great perfect yogis with excellent accumulation (*yongs su rdzogs pa'i rnal 'byor pa chen po bsags pa rab dang ldan pa*)

**Scope:** Not for beginners; requires vast merit and preparation from lower vehicles.

[3554-3595] [CONTEXT] Scripture Quotation: Rig pa rang shar
**Analysis:** Lines 3555-3595 present extensive quotation from *Rig pa rang shar* on Atiyoga:

**Invocation [3555-3556]:**
- "Emaho Secret Lord, listen!"
- "Hold well what I teach"

**Core Verses on Great Perfection [3557-3564]:**
- One perfection, two perfections, all perfections
- One arising, two arising, all arising
- One non-existence, two non-existence, all non-existence
- Created phenomena (*byas pa'i chos*) do not exist from the start
- No creator and created

**Samaya Beyond Protection [3565-3568]:**
- No samaya to protect (*bsrung du med*)
- Separated from primordial protection (*ye nas bs rung dang bral*)
- Non-existence, evenness, spontaneous, sole
- Great intent of all secret mantras

**Lion Simile [3569-3574]:**
- Atiyoga like lion among wild beasts (*gcan gzan seng ge*)
- Self-knowing made definite (*rang rig gtan la 'bebs*)
- Overwhelming lower vehicles (*theg dman zil gyis gnon*)
- Three kayas dissolved into self

**Knot Simile [3575-3580]:**
- Snake's knot (*sbrul gyi mdud pa*) — self-untying
- Self-liberated, self-dissolved, great

**Hope and Doubt [3581-3584]:**
- No hope (*re*) or doubt (*dogs pa*) in Atiyoga vehicle
- Hope and doubt are causes of falling

**Self-Arising and Dissolution [3585-3586]:**
- Phenomenal characteristics (*chos can mtshan ma*) appearing
- Arising from self, dissolving into self

**Final Verses [3587-3595]:**
- No accomplishment, free from accomplishment
- No arising of uncreated
- Non-ceasing, pervasive, spacious, enlightened
- Unchanging great expanse
- Great Buddha without dharmas
- No abiding, no wisdom
- No mind, no intellect, no Buddha

[3596] [STRUCTURE] Three Sections of Atiyoga
**Analysis:** Line 3596 marks the transition to the threefold division (*sde tshan gsum*):

**Source:** *Ati bkod pa chen po las* — From Great Ati Exposition

**Three Sections:**
1. *sems kyi sde* — Mind Section
2. *klong gi sde* — Expanse Section  
3. *man ngag gi sde* — Instruction Section

**Scriptural Quotation [3597-3601]:**
- "For those with mind (*yid can*), Mind Section"
- "For those with space (*nam mkha' can*), Expanse Section"
- "For those free from sequence/effort (*rim rtsol bral*), Instruction"
