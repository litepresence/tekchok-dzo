[1059] [STRUCTURE] Spatial Symmetry of the Cosmological Plan
**Analysis:**

The "equal measure" (*snyoms par byas pa'i tshad*) between directions establishes the symmetrical, mandalic structure of the Thirty-three gods' realm. The four parks (*skyed mos tshal bzhi po*) maintain equivalent dimensions, reflecting the balanced nature of divine environments.

[1060-1063] [CONTEXT] The Wish-fulfilling Tree (*yongs 'dus brtol*)
**Analysis:**

**Botanical Specifications:**
- Name: *yongs 'dus brtol* (Complete Gather Burst)
- Location: Northeast (*byang shar*)
- Root depth: 5 leagues (*dpag tshad lnga 'thur du thug*)
- Height: 100+ leagues (*gyen du brgya 'phags pa*)
- Canopy: Umbrella-like spread (*gdugs ltar gyes pa*)
- Circumference: 50 leagues (*'khor yug dpag tshad lnga bcur 'khyil ba*)

**Adornments:**
- *nor bu'i dra ba* — Jewel net
- *lha'i gos dpyangs pa* — Hanging divine garments

**Doxographical Note:** The *kalpavṛkṣa* (wish-fulfilling tree) motif appears across Indian religions. In Buddhism, it represents the abundance of merit. In Dzogchen, it symbolizes the natural display (*rang snang*) of awareness's creativity.

[1064] [PHILOLOGY] Indra's Throne and the White Rock Slab
**Analysis:**

**Location:** Near (*drung na*) the wish-fulfilling tree

**Features:**
- *ar mo nig lta bu'i rdo leb dkar po* — White rock slab like armonig (harmonious jewel)
- Width: 50 leagues (*chu zheng dpag tshad lnga bcu*)
- *dbang po'i khri* — Indra's throne
- *me tog gi char 'bab pa* — Flower rain falling

**Symbolism:** The white rock (*rdo leb dkar po*) represents the vajra seat (*rdo rje'i gdan*) — the immovable ground of enlightenment. Indra's throne here prefigures the universal monarch's connection to dharmic sovereignty.

[1065-1067] [STRUCTURE] The Assembly-place Dharma Good (*chos bzangs*)
**Analysis:**

**Location:** Southwest of Beautiful-to-see (*blta na sdug*), 4.5 leagues distant

**Physical Characteristics:**
- Shape: Perfectly round (*kun nas zlum pa*)
- Surrounded by: Jewel trees (*rin po che'i shing*) and walls
- Width: 300 leagues (*chu zheng du sum brgya*)
- Circumference: 900 leagues (*mtha' skor dgu brgya pa*)

**Interior:**
- Center: Indra's throne (*dbang po'i khri*)
- Arranged gods' seats (*lha rnams kyi stan bsham pa*)

This assembly-place functions as the cosmic court where divine governance occurs.

[1068-1072] [CONCEPT] Celestial Distances and Higher Heavens
**Analysis:**

**Higher Desire and Form Realms:**
- *nam mkha' la brten pa'i gzhal med khang* — Sky-supporting measureless houses
- Located progressively higher (*gong nas gong du*) like spread planets/stars (*gza' skar bkram pa ltar*)
- Dimensions double (*nyis 'gyur gyis*) with each ascent

**Spatial Calculations:**
- Interval between Jambudvīpa and Thirty-three: 80,000 leagues
- This distance equals the space between Thirty-three and Free-from-war (*'thab bral*)

**Doxographical Significance:** These calculations establish the cosmological framework for understanding:
- The vertical axis of saṃsāra
- How concentrated mind (*bsam gtan*) produces increasingly subtle bodies in higher realms
- The graduated path of Buddhist cosmology

[1073-1079] [CONTEXT] Cosmogony: World Formation
**Analysis:**

**Temporal Framework:**
- *bar gyi bskal pa gcig* — One intermediate eon (*antarakalpa*)
- World is "empty by nature" (*stong pa'i rang bzhin*) and "free from beings" (*'gro bas dben pa*)

**Environmental Development:**
Mountains and hills develop:
- Trees, grass, flowers of various kinds
- Valleys become forests and thicket-continents
- Rain falls from sky
- Jewel trees expand

This describes the *vivartakalpa* (eon of formation) when the world-system evolves from primordial elements.

[1080-1082] [PHILOLOGY] The Genesis of Sentient Beings (*bcud chags tshul*)
**Analysis:**

**Process Description:**
1. *bdud rtsi'i char babs pa* — Nectar rain falls
2. *sa la rlangs phyung ste* — Vapor releases from ground
3. *rin po che'i shing sna tshogs* — Various jewel trees
4. *pad+ma'i rang bzhin du smin par byas pa* — Ripened in lotus nature

**Grammar Note:** The chain of particles (*-s*, *-te*, *la*) indicates sequential causation:
- Instrumental (*-s*): Rain causes vapor
- Connective (*-te*): Vapor enables tree growth
- Locative (*la*): Ground as substrate

**Dzogchen Connection:** This cosmogonic sequence mirrors Dzogchen accounts of how awareness (*rig pa*) manifests as the five elements and worlds. The "nectar rain" parallels the spontaneous display (*lhun grub*) of primordial wisdom.

**Structural Transition:** Having established the cosmological container (*snod*) in exhaustive detail, Longchenpa will now turn to the contained (*bcud*)—how sentient beings arise and evolve within these realms.
