**Marginal Illustrations:**

**Root and Vowel Analysis [5466-5467]:**
- *rtsa ba'i ka ni ka rkyang la* — base KA is simple KA
- *klad kor ni kaM du bod pa* — klad kor pronounced as KAṂ

**Na-ro and Appendages [5468-5470]:**
Na-ro, etc. each read with subsequent ones having length and ring cha. This considers simple letter with appended branch.

**Complex Letter Distribution [5471-5490]:**
For vowel-appended branches, when multiple vowel signs appear on a single letter:
- Base KA and vowel L receive upper and lower na-ro, etc., split half-and-half
- Klad kor given to L
- Ring cha serves both as favorable condition

**Example Sequence [5489]:**
KĪLĪ KŌLŌ KĒLĒ KŪLŪ KYŪLŪ YA KRA LĀ RA LAṀ

When LA and RA are not compatible in vital force (*srog mi mthun*), the acquired L attachment (*la thob*) and ra-btags are placed in the sequence (*gral*).

**Quantity Distribution [5491-5493]:**
If upper and lower branches have unequal quantity, give the remaining to the vowel letter.

**Pronunciation Hierarchy:**
- Klad kor has authority over the vowel
- If only one from above (na-ro, etc.), pronounce as elongated
- Four ornamental letters given to vowel
- If zhabs kyu, etc. missing from below end-support, give to base, pronounce vowel as simple

**Four Ornamental Letters (*rgyan gyi yi ge bzhi*) [5504]:**
Na-ro, gu-gu, 'greng bu, and klad kor.

**Four Support Letters (*rten gyi yi ge/rgyan gyi yi ge bzhi*) [5505-5506]:**
Ya-btags, ra-btags, zhabs kyu (three) called end-support (*mthar rten*) or support letters.

[5508-5512] [PHILOLOGY] Double-Stacked Pronunciation
**Analysis:** Lines 5508-5512 analyze double-stacked vowel pronunciation:

**Gu-gu Stacking [5508]:**
- Single gu-gu with ring cha: pronounced long
- Example: KĪ

**Na-ro Stacking [5509-5510]:**
When two na-ro stacked: called "upper suppressed, lower" (*stod de smad pa*)
- Example: KO'U — one with zhabs kyu

**'Greng bu Stacking [5511-5512]:**
When two 'greng bu stacked: called "'greng suppressed, bent" (*'greng ste bkug pa*)
- Example: KE'I — one with gu-gu
