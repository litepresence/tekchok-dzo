**Technical Terminology:**
- **Skyes bu dang 'brel ba ma grub** (line 1): No established relation to person - epistemological criterion
- **Sgron ma'i spyan rgyas par byed** (line 20): Expands the lamp-eye - visual capacity
- **Dbang po'i yul la dngos su shar** (line 21): Directly appears to sense faculties
- **Dran pa rlung sems rgyu rkyen** (line 18): Memory, wind, mind, cause, conditions - fivefold binding

**Etymological Compounds:**
- **Rgyang** (line 13): Ring mo nas...rgyu rkyen gyi dang ma'i dbus sa 'jug (entering from afar)
- **Zhags** (line 19): 'Ching bar byed (that which binds) - from dran pa, rlung, sems, rgyu rkyen bzhi gcig tu bsdus
- **Chu** (line 20): Ro drug gi bde bas 'phel (expansion through six tastes)

**Key Distinction:**
- **Nang gi nam mkha'i gdangs** (line 3): Internal sky-essence - authentic dbyings
- **Phyi'i dbyings** (line 3): External field - derivative appearance
- **Dbyings dngos ma yin** (line 4): Not the actual field - merely ground/place of emergence

## [CONTEXT]

**Doxographic Critique:**
The text critiques **naive realism** (lines 1-10):
- Mistake: Identifying external cloudless sky as dbyings
- Reason: Space has no established relation to person, while sgron ma follows existence/non-existence of person
- Correction: Internal sky-essence (nang gi nam mkha'i gdangs) appearing externally is true phyi'i dbyings

**Embryological Frame:**
The etymology ties to **conception narrative** (lines 13-16):
- Wind propels from "above" to mother's womb
- "Rgyang" indicates long distance traversed
- Demonstrates karmic-propulsion mechanics

**Sensory Physiology:**
**Chu etymology** (lines 20-21) connects:
- Ro drug (six tastes) at tongue → expanding lamp-eye
- Dwang po lnga'i las (five sense functions)
- Ye shes lnga'i bye brag (five wisdom differentiations)
- Mngon par rtogs (direct realization) of chos nyid

**Pedagogical Structure:**
The passage uses **etymological decomposition** (ye don brjod pa):
- Each sgron ma name analyzed into component meanings
- Each component linked to specific physiological function
- Functions aggregated into soteriological mechanism

---

## [CONCEPT]

**Dbyings Topology:**
The text establishes **three-tier spatial schema**:
1. **Nang gi nang dbyings** (line 6): Inner-inner field - pure dharmadhatu
2. **Nang gi nam mkha'i gdangs** (line 3): Internal sky-essence
3. **Phyi'i dbyings** (line 3): External field - projected appearance

The relationship is **emergent, not spatial**:
- "De las de byung bar snang ba'i phyir" (line 9): Because it appears to emerge from that
- Not "nang tshur/byas phyi phar/byas" (line 8): Not inner-facing vs. outer-facing

**Rgyang Zhags Soteriology:**
The distant-snare lamp serves as **perceptual gateway**:
- **'Char byed sgo** (line 18): Door of emergence (from Mu tig 'phreng ba citation)
- **'Gro ba'i lam** (line 17): Path of beings
- Capable of seeing forms from far distances (line 14)

**Thig le Stong pa Threefold Analysis:**
When applied to **kun rdzob rgyu'i thig le** (conventional causal thigle):
1. **Thig** (line 24): Posture-based descent of essence ('dug stangs kyis dngas ma 'bebs)
2. **Le** (line 25): Verbal hūṃ etc. binding ('ngag gi hU~M la sogs pas gsang bar bzung)
3. **Stong pa** (line 26): Reversing thigle, striking capacity, mixing with wind

When applied to **don dam ye shes kyi thig le** (ultimate wisdom thigle):
1. **Thig le** (line 28): Gazing posture triad of unchanging rig pa
