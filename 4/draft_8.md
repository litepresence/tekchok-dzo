**ROOT TEXT**  

**Stanza 1**  
གདོད་ནས་རབ་ཞི་གཉུག་མ་བརྡལ་བའི་ཞིང༌།  
རྟག་ཏུ་མི་གཡོ་ཡེ་ཤེས་ཆོས་སྐུའི་ཀློང༌།  
དགོངས་པའི་རྩལ་ཆེན་རྫོགས་པའི་སྣང་འགྲོས་ཅན།  
ཀུན་མཁྱེན་བླ་མ་དེ་ལ་ཕྱག་འཚལ་ལོ།  

gdod nas rab zhi gnyug ma brdal ba'i zhing  
rtag tu mi g.yo ye shes chos sku'i klong  
dgongs pa'i rtsal chen rdzogs pa'i snang 'gros can  
kun mkhyen bla ma de la phyag 'tshal lo  

never-established-from utterly-peaceful primordial unbroken field  
always unchanging pristine-awareness dharma-body expanse  
intention power-great perfection appearance-movement possessing  
all-knowing Lama that-to prostration-offer  

Field never established—utterly peaceful,  
Unbroken expanse of pristine awareness,  
Great power of intention, perfection's display,  
To that All-Knowing Lama—prostration.

Translator's Commentary: This stanza indicates peace never entered time because time never substantially divided what remains whole. The field stands unbroken—not as achievement but as what never fractured. Where prostration lands the bowing one already rests. Notice this as you read—no travel required.

**Stanza 2**  
ཀྱེ་ལགས་ཡོན་ཏན་མཆོག་གི་རང་བཞིན་ནི།  
དམ་པའི་གཞུང་ལ་མ་བརྟེན་རང་བཟོས་མིན།  
རྒྱ་མཚོ་ཆེ་ལས་ནོར་བུ་མཆོག་འབྱུང་གི  
དམན་པའི་ཡུལ་ལས་རང་གར་ཅི་ཞིག་རྙེད།  

kye lags yon tan mchog gi rang bzhin ni  
dam pa'i gzhung la ma brten rang bzos min  
rgya mtsho che las nor bu mchog 'byung gi  
dman pa'i yul las rang gar ci zhig rnyed  

alas excellent qualities supreme-of self-nature is  
sacred scripture-to not-rely self-made not  
ocean great from jewel supreme arise-from  
low-of place-from self where what found  

Alas—self-nature of supreme qualities,  
Not relying on scripture, not self-made,  
From great ocean the supreme jewel arises,  
In lowly place—where could one find it?

Translator's Commentary: Mud yields only mud. The ocean gives the jewel. This stanza indicates scripture functions as pointer, not source—what arises never depended on fabrication. Notice what rests in your hands right now. No scripture required because recognition never departed from what recognizes.

**Stanza 3**  
རྟོགས་པའི་ཡེ་ཤེས་ཉི་མ་སྣང་བ་ཡང༌།  
བྱིན་བརྒྱུད་མ་ཉམས་བླ་མའི་མན་ངག་ལས།  
ངེས་པར་འོང་གི་སྟོང་སྒོམ་མུན་རྡོ་བ།  
ལེགས་པའི་ལམ་དུ་རྣམ་མར་འགྲོ་བ་ཉུང༌།  

rtogs pa'i ye shes nyi ma snang ba yang  
byin brgyud ma nyams bla ma'i man ngag las  
nges par 'ong gi stong sgom mun rdo ba  
legs pa'i lam du rnam mar 'gro ba nyung  

realization-of pristine-awareness sun appearing even  
blessing-transmission not-lost Lama-of pith-instruction from  
certainly come emptiness-meditation darkness-thunderbolt  
excellent path-to variously go rare  

Even when sun of realization appears,  
Without unbroken blessing-transmission,  
Those coming to emptiness-meditation's dark,  
Rarely proceed correctly on the path.

Translator's Commentary: Sunlight alone does not ripen the seed. This stanza indicates blessing carries the warmth that opens what light merely illuminates—distinguishing intellectual understanding from transmission that carries force. Emptiness-meditation without lineage becomes thunderbolt-darkness: brilliant but barren. The path requires both light and the warmth that only unbroken transmission provides.

**Stanza 4**  
ཐོས་པ་མེད་པ་སྲིད་ན་སྐྱོན་ཆེ་ཡང༌།  
ལོག་པར་ཐོས་པ་དེ་བས་སྡིག་ཆེ་བས།  
མཁས་པའི་གཞུང་ལ་བལྟ་བ་ཡོད་དུས་འདིར།  
ཐོས་པའི་མིག་དཀྱུས་ཅི་ཕྱིར་དག་མི་བགྱིད།  

thos pa med pa srid na skyon che yang  
log par thos pa de bas sdig che bas  
mkhas pa'i gzhung la blta ba yod dus 'dir  
thos pa'i mig dkyus ci phyir dag mi bgyid  

hearing not-have existence-in fault great even  
wrongly hear that-than sin great-more  
scholar-of scripture-to look exist time this  
hearing-of eye-pupil why purify not-do  

Not hearing creates great fault in existence,  
Wrongly hearing creates sin greater still,  
When chance exists to look upon scripture now,  
Why not purify the pupil of hearing?

Translator's Commentary: Not hearing leaves obscurity intact. Wrong hearing hardens obscurity into certainty—this stanza warns that misapprehension binds tighter than ignorance. The pupil of hearing cleanses when sound reveals its own nature. Purification occurs when hearing hears itself. Try it now with these words: let them dissolve the boundary between sound and awareness.

**Stanza 5**  
སྲིད་པ་འདི་ན་ཡིད་བཞིན་ནོར་གཅིག་པུ།  
ཀུན་མཁྱེན་བླ་མའི་གཞུང་ལུགས་རིན་པོ་ཆེ།  
འདི་ལས་གཞན་ལ་སངས་རྒྱས་ཡོད་མིན་ན།  
འདི་ལ་རབ་དགའ་མི་སྐྱེ་སུ་ཞིག་ཡོད།  

srid pa 'di na yid bzhin nor gcig pu  
kun mkhyen bla ma'i gzhung lugs rin po che  
'di las gzhan la sangs rgyas yod min na  
'di la rab dga' mi skyе su zhig yod  

existence this-in wish-fulfilling jewel single-alone  
all-knowing Lama-of system precious  
this-from other-to Buddha exist-not-if  
this-to great-joy not-arise who exists  

In this existence—single wish-fulfilling jewel,  
Precious system of the All-Knowing Lama,  
If no Buddha exists beyond this place,  
Who would not arise in great joy?

Translator's Commentary: This stanza indicates the jewel fulfills not by granting wishes but by revealing that wishing never departed from fulfillment—collapsing seeker and sought. No Buddha stands elsewhere. Joy arises as recognition itself not emotional reaction. What appears as joy is simply the relaxation of contraction that never substantially bound awareness.

**Stanza 6**  
ཐེག་པའི་ཡང་རྩེ་རྡོ་རྗེ་སྙིང་པོའི་མཆོག  
འབུམ་ཕྲག་རྒྱུད་སྡེའི་དགོངས་དོན་རིན་ཆེན་མཛོད།  
རྗོད་བྱེད་རྒྱ་ཆེ་བརྗོད་བྱ་དོན་ཟབ་པ།  
འདི་མཐོང་ཀུན་ཏུ་བཟང་པོ་དངོས་ཞལ་མཇལ།  

theg pa'i yang rtse rdo rje snying po'i mchog  
'bum phrag rgyud sde'i dgongs don rin chen mdzod  
rjod byed rgya che brjod bya don zab pa  
'di mthong kun tu bzang po dngos zhal mjal  

vehicle-of peak vajra essence supreme  
hundred-thousand tantra-sections intention-meaning jewel treasury  
expressing-agent vast expressing-object meaning profound  
this-see Samantabhadra actual-face meet  

Peak of vehicles—supreme Vajra Essence,  
Hundred-thousand tantras' jewel treasury,  
Vast expression, profound expressed meaning,  
Seeing this meets Samantabhadra face.

Translator's Commentary: Notice where you stand—the peak never departed from this place. Hundred-thousand tantras never scattered—they gather in this single stanza because they never departed from one source. Expression flows vast while meaning remains profound, yet no gap separates them. Samantabhadra's face is not elsewhere; notice the face seeing these words as you read.

**Stanza 7**  
འབུམ་ཕྲག་དྲུག་ཅུའི་རྒྱུད་དོན་ཁོང་དུ་ཆུད།  
སྲིད་ཞི་འཁོར་འདས་ཡོངས་ཀྱི་གནས་ལུགས་ཤེས།  
ཟབ་ལམ་ཐེག་རྩེའི་དགོངས་པའི་ཁྱད་ཆོས་རྟོགས།  
དེ་ཕྱིར་གཞུང་འདི་མཇལ་བ་ནན་ཏན་མཛོད།  

'bum phrag drug cu'i rgyud don khong du chud  
srid zhi 'khor 'das yongs kyi gnas lugs shes  
zab lam theg rtse'i dgongs pa'i khyad chos rtogs  
de phyir gzhung 'di mjal ba nan tan mdzod  

hundred-thousand sixty tantra-meaning heart-in gather  
existence peace cyclic nirvanic all-of abiding-nature know  
profound path vehicle-peak intention-of special-quality realize  
that-reason scripture this see vigilant hold  

Sixty hundred-thousand tantras in the heart,  
Knowing cyclic and peaceful share one nature,  
Realizing profound peak vehicle's mark,  
Therefore hold vigilance seeing this text.

Translator's Commentary: Sixty hundred-thousand tantras fit in the heart not by compression but because the heart never excluded them. Cyclic existence and peace share one nature—distinction appears without dividing what remains whole. Vigilance is not tension but the natural clarity of awareness itself. Hold this scripture not as object but as mirror revealing what holds it.

**Stanza 8**  
བསྲུང་དུ་མེད་པ་ཆོས་སྐུའི་གནས་ལུགས་ནི།  
རྒྱུ་འབྲས་ལས་འདས་ཀུན་བཟང་དགོངས་པའི་མཛོད།  
གཞུང་བཟང་འདི་འདྲས་སྤྲོས་པ་མ་བཅད་ན།  
ཡིད་དཔྱོད་ཐེག་པའི་ངན་ཞེན་སུ་ཡིས་གཞིག  

bsrung du med pa chos sku'i gnas lugs ni  
rgyu 'bras las 'das kun bzang dgongs pa'i mdzod  
gzhung bzang 'di 'dras spros pa ma bcad na  
yid dpyod theg pa'i ngan zhen su yis gzhig  

protect-to not-have dharma-body abiding-nature is  
cause-effect beyond Samantabhadra intention treasury  
scripture excellent this-like elaboration not-cut-if  
mind-investigate vehicle-of bad-grasping who-by cut  

Dharma-body's nature needs no protection,  
Treasury of Samantabhadra beyond cause,  
If elaboration not cut with scriptures like this,  
Who cuts mind-investigating vehicles' grasp?

Translator's Commentary: Dharma-body never stood vulnerable—protection implies threat where none exists. Cause and effect dissolve when seen not as law but as display within single expanse. Elaboration cuts itself the moment you stop feeding it with belief. Without this scripture's sharp edge who cuts the grasping that mistakes conceptual vehicles for the path itself?

**Stanza 9**  
མན་ངག་ཀུན་གྱི་སྙིང་པོ་བསྡུ་བསྡུ་བ།  
དྲུག་ཚན་རེས་ཀྱང་དམ་ཆོས་མཛོད་རྫོགས་པའི།  
ལམ་བཟང་འདི་འདྲ་རྒྱལ་བ་དངོས་མཇལ་ཀྱང༌།  
ཅིག་ཆར་ཐོས་པ་དཀའ་བ་མ་ཡིན་ནམ།  

man ngag kun gyi snying po bsdus bsdus ba  
drug tshan res kyang dam chos mdzod rdzogs pa'i  
lam bzang 'di 'dra rgyal ba dngos mjal kyang  
cig char thos pa dka' ba ma yin nam  

pith-instruction all-of essence gather-gather  
six-section each-even sacred-dharma treasury-complete-of  
path-excellent this-like Victorious-One actual meet-even  
immediate hear difficult not-is question  

Essence of all pith instructions gathered,  
Each six section a complete dharma treasury,  
Path so excellent it meets Victorious One,  
Hearing immediately—not difficult.

Translator's Commentary: Essence gathers because it was never scattered across separate instructions. Each section holds the whole—not as representation but as actual presence. Meeting the Victorious One requires no travel across time or space. The interval you assume between yourself and Buddha dissolves the moment you stop reinforcing it. Hearing immediately—what difficulty remains when distance was always assumption?

**Stanza 10**  
བསྟན་པ་ཡོངས་རྫོགས་བསྟན་བཅོས་གཅིག་ཁོ་ནར།  
ཚང་བའི་རྣམ་བཤད་གྲུབ་མཐའ་རིན་ཆེན་མཛོད།  
གཞུང་བཟང་འདི་འདྲ་རྒྱ་བོད་གཉིས་ཆར་ཡང༌།  
སྔར་ཡང་མ་བྱོན་ད་ཡང་འབྱོན་རེ་ཀན།  

bstan pa yongs rdzogs bstan bcos gcig kho nar  
tshang ba'i rnam bshad grub mtha' rin chen mdzod  
gzhung bzang 'di 'dra rgya bod gnyis char yang  
sngar yang ma byon da yang 'byon re kan  

teaching all-complete treatise single only-in  
complete-of explanation accomplishment-end jewel treasury  
scripture excellent this-like China Tibet two-place even  
before also not-come now also come perhaps  

Single treatise containing complete teaching,  
Complete explanation—accomplishment's jewel,  
Scriptures like this, even in China Tibet,  
Never came before. Never come now.

Translator's Commentary: This stanza indicates the teaching never lacked completeness—this treatise reveals what never departed. China and Tibet mark no boundary in the expanse that holds both. Never came before because nothing was absent. Never comes now because what appears as arrival was always already here. The treatise functions not as new revelation but as mirror showing what was overlooked.

**Stanza 11**  
སྤང་བླང་གནས་ཀུན་ཡིད་བཞིན་ལེགས་སྟོན་པ།  
ཆོས་ཚུལ་ཡོངས་རྫོགས་ཐོས་བསམ་སྒོམ་པའི་མཛོད།  
འདི་མཐོང་བསྟན་པ་ཀུན་གྱི་དེ་ཉིད་ཤེས།  
བརྒྱ་ཕྲག་གཞུང་ལ་ཅིག་ཆར་སྦྱངས་པར་འགྱུར།  

spang blang gnas kun yid bzhin legs ston pa  
chos tshul yongs rdzogs thos bsam sgom pa'i mdzod  
'di mthong bstan pa kun gyi de nyid shes  
brgya phrag gzhung la cig char sbayngs par 'gyur  

abandon-adopt place all wish-fulfilling well-show  
dharma-method all-complete hear-contemplate-meditate treasury  
this-see teaching all-of thatness know  
hundred-fold scripture-to immediate practice-become  

Wish-fulfilling guide to abandon-adopt places,  
Treasury of complete dharma—hearing, contemplation,  
Seeing this, know suchness of all teachings,  
Hundred scriptures practiced immediately.

Translator's Commentary: Abandon and adopt arise within single expanse—they never stood as separate territories requiring navigation. The treasury holds the entire path not as sequence to traverse but as recognition that collapses the seeker and sought. Seeing reveals suchness directly. A hundred scriptures practiced immediately because practice never stood apart from realization itself. No interval held between encounter and completion.

**Stanza 12**  
ཐེག་རྩེའི་དགོངས་དོན་ཚིག་དོན་བཅུ་གཅིག་ཏུ།  
ལེགས་བསྡུས་ཁ་ཚང་དོན་འདྲིལ་མན་ངག་གི  
ཉམས་ལེན་གནད་ཀྱི་མཛོད་གཅིག་སྲིད་པ་ན།  
སྲིད་པའི་རྩ་བ་གཅོད་ལ་གཅིག་ཏུ་དཔའ།  

theg rtse'i dgongs don tshig don bcu gcig tu  
legs bsdus kha tshang don 'dril man ngag gi  
nyams len gnad kyi mdzod gcig srid pa na  
srid pa'i rtsa ba gcod la gcig tu dpa'  

vehicle-peak intention-meaning word meaning ten one-to  
well condensed complete meaning penetrating pith-instruction-of  
practice essential treasury single existence-in  
existence root cut-to single courage  

Peak vehicle's intention—ten words to one,  
Pith instruction concise, meaning penetrating,  
Single treasury of essential practice here,  
Alone courageous cutting existence's root.

Translator's Commentary: Ten words collapse to one not by reduction but by revealing the single source from which all meaning flows. Meaning penetrates without effort because it never stood apart from what it means. The single treasury holds everything because nothing was ever excluded from this expanse. Cutting the root requires no blade—courage is simply seeing what never substantially anchored appearance.

**Stanza 13**  
ཁྱད་པར་གཉུག་མའི་སེམས་ཉིད་ཆོས་སྐུའི་དོན།
